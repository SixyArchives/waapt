Route 7 (Rivière Walk)

Making his way over to the crowd, Rex was a bit curious as to what was going on. Fires of Miracles? What on Earth could that be? Was it pertaining to some kind of Kalosian legend?

As he melded with the audience, he found himself getting a closer look at the girl. Or, rather, the two Pokémon with her. One was clearly a Houndoom, the Dark Pokémon looking out at the audience with an impassive stare. And the other was some kind of fox-like creature, its appearance reminding Rex greatly of Fennekin. It must have been its fully-evolved state, he reasoned to himself.

Grandstander Girl: As you all may know, Delphox is renowned for its mystical abilities.

She gestured to the fox, the Delphox in question taking a bow as attention shifted to it.

Grandstander Girl: But impressive as they may be, it is still only a Psychic-type, incapable of affecting a Dark-type with its power.

Well, that wasn't entirely true, Rex noted to himself. Even if it couldn't learn something like Miracle Eye, it could still deal minimal damage with its Fire attacks or attacks of other types.

Grandstander Girl: But my darling Kurama is different.

Still, this was a performance of some kind. She was likely exaggerating for effect.

Grandstander Girl: His power is more than capable of affecting a Houndoom. Be the first to witness the powers of a kitsune at work!

At her words, the Houndoom stepped forward, standing just before the Delphox as it looked out at the audience. The fox in turn produced a flaming stick from betwixt its tails, twirling the object before the audience's eyes.

Artist Georgia: Miracle flames?

Artist Pierre: This might make an interesting picture...

Rich Boy Orvault: Hmph. Just a plebeian's childish tricks.

The latter's words only elicited a grin from the girl, an explosion of fire suddenly appearing around her Houndoom at her Delphox's command. Rex and the audience watched entranced as the flames raged atop the hound's body, not a soul keeping their eyes on its trainer.

And then, before their very eyes, the fires evaporated, revealing...

Artist Georgia: I-It changed!
