Sinnoh North

-Jonathan strides forward to where snow has gathered around a small stone foundation. He drops to his knees in the snow and closes his eyes as he flashes back briefly to the same clearing in a very different time period-

Sunlight on the snow and laughter ringing out among the trees as sprays of snow are kicked up by a mon and his son romping about in the yard with wooden swords.

-Jonathan closes his eyes, tears slowly sliding down his face-

Jonathan: Faolan…

The smell of fresh cooking wafting out through open windows, leaning on the window sill to peer in at the woman moving about the small kitchen sectioned off from the rest of the house.

Jonathan: Ai—Aislin…

Holding a baby boy wrapped tightly in swaddling clothes, smiling warmly down at him as he sleeps.

Jonathan: Halim…

-Kori places a paw on his shoulder-

Kori: <Amhlaidh.>

-Jon grabs her paw and looks up at her, tears falling faster now-

Jonathan: <Ciara… Ciara, we left them…>

Kori: <Amhlaidh. Calm yourself.>

-Jon clutches her paw tighter, turning to face her-

Jonathan: How could we leave them?! My sons grew up without a father, all so I could serve a madman!

Kori: <Amhlaidh, you know that's not—>

Jonathan: Ciara, my son never knew his father. I spent eight years repaying a debt to a warlord while my youngest son went through childhood without me.

Kori: <Amhlaidh—>

Jonathan: And Faolan! He had to provide for his mother because I abandoned her! I left my Aislin alone with the boys to leave for some fool's errand, and I never returned! Did they ever forgive me? Could they ever forgive me…?

-Kori wrests her paw from his hands and clamps both paws onto his shoulders-

Kori: <Jonathan!>

-Jonathan starts and lowers his head-

Jonathan: I… I'm sorry.

Kori: <Jonathan, in all the longer years I have known you, I have never seen you lose yourself like this. Tell me, do you see me breaking down?>

Jonathan: N-no, but—

Kori: <But what? Do you think I don't miss my family?>

Jonathan: No! No… Arceus, I'm sorry, I should never have said—

Kori: <I lost my mate and daughter the same day you lost your wife and sons. Do you think you miss Halim or Faolan any more than I miss Rioghan? Or that you miss Aislin more than I miss Luan?>

Jonathan: No, Kori…

Kori: <The loss of my family eats away at me every bit the loss of yours eats away at you. We can mourn. But do not break down on me, Jonathan. You're stronger than that.>

Jonathan: …

-he sighs-

Jonathan: You're right. I'm sorry.

-Kori brushes a tear from his cheek, then hugs him tightly-

Kori: <Don't be. But remember, we do have family here. We've both lost a lot. But look at what we've gained.>

-Jonathan smiles and pulls back-

Jonathan: Of course. Lina, Crewe, all these people around us… and, of course, these Sneasel of yours.

Kori: <Speaking of which, we've kept Crewe waiting long enough, don't you think?>

-Jonathan looks up at Crewe, who is awkwardly standing at the edge of the clearing. She gives him an awkward smile and wave as she notices him looking at her. Jonathan nods and stands up-

Jonathan: Yes, of course.

-he turns his back on the foundation and walks back over to Crewe. After a pause, she wraps her arms around him in a tight hug. He hugs her back-

You can cry if you want. I don't mind.

Jonathan: Thank you, my dear, but I think I've done my crying for now. Shall we go see these Sneasel of yours, then?

-Crewe steps away from him-

Um… can I have a second here, actually? I'll meet you back up by the lake.

-Jonathan blinks, but nods-

Jonathan: Yes, of course… Come on, then, Kori.

I'll just be a sec.

-he nods again and starts walking back towards the lake, Kori following him. Crewe walks over to the foundation-
"Run away! They have stick technology!"
