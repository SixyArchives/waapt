Isolde's Cave

-Crewe is back in the cave, feeling a little better physically but extremely unnerved by the very careful attention Isolde has been paying her-

Isolde: <My Amhlaidh... he would get so frustrated whenever he was separated from his master. Like this one time, he was sent off on a scouting mission. That mission is a whole big story all by itself!>

You know, there seem to be a lot of stories.

Isolde: <Well of course there are! Amhlaidh and I had a long time together! Not nearly as long as I wish it had been, but we had some good years together.>

And... you want to tell me all of them.

Isolde: <Of course! You're the only one I've had to tell them to in... too long. And a descendant of Kori herself! I couldn't possibly let you miss any of them!>

-Crewe shifts uncomfortably-

Um... I don't suppose there's a way to tell them... faster?

Isolde: <...>

Well, I mean, if you want me to hear all of them—

Isolde: <Of course! Why didn't I think of it before? And this is much better! I couldn't possibly do justice to Amhlaidh, but this way... oh, it's perfect!>

Um, what—

-she's cut off as Isolde swoops down close and presses her arms to either side of her face. Crewe shivers from the cold radiating off of the Froslass, but notices that while she can feel the pressure of Isolde holding her face, she seems somewhat... insubstantial. She is distracted from this train of thought, however, as Isolde stares into her eyes, the Froslass's eyes glowing slightly. After a moment, Crewe finds herself standing in the main room of her mindscape. She notes that the shelves are empty of Pokeballs again, but a few odds and ends such as feathers and rocks have been added-

... Wait, why am I here?

Isolde: <I brought you here!>

Bwah!

-she jumps and turns to face Isolde, who looks rather pleased with herself-

But—how—

Isolde: <Years ago, I found a little disc, and when I experimented with it, I found myself learning a new technique that allows me to manipulate minds! You may have a headache for a bit.>

What—manipulate my mind?

Isolde: <Oh, I would never hurt you, Crewe! I just want to leave something here for you!>

What?

Isolde: <A copy of my memories! All of my memories of my Amhlaidh, for you to relive! It's much easier than telling stories, and more accurate!>

That's... uh... that's...

Isolde: <Wonderful! Of course, I can still tell the stories aloud, but this way you can experience them much better! Now, where were we?>

Um, if you're gonna tell stories again, can we do it somewhere besides my mind? Now I'll have to find somewhere to store even more memories... the town is gonna get crowded...

Isolde: <Oh, all right.>

-the mindscape fades from around them, and Crewe wakes up back in the cave. Isolde drifts back to her spot in front of the entrance-

Isolde: <There we go! Now, we were on that scouting mission...>
