-he goes quiet-

-Crewe waits for a few seconds, biting her lip-

... Well.

Clockwise: <... That is quite the question, Trainer.>

I know...

Clockwise: <Hm... there is really only one answer I could give you, but I somewhat doubt you will be satisfied.>

Still. Anything.

Clockwise: <Very well, then. The simple answer is this.>

-he fixes his eyes on Crewe's-

Clockwise: <I love you, Trainer. I love you so much that the mere thought that I would not return to your side was so unfathomable that I emerged for the first time outside of battle in many years.>

-Crewe gives him a long look-

But... why? What have I done to deserve a Pokemon like you, Clockwise?

-Clockwise considers this-

Clockwise: <... I couldn't tell you. Perhaps it is simply my nature. Or perhaps it is something about you. I am not particularly... emotional. Perhaps that is why I am what is reverted to when I am confused.>

I... I see. Thank you, Clockwise.

-he tilts his head-

Clockwise: <You do not look as if I've helped you, Trainer.>

I'm sorry.

-he tilts his head the other way-

Clockwise: <For what, Trainer?>

You wanted to help. I'm sorry.

Clockwise: <... How odd.>

... What?

Clockwise: <Hm. Perhaps I do understand, somewhat.>

Understand what?

-he shakes his head-

Clockwise: <I'm afraid I can't adequately explain.>

-he pauses-

Clockwise: <Perhaps you should recall me, Trainer. I believe you may have more success speaking with me after I have... returned to myself.>

... All right.

-she takes his Poke ball, hesitates, then lays a hand on his beak-

I love you too, Clockwise. Even like this.

-Clockwise tilts his head back the other way, gives her a long look, then leans forward and taps his beak on the Pokeball, recalling himself-
