Mountains S of Rt.36, some days ago

(About the same time as this post)

<Up! keep up! Or down, for that matter...>

A group of Nidorino are sprinting down a rocky ladder, accompanied by some Zigzagoon, Loudred, Furret and a very nonrandom Jolteon, after finishing their morning exercises. The lead Nidorino awaits atop a rock for the last members of the group to catch up then follows them to a nearby creek where they are all heading.

After some chitchat, resting and drinking the Pokémon scramble up, most of them going their own ways; most of them more confident now in looking interesting to passerby Trainers, some others worried about the chores of the day such as collecting Berries. The nonrandom Jolteon goes reunite with a small group of Nidorino.

<Hey, Pierce!> greets one of the Nidorino waving a paw. <More training for Double Kick today?>

<Yes!> barks the Jolteon. <I want to learn it well.>

Another Nidorino also chimes in. <Eevee boy is doing well, [Terio]. We could use more Eevees around here.>

Maekrite walks up and carefully pokes Pierce. <You are not tired.> Seeing the Jolteon simply smile and blink, the Nidorino shrugs. <Oh well we can do more training then.>

The group marched to the north, towards a lower, more forested ground; they didn't talk much except for Maekrite and Pierce talking about Sludge Bomb. This lasted for a couple of minutes until the Pokémon found some good trees to practice at.

<Try not to tumble these down, guys?> pointed out Terio a bit annoyed. <Ametsa chided us pretty badly because of the trees we tore down at the eastern side.>

<We will be more careful, > answered one of the Nidorino bowing.

The group arranged themselves in pairs for training. One Nidorino would stand near the tree as a support for his partner, who would run at the first to leap on the first as on a stepladder and gain some altitude and momentum for the attack. Maekrite and Pierce were paired together, with Maekrite resting on the grass, serving as the springboard.

Pierce charged at the tree several times. <Wheeeeee~> was his sort-of-battlecry. Each time he would hit the tree with his foreleg in the midst of a leap, then use the momentum to propel himself backwards to the ground. The first few times he miscalculated and ended up noming grass or sitting in a ridiculous pose, but as time passed he acquired more confidence and proficency.

Maekrite did not comment much on this though; he mostly limited to stare at Terio practising with his partner, painful memories of another timeline surging every once in a while, in particular when he heard the other Nidorino randomly commenting on his everyday life.

After Maekrite spent some time distracted on some thoughts he could not sort out, he felt a stinging sensation in his leg. Growling, he turned around to see Pierce sat besides him, poking his leg insistently.

<Big brother! How am I doing?> the Jolteon inquired.

<Uhm... y-you are doing-> Maekrite turned his head to examine the tree. <Ah yes, very well.>

<Really? You think my backflips are nice?>

Maekrite turned towards Pierce and blinked; the gesture was met with a certain disapproval by the Jolteon, who leaned his head down and changed his tone slightly. <You are not paying attention to me, big brother?> he asked.

Maekrite shook his head. <S-sorry, Pierce... it's just that I-I think I forgot something.> The Nidorino faced to the ground. <And I can not remember what it is.>

Pierce paced around for a moment, looking in all directions, then he came to sit by Maekrite again. <Something important?> he asked. He was met with a halfhearted nod.

Back from the other members of the group there was a short clamor for nap time, to which the lead Nidorino agreeded and called the group to gather at a larger tree to rest under the shade. Pierce and other Nidorinos were commenting on their technique.

<Hey, Maekrite, we never told you of Arakosa, right?> one of the Nidorino asked.

The Nidorino's ears flickered. <...Arakosa? I don't know that name.> He went to sit with the group.

<Ah yes, he was my parli...> answered one of the random Nidorino in the group. <His family migrated from the [Viridian Forest] years ago, when his own parli was a rooli. And he told Arakosa that Double Kick training was very different back there...>

<Really?> Maekrite and Pierce inquired, their interest piqued.

The Nidorino nodded. <Many years ago the training of Double Kick took place in a very secluded part of the forest, parli told me.> He pointed towards the deeper part of the forest. <Because Fighting moves were sought after to beat a nearby Gym by commencing Trainers, the colonies back there held their training of Double Kick as a secret, so Trainers wouldn't know that we could learn that attack until very late in our lives... thus they would not chase us and catch us.>

<That sounds mean.... but yeah, > muttered Maekrite, <we have done things like that.>

<Yeah. The secret was held for several generations, giving the impression that we took years to learn such kind of attacks. But parli said, it all ended one day when a human in black clothes entered the forest.>

Maekrite's ears picked the comment, as did most of the other Nidorino.

<He followed the Nidoran, ventured into the forest. Then he caught some of them and studied them, until he discovered we could learn that attack very early in our lifetimes.> He gave a weird look at the group. <Parli said the man wrote an evil book so that everyone would know. And after that, all the annoying human toddlers would chase and catch us so that they could beat that Gym easily.>

<Who was the bad man?> asked Pierce, not catching much of the reference.

Maekrite leaned towards the Jolteon and spoke to his ear. Pierce let out an annoyed grunt.

<Aaaah, I remember now....> spoke Terio, scratching his ears. <We got a migration of Nidoran from Kanto that year.>

<Yeah, grandpa came here with them, eventually... he said the only reason he resented moving was because mates here are way too demanding.>

There were some veiled laughs across the group as they pawbumped and stuff. Only Pierce and Maekrite did not laugh with the group. The Jolteon simply blinked, savoring the moment; the Nidorino had suddenly paled.

<Something happened, Maekrite?> asked Terio.

<Uh...> Maekrite shuddered, avoided the gaze of the others. <I remembered I have to go... fetch Berries later.>
