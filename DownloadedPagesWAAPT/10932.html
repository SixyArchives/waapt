Pugilis Dungeon, 600 years ago

Amhlaidh: I do not know everything my lord knows. I would not be surprised to learn that he does know I am here, but I do not believe he does.

Isolde's Cave, now

Isolde: <The time in the dungeons was terrible. I didn't know what was happening to him... I hated not seeing him I still do>

-she drifts closer, uncomfortably close-

Isolde: <You look so much like him>

-Crewe inches away from her-

I dont know why.

Isolde: <Neither do I But how can you look human at all, dear, and also be a Sneasel?>

Well I do know that one. Its quite the story.

Isolde: <Isnt that what were doing, dear? Telling stories?>

Uh, all right I guess its my turn to tell one

-she clears her throat-

Well, I was a Sneasel first, but not for very long. Soon after I was bornit might have even been the day I was born, Im not suremy parents died. Im not sure how, and I dont know where my siblings were, but I got lost. I wouldve died, but, and thisll sound a little crazy a Mew found me, and turned me into a human child. There was a couple living in Twinleaf Townthats a small town in Sinnoh, a long way away from herethat wanted to have kids, but couldnt. So she left me with them, and they raised me like their own. I never knew I was any different, except Ive always had this terrible fear of Legendary Pokemon. There were a few other things different about me, but they never really came to light.

-she fidgets a little-

About a year or so ago, I was in Hoenn with some friends of mine. While I was there, we found a Mew messing with the local Pokemon. We confronted her, and she told us about me.

-she swallows and looks away, a pained and guilty look on her face-

I didnt take it well. I caused a lot of trouble for everyone around me. I still feel like I owe people apologies. Not long after, I left the group and found my way back up north. I lived there for a while with other Sneasel, but I couldnt stay there. I rejoined the group again, and Ive been traveling with them.

Isolde: <Yes, yes but your face, dear. Where do you get your looks?>

Oh uh well, I look a lot like my dad. My step dad, I mean. Mew did a pretty good job making me look like I was the kid of the two humans she left me with. I guess she is the genetics mon or something like that.

Isolde: <Your father? So then he must be he must be a descendant of my Amhlaidh! Where is he now?>

-Crewes face hardens-

Hes dead. He died when I was young.

Isolde: <Oh thats so sad weve both lost him, then>

-she drifts closer to Crewe again, laying one of her arms against her cheek. Again, Crewe feels that she is not quite substantial, even compared to other Ghosts-

Isolde: <But thats all right we have each other and we always will, forever. I promise.>

-Crewes face snaps around and she clambers to her feet-

Whoa, whoa, forever? No, I cant stay here much longer. I have friends and family waiting for me. I need to go.

-Isolde moves between her and the cave entrance. Her eyes glow slightly-

Isolde: <Now, now, dont be hasty, Crewe. I can keep you here by force if I really must, but dont you want to stay with me? Dont you want to hear my stories?>

-Crewe is starting to sweat, despite the frigid air in the cave-

Look, the stories have been real interesting, and its been nice meeting you, but Im leaving. Ill fight you if I have to.

-she reaches to her neck, but finds the string holding her pendant gone. Panicked, she looks around for it, and sees it floating beside Isolde-

Isolde: <No, Im afraid you wont be. Calm down, dear, theres no need for violence. Sit back down, and I can keep telling the story.>

-Crewe clenches her fists-

No. Im leaving, Isolde. Give me back my necklace.
