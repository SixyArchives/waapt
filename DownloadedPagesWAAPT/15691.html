PEFE HQ

Fool is wandering the halls of the large facility, chatting with her aide along the way.

Fool: ...So that was how the first half of my holiday went.

Matthew: Oh! That sounds quite pleasant! Er... Though I imagine how hard the last part was on you...

Fool briefly looks down.

Fool: Yeah...

Matthew gives his senior a worried look, before patting her on the shoulder.

Matthew: There, there. It's best you found out the way you did rather than in some sort of... unsavoury condition (Beat) So what are you going to do when you inevitably meet him again?

Fool looks out a nearby window.

Fool: I'm going to cut him off completely. Treat him like a stranger. I honestly want nothing more to do with him after he played me like a Arceusdamned harpsichord.

There is an awkward silence as Matthew pulls at the collar of his shirt, but that silence is later broken by the buzzing of Fool's X-transceiver

Fool: Hmm? (She checks the message)

Matthew looks at at Fool curiously.

Matthew: Who's the message from?

Fool: ...

Matthew: Sol-

Fool: Sorry Matty. Just hold that train of thought. I need to come up with a suitable reply.

And with that, she starts typing.

To: Crewe
From: Solana@PEFE
(PS: Fool here. This is my work address.)

First of all: All things considered, net gains against net losses, I think that spilling some of your most personal details to a rather morally ambiguous syndicate that is known to house a few... Unsavoury individuals is a bad and highly dangerous move. So that is a hell no from me

Secondly: ...I'm sure Echo seems nice. Pleasant, cordial, charming, somewhat mysterious with possibly a few skeletons in the closet but overall, someone who is just misunderstood. That is the image they present of themselves as a cover to win over the opinion of others. They may even become close friends to you, when you feel that you can ignore the red flags and trust them with some of your dearest secret.

But what happens when their facade cracks or you finally see how they act when you are not around? For an institution such as Illusion which seems to be able to let in any snake who can spin a good sob story I don't think Echo is all who she presents to be. Any secret information you spill could be used against us, putting us in very real. What do you know will happen when the smoke and mirrors are cleared?

Trust me, Crewe. I have fallen for this trick before, and Arceus knows how many people I've endangered with the secrets I've shared. Don't make the same mistake I did.

And with that, she presses send.

Matthew: Finished now?

Fool: Indeed. As for who it was, I'd rather not say at this moment.

Matthew: Ah, I see. (Beat) Want ice cream or gelato?

Fool: Matt, you know me by now. What is the answer to that?

Matthew: That's one double scoop Blood Sitrus Sorbet gelato for you and a single scoop Cookies and Cream ice-cream for me then.

Fool: Damn straight sonny boy.

Matthew: Right. I'll be back in a jiffy, with the sweet bounty in tow.

Matthew starts walking down the hall, but stops and turns back to face Fool.

Matthew: By the way, you said you gave your boyfriend that Litwick Egg for his birthday, wrapped up in that cloak. Right?

Fool: (Nodding) Yes

Matthew: Well, it's just Satine doesn't strike me as the type to do any breeding... Unless it was with Lifealope.

Fool: She's most certainly not that type, yes.

Matthew: Then how did she produce that egg?

Fool merely raises an eyebrow at Matthew, which sends him all the information he needs to know.

Matthew: How does that even work!?

Fool: I don't know, I'm not too sure what Lifealope even is. I don't question half the things he does. Siring children with a Pokemon outside of his perceived egg group is one of them.

Matthew blinks, before turning around and walking down the stairs. Still rather confused.
