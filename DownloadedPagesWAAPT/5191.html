Oreburgh Gym (The Future...Last Night)

Rex fiddled with his gloves absently as he mulled things over, thinking quickly over his past experience with Rock-type leaders to consider how to best deal with the threat at hand in the event that Claudia was fainted.

'Okay, factoring in everything I know about Rock-type Gym Leaders, their signature move is usually something that causes damage, inhibits their opponent's movement, or, more commonly, a combination of both. Brock had Bide for a while, then switched to Rock Tomb, and finally Rock Slide. If I'm not mistaken, I heard one of the Leaders in Hoenn also had Rock Tomb. So, following that logic, Roark should have something like-'

"Larvitar, use Stealth Rock!"

'...Oh, son of a bitch!'

Larvitar began to spin, pointed stones ripping themselves up from the Gym floor and hovering around its Castform foe. She looked them over nervously, but eased up somewhat when she saw they weren't coming in for an attack...yet.

"Oh, that's going to be trouble, " Rex groaned, putting a gloved palm to his forehead. "...Well, hopefully I won't need to switch. Claudia, Weather Ball!"

A cloudy gray orb shot from Claudia's mouth, shooting into the air and hovering about the still spinning Larvitar. It paused for a brief moment, absorbing some of the still falling rain. Then, it fired a stream of water at the creature, the Larvitar wailing in discontent as it was struck directly. It flailed its tiny limbs in protest, growling angrily. Then it slumped to the ground, fainting weakly as the spray finally ended. However, the threat it had presented to the field was still in place...and Roark still had two Pokemon left.

"Not bad!" Roark said with a smile. "But the real fight starts now!"

Onix roared and floored Claudia with its tail smack, sending her sprawling to the ground.

<It's...always...sunny in Lilycove~ @_@> she croaked, barely hanging on. The effects of Dragon Tail sent her back into her ball, dragging Ponyta out again in her place.

<This is really get ol-> BAM!

"Actually, Minty doesn't know Solarbeam, " Rex admitted, a grin spreading across his face. "But she does."

Roark's eyes widened in alarm, realizing the mistake he'd just made. "Onix, Drago-"

"Ponyta, Solarbeam!"

With a whinny of frustration, the equine opened her maw wide, her back flaring brilliantly in the pseudo-sunlight. A beam of concentrated NATURE shot from her jaws, slamming into the Onix. It roared in horrible agony, the 4x effective move piercing into its very core. Then, it exploded into several boulders, scattering across the gym floor in pieces.

Normally, a sight such as that would've caused Rex to panic, as it had some years back. But, as he had become well aware, Onix could sometimes fall apart when fainted. They did, after all, have a magnetic signal that kept them held together. Sure enough, Roark seemed only slightly bothered by his creature's collapse, recalling the head into his Pokeball.

"Not bad, " he admitted. "But I still have my ace."
