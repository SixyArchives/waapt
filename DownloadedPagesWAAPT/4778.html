Rex's Mindscape

With a scowl, Rex turned his back on the vision of his father, folding his arms petulantly.

Rex: You're not real. I don't know what you are exactly...but you aren't my father.

The image of his father scoffed, shaking his head.

Rex's Father: I see knack talent for pointing out the obvious hasn't left you. Tell me, what was the point of hiring tutors to educate you if you insist on making the most asinine of observations? Why even bother speaking if you have nothing to contribute to a meaningful conversation?

Rex: ...

Rex scowled, turning back to him with a glare.

Rex: What do you want?

Rex's Father: I was hoping that you'd understand by now that this "adventure" as you call it was a waste of time.

Rex: You're wrong! I've changed a lot of things about myself over the course of my journey! I've become wiser as I'v-

Rex's Father: Changed? Wiser?

He laughed, a hollow bitter laugh devoid of any humor.

Rex's Father: Tell me, Rex, what have you learned? That ice can freeze over grass? That you can get a government grant to study a few Pidgey for an hour or two?

He shook his head, turning his cold eyes back to his son.

Rex's Father: Whatever you've learned is immaterial compared to what I offered you. These little games you play with your Pokemon, mindlessly going from Point A to Point B...they mean nothing in the long run. How will you provide for yourself? What will you leave behind? Are you so content with being a nameless vagabond that you are incapable of realizing that there is a greater life for you? I offer you the chance to have your own business. To be among the true elite, not some fools who still dress up in capes. You could own your own Silph Co, making millions off of these manchildren stuck in their fantasy land!

Rex: ...You could never understand. You never did understand.

Rex's Father: So enlighten me. What have you done with yourself since you left home? Collected all of the badges in Johto?

Rex: ...

Rex's Father: What about Kanto?

Rex: ...

Rex's Father: Learned how to ride a bike? What about cooking? Surely, you've done something useful with the peanuts you collect from your little battles?

Rex: ...

Rex's Father: No. You haven't. You are single-mindedly fixated on this ridiculous notion that you can one day be a Champion. And what then, Rex? What will you do as Champion? What does the position entail?

He circled his son, sneering at his inability to answer.

Rex's Father: Tell me, would a Champion cry helplessly while his friends laid helpless on the ground?

Rex: ...

Rex's Father: At least that one boy had the decency to fight against the forces that wronged him...albeit it in a primitive and mindless way. But what did you do? Talk?

He placed his hands on Rex's shoulder, staring intently at the boy's face. Rex merely lowered his head, trying desperately not to let his father see just how much his comments were getting to him. He blinked back tears, swallowing the lump in his throat.

Rex's Father: She paid you no mind, did she? And do you know why? It's simple. You don't fit in with them. You simply lack the capacity.

He shook Rex, trying to get him to meet his gaze.

Rex's Father: Do you own your own "Legendary"? Have you ever "saved" a region? Is your fanclub more than just a few feeble-minded souls who simply flock towards the nearest thing that sparks their deluded romantic fantasies? You have come from greatness, and yet you lower yourself to fit in with a group whose actions mean nothing! Do you understand how foolish you've been!?

When he received no answer, he sighed, withdrawing his hands and turning his back to Rex.

Rex's Father: I suppose I'll leave you with that thought. Presumably, it'll be the first one you've had since you left on this childish endeavor.

Without another word, the image of Rex's father stepped into the darkness, leaving the boy alone to stare into the abyss.
