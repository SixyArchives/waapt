PEFE HQ - Library Module

Of all the places that he had expected Comet to be, the last Treeo would have thought to look in would be the PEFE's Library.

Yet he had been told by quite a few of the other members of the team that it was where the Charizard currently was that he'd thought he might as well take a look.

As he entered the huge module, gazing in awe at the rows upon rows of bookshelves stacked on top of each other, all bursting with books and tomes of knowledge. Even in the days of the internet, it was still a fantastic sight.

But that wasn't what the Sceptile was here for.

Treeo: <Comet?>

<SSH!>

Treeo jumped in surprise, turning to face the front desk where the Librarian was sitting. Realizing that he was supposed to keep quiet in a Library, he flashed an apologetic yet awkward grin, then set off in search of his friend.

It didn't take too long for him to track Comet down. Treeo eventually came across her sat down at a desk, pouring over various different maps of the world.

Very strange.

Treeo: <Comet? What are you doing?>

Gazing up from studying a map of the Sinnoh region, the Charizard glanced back over her shoulder at him.

Comet: <Well, I was going to surprise you, but the truth is? I'm learning to knit so I can make little mittens so my claws don't get cold.>

An awkward pause followed.

<What does it look like I'm doing?>

Treeo frowned, her blunt mannerism tipping him off to the fact that Comet seemed a little stressed.

Treeo: <It's not like you to be doing research. You don't like Library's because their too flammable...>

Comet: <The Librarian shares my sentiment. I had to sneak past her to get in.>

Treeo: <You keep telling me that you don't like reading because there's much better things you could be doing with your time.>

Cocking his head, the Sceptile frowned.

<And you can't even read!>

Dismissively, Comet rolled her eyes, then went back to scanning the map she had open.

Comet: <Reading words and reading maps aren't the same thing, plus I know enough geography to know where everything is without having to read the names.>

Treeo: <Alright...so, why are you doing this?>

Comet: <Because of Cresselia, Treeo.>

Treeo: <Cresselia?>

Once again, Comet stopped to look at Treeo.

Comet: <Don't you remember? The reason why all those Dream Rifts were opening up in Unova was because somebody had made Cresselia by experimenting on her?>

She stopped briefly to snort in annoyance, an angry black puff of smoke escaping from her snout.

<It's disgusting. And as a member of the Dream Guard, it's my job...no, my duty to find out who did this and show them that nobody - neither human nor Pokémon - messes with our world to and gets away with it.>

Treeo: <Oh. I understand, but don't you think you could take a little break for a while, at least? I haven't seen you since breakfast. You must have been in here for hours!>

Pausing to sigh, Comet shook her head, sadly.

Comet: <I can't just take a break. It took me five years too long to finally get on the Dream Guard. I'm done letting peoplemons down, so I'm not going to rest until I find this strange land that Cresselia said these guys were hiding in!>

Treeo: <...Okay. If you're not going to stop then, can I least help?>

Treeo gave Comet a warm smile.

<You know what the humans say. "Many hands make light work." Or rather...>

He held his arms up, showing the Charizard his pair of claws.

<It's many claws in our case. Or rather...a few. Should have gone with a different saying.>

Comet: <...Okay. If you wanna' help, feel free. I was getting kinda' lonely, anyway.>

Treeo: <Cool.>

As the Sceptile sat himself down next to Comet, she gave him a questionable look.

Comet: <You know, sometimes I wonder why you put up with somemon as stubborn as me.>

He ended up responding with a rather sly smirk.

Treeo: <Because you're worth the hassle. Most of the time, anyway.>

Shocked, Comet blinked in surprise, narrowing her eyes at the Sceptile.

Comet: <WHAT?!>

<SSSSSSH!!!>

She froze, feeling put on the spot by the Librarian's sudden order to keep quiet. Shooting Treeo a foul look, the Sceptile simply chuckled quietly.

Dragonspiral Tower

After a fun day in nearby Opelucid, our..."Hero", Stratus, had flown over to the nearby Dragonspiral Tower. Because if he was in the neighborhood, it would be a waste not to do a little sightseeing.

Landing on the bank surrounding the moat, the Dragonite gazed up at the tower, an impressed look on his face.

Stratus: <Wow. Hard to believe something so old is still standing. And not only that, but it's home to a pair of legendary Pokémon, too!>

He paused to ponder something for a few moments.

<I wonder if they still remember me? Or if Rayquaza's told them about us being best buddies? Maybe I should go up and a->

<HEY!>

The Dragonite jumped in shock, turning around to find a Druddigon behind him, which gave him even more of a fright. Well, they were pretty intimidating.

Stratus: <EEP! I WAS JUST LOOKING, THAT'S ALL!>

The nettledragon blinked, having not expected the Dragonite to react in such a way.

Druddgion: <...Okay, but what are you doing out of the moat? Your shift isn't over yet!>

Stratus: <Shift?>

Somewhat annoyed now, the Druddgion frowned.

Druddgion: <By the Gods, did you miss the last meeting? We specifically went over your timetable! During the Summer, moat dragons like you work from mid-morning until sunset, with a break for lunch at mid-day! And since lunch was an hour ago, it's clear that you must have been out here slacking off!>

Flustered, Stratus raised a claw, shaking nervously, as anybody would be if they were being accused of shirking a job they didn't even have.

Stratus: <L-look, let me explain...>

Druddigon: <Don't. You've already wasted enough time as it is! Just get back to your post before you make anything worse and I'll have to dock your meals. Understand?>

It didn't look like that Stratus would be getting away from this Druddigon without being shouted at, and he was far too intimidated to explain that he didn't work here, so he just nodded nervously.

Stratus: <O-okay! I'll get back to my p-post, right n-now!>

Satisfied, the Druddigon nodded.

Druddigon: <Good. Go on, chop-chop!>

Watching to make sure he did what he was told, Stratus nervously walked over to the side of the moat, then jumped in, diving underwater. With the Dragonite gone, the Druddigon raised his claws in dismay.

<You just can't get the staff these days...>
