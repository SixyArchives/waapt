But, and Im going to get a little personal here, thats the essence of life, isnt it?

Sometimes you go through things that seem huge at the time, like a mysterious glowing cloud devouring your entire community. While theyre happening they feel like the only thing that matters, and you can hardly imagine that theres a world out there that might have anything else going on.

Martin sits near the edge of the water, staring out over its surface.

Helps me think.

He was writing in his own personal notebook; the one he had previously used as a dream journal.

But the dreams had stopped, just like that.

He didn't know why.

But they had.

He tore the pages out; the mad ramblings of a mind ravaged by a plague of confusing nightmares. Fears, insecurities.

He cast them all out into the water.

No more.

He gripped the notebook tightly. A blank page stared him in the face.

"Now what?" He said aloud.

He felt confused. Alone. Lost.

He looked out in the sky.

"Now what?" He repeated.

It could be that someone would find him sitting there; someone who would know what to say to him. Or someone to even just take his mind off of things.
