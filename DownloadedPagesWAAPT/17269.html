Rawford Park, past

Locke and Geo walked closely together, Geo's nostrils flared as he kept smelling the rusty whiff of blood in the air, Locke doing as best as he could to follow the trail of blood. They looked around the park, in a bit of winter, so there was slight tints of frost all about. The air seemed nice and pleasant, and the park was filled with people and mons, happily bustling about and playing. Geo flinched as he was hit in the back of the leg, but then turned to see it was just a frisbee, thrown by a sheepish child with a Herdier. With a little smile, he tossed it back, nodding to the Herdier."<Keep a watch on this boy, kind sir.>" The Herdier just grinned right back at him as they left.

The park seemed tranquil and pleasant, with it's chirping Pidoves and Fletchlings, the laughing voices, it's calm atmosphere rather dissonant to the grumbling unease in Locke's stomach...

Then, chills ran up their spines as they heard a howling, which seemed to be emanating from a broken down old store room a mile away, and the howling seemed to get louder and louder as they ran faster and faster, not noticing that the trail was going the same way, bursting through the door right after the howling ceased...
