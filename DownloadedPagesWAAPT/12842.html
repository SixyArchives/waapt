Svavel: "<My parents... I have no idea who my parents are. Or were, as the case may be. I was the chick of an extensive Pokémon breeding programme, which means my parents were almost certainly killed in cold blood before I had even hatched. That's what these professional breeders did - they slaughtered adult Pokémon to get their hands on the eggs.

I spent my entire childhood in a tiny metal room. I remember what it was like clear as day. There were no windows, just these cold neon strips hanging miles above your head. The food was minimal - tap water, and this weird-tasting sludge which was almost certainly made of liquidised anabolic steroids. Every day, these humans in starched suits would come in. They'd spent half an hour or so filling my head with this nihilistic bullshit - how nicety was irrelevant, and how the only thing that mattered was proving yourself in combat, and yadda yadda yadda. Then they'd release some unlucky Pokémon, always a level or two lower than me, and I would sic it until the poor thing was barely breathing. Sometimes even past that... (tears up a little, then composes himself) Arceus, less than two minutes and I'm already confessing to murder. This is going really bloody well.

After a year or two of this, the higher-ups decided I was strong enough to be sold on. So the starch monsters bundled me into the exact same cage my opponents had spent their last few minutes on Earth in, threw it in the back of a dusty old SUV, and drove off to sell me to a pet shop in Rustboro. The car was boiling, but I'm a Fire-type, I like extreme heat. What perturbed me was the world I saw through the windows. All these wonderful colours, all these buildings and plants and people and free, happy Pokémon, and they'd never told me they were there? The... (grimaces, sobs slightly, then composes himself) Urgh. Let's just continue.

Anyway, I reached the store without incident. In hindsight, I should have used Ember and blown the SUV up with me inside - 'twould have spared a lot of heartache for me, and nobody was gonna miss the starch suits. But I just sat back and waited, and eventually, a kid who looked a bit like that - (gestures to Alex, who's flicking through his book of art) - came in and bought me. He had no social skills and even less English, but he was rich and liked picking Pokémon battles, which as far as I knew were the only traits a Trainer needed. (grins slightly) His English has improved greatly. His interactions with others haven't. (face falls as he goes serious again)

So anyway, this little Torchic was the first Pokémon Alex ever owned. I was quite cute, apparently! And I wasn't at all malicious back then. I didn't have a personality at all, really, since battling was all I knew. But as he travelled around, met people, found two friends for me - (gestures to Spalje and Vattenfallet) - I... changed. By the time I was a Combusken... (biiig long sigh)

...I was a jerk. I was a sadistic, amoral jerk with a knife dipped in venom where most Mons have a tongue, and I knew it. And I was proud of it. It all stemmed from what I saw as a totally logical argument... see, all those peoplemons, they seemed so happy. With few exceptions, they were always laughing and smiling. And all of them, they'd talk about how great life was and how much they loved their Pokémon... and it made me sick! I was right there! I was living proof that life wasn't so fucking great! These people... (having slowly grown louder and louder, he clears his throat and restarts in a much calmer tone) They just didn't seem to care that I'd been raised in blood and pain right under their noses. Nope, it was fine, everything was sunshine and roses, the world was soooo lurvely. Fuck that, I thought, no beings so naïve should get respect! If anything, they should get the opposite!

And then, we come to Orre. Alex was hunting Ivy's ghost, which, if I'm not mistaken, we saw in the Colosseum a few hours ago. As usual, I was having none of it - human ghosts! Pah! I didn't even think Ghost-type Pokémon were the spirits of dead peoplemons, why did he expect me to believe in full-blown undead wanderers? I thought it was ridiculous.

Naturally, seeing Ivy Amarin standing right there was a huge blow. What the fuck - I was Svavel! I was never wrong! And then, within that same instant... a horrible thought occurred to me: If I was wrong about her, what else had I been wrong about all these years? It was more than an ego-driven Mon like me could bear... hence, my reaction upon seeing her.

As soon as we got to the Pokémon Centre, I nicked one of those free netbooks and did some browsing on every single thing that defined my worldview. Some were right, but a good deal more were wrong... heck, it was the first time I learned about human murderers. If the first human I'd met was despondent because some psycho had just killed their Daddy, I would've turned out differently, mark my words.

Then, the most important thing of all happened. It started off simple enough - I decided to look up my own past, so I Googled "pokemon breeding programmes" and read the results that came up. What I discovered was that, a year after they'd shipped me off to Rustboro, some intrepid folks had finally found out what these evil shits were doing to innocent Pokémon. And when they leaked their findings, the rage began. Death threats through mail, petitions to the Government, protest marches, mail-bombs with Team Plasma's insignia on them - the ol' starch suits were getting it all! A year and a half ago, the whole thing culminated in an official ban on their particular method of Pokémon breeding; all the business's bigwigs were thrown in prison, and so far, not one has resurfaced. It was a... (starts sobbing slightly, then composes himself and gives Dune a Tearful Smile; Spalje's jaw drops open at what he perceives as a huge out-of-character moment)

...they cared. It was that simple. They just didn't know what had happened to me, and when they found out, they gave the Men In Black their just desserts. It was a wonderful revelation... but also a terrible one, because if they really did care, then I'd been giving them abuse for no reason whatsoever. Throughout my entire life.>"

(Svavel rises up to his full height, which is easily more than six feet. He extends a claw to Dune.)

Svavel: "<So, with you as my messenger, I sincerely apologise to your entire species. Here's hoping I can start again on better grounds.>"
