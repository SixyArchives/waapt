Gaston: The lady who opposed me at this moment was beautiful. Long, elegant blue hair with calming teal eyes and soft, round features and dressed in the finest sea-coloured silks that Ignis could offer, I could only assume her as the wife of the Ignis lord. The beautiful silver necklace she wore only accentuated her beauty. However, her tone did not match her appearance. By how she acted around me, she seemed sharp and intelligent as well as cold and aloof. I could tell that she was irritated by my presence, but from what I've heard she was irritated by everyone. There was another thing that seemed to stand out from the pack too, she looked quite different from her maids-in-waiting. Maybe she was from a foreign land like me? I could not help but to inquire.

Gaston: -Nervously- Ah... You must be.. the mistress of the house?

Izumi: That I am. You may refer to me as Izumi Tomoe if you wish.

Gaston: I see... -Pause- You don't look like you're... from here.

Gaston: At this point, she broke eye contact with me and glanced off to the side. Her teeth forming a restrained but feral snarl and her fingers idly fiddling with the pendant on her necklace. I must of touched a nerve, it seemed like her heritage was a bit of a sore spot. I decided not to press further with matters of geological origins.

Izumi: -Sharply- Let's go back to discussing trade like we planned, okay?

Gaston: -Retracting- Er.. Alright. Just one more question.

Izumi: -Sighing- What?

Gaston: Where is the Fire Lord? Surely he will be joining us in such an important matter of his kingdom such as trade?

Gaston: The look she gave me was like if I asked the stupidest question on earth, and that she was wondering if I was worth her time and breath.

Izumi: -Deadpanning- Ryuku is too busy with his... extentious... plans at the moment to be bothered with such things as trade. Hence why you're talking to me. But enough questions.

Izumi beckons over her maids, and orders them to pour both her and her guest a cup of Jasmine Tea.

Izumi: -Sipping Tea- We're here to talk about trade.

Gaston: So it's at this point we actually started discussing trade'.

Izumi: -Placing cup down and glares at Gaston- What can you offer us?

Gaston: -Intimidated- Um.. Well... We have rare and exotic furs and live Pokemon we could trade you that are only found in Kalos. Likewise we can provide you with herbs that only grow on some native pokemon.

Izumi: -Groans- That's all well an done, but every country that wants to trade with us can offer those. What can you offer that's new.

Gaston: -Gulps- Well. There is... um... oh! We could offer you ores of coal, silver and lead! We have some rather large deposits of them back in Kalos.

Izumi: -Interested- I'm listening.

Gaston: We can also produce, for you, Kalosian styled jewelry and textiles. Sure to be exotic in a nation like Ransei.

Izumi: -Smirking- Well the, maybe we can strike a deal after all. Now what do you want from us?

Gaston: I started discussing with her the bounties of Ransei that my father back in Kalos was so interested in. Mainly in the resources of fine teas, silks and gunpowder. All this while, Izumi was gazing at me. Spiing her tea and making hums of approval or grunts of disapproval. Though, in the middle of the meeting, a messenger came in and interrupted our talk.

Messanger: I am sorry to interrupt the meeting going on your ladyship. -Bows- But urgent news has come from both Spectra and our Fontaine boarders. I need to deliver these messages to Lord Ryuku this moment.

Izumi yawns and holds out her hand.

Izumi: -Glaring- Lord Ryuku, servant, wishes not to be disturbed at the moment. If you wish to lose your head. Go on. Go into his chambers. If you wish to live another day, you would give those messages to me.

The messenger, quite scared by Izumi's talk and actions, hands over the messages.

Messenger: Err... Yes ma'am... Sorry for my insolence...

Izumi: And in the future, if your receive any more messages for my husband, you will bring them directly to me. Alright?

Messenger: ...Yes madame -Bows-

Izumi: Good. You may leave. -Izumi makes a gesture and the messenger leaves the room quickly.

Izumi turns back to Gaston and puts on a face of insincere disappointment.

Izumi: I am so sorry that we cannot continue this conversation tonight. But I have duties of the nation to attend to, and it's getting late on top of that. -Claps and beckons her maids to surround her- Chikiko will show you to your lodgings for the time you will be spending here. As for I, I shall retreat into my own chambers -yawns again- See you tomorrow, Gaston.

Izumi leaves the room and her chambermaids follow. The one remaining, Chikiko, walks over to Gaston and bows in front of him.

Chikiko: Follow me sir.

Gaston: 'I must say, for my first meeting with Izumi. She terrified me. It seemed clear to me that the power politics in the Ignis house were much more complicated than anticipated, and that the person who I THOUGHT was ruling the nation didn't seem to be that important at all. Though I was curious, what were the messages about that were urgent enough to stop our meeting. I didn't think too much about it though, for I was more concerned with finally being able to sleep on dry land for the first time in months.
