Terrera

-Sabaku grunts as he dumps another load of sand into the empty center of the new wall. He turns his wheelbarrow around and heads back to get another load. A rather mousy young girl in scribe's robes comes running up in a fluster, her glasses nearly falling off in her rush.-

Young Scribe: My lord! What are you doing?! Please, leave the construction to the workers!

Sabaku: Oh, calm yourself, Shiko. And please, just call me Sabaku. I'm not yet used to my title.

Young Scribe Shiko: B-but, my lord Sabaku, we need to go over the Merchant's Guild's trade agreements, the Forgemasters' supply requests, and the request from the Sandstorm Academy for a new Weatherworker's licence!

-Sabaku sighs at the girl's distraught state.-

Sabaku: Shiko, all of that can be handled over dinner, after the sun has fallen. In the meantime, all hands capable of work are needed to make sure our people are fortified and safe. These rumors coming from across the border have been ringing in my ear for some time now, and I have no intention of letting my people fall under the bloodthirsty rule of some would-be conqueror. Master Qaures' plans were bad enough - I shudder to imagine someone like him ruling over us yet again.

Shiko: Oh... alright... but please, don't strain yourself. And why do you still insist on calling the old Mad Lord "master"? It's been almost a year since he was exiled.

Sabaku: Mad or not, he still taught me everything I know. I couldn't bring myself to see him any differently.

-Sabaku takes his refilled wheelbarrow back to the wall and dumps another load.-

Sabaku: *Clapping his hands together* Alright, good work everyone! Let's get the masons up here to cap this section off and move on to the next!
