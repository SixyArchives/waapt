Gaston looks up to the small amount of light filtering in through the barred window through his own barred cell. He notices the shadow of a Murkrow shillouetting most of the minimal light.

Gaston: Ah, the Murkrow. Back at home, those corvids were associated with wrongdoings, evil and death... -Chuckles soulessly- How befitting of our fates, Tsubasa...

There is no reply

He pulls out a scrap of paper, a pen from his belt and his knife. He makes a small cut on his arm with his knife, drawing blood, and dips the pen in his blood and begins scrabbling on the paper.

Gaston: I am faced with the most bitter descison of my life while waiting for that penultimate hour in which I will meet my demise and leave the world of the living. I have nothing left. My name means nothing here, my honor was lost when we were found out, my wealth has been reduced to rags on a petty criminal's floor and my most loyal compainion, Charlemagne, is no-more.

But probably the biggest ailment to my heart is my death way from my most treasured Kalos. O' the sun that beamed across the golden feilds of sunflowers, carresed by solf, warm earth. O' the magnificent forests in which Pan himself danced in. O' the rivers and her sirens that beckon me to her soft, lapping waves. It pains me that I will die far away from you, my dear Kalos.

Nevermore will I laugh by her rivers, or enjoy the smiles on girls faces, or feel soft sands, earth and waves on my feet. The sun will no longer anoint this face in which has been bethrothed to the sick, twisted devils of death. Why should it? I have betrayed it's people and put them further into the clutches of Satan himself. The punishment I will recieve is befitting for one who has and will cause them much misery

And yet, in probably the darkest, deepest, sickly crevice of my now worthless life. I have felt somehow... freer than before. Like I had been liberated or emancipated in some way or form by a greater good. Quand vous n'avez rien, vous n'avez rien à perdre it seems. I am invisible, and thus, have nothing to conceal. I have reached the enlightenment. And yet, will never be able to share what I have learnt

To any soul who finds this, and reads this. I beg of thee. Do not scruch this up and toss it aside like trash or junk on the prison floor. Ahead to my words, there is still hope for Spectra. We may die, but the common ideal of Freedom and Liberty is still warm in your hearts, and will linger until the floodgates are open. Tell the King of Kalos about how his subjects need him, how the young bud of one of his most trusted officials has been cut off before it's bloom. Your cries will not fall on deaf ears.

For only then will our punishment will finaly an end. O' merciful God, please forgive my sins...

He places the paper down and sighs. After a while, there is a soft sobbing sound from his cell.

Gaston: -Smiling, though with tears rolling down his face- Viva la Révolution...
