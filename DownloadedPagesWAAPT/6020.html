Dragon's Den - Johto

Scalea the Dragonair's latest student was providing a rather large challenge for her.

In the several weeks that this Dragonite called Stratus had been training under he guidance, he had made very little progress. Not only was he having trouble understanding her instructions, but more than once, he had sneaked off to take a nap outside, or to catch a Magikarp to eat from the Den's waters.

In fact, the local Gyarados population that as a result of this, the Magikarp numbers in the den were the lowest they had been in generations.

As if this wasn't enough, the other elder dragons in the den had been telling her to simply give up trying to teach him, saying things like "He's hardly worth the time and effort" and "If you don't give up on him you'll go crazy. Heck, we'll all go crazy!"

But Scalea had never failed to train a student yet. And she wasn't about to start now.

Scalea: <Come on, young wyrm. You can do this, I'm certain.>

Stratus: <But...I can't! I've been doing everything you've told me too, but I just can't focus!>

The Dragonair pauses, rubbing her chin thoughtfully.

Scalea: <Well then, perhaps your inability to focus is the problem here...tell me, young wyrm, what is it that keeps causing your mind to wander like a curious Furret?>

Stratus frowns, looking rather embarrassed.

Stratus: <Er...I don't want to say. It's too embarrassing.>

Scalea responds with a concerned frown.

Scalea: <There's no need to be embarrassed, no matter what your answer may be.>

Stratus: <...Are you sure?>

Scalea: <Positive. Now tell me, what keep distracting you?>

Stratus hesitates, staring down at the rocky floor of the cavern, before quietly whispering his answer.

Stratus: <...Food.>

Scalea: <...I couldn't hear that, young wyrm. You were almost as quiet as a Whismur. Can you please repeat your answer just a little bit louder, please?>

Embarrassed, the Dragonite buries his head in his paws, giving Scalea a bit of a start.

<Oh! Or you could whisper to me, if that would help!>

The Dragonair slithers over to Stratus, placing her head close to his.

<You can trust in me, young wyrm, I won't tell another living soul, promise.>

This seems to help, as Stratus pulls his head out of his paws, briefly looks around to see if anymon else is listening, then leans in close to whisper to his teacher.

Stratus: <I keep thinking about food.>

Scalea pauses, then nods in response. Knowing her student, this answer came as little surprise to her.

Scalea: <I see...>

The Dragonair slithers away from the Dragonite, moving in a circle around the dragon as she briefly considers something.

<...I think I may have some idea how to use that to our advantage, like a Hariyama using it's opponents own strength against it in a Sumo contest.>

Stratus: <Oh...but how?>

Scalea halts her pacing, having returned to her position a few meters away from the Dragonite.

Scalea: <Well, young wyrm, I want you to close your eyes and focus on what I'm about to tell you.>

Stratus: <But I'm having trouble focusing!>

Scalea: <You won't have any issues focusing on this image, let me assure you.>

Stratus: <Well, if you say so.>

The Dragonite shuts his eyes, trying his hardest to focus on his teachers' words...

 150494 Luke 924, Sun, 8th Apr '12 10:58:47 AM Get RP Mod
Scalea: <Now, imagine that you're on the beach, and you've just succeeded in hunting your favorite type of fish Pokémon from the sea, like a Sharpedo ruthlessly hunting an injured Lapras...>

Scalea observes Stratus as he focuses, noticing the the Dragonite is licking his lips, obviously thinking of her favorite fish, as she instructed.

<But then, before you can rightfully consume your catch, a wild Gyarados emerges from the sea and demands that you hand over your fish to him!>

The Dragonite's expression changes to a look of shock, before becoming rather annoyed. Scalea smirks at this.

<Now, young wyrm, I want you to pretend that I'm that Gyarados, okay?>

Stratus nods in response, Scalea briefly pausing to compose herself before beginning to imitate the voice of a Gyarados, taking inspiration from a particular member of the species that lived in the den that she wasn't too fond of.

<Now, you listen here, whelp. If you don't give me that fish...>

The Dragonair pauses briefly, beginning to hover in mid-air quietly.

<...I'm going to make things painful for you. So let me ask you again, are you going to give it to me?>

Stratus briefly hesitates, then shakes his head defiantly, his eyes still closed tight.

Stratus: <No! It's my fish, go and hunt your own!>

Happy with how things are progressing, Scalea prepares a Dragon Rush attack.

Scalea: <How dare you, whelp! I 'ought to smack you about a bit. But...I'm feeling generous today, so I'm going to give you one last chance: Give me the fish or I'll thrash you!>

Stratus: <...N-no! It's mine!>

The Dragonite raises his paws, assuming a fighting stance.

<Back of or...I'll be the one thrashing you!>

Scalea briefly smiles to herself, things having gone just as had intended.

Scalea: <...Suit yourself, whelp.>

And with that, she darts at Stratus with high speed. However, Stratus senses something moving towards him despite having his eyes closed, and upon opening them, he finds his teacher a split-second away from striking him with a powerful attack.

Stratus: <EEP!!!>

Shutting his eyes tight again, Stratus can't help but replace Scalea with the mental image of a Gyradoa rearing forwards to bite him intending to steal his food.

His food that he had worked so hard to catch.

No. He wasn't going to let that Gyarados take his food! Even if he and the fish in question were imaginary, it was still the principle of the thing!

Thinking quickly, the Dragonite throws himself on the ground, a split-second before Scalea passes through where he had been standing just moments prior. Still moving at a high speed, the Dragonair expertly U-turns, then flies back at the Dragonite, attempting another attack.

Scalea: <Come on, young wyrm! Put your training into practice!>

Now feeling confident, Stratus pulls himself up, opens his eyes, then clasps his hand together. He then begins to concentrate on a single thought: keeping the (imaginary) Gyarados away from his (imaginary) food.

With Scalea closing the gap between them, the Dragonite lets out a raor, then pulls his hand apart, the action causing a wall of force to erect in front of him, much to his surprise.

Stratus used Barrier!

WHAM!

With no time to steer around the barrier, Scalea slams into it, much like a bug type accidentally flying into a closed window.

Stratus: <OH! Scalea, are you okay!>

The Dragonair winces from pain as she slides down the barrier, then falls off it backwards onto the ground. Stratus looks on worried, but calms down slightly when Scalea raises her head, giving the Dragonite a small smile.

Scalea: <Urgh...I'm fine, young wyrm...well done.>
