 268537 luke 924, Mon, 24th Jun '13 12:40:43 PM Get RP Mod

PEFE HQ - Paleontology Lab

Luke: "Are we ready to go?"

<Almost...>

The tapping of a keyboard echoed through the room as a certain Flygon buzzed contently.

Dusty: <And...done! Calibrations are complete! Ready to begin the revival process!>

The human and gathered Pokémon gazed through the thick safety glass separating them from the complex machinery on the otherside, which was lowered into place just above a small chunk of rock. To the casual observer, it appeared to be just any old rock you could find lying around anywhere.

But in actuality, it was the fossil of an ancient Pokémon that lived long, long ago. And with the amazing power of science, they were going to bring it back from the dead.

Woolly: <Mmm. So what kind of Pokémon is this supposed to be again?>

As she awaited her answer, she grabbed a handful of popcorn out of a rather large bucket she had brought with her, munching on it nosily.

Treeo: <You said it was a Shieldon, right Dusty? Wait, where did you get that popcorn?>

The Flygon nodded as he flipped the safety switches on the control panel.

Dusty: <That's right! It's a small herbviourous Pokémon that lived ninty-one million years ago!>

Comet: <Whoa, whoa, WHOA. Hold on a minute there, buddy! Did you really just say that hunk of rock was ninty-one million years old?!>

Dusty: <It is rather hard to imagine, isn't it? That these remains predate The Vow, Human Civilization, and even the Alakagross Empire!>

Comet: <You're telling me...>

Luke: "That's all well and good, but can we get this started? I'm quivering in excitement here! Look!"

Luke held up his arm, which was positively quivering in anticipation.

"See that? Pure excitement, right here. Or maybe it was the second can of Croak I had with lunch."

Dusty: <I couldn't agree more, anything to speed along the never ending march of science. Alright peoplemons, goggles on!>

The human, Ampharos, Charizard and Sceptile each pulled a set of goggles over their face to protect their eyes as the machine whirred to life slowly. Dusty, of course, didn't need a pair since they came with Flygon as standard.

Treeo: <It's a shame Lake isn't here for this. You know how much he just loves Dinosaurs.>

Comet: <Eh, we have a saying in the Dream World: You snooze in one world, you miss something in the other.>

Treeo: <...Appropriate.>

Dusty: <Firing a the laser!>

A bright flash illuminated the room as a crimson laser beam shone down into the fossil, scanning the precious D.N.A code hidden within.

<D.N.A scanning complete!>

The Flygon glanced down at the machine's display as it revealed the data it had collected.

<Let's see. What we have here is a female Shieldon that lived to be four years old. Huh. Poor thing, cut down in the prime of her life.>

The Flygon smirked.

<But hey, a little Science can fix any problem! Let's begin the reconstruction sequence!>

Woolly: <WOO! GO SCIENCE!>

Several panels of metal erupted from the floor as the laser disappeared, forming a large box around the fossil as various tubes snaked their way in and connected to several funnels in the sides. Flicking a few more switches, the large box began to hum as the reconstructed process begun.

Dusty: <Ah, technology never ceases to amaze me! It used to take months of rigorous and delicate work to revive a fossil! But now it's as easy as baking a cake!>

Content, the Flygon began to hum as he turned dials and flicked switches on the control panel, as various different substances wormed their way through the tubes and into the box.

<Start with whole lot of carbon for the base materials, add a bunch of calcium for the bones...>

Dusty continued to list out various substances as Comet frowned in disapproval.

Comet: <Okay, as a cook, I'd just like to point out that baking a cake is nothing like this!>

Treeo replied with a shrug.

Treeo: <Well he's a Scientist, not a cook. You can't expect him to know about stuff outside his area of expertise!>

Dusty: <...Mix it all together with a little Deoxyribonucleic acid - that's DNA to you and me - and...wa-la!>

The Flygon buzzed loudly in content as the tubes retracted into the ceiling, the humming of machinery gradually quieting down.

Luke: "Did it work?"

Dusty: <We'll know in a second. But first we need to confirmation of a heartbeat.>

The human and Pokémon crowded around the monitor, which was now tracking the newly revived Shieldon's heart rate. Worryingly, it wasn't registering anything.

Comet: <Isn't it supposed to have started by now?>

Dusty: <It should of...maybe it just needs a few more seconds.>

The half dragon half insect Pokémon furrowed his brow as he watched the screen intently, waiting for any sign of life. But a few more seconds had past and there was still nothing.

Treeo: <Oh no. I hope something didn't go wrong...>

Dusty: <Come on, come on!>

He grabbed the sides of the monitor with his graspers, staring at it intently.

<We just brought you back from beyond the brink, little one! Don't slip away again!>

Everyone watched with baited breath as they waited desperately for any sign of life (This didn't stop Woolly from eating another handful of popcorn, though).

But suddenly, to everyone's relive, the monitor beeped as the now restored Pokémon's heart finally sprang to life.

Treeo: <YES!>

Comet: <Phew, that was a close one!>

Woolly: <YAHOO! Science wins again!>

Dusty: <YES! YES!!! IT'S ALIVE! IT'S ALIVE!>

Everyone stared at Dusty awkwardly for a few moments, the Flygon eventually turning back to them and grinning Mareepishly.

<Ah-heh, sorry. Not every day you get to say that.>
