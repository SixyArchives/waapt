Nimbasa City, some time after the Crewe conversation

-A figure clad from head to toe in a dark purple costume and mask is perched on a rooftop, sparring with a Scizor. A slip of paper lands at her feet.-

Lillianna—

I need to talk to you. Where are you?

-Beneath her mask, the figure frowns and lowers her sword.-

Lillianna: One moment, Bishop.

-The Scizor halts obediently as Lillianna turns the note over and writes down a single-word reply before letting it go.-

Nimbasa.

-Moments later, the sender of the original slip of paper blips in, a distinctly relieved look on her face.-

Sarah: ...Lillianna...

Lillianna: I put that name aside when I first took up the mask. It was a reminder of a past I would rather leave behind.

Sarah: Would you prefer a different name, then?

-Lillianna shrugs.-

Lillianna: I go by whatever they choose to call me. An ideal doesn't need an identity.

Sarah: Very well I'd call you something along the lines of "The Second Avenger", but not only would you probably not appreciate it, but considering recent events...

Lillianna: ...You were closer to him than I ever was. I can understand your grief all too well... even if I cannot bring myself to share it.

-Sarah looks away.-

Sarah: ...Close enough that I fell to the same dark point he had gotten to and couldn't see sense until it was too late...

-Lillianna sighs and closes her eyes.-

Lillianna: In the time I came from, Father never saw the error of his ways. Even with your counsel, and the support of those closest to him, he lacked the strength and sanity to choose a better path for himself. You, on the other hand... I will not deny that you made mistakes, but though I may have put my old life behind me, you are still my mother. I can forgive a moment of weakness brought on by a lost love's mourning. We cannot change the past, as I learnt to my chagrin. All we can hope for is a brighter future.

-Sarah nods, looking back up at Lillianna-

Sarah: Which is why I'm here. Once it finally sunk in that what's lost is lost, one of my biggest worries was that you'd disappear as well, without getting that future. Now that I know you're still here, though I'm willing to help you however I can, if you ever need it. As much as I dreaded your existence when he was alive, I've finally seen sense again.

-She sighs-

Sarah: I don't know what's going to happen now, but whatever it may be, I'll be there for you. I still can't think of you as a daughter, but at the very least, as a friend. If you'll accept it, of course.

Lillianna: ...I will. Thank you, M- Sarah. This new life I have chosen for myself is a lonely one, but that needn't mean I must refuse all company offered to me.

-Sarah nods.-

Sarah: It's the least I could do Good luck.

-After a moment of debate, Sarah offers Lillianna a hug, which she accepts rather awkwardly.-

Lillianna: Keep your luck. You may need it more than I do.

Sarah: I suppose Either way, be careful.

-She breaks the hug.-

Sarah: If you ever need me, you know how to contact me. No matter where I am.

Lillianna: Of course. You taught me well in that regard.

-She smiles, turning away.-

Sarah: See you around, then.

~blip~
