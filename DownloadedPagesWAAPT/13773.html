Hermit Jarod's House

Valorie yawned and did her best to brush her hair with one hand, sitting on the couch she'd made her temporary bed for the night. There was no excuse for not looking good! It wasn't like she was in a rush to be anywhere anyway.

After a few long moments of mild struggle with the few beauty products she carried around 'just in case' she heard footsteps from a nearby room; it seemed Jarod was up.

The hermit moved into the room casually, strolling over and beginning to mash up a herbal medicine. Valorie supposed it was the best he could do without the power of science to aid him and took the oddly sweet medicine without hesitation. It must've been made using berries.

"Sorry again about the whole Rex attacking you thing." he mumbled tiredly. "I just can't control him all the time, y'know?" he quickly added

"All of my Pokemon know better than to attack humans." Valorie slightly snapped, peering at Jarod. In all honesty, she really did not care for his excuses right about then.

"What—Did'ja teach them that..?" Jarod asked in a quiet tone.

"...Yeah. It was like, one of the first things I did." Valorie replied, still in a somewhat bad mood. She'd probably feel better after she'd gotten that thorough shower.

"Damn... You kids are a lot better than I was at your age—Now even." Jarod replied, somewhere between disappointed and apathetic. "So what's a rising starlet like you doing wandering around so far off the road?" he added after a moment.

Valorie shrugged. "...I was trying to find a wild dragon type pokemon." she replied nonchalantly.

"That's all..?" Jarod asked; it was his turn to be a tad surprised.

Valorie hesitated a moment, considering the question. "...Well, I'm also trying to understand the bond between humans and pokemon. I want to get to know them better." Valorie offered.

Jarod was quick to jump on this. "That? Heck, I can teach you about that." he offered in an overtly light-hearted tone. Shu bit his non-existant tongue ever so hard and Valorie declined to comment. Jarod seeemed to be on a roll anyway. "It's all in your head. You just have to... become one with your pokemon. Y'see what I'm getting at?" he continued, gesturing to his own skull.

Valorie offered Jarod an awkward smile in an effort to be polite. Sure, it sounded like bullshit but that didn't mean she had to call him out! Shu burst out chortling and cackling; it was a horrifying sound in his ghostly voice, reverberating and echoing in their heads. Valorie and Jarod just sort of stopped to give eachother uncomfortable looks as the moment passed and Shu regained his composure.

"...Sorry." Valorie apologized in a lame tone.
