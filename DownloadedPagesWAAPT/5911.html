Shahinne: <Harriet tells me a story before I go to sleep.>

Roc: <Shahinne...> — stares at her.

Roc: <I really want to sleep... if I tell you a short story, you promise to sleep?>

Shahinne: <Yes!!!>

Roc nuzzles Shahinne and hugs her, giving her warmth. Then she starts telling a story he's been thinking of for a couple of days...

«...and the [Honchkrow] cared for her family very much. Every day she went to the sea to catch some fishes for the family to eat and her daughter to grow strong and beautiful...

«...but while there was food for everymon and she had learned to fish well, she returned emptybeaked... even though she was successful. Because the [mean birds] owned the skies and demanded a tax for any other bird that crossed their territory...

«...she diligently paid the tax for her family so that they would not be in [danger], even if it meant she would not be able to bring food to the nest. So long as the little bird was safe it would be fine, that was the promise of her heart...

«But for the mean birds the tax was not enough. They demanded fear, so sometimes they viciously attacked the other birds, even tearing their feathers. The [stress] was unbearable for the dedicated mother, and she started losing her sleep and her plumage, as many other birds in the city because no mon stood up to the mean birds...»

Roc looked down at his daughter, who was long asleep on his chest, faintly snoring. He looked at her sternfully, then allowed himself to brush his face against her plumage in the darkness.

Roc: <Sorry, Shahinne. These things you'll have to learn and deal with by yourself.>

The Staraptor ruffled his plumage and, covering his daughter, delivered himself to sleep.
