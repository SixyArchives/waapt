Crewe's Pokeball

Eventually, the pain became her entire reality. Every so often there were glimmers of shadows of things that were not pain. But after enough time had passed, she had herself convinced that they were imaginary, dreams of a time before the pain, dreams that she couldn't remember when she woke up. Not long after deciding this, she resolved to die. How, she wasn't so sure. For all this time she'd managed to hold up, and she couldn't remember if she'd meant to. But perhaps, now that she meant to give up, it would just happen on its own. Yes, dying must be easy if you wanted to.

The satisfaction that came of having made this decision cut through the pain for an instant, but it came back just as quickly as it left.

I'll die soon, she thought, sure that she couldn't take much more.

No.

The sudden intrusion of a dark presence in her mind startled her. This was an invitation to more pain; any letup was, she'd found. She wondered if dying counted as a letup, if that would make the pain worse. But no, that must end it.

No. You must live.

Why? she thought idly. I have nothing to live for.

There is nothing to live for.

Yes, I thought so… This revelation wasn't really a revelation; she'd decided it a long time ago. I suppose I'll go about dying now, then.

Dying is weakness. There is nothing to live for. But you are too strong to die, so you must live.

That seemed reasonable enough, truly. But still... life meant pain. Outwardly, she curls in on herself as the thought of it brings new waves down over her. Being weak really wasn't that big a deal, right? It was easier, at least.

Being strong is all there is. You have too much behind you to be strong.

Now, that was rude. She feels a brief flicker of annoyance. Maybe she wouldn't die after all, just to show that she could.

That's right. Live. Put everything behind you and be strong.

Yes, she could be strong. She was sure of it now. She could draw strength from her memories, from her friends. Straw—

The pain presses down on her and her body whimpers. The presence washes down over her.

NO.

For a few moments, all she can do is hold the pieces of herself together, unable to think clearly, or even properly feel the pain. If anything, that made it worse.

The only way to beat the pain is to lock yourself away. Being strong is the only thing that matters. Lock yourself tightly away where the pain cannot reach you. Then be strong, and live.

After a few moments, she makes the decision. Deciding cuts through the pain, and this time it slowly begins to ebb. Outwardly, she flexes her claws.

Yes. Being strong is all there is.
