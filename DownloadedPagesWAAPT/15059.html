A couple of workbenches with the remains of failed attempts at gadgeteering scattered across them can be seen, as well as a large object hidden beneath a sheet of tarpaulin. The centrepiece of the makeshift headquarters is a large computer system rigged up to several monitors, which Gaia is currently sat in front of, browsing the internet.-

Gaia: All told, I believe this has been a reasonably successful first week for us, if I do say so myself. So far we've thwarted several muggings, disrupted a Neo-Galactic supply shipment, and rescued a Purrloin from a tree.

Kaylee: <Does that last one count? I dunno if that last one really counts.>

Shrike: <Hey, it was a really tall tree!>

Foxglove: <...We can fly, dear.>

Shrike: <Yeah, I know, but still!>

The Auric Avenger: Wait, this is only our first week? I thought we'd-

Gaia: I'm not counting our disastrous first escapade or the period of recuperation immediately following it.

The Auric Avenger: Oh.

-He scratches his exposed chin.-

The Auric Avenger: Still, I'm kinda concerned. Neo-Galactic can't have been based in the area for more than a couple of months, and they're already operating as far out as Fall City... it's stuff like this that makes me think we might need some help.

Gaia: These are early days yet. If anything, you should be grateful that we aren't drawing attention to ourselves.

The Auric Avenger: How so? I thought part of the thrill of being a superhero is in hearing people talk about you. Plus, I'm a trailblazer. First of my kind and all that. I should be way ahead of the curve.

Fulgrim: <How typical of you humans to try and turn a noble endeavour into a popularity contest.>

Gaia: Being first is no guarantee that you will be able to maintain a position of promenance. I doubt The Phantom is particularly relevant nowadays, for instance.

-She gives a wave of her hand, and the window she was looking at minimises itself.-

Gaia: Besides, if we are being pedantic, then technically Essentia preceded us.

The Auric Avenger: ...Essence-who?

Gaia: Exactly. As strange as it sounds to say, we are not the first to attempt this sort of exercise, and I doubt we will be the last.

-She spins around in her chair to face the Auric Avenger.-

Gaia: It is also worth mentioning that celebrity status invites speculation, brings details of our characters into the public eye. Including, if you will excuse the usage of a rather cliched term, our weaknesses. A certain amount of reputation would undoubtedly make things a lot easier for us, but too much and we will end up more focused on maintaining an image than actually doing our job. Better to remain a background force, whose effects can be inferred but not directly observed.

The Auric Avenger: Yeah, I suppose...

-He exhales audibly.-

The Auric Avenger: I'm gonna go get changed and make my way back to Fiore. It's been a long week, and I'm pretty sure I'll end up drowning in my own sweat if I don't get out of this costume soon.

Gaia: Very well. Just remember that we cannot afford to become complacent.

The Auric Avenger: I know, I know. I'll be back if anything comes up, but for now, I just need a weekend off. I reckon we've earned it, after all.

-He tugs off his mask and starts making his way over to his own little alcove.-
