Uh, Crewe.

Froslass: -starts drifting in a circle around her- <You look so much like him... But of course you wouldn't know him... No one knows him anymore...>

... I'm sorry?

Froslass: <Please Crewe, come inside.>

You're not kicking me out?

Froslass: <No, no, of course not... Please, would you mind if I told you some stories?>

-confused-

Stories?

Froslass: <About a man I once knew. You look so much like him, I can't help but be reminded. Please, it's been so long since I've been able to speak about him to anyone else.>

Um... I guess I can listen to your stories.

Froslass: <Thank you. I'm sorry about the cold. I don't usually get visitors.>

That's okay. I don't mind the cold.

-she follows the Froslass farther into the cave and finds a seat against the wall-

Froslass: <Let's see... where shall I start... I suppose his name would be the first thing to tell. Oh! I haven't even told you my name!>

Oh! Yeah, what's your name?

Froslass: <Well, when I was young and became partnered with this man, he called me Isolde. And his name was Amhlaidh.>

Isolde. That's a pretty name. His is kind of a mouthful, though.

Isolde: -her eyes flare briefly- <It's a good name! It's easy to say once you get used to it!>

-holds up her hands defensively-

Okay, okay, whatever you say.

Isolde: <Yes. Well... Amhlaidh was born very far from here, in a place I haven't seen in so long, its name has completely faded from my memory. But most of his life he spent in a different place. The entire region was called Ransei, but he lived and worked in a country called Spectra, under the ghost lord.>

I've... never heard of either of those places.

Isolde: <I knew them a very long time ago. They may have new names now.>

Or I just don't go to new places often. How long ago did you know this guy, anyway?

Isolde: -gets a far-off look in her eyes- <Oh, it must have been... about six hundred years, now.>

-Crewe coughs-

Right. Ghost types.

Isolde: <Yes, it's been a very, very long time since I last saw my Amhlaidh...>

-Crewe lets her reminisce for a few moments, but starts fidgeting-

Um, you had a story you said you wanted to tell?

Isolde: <Oh, yes. Let's see... the most exciting time we had together must have been the war. You see, when Amhlaidh first came to Ransei, it was by mistake. He was traveling somewhere else entirely... the purpose of the journey doesn't matter now, as our ship crashed on the shores of this unfamiliar region. Fortunately, the lives of Amhlaidh and my compatriot, a Weavile he later renamed Kori, were saved by a young native man. As it turns out, this man would soon take power in Spectra. Because he saved us, Amhlaidh swore his life to him, and worked as a bodyguard of sorts. In his service, we fought in a great war... The Ghost Lord wanted something, I never cared what, and to aid him, my Amhlaidh fought by his side. It was an exciting time.>

I see...

Isolde: <Oh, you should have seen him as we went off... he never wore the armor of the Ransei people, always the armor of his native homeland, and he carried a great sword... his father's sword, in fact, though he was a far better swordsman than his father.>

-Isolde prattles on for some time, Crewe eventually pulling on her coat and settling in, knees hugged to her chest-

Spectra, some 600 years in the past

Kori: <It doesn't matter. The only thing that matters is where we're assigned to go next.>

Kori: <It's not supposed to be exciting. We have a job to do. That's all.>

Amhlaidh: I'm glad you're excited, Isolde. It makes things easier.
