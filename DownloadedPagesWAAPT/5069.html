Bravely Fashionaaabluh

Fortree City

Rex and Joey made their way back into town, the two finding they had much more in common than they initially thought. Both of their initial captures were Rattata, both were from Johto, and both found that using their favorite species was a more enjoyable way to battle than by using what was popular.

Joey: The reason I talk about my Rattata so much is simple: it's because no one expects great things from a Pokemon that most trainers start with and dump. By the time you get to the Pokemon League, you're expected to have left behind your Furret and your Pidgeot and move on to better things.

He shook his head, frowning at the very thought of that.

Joey: What kind of fun would battles be if everyone just used the same handful of Pokemon, moves, and strategy all of the time? It's too easy! Being a Pokemon Trainer is about the struggle to get to the top, making choices, mistakes, and growing along the way. Sure, Rattata may not be the best Pokemon in the world, but if I stick with him, he could probably give a lot of those guys a run for their money.

He looked over at Rex, feeling a bit relieved to see that the other boy seemed to be taking his words seriously. Most people tended to take most of his words with a grain of salt. Eventually, the duo reached the Pokemon Center again, looking around for any sign of a contest.

Joey: I guess they haven't started yet. Well, that's fine. That gives me more time to train with Tailow.

He began to make his way over to the path leading to Route 119, letting out the bird Pokemon. As it roosted on his shoulder, he turned back to Rex, grinning happily at his new friend.

Joey: See you later, Rex! Maybe next time we meet we can have a battle between our first catches!

The other boy nodded and waved, Joey turning back to the route. The Tailow trilled happily, fluttering happily around his trainer's head as they made their way back into the forest. Then they were gone, leaving Rex alone in the center of the city.

Rex: Hmmm...about the struggle, huh?

He mulled that thought over as he began to stroll around, deciding to see just what Rascal and Spathi had been up to in his absence.
