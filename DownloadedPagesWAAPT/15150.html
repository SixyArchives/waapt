Gaia: Very well then. What happens to be our first order of business?

The Auric Avenger: Well, to start off with, you remember that Shingeko guy we heard about? Well, if we're not the only people crazy-

Foxglove: <I prefer the term "mentally unique", for future reference.>

The Auric Avenger: ...Okay then. As I was saying, if we're not the only ones mentally unique enough to dress up like Pokemon and go out at night to fight crime, then I want to see if we can rope anybody else with the same idea into working with us.

Gaia: ...Why, exactly?

The Auric Avenger: Because I figure we're going to need some help if we want to make this thing work properly. Besides, the more superheroes the better, right?

Gaia: I disagree. Any imitator of our methods is unlikely to have put as much time, forethought and preparation into the idea as we did. They are also under no obligation to abide by our rules. Not to mention that there is no discounting the possibility that they may be a plant of some description, an agent from Neo-Galactic or Illusion ordered to pose as a costumed vigilante in an attempt to gain our trust.

The Auric Avenger: ...Point. I still say this Shingeko guy's worth meeting, though.

Gaia: If only to dissuade him from further heroics.

The Auric Avenger: We'll see. From what I've heard, he's quite the glory hound, so I reckon we'll run into him soon enough. Anyway, onto number two. Turns out that the machine we wrecked last night was some kind of emotion-amplifying device set up by Illusion, and there's more of them around the place.

Gaia: ...I see. I suppose that would explain some of the eccentricities that Kaylee has been demonstrating today.

Kaylee: <I just... I love everybody so much, you know? They're just so unique and wonderful...>

The Auric Avenger: ...Come to think of it, that does explain a lot. But more to the point, I say our top priority should be taking out as many of these machines as we can. It'll help send a message to Illusion, and hopefully make people around here a little bit less stressed and a little bit more well-inclined towards us. Of course, this job would be much easier if we had some help...

Gaia: Foxglove is the only help I will need. You are welcome to continue your efforts at recruitment, but please bear in mind my earlier warnings. Now then, if that is all you wished to discuss, I have members of the Illusion Project to be tracking down.

-She clambers onto Foxglove's back.-

Gaia: Oh, and do be more careful when it comes to disclosing hints about your identity in future. After everything you told Verax, I would not be surprised if you find press teams at your door at some point in the near future.

The Auric Avenger: ...Noted. I'll try to keep a lower profile, then. Insofar as a guy in a purple Lucario costume can keep a low profile.

Gaia: Excellent. Then I shall be off.

-She takes off and starts flying over the city. After a moment, the Avenger sighs, then starts patrolling across the rooftops, doing his best to keep an eye out for suspicious activity whilst remaining unseen himself.-
