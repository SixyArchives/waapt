Dragonspiral Moat - Dragonite Caves

Alto: <And just what have you got there?>

All the Dragonite gathered around as Stratus dropped a large stack of flat cardboard boxes down on the table, taking one of the top and flipping it open, revealing a freshly baked pizza.

Stratus: <This...is pizza.>

Alto: <...Pizza?>

A rather skittish looking Dragonair slithered forward, gazing at the food curiously.

Dragonair: <I swear this what's in all those large boxes that lord Zekrom asks for. Just much smaller.>

Alto: <How did you even get these...pizzas down here without getting them wet, anyway?>

Stratus smirked.

Stratus: <Dive isn't just for breathing underwater, you know.>

Alto didn't know. As he didn't often carry passengers down to the caves, he didn't know Dive.

<Well, you guys are hungry, right? Go ahead and help yourselves! There's enough slices here for everymon! It'll be one of the best things you've ever tasted, I swear.>

<He's not lying!>

Everyone turned their attention to Nacre, who had a mouth stained with cheese after having eaten a few slices herself.

Nacre: <I had some on the way back! It was amaaaaaa-zing!>

Alto: <...Hmph.>

Alto reluctantly grabbed a slice, gazing at it with scrutiny.

<We've lived on Basculin for generations. Surely this cheap, processed rubbish can't be any better...>

He stopped to take a bite, chewing on it thoughtfully for a few seconds, and...

He paused, his eyes glazing over as the rich taste of cheese and pepperoni flooded his mouth. The warm bread embracing his tongue as if embracing it in a hug. Nervously, he swallowed, the warmth spreading to his body as it made his way to his stomach, leaving him energized yet relaxed at the same time.

It left him with a feeling of awe he hadn't felt since he had first laid eyes on the Tao Dragons themselves when he was just a tiny Dratini.

Stratus: <...Hey, you okay, buddy?>

Stratus waved a claw in front of Alto's dumbstruck face, who blinked as he returned to the real world.

<So, did you like it? You did go quiet for a whole minute just there.>

Still in awe of this most wonderful of food, Alto turned to look at Stratus, his mouth a-gape as several other dragons began to take some slices for themselves so they could see what the fuss was all about.

Alto: <...I don't know who you are...>

Grabbing Stratus by the shoulders, Alto stared down straight into his eyes.

<But I want you to teach me everything you know.>

Somewhat Mareepishly, Stratus grinned back.

Stratus: <It would be my pleasure!>
