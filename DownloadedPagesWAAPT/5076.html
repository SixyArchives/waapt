Fortree Park

Since Tagg wanted to see all of us about something later on, there wasn't much we could really do until then, so I had taken my team to the local park to relax for a bit while we wait. Although some members of my team were enjoying themselves more than others...

Comet: <This is boring.>

The Charmeleon sighs, shooting a glance at Treeo, who was sunning himself on a rock next to her, something the Sceptile notices.

Treeo: <Why are you bored?>

Comet: <Because we can't do anything right now just because Tagg wants to lecture us all about something later.>

She crosses her arms in a huff.

<It's stupid.>

Treeo rolls his eyes. Although he was rather fond of Comet, her Hasty nature could prove to be a pain sometimes.

Treeo: <What's wrong with just sitting around every once and a while?>

Comet: <Because I could be using this time to be doing something much more exciting!>

Treeo: <Hmm...That is true. But there's a lot to be said for just sitting back and letting the world go by every now and then.>

Comet glances downwards, beginning to trace shapes in the dirt with her claws idly.

Comet: <There's no thrill in that, though.>

Treeo: <I wouldn't say that. Sure, it's not the pulse pounding excitement you would expect from something like a battle or the Pokéthlon...but just taking some time to yourself can be exciting in a different way. Besides, if life was one constant rush without any quiet moments, everyone would be worn out all the time!>

Comet: <Maybe some other types...but us fire types don't go out so easily.>

Treeo smiles smugly.

Treeo: <But even the largest of infernos burns out eventually...>

The Charmeleon gives the Sceptile a blank stare.

Comet: <Are you always in philosopher mode?>

Treeo: <Well, we Grass Pokémon have a lot of time to think about things...>

Navel Rock

Lugia: <...And this is where Ho-oh likes to perch.>

Pleo: <Oh, wow! We're so high up!>

Pleo flaps over to the edge of the rock, peering down at the Ocean far below.

Lugia: <Yeah, Captain Rainbow was always a sucker for high places. You should have seen how happy he was when the humans built him that lovely tower to perch on.>

Lugia glances skyward, reminiscing about days long past...until he catches site of a handwritten note stuck to a nearby rock.

<What the?>

The great bird Pokémon telekineticly lifts the note from the rock, holding it up to eye-level to read it. Pleo, noticing this, gazes at his father curiously.

Pleo: <What does it say, Dad?>

Without a word, Lugia glances at his son, a neutral expression on his face, yet one Pleo could tell was not a look you wanted to see.

Lugia: <Nothing for you to worry about, son. I'm just going to go downstairs for a bit. I'll be back in a few minutes, okay?>

Pleo: <Okay...>

Lugia quickly heads back downstairs, leaving Pleo to wonder just what poor Pokémon was about to incur his wrath this time.
