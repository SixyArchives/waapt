Psyche digs around. ~Let me... Oh...~

She pulls out another pair of notes from the stack.

Dealing with Empathy in Hard Times

Dear readers, I want to talk to a specific section of my audience. Other Aurics and Empaths like myself. More so than other members of the population, empaths are at risk for many mental health issues, specifically depression. The worst thing an empath can do in a hard time though, as counterintuitive as it sounds, is retreat from society. Attempting to do so is simply going to make your loved ones try and comfort you, which their worry about you is only going to make you feel better, rather than worse. Find someone you trust, instead, and simply... talk. Not about what bothers you, but what makes you feel good. Then ask them what makes them feel good. Their happy memories will make you feel better. When you can feel yourself ready to talk about it, first expand this exercise to a few more trustworthy people. When you have a positive support group, then try talking about what's making you feel down.

This one has a date more recent on it, which those who knew Elizabeth would realize is the week of her death.

Dealing with one's own Actions.

More than almost any other form of grief, one's own actions are heart-rending, when they run against one's code. This is a form of grief that... well... It's hard to deal with. I still have problems reconciling issues from my own past. And this is part of why, dear readers, I want to write this before I pass away.

One must always air one's dirty laundry. Even if no one actually knows it before your death, writing down your issues is the best thing to do. It can ease your pain, your suffering.

I recommend telling a trusted friend or significant other if you feel able. Then again, if you don't feel able, I don't recommend them being a good enough friend for you anyway. The best friends should be able to accept you for anything you do. Anyway, even if you don't feel able to tell someone, I still recommend writing it down. Then burn the note. Write it again. Burn it. Tear it apart. Write it. BURN IT. WRITE IT. BURN IT.

When you get through, eventually you will begin going from denial, to anger, to bargaining. You will try to beg your friends, your religion, your family for forgiveness. They can't. The only one you can hope to forgive you is yourself. It's hard. ...So hard... But it's possible.

The next stage is depression. You will hate this step. I promise. But. It passes, just as everything does. You have to wait it out.

Lastly, you will accept it. It's hard... But it happens. I promise. You just have to let yourself accept it.

This is the part where I air my own dirty laundry... I haven't spoken to my brothers in years. I wish I could have helped Lysander... I wish I could help Lawrence. But, no. I missed my shot. And now Lysander is- [[illegible scratchings]]

"I know who I am. I am not a boy. I am Alesha, like my grandmother before me." Alesha, Who Smiles At Death
