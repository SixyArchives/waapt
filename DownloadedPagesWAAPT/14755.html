A few days ago: Goff Lemure (Outside)

Opening the basement door, Rex returned outside to see a Larvesta gumming up a refrigerator door with String Shot as Rebecca and a Wobbuffet (Continued was its name, as he recalled) watched.

Rex: Do I want to know?

Rebecca: Just give me my Houndoom.

Seeing the girl was still in a foul mood, the boy simply shrugged and placed Skulk on the ground, undoing his bindings and freeing the dog, sending him over to Rebecca where she appraised him carefully.

Rebecca: Ugh, what is this black coloring? Did they cover you in spray-paint or something? Can't believe these...

Rex nodded as he saw all was well, preparing to walk off and go about his business. He'd just about left when...

Rebecca: Hold it!

He paused in place and turned around, an innocent expression on his face.

Rex: Hm?

Rebecca's face was even darker if possible, her fists balled at her side.

Rebecca: Is this a joke? You think you can just pull a fast on me?

The girl held up her Mega Stone to Rex, the boy tilting his head in confusion.

Rex: It's a Mega Stone. What about it?

Rebecca: It's Heracronite. The Heracronite I gave to you.

Rex: Is it? That's... strange.

He shrugged, before smiling.

Rex: But don't worry, I'm sure you can get a lot of use out of it if you can master it.

There was a blur as a figure darted from the basement, holding the Houndoominite squarely in their paws.

Rex: Unless, you'd like to wager on your Pokémon being stronger than mine.

Kim stood firm between the two trainers, the Mega Lucario sneering at her opponents as a visible aura began to drift off of her.

Rex: And I wouldn't take that bet, were I you.

Rebecca was silent for a moment, shivering with rage as she assessed the situation.

Rebecca: ...You...    you   ...

Then, she sighed, unclenching her fists and shaking her head.

Rebecca: You're really annoying, you know that? That's exactly the kind of move I would have made were I in your position.

She shook her head, running her fingers through her hair.

Rebecca: Fine. You can have it for now. Maybe you can even trade it away for something nice. See what I care.

Recalling Skulk and the Larvesta, the girl made her way over to Hegemone, climbing atop the Tropius' back and fixing them both with a glare.

Rebecca: But I'm make you regret this stunt someday. Remember that.

Without another word, the girl took off into the air, leaving behind Rex and Kim to stand in silence. Then, they both sighed in relief, glad that she'd fallen for the bluff. The aura around Kim dissipated as she reverted to her normal Pokéform, turning to Rex to hand him the spare Mega Stone.

Lucario!Kim: <What made you think she'd fall for the bluff? Didn't she say she'd be watching us?>

Rex: She might have been. At first, anyway. I doubt she had any real belief we'd be able to use that stone, even if I actually had a Heracross.

Lucario!Kim: <Speaking of which... aren't you a little upset? I mean, without that stone, Damian can't...>

Rex: That's alright. Damian is fine as he is. And besides, I'd hate to just use any possible deep friendship we could forge as a shallow means to just increase his battle potential.

Kim nodded in understanding, smiling at the boy's words.

Lucario!Kim: <We should probably go. Your mom and Lyra will be waiting for us.>

Rex: Yeah, and I don't have anything that can fly us back either... Guess we'll have to walk.

Lucario!Kim: <Well, that's what happens when you pull a gamble on our ride.>

Rex: Hey, it was a good plan! I just didn't account for that one part...

The two walked off, mock bickering with each other all the while as they made their way back to the meeting site proper.
