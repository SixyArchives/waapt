Boat to Kalos

-Arika is on the deck with a few other people, staring out to sea and waiting for Kalos to show up on the horizon-

- Waka walks up nearby and sees her-

Waka: Theres that dudette... Hangs out with Amaterasu and the Ghost Lord... But... I dont know much about her...

-Out of curiosity, he inspects her psychically-

-Something catches his attention, and he probes more-

-His eyes suddenly widen. He approaches her-

~Hey dudette.~

Arika: -turns to him- Oh! Hi! Youre that friend of Ammys!

Waka: ~Yup. Names Waka, dudette.~

Arika: Mines Arika. Why arent you on the Ark with the others?

Waka: ~Wanted a change of pace.~

Arika: I wanted to see Kalos from the sea.

-She smiles-

Its so awesome you were on that adventure with Ammy. What was it like?

Waka: ~Who, me? Well, I tried to help, but I also got on the nerves of Amaterasu and the dudes with her. I think grumpy dude still wants to kick me in the balls.~

Arika: Aww, Im sure you helped.

-She looks around, then leans in-

But you know what Im really curious about?

Waka: ~Hit me, dudette.~

Arika: ...The Lunar Pendant.

- Waka raises an eyebrow worriedly-

Waka: ~You really want to get your hands on something that dangerous?~

Arika: Pfft, of course not. Im just curious about its story. I mean, you had it once, correct?

Waka: ~Yes...~

-He sighs-

~It was created by Xerneas for Palkia long, long ago, as part of a jewelry ensemble for the latter. Eventually Palkia tired of her gifts, and it fell into the hands of mortals - most of which didnt know its wish-granting power.~

-He shifts a bit-

~I found it in my travels and thought it was just a pretty trinket that reminded me of home. I didnt know that Orochi and Yami were looking for it... Or that they would destroy my new home because of it. I ultimately lost it fleeing Yami.~

-He looks up at the sky-

~From there, it wound up in the hands of many others until it reached Amaterasu. When I saw she had it I didnt say anything because I thought we had more important stuff to take care of. Then the Ghost Lord told us its real role. After we beat him, Amaterasu and I decided it was too dangerous and threw it in the ocean.~

-He looks back at Arika-

~But when the pendant was with Amaterasu, I noticed something curious.~

Arika: Oh?

Waka: ~Amaterasu mentioned an assassin that was mysteriously stopped by the pendant, correct?~

Arika: Oh yeah. Why?

Waka: ~The assassins soul had been sealed inside the pendant.~

Arika: ...Wow. Huh.

-She looks out to sea-

You know, I wonder where it ended up.

Waka: ~Certainly worth wondering about.~

-He smirks-

~But do you know what I wonder, dudette?~

Arika: What?

Waka: ~What if... The pendant had a mind of its own? Its own, dreams, desires... Wishes? What if... It could grant its own wish?~

Arika: ...I dont get where youre going here. What kind of wish would an uber-powerful wish-granting pendant have?

Waka: ~Mabye it wanted to stretch its legs, so to speak.~

Arika: ...Huh?

Waka: -chuckles- ~You really dont get it, do you, dudette?~

Arika: What?

Waka: ...

... ...

... ... ...

~You ARE the Lunar Pendant.~

Arika: ...Wait, WHAT?!

Waka: ~Welcome to the Twilight Zone, Arika.~

Arika: I... I...

-She suddenly becomes incredibly dizzy, stumbles, and collapses, unconscious-
