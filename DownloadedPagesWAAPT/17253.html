Solidad Cafe

Wesker is just chilling, licking some cookies and creme ice cream. He leans his head back and smiles, as he enjoys the music. The cafe had a classy feel to it, with velvet carpets lining the windows and all the surfaces being so polished he could see his reflection anywhere he wanted, which he heartily appreciated. Distortion, even the chairs were lined with silver. He felt rather princely at the moment, and was grateful he could find a cafe with a pianist, which he showed by leaving a rather hefty tip.

However, a young woman came in, listening to something on her music player. However, the headphones weren't plugged in, so the soothing sound of piano keys was replaced by Pour Some Sugar On Me.

Wesker's eyes snapped open. The other people seemed a bit mad, but the sound wasn't really loud enough to replace the piano...at least, not for them.

He whispered something to the chef, who nodded. Wesker then tapped the shoulder of the woman, and, with a calm voice, with a large dose of charming, said "Madame, could you please come behind the counter for a bit?" She seemed interested, and nodded.

Five minutes later, Wesker came out, cleaning his hands with a rag, and pat the chef on the shoulder."Thanks for leaving me to do my work in privacy, mon ami." The chef nodded, frowning a little."Might I ask what you did?"

Wesker smiled."Oh, I simply took her outside, in as sweet a manner as I could muster. Thank you for the ice cream, I hope to see you again." He left a tip on the piano as well, and left, whistling to himself, leaving the woman tied up and covered in honey and sugar outside the cafe, hidden in a dark nook next to the garbage bin, unconscious.

Lost Souls Hotel

Marie closed her laptop, looking satisfied, a towel around her head, and around the lower half of her face."Okay, all social media accounts shut down too, new passport and ID made, and now, with some minor changes, " she took off the towels, revealing her previously fiery red hair now being dyed to a dull auburn, and the addition of hazel contact lenses, as well as a small tattoo of a radiating sun on her cheek, "the old Marie won't have existed, at least for a while."

She went to look at herself in the mirror, and smiled a little, though she felt a bit sad."Pleased to meet you, Catherine Heywood. Let's hope we get along, huh?"
