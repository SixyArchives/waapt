They were plastic bags stuffed with Combusken bones and hot sauce, their foul odor blossoming in the muffled dark. Jane's nostrils begged her brain for mercy. She flew upwards, away from the saucy mysteries below. The smell grew faint, calling her to return. Jane ignored their lies, flying beyond the lips of her prison. She was in a laboratory, with machines that had no purpose beyond blinking lights and soft hums. "Hello, my girl Daughter! You make a father so good!" Jane had flown out of the nose of an old Scrafty. This Scrafty wore a white coat, yet was drawn by the hand of an idiot. His voice came not from his mouth, but from elsewhere, a sad attempt at monanity. "I know you'll do so well! Now you choose! The Scrafty reached into his coat and laid out three bags, each brimming with the spicy bones of the nose prison. Arrayed before Jane was a Tirtouga, the reptilian body so frail that it seemed an afterthought to the shell, a bald Linoone with toothpicks for legs, and a wrinkled Poliwag in a glass of water.
