248125 luke92429th Apr 2013 01:10:02 PM Get RP Mod

Chargestone Cave - Woolly & Unite

Unite: <Urrrrrrrgh...Hah!>

With all of his strength, Unite pushed on a floating rock, knocking it towards a larger rock embedded in the ground, clearing the way forward. Wiping his brow, he continued onward, Woolly in tow.

Woolly: <Okay, I do have one problem with this cave?>

Unite: <And what's th- Oh. Oh right.>

Several Joltik were crawling all over Woolly, much to her displeasure. The tick Pokémon attempting to suck electricity from the electric sheep.

Woolly: <Normally, I wouldn't mind little fuzzy things crawling all over me. But when they're trying to drain me dry? That, I'm not okay with!>

Unite frowned, not sure what to do in this situation. Especially since Joltik were capable of giving a nasty Electric shock, despite their small size.

Unite: <Don't worry about it, Woolly. They won't drain you dry, and if you leave them alone, I'm sure they'll have their fill and leave eventually.>

Woolly: <...If you say so.>

Unite: <Now, let's keep goi->

???: <Pokémon of Chargestone Cave! Lend me your ears for just a moment, please?>

Unite: <Eh?>

Woolly: <Who's that?>

Unite: <I think it's coming from up ahead...>

The two headed onward, eventually coming across a rather large gathering of wild Pokémon listening to what looked like a Sawsbuck. Much to Unite's surprise.

<What's a Sawsbuck doing in here?>

Woolly: <Is it lost?>

Unite: <Wait, I think he's...giving a speech?>

The two Pokémon stopped to listen as the Sawsbuck began.

Sawsbuck: <Wild Pokémon! I'm sure you're well aware of Pokémon Trainers, humans who claim their goal is to better themselves and Pokémon by working together! For as long as anyone can remember, it has always been this way. But...>

The Sawsbuck stepped forward, holding his head high.

<Why does it have to be this way? Why must Pokémon answer to humans when we are clearly capable of looking after ourselves? Do we really need humans to reach our full potential?>

Murmurs erupted throughout the cave, the Pokémon shocked at the radical ideas that this stranger was proposing. However, he had more to say.

<By giving ourselves over to humans, we Pokémon sacrifice our freedom to make our own choices! Trainers decide where we go, who we fight, how strong we are allowed to be.

And if we don't meet their expectations? They shove us into their PC's and forget we even exist, and for what? Power? A steady source of food? Protection from predators? Is any of this worth giving up the freedom to make our own choices?>

The crowd of gathered Pokémon buzzed with excitement as Woolly and Unite just looked on, perplexed.

Unite: <Jeez. You get the impression that this guy doesn't like trainers much?>

Woolly: <Duh! He could only be less subtle if he was wearing bright glowing letters on his antlers that spelled out "I hate trainers!">

Sawsbuck: <Wild Pokémon! It is time we made our stand against the oppression of trainers! The choice is yours to make, please don't throw it away by giving yourself over to them!>

His speech finished, the Sawsbuck bowed politely.

<I thank you for your time, Pokémon. I do hope you will think about the things I have said today.>

As fast as he had appeared, the Sawsbuck galloped off, heading back towards the Route 6 entrance, most likely to spread his word further afield, leaving the local Pokémon to talk among themselves.

Wild Galvantula: <Are Pokémon that go with humans really giving up their freedom?>

Wild Ferroseed: <I dunno, but he sure made sound like being with a trainer horrible! I'm gonna' make extra sure to avoid trainers from now on!>

Wild Nosepass: <But not humans could be like that...could they?>

As the Pokémon continued to chatter, Woolly and Unite turned to each other, the latter with an perplexed look on his face.

Unite: <What was that?! Is their a revolution going on that we haven't heard about?>

Woolly: <It can't be too big a deal. You're right, we would have heard about it, otherwise.>

Unite: <But we belong to a trainer, don't we? Wild Pokémon don't talk to us very often...maybe there is something going on here?>

Woolly: <This is really really weird...>
