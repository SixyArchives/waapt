Above Driftveil City

| Zeta and Glossa are flying atop Cetus's back. Kaminari is nowhere to be seen, and the rest of her party of retreated to their Pokeballs.

| Zeta is meditating once more.

| She falls deeper and deeper into a trance.

| She can feel the sun beating down on her face. She feels sand beneath her. The wind howls, with that tone unique to the wide open expanses of a desert. Zeta opens her eyes.

Zeta's Mindscape

| Zeta is in a desert. It stretches on in all directions, farther than the eye can see. Nearby, there are old ruins, of the sort you might find in Orre. The sky appears to be split into quadrants, centered on where she is now sitting.

| To the north, the sky is clouded and storming, with lightning flashing and sand being whipped into the air. It is dark and grim in that direction, and Zeta shudders as she looks at it. The path exiting the ruins to the north is barely visible, covered in sand.

| Looking south, she sees the sun beaming down, bright and cheerful and happy. She can see an oasis, off in the distance. She can feel herself relaxing, just at the sight of it. The path leading off to the south is narrow and under-used, barely maintained at all.

| To the east, the sky is gray and overcast. The sand there is coarse, gritty. The land is heavily cratered and torn. It has obviously been bombed and destroyed, as if only recently the fighting had stopped. The path leading in that direction is wide and well maintained, the sand packed hard and solid.

| West, the sky is... hard to look at. It swirls with colors, constantly shifting and changing. The colors disturb her. No sky should turn those shades, those hues. The sand there twists and changes colors as well, forming shapes and images. Sometimes they appear to be stories, sometimes merely geometric figures. She can feel her sanity slipping away as she stares. She quickly looks away. The last thing she sees is that the path leading to the west moves, fluidly, like a snake or a vine blowing in the wind.

| She looks down. She is sitting on an intricate seal. It curls and wraps around her, soothing her mind, healing the damage done by the Western Desert. She calms down.

| Examining the ruins themselves more closely, she notices three things: A spear, a veil, and a torch. They float above her head, and she feels them to be significant, though not exactly sure in what specific way.
