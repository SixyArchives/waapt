Pokémon Battle Simulator A-2 - PEFE HQ

Upon hearing that the PEFE was equipped with a state of the art holographic Pokemon battling simulator (Based on technology developed in the Orre region), Flare was eager to give it a try, and had convinced Wulfric to come and see it with her.

However, the Lucario was far too busy to worry about his pack in Unova to watch as she fought against a holographic Alakazam. Although he was slightly worried that it would prove too difficult for her to handle, the Blaziken had told him that "It wouldn't be any fun if it wasn't a challenge", and had proceeded to set the AI on the hardest difficulty.

That being said, she was equally matched with her holographic opponent, despite the type disadvantage.

Holographic Alakazam♂: Playing file: Taunt4.OOG: <You fight like a Miltank farmer!>

The virtual Alakazam's eyes glow as it prepares to assult Flare with Telekinetic force. However, he finds himself being kicked hard between the eyes by a High Jump Kick from his much more real opponent, disrupting his concentration.

Flare: <How appropriate, you fight like a Miltank.>

Quickly executing the Recovery.exe file, the Alakazam raises his spoons and launches a Psybeam at the flaming chicken, who easily dodges by leaping several feet into the air.

<One spicy chicken leg, coming up! Hold the Onion Rings!>

Smiling to herself, the Blaziken sets her leg a-blaze, then swings it down at the Alakazam, only to have it blocked with a hastily erected Reflect.

<Oh, you are crafty for a bunch of zeros and ones...>

Flare could have sworn she saw the holographic Psychic type smirk at her as he grabbed hold of her with his Psychic power, then flung her away, the Fire Type chicken slamming against the arena's far wall.

<...Ow...>

Dropping down to the floor, the she winched in pain before turning her attention to Wulfric, who was watching in the stands at the edge of the arena. Or at least, she thought he was. Instead, he was slumped over, not paying much attention.

<HEY!>

The canine Pokémon jumped, startled at Flare's voice. Quickly realizing she had seen him barely paying any attention, he lowered his head to avert his gaze from the bird, seemingly very embarrassed.

With a sigh, Flare shoots a glance at the Alakazam hologram, who appeared to be preparing another attack while she was distracted.

<Okay, time out!>

Reacting to her command, the virtual Pokémon froze in place, seemingly suspended in time. Now sure that she wouldn't be ganked while her back was turned, the Flare trots over to Wulfric, a somewhat concerned look on her face.

<What gives? I thought you wanted to watch?>

Wulfric continues to stare at the floor for a few more seconds, then finds the courage to meet her gaze again, before speaking to her in a small, seemingly rarely used voice that would have been almost inaudible had the two not been in close proximity.

Wulfric: <I'm sorry...I was...just thinking about my pack...>

Flare: <Oh, you mean those mons in the Challanger's Cave where Luke caught 'ya?>

Wulfric nods, a frown crossing his face at the thought.

Wulfric: <I'm worried that they might not be able to cope with the cold weather...>

With a sigh, he slumps over, resting his head on his paws as he gazes into the middle distance, a concerned expression etched onto his face.

Flare shook her head at this, pressing a talon up to her forehead as she shut her eyes briefly, trying to think of how to cheer her friend up. Emotional issues were not a field she excelled in.

Flare: <Maybe they'll be fine? I hear it gets really cold in Unova during the winter, after all.>

Wulfric turns his head slightly to look at the Blaziken, still looking rather downcast.

Wulfric: <This is completely unexpected, though. They wouldn't have had time to prepare being caught off-guard like this.>

Flare: <Oh...I see.>

Not sure how to proceed from that statement, she goes over and sits down next to the metal jackel, reaching down to pat him affectionately on the back with a talon.

<Come on, it can't be that bad. Your pack's probably been through worse, AND they have a lovely warm...dark, dank cave to hide in.>

She couldn't help but feel disgusted at the thought of this. Eurgh, who in their right mind would want to live in a dump like that?

Wulfric: <...I can tell what you're thinking, you know.>

...Drat. She kept forgetting that he could do that. To try and save face, Flare quickly tries to change the subject.

Flare: <Come on, you know as well as I do that you Lucario's are tough stuff. It would take more than a bit of unexpected cold to take you down, right?>

Wulfric bit his lip, mulling the thought over. He had been through some harsh winters when he was a pup, but they had always pulled through, if not without a little bit a trouble.

...Perhaps he was getting worried over nothing?

Wulfric: <...I suppose you might be right.>

Smiling to herself, Flare rubs the Lucario's head affectionatly, happy to see him feeling better, if just a little bit.

Flare: <There, nothing to worry about. Now, how about you come down and practice with me? Kicking things in the face always makes me feel better when I'm down in the dumps.>

Wulfric sits up and rubs the back of his head with a paw, not if violence would really make him feel better...and slightly worried that was how Flare dealt with her frustrations.

Wulfric: <...I'll pass, thanks.>

Flare shrugs in response, then hops over the railings, striding back into the arena to continue facing off against the still frozen Alakazam.

Flare: <Suit yourself, I guess.>
