Skye initially gets nervous by instinct. After all, foxes tend to hunt birds. Despite this, she forces herself to relax. After a minute of memory-browsing, one significant memory does come up...

Ecruteak City, about a year ago

It is nighttime. A Houndour is sitting just outside of Eskay's house. Skye notices the Houndour, and flies out of the open window to talk to him.

Skye: <What's wrong? You're never up this late.>

Houndour: <I couldn't sleep, so I wanted some fresh air.>

Skye could tell that wasn't the only reason.

Skye: <Come on. There's more than that on your mind.>

Houndour: <...I've been thinking of leaving the team...>

Skye: <What!? You can't be serious!>

Houndour: <I'm sorry, it's just... You know I've always preferred doing stuff my own way... Don't get me wrong, you all are great, I just... I feel like I'm not able to contribute...>

Skye understood what he meant. Eskay couldn't understand a word they said, what with being human.

Skye: <I can see why you'd feel that way, but try to reconsider>

Houndour: <I have tried. I'm sorry, Skye, but I've made up my mind... Tell the others I said "Goodbye"...>

The Houndour starts to walk away...

Skye: <...Goodbye, Tartarus...>

Lake's Mind

Deluge the Feraligatr♀: <Okay, now pay attention and keep quiet, or else you'll scare him away...>

Wow, Mom was huge especially to a Totodile. He had forgotten how small he had been too. Everything else was so large compared to him all of a sudden...

Or it would be, if he could somehow break the misty fog covering everything.

Totodile #1: <Ooh, what's Mom doing?>

Totodile #2: <Hush! She told us to keep it down!>

Totodile #1: <Sorry, my bad.>

Lake decides to pay attention for the time being. He watches as his mother swims through the water, only the top of her head visible, as she moves towards her prey, a...Zebstrika?

No, that can't be right! Zebstrika didn't live in Johto!

Upon realizing that, the Zebstrika was replaced with...ooh, what was it again? Oh! A Ponyta!

With that detail corrected, Lake continued to observe as she swam closer and closer to the horse Pokémon as it drank from the murky water.

Hmm...what kind of places had murky water?

Totodile #1: <What's she gonna' do?>

Totodile #2: <Be quiet!>

Deludge paused for a second, staying incredibly still.

Then, in one lightning-fast motion, she snapped forward and closed her jaws around the Ponyta's neck, the horse Pokémon whinnying loudly in pain. It began to struggle furiously, kicking at the Feraligatr, but it was already too late.

With a flick of her tail, Deluge rolled to the side, Lake recognizing it as a common hunting technique used amongst the Feraligatr line: The Death Roll.

Naturally, it was a technique banned in pretty much every form of official Pokémon battling.

A sickening crack rung out in the air, then the Ponyta's body went limp, dead from having it's neck snapped. Grinning to herself, Deluge lets go of the dead Pokémon's body, leaving it's badly torn corpse behind, the scent of blood now hanging heavily in the air as it poured from it's open wounds.

The Feraligatr licked her lips hungrily, then waved to her concealed children, who had witnessed the entire gory spectacle.

Deludge: <Did everyone see what I did there?>

Totodile #2: <Yep!>

Totodile #3: <That was awesome, Mom!>

Totodile #1: <Do it again! Do it again!>

She couldn't help but let out a chuckle.

Deludge: <Later, okay? Now come and get some meat! There's plenty to go around!>

<"YAY!!!">

The Totodile happily jumped into the water, swimming over to the opposite shore, then beginning to savagely tear of pieces of the Ponyta's flesh to eat.

Lake hesitated every so briefly, then swam over to join then, now reminded of why he had always disliked hunting.

Lake's Mind

Lake hesitantly approaches the Ponyta, it's lifeless eyes staring at him, an terrified expression permanently etched onto it's face (Or at least, it would be until Deludge ate it), staring at him in a way he would never forget.

...Wait, he had forgotten. Well, he wouldn't be doing that anytime soon.

He suddenly felt somemon nip him, so he turned to find one of his siblings giving him a quizzical look.

Totodile #1: <Are you going to eat your share? Or can I have it?>

Lake: <Er...>

His stomach suddenly growled, giving away the fact that he was still hungry despite witnessing the brutal mutilation of an innocent Ponyta.

<...Yeah, I am.>

Totodile #1: <Aww...>

His sibling shakes his head, then resumes savagely eating his own torn piece of flesh. Lake hesitantly eyes the corpse, then bites down and rips of a piece of flesh for himself, tearing off a small chunk and swallowing it quickly.

Now that the trauma was done with, Lake glanced around, trying to piece together where he was. Despite the haze covering everything, the it all felt so...familiar. As if he had been here recently.

He stopped and sniffed the air, which was damp and musky. Almost like...

...A swamp! They had lived in a swamp!

But...a swamp in Johto? There were no swamps, apart from the one...

...the one Bluescale was built in.

Suddenly it all fell into place. He suddenly knew why his simultaneous past and future self had chosen that place to build Bluescale. He had constructed it there...

...because that was where his family was.
