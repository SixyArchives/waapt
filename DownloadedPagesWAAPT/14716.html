Rex: Um... alright?

Seeing that her son was accepting her terms, the woman reached into her purse and produced a Pokéball, the capsule opening in a flash of light and releasing a Kangaskhan to stand before her.

Faith: Rex, I'm going to use Return three times with Brynhildr. I want you to choose one of your Pokémon to deal with that situation and react accordingly.

Rex stared uncomprehendingly for a moment before noting the Parent Pokémon drop into a combative pose, the steel behind his mother's eyes telling him not to hesitate in his choice.

Rex: Er, umm... r-right. Well, then... Lian, I'll go with you.

The Mienshao nodded in understanding, leaping forward in front of her trainer and watching her opponent carefully. Then, sure enough enough, Brynhildr lunged for her.

Rex: Dodge to the left and use Force Palm!

It might have been just training, but experience had taught Rex not to take his mother's training lightly. Not unless he wanted to invite pain for his Pokémon... and himself.

Lian reacted immediately, easily evading the attack, the Kangaskhan's body sailing harmlessly past her before coming to a rest with her back to the Mienshao.

Faith: I see. You chose Lian for her speed to set up for an easy counterstrike. A smart choice.

Lian moved in for the Force Palm, mere moments away from landing the blow.

Faith: But how will you react to this?

And then a shell of light surrounded Brynhildr, the purple mink stopping her attack short in awe as an explosion of energy formed around the Kangaskhan, its child hopping free of its pouch with a tiny roar.

Rex: Wh-

Faith: Return.

The tail of Brynhildr lashed out suddenly, catching Lian in her side and sending her tumbling backwards, the mink managing to right herself just in time. However, Brynhildr was upon her once again immediately afterwards, readying a punch.

Faith: Return.

Rex: Detect!

Lian managed to shift to the side to avoid the strike, which came down on the ground just beside her, still on guard for any further attacks. However, none came, the mother Kangaskhan instead wandering back over to retrieve her child, which had plopped onto the ground waiting patiently for its mother to return.

Rex: That was...

Faith: Mega Evolution. Or so you thought.

Rex: Huh?

Holding up her bare wrist for her son to see, Faith simply smiled, the implication sinking in.

Rex: You don't have a Mega Stone? But then...

Lyra: Oh, I can explain that.

Lyra: It's been such a long time since we've last met up, I never got to introduce you to Púrpura.

Faith: Do you understand what this was meant to test, Rex?

Faith: You're certainly skilled enough, but you're still not good at reacting to situations on the fly and miss a lot of opportunities as a result. Were my Kangaskhan truly Mega Evolved, those attacks would have been concurrent, providing less room for error. Instead, you allowed my second strike to connect, which left Lian damaged and your chance for a counter diminished.

Faith stood before Rex now, looking her son in the eyes.

Faith: Your mind is brilliant and has offered you a lot of opportunities. But your edge has been dulled by far too much thinking. You tend to analyze things far too greatly, when far simpler solutions exist.

Rex seemed embarrassed by his mother's words, looking down from shame. Seeing that, Faith's face lightened and she stepped forwards, pulling her son into a gentle embrace, a hand gently massaging the back of his head.

Faith: This isn't a scolding, Rex. There's no need to feel ashamed about what I've said. I just want you to realize your faults and grow from them.

She released him and smiled, ruffling his hair now which brought a whine from the boy.

Faith: You're much further along than I was at your age, you know?

Blushing, Rex wiggled free of his mother's hand and began to straighten his hair, nodding in understanding.

Rex: I guess so... But, what exactly is this a lecture about? Why are you telling me all of this?

Faith: It's regarding your issues with Mega Evolution and why Damian won't transform. I want you to think about what you can do to get closer with him. But, I also want you to give me your Pokédex to hold onto.

Rex: Huh? What for?

Faith: This is a matter of heart, not a matter of information. The less distractions you have, the sooner you should be able to arrive at your answer.

Rex was silent for a moment, but nodded, handing over the blue-green device with no further complaints, his mother placing it into her purse before zipping it away.

Faith: Now, then, it's late. We should find somewhere to rest for the night.
