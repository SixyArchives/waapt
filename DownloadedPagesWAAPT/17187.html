Marie and Ray's Room

Ray was currently laying on the bed, woozy, having spent the last minute puking in the bathroom. The effects of the alcohol were wearing off. The windows had been left open to provide air.

"What the hell?" came a demanding voice, as Marie came in to the room from the window.

Ray brightened up."Mar!" Her face then took on a worried look, as she said "Listen, Mar, I need to tell you something.."

"Save it." Marie growled."I saw what happened back there, Ray. How could you?!" Ray bowed her head."I..I was drunk..and he was comforting me..because..because you weren't here." Her head rose, her eyes challenging.

"How come you're so late, huh? How come you've been so distant lately, huh? I mean, you'd have to have a reason for why it took you this long to come back, right?"

Marie looked uncomfortable."I..I had work, Ray.." "Optional work, " Ray corrected her."There was no need for you to stay in Sophian, I saw your mission briefings, it could just as easily have been done here, in Goldenrod. So tell me, Marie. Why've you been staying away?"

"Because..because I.." Marie tried to speak, but was fumbling for words."Because you can't bear to be seen with me?" Ray guessed quietly.

Marie's eyes grew wide."N-no, that's not.." "I mean, really, did you think I didn't notice? All the times in public when I tried to show even a bit of affection, you would move away..are you embarrassed by me, Marie?"

Marie's eyes narrowed."N-n..n-..well, if you weren't so childish all the time, I wouldn't have to be a mother for you in public, now would I? If you could just act your age in public, maybe I wouldn't be a bit aloof every now and then! And what gives you the right to be mad at me, after what you just did?"

"I made a mistake, yes, and I'm sorry. But at least I'm not afraid or embarrassed by who I am..unlike certain others."

Both Marie and Ray stood there, looking full on at each other, both angry, incredible amounts of bitterness washing out of them both.

"Y'know, if you've always felt like this, why's it all coming out just now, eh?" Marie said, in a quiet tone."I guess being outside of the hometown after so long makes me think things like this that maybe...maybe we aren't fit to be together."

Marie turned around and gripped the door handle."If that's what you think...then alright." She walked out, and as she did, Ray kept looking at the door for another few seconds, before running to it, trying to open it, but it was no use, as she slid down against it, right where Marie was on the other side, and they both began to weep.

The door remained closed.
