Beach Cave - PMD-A

Apparently there was another way of earning Poké in this world: finding it lying around in a "Mystery Dungeon."

The Flygon that Lake had spoken too earlier had told him that there was a Mystery Dungeon in the cave on the beach that he had been too the other day. Although he had been warned that Mystery Dungeons could be dangerous, Lake didn't have any trouble making his way through it.

That said...it was a rather strange experience. Finding his way around was rather hard in a place where space itself was twisted. He found corridors that led to nowhere, had followed a path out of one chamber only to enter it from another end without changing direction, and at one point, he could have sworn he had descended several flights of stairs, only to somehow wind up back at the cave entrance!

Just trying to make sense of it all made his head spin, so he decided not to worry about it. For the sake of his sanity.

As if the warped space wasn't bad enough, the dungeon was also populated with odd Pokémon that didn't speak and tried to attack Lake on sight. Of course, they were all rather low level, and no match for the fully trained Feraligatr, but it was an annoyance.

Lake could only guess that they had been driven to madness by being stuck in the twisted space of the dungeon...which only made him want to get through it quicker.

Thankfully, after a bit more wandering (after finding several hundred Poké, as well as a few items like some Oran Berries and an Apple), Lake made it to the end of the dungeon: a small scheduled cove some way from the beach.

And it was there that he found a piece of crystal lying on the sand.

Lake: <Oh, a shard!>

He went to pick it up, but stopped, hesitating. The last shard he had found had sent him to this world, and he wasn't sure he wanted to pick it up and risk getting sent to a different world. Who knew what kind of place he could potentially end up in?

But then...

<Well, it already happened once, what are the odds of it happening again?>

With that, the Feraligatr picks up the shard...

Blip!

...And finds himself about fifty feet in the air in a completely different place.

<...Me and my big mouth.>

It's then that gravity decides to take effect, sending Lake falling down to the ground below.
