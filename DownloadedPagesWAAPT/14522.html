Cafe Soleil

-Herbert purses his lips and looks away from Aline.-

Herbert: ...I see. So he decided to tell you himself, and that's what he chose to say... well, it was a lie. Not a flat-out denial of what actually happened, but rather the kind of white lie you tell to children when you don't want to tell them the whole truth.

-He sighs, and starts fiddling with the end of his scarf.-

Herbert: The true story... isn't a particularly happy one, I'll admit. It's a story for grown-ups that kids just happened to get mixed up in. But I think it'll help you understand some things. If nothing else, it's proof that not every fight is as cool or exciting as it looks on the television.

-He leans back in his chair and clears his throat.-

Herbert: ...I'll start from the beginning.

Once upon a time, there was a boy, a girl, and a Lucario. The boy and the Lucario were very good friends, and the girl and the Lucario were even closer. The boy and the girl didn't like each other very much, but they were still friends, and the Lucario made sure they didn't get into any fights... most of the time, at least.

Anyway, the three of them were happy. Then one day, a masked man kidnapped the Lucario whilst he was out training and took him to a faraway desert. The boy was angry, of course. He told the girl that he was going to go to the desert and rescue the Lucario. But the girl was scared. She didn't want to get kidnapped too. They argued. The boy got angrier. Eventually, he left in order to travel to the desert. And the girl was alone.

After the boy had gone, the masked man came to talk to the girl. He told her that she could save the Lucario if she agreed to work for him. The girl, scared and desperate, thought that she could outsmart the man and escape with the Lucario. And so, without sparing a thought for the boy, she accepted the man's offer. But once they arrived in the desert, she found that the masked man was smarter than she thought. He made the girl wear a mask and made sure that she was watched at all times.

As for the Lucario... the man tortured him. He put the Lucario through unimaginable pain. Eventually, the pain made the Lucario forget everything. All he could remember was how to fight, how to kill, and how to inflict pain. And the girl. Even after everything he went through, he still remembered that the girl was important to him... but he forgot the boy.

Meanwhile, the boy was searching the desert for the masked man. He never found him, of course. But eventually, he found the girl and the Lucario. The boy didn't recognise the girl because of the mask. They fought. The boy lost. The Lucario continued to attack him. After all, the boy meant nothing to the Lucario. The girl didn't stop him. She was scared of what the masked man would do to her if she did. She'd never liked the boy anyway.

The Lucario beat the boy to within an inch of his life. He nearly died. There came a point when he hoped he would. But before the Lucario could finish him off, one of his friends arrived and scared the girl off. The Lucario followed her. The boy spent a week in hospital, lying in bed as doctors and nurses knitted his broken bones back together. He told himself that he'd be stronger next time. He didn't want to admit that he was terrified of what would happen if he lost again.

I'll try to make the rest of the story quick. The next time the boy, the girl and the Lucario fought, the boy won. He managed to catch the Lucario. Thinking that she was finally safe from the masked man, the girl took her mask off. The boy... he was furious. He felt confused. Betrayed. Alone. He hit her. He screamed at her. In the end, he ran away. He didn't think he could trust anyone. So he decided to try and recover the Lucario's memories himself.

But the Lucario was angry too. He remembered that the girl was important to him, and seeing the boy attack her made him an enemy. So he made the boy's life a misery any way he could. He still didn't remember the boy. Not until the very end. The boy, already broken by the girl's betrayal, started to think that maybe the Lucario had never been his friend in the first place. For the first time in his life, he felt well and truly alone.

The story ends well - for the girl and the Lucario, at least. The boy had saved them both, and even managed to help the Lucario get his memories back. And the two of them were happy together. But they never caught the masked man, or found out why he kidnapped the Lucario in the first place. Without something to fight for, the boy began to dwell on bad memories. He started to hate the girl. He started to hate the Lucario for not hating the girl. And most of all, he started to hate himself.

And yet... at some point during his depression, after failing to catch the masked man time and time again, the boy realised that spending his days wallowing in his own self-pity and lashing out at the few friends he had left wasn't going to help him. He became more determined. He resolved never to let what had happened to him happen to anyone else. And above all, he swore that he'd catch the man that put him and his friends though all of this, so that things could finally go back to the way they were before. So that they could be happy again.

-Herbert looks Aline in the eye for the first time since he began telling the story.-

Herbert: ...That's it, I'm afraid. I don't know how the story ends. Maybe it doesn't have a happy ending. Some stories don't. But what I do know is that the boy doesn't get a happy ending, then he'll try his hardest to make his own, no matter what it takes to get there. Because he doesn't want to have to tell that story again.
