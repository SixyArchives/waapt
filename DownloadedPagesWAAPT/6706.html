Undersea Palace - PMD-A

To be honest, Treeo hadn't wanted to stop and sleep, especially when they hadn't really gotten anything done, but he would have been lying if he had said that dodging all of those traps hadn't tired him out...

So the Sceptile had curled up to sleep next to Comet, who still seemed rather on-edge, even though they were out of the water for the time being. Despite some difficulty, he had managed to get a few hours sleep, and although he wasn't as well rested as he would have liked to have been, he didn't feel fired enough for it to be a problem.

Now that he was awake again, he rolled over to gaze at Comet, funding that she was already awake, looking slightly calmer, if somewhat still somewhat nervous.

Treeo: <Hey. How did you sleep?>

The Charizard gazes back at Treeo, a pitiful look on her face.

Comet: <Terribly...>

She sighs, gazing down at the floor.

<I hate this place.>

Frowning at Comet's sad state, Treeo shifts a bit closer to the Charizard (being careful not to accidentally brush against her tail flame and set himself on fire).

Treeo: <I don't blame you for feeling that way. Even when you can breath underwater, this place is still a death-trap.>

Comet: <I know...but at least you can do something to help. All I did yesterday was curl up in Drift's bubble, preying to all the legendaries I could think of not to die. I did nothing to get us past all those hazards...>

She sighs, burying her head in her claws.

<Perhaps my coming along was a bad idea. I'm more of a hindrance than a help...>

Feeling bad for her, Treeo gently pats Comet's neck with a claw, trying to make her feel better.

Treeo: <Don't say that. You're a huge help, Comet.>

Comet: <No I'm not. I was useless yesterday, and I'll probably be useless again today, too...>

Treeo: <Come on, you don't know that yet. We might not even have to go underwater today.>

Comet snorts.

Comet: <I bet we will. Most of this castle seems to be submerged. What's up with this place, anyway?>

Treeo shrugs.

Treeo: <I get the impression that if there's anyone living here, he or she must not be keen on guests.>

Comet: <No duh. Why else would you install spinning blades of death in the hallway? They sure don't give the impression that the guy that owns this place wants you to come in and make yourself comfortable. Not that you could...>
