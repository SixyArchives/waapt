Somewhere in the far north of Sinnoh

Two Sneasel are walking—well, more like trudging—along through the snow, not talking all that much. The leader has a Reflect screen up in front of them to protect against the wind, while the other sticks close.

<Y'know, the cold is still a pain, > she mutters, shivering. The leader glances back and pats her shoulder absently.

<You'll get used to it eventually. We did!> He offers a smile, but she rolls her eyes.

<Martin, you've lived in it your whole life. I don't think you so much "got used to it" as "were born into it", > she mutters.

<Well, fine. Be grumpy if you like, > Martin sniffs, turning back around.

<Aw, don't be like that, > she says, hurrying to keep up as he starts moving faster. Turning her eyes to the ground to avoid tripping over branches and the like hidden beneath the snow, she nearly runs into him when he stops abruptly in front of a large cave.

<What? What is it?> she asks, peering over his shoulder, through the pink screen, towards the cave. Martin motions to be quiet with one paw and stands silently for a few moments. Just as he's about to start moving again, a roar sounds from within the cave. The younger Sneasel shrinks back, hiding behind Martin. He ignores her, smiling widely as a Piloswine emerges from the cave.

<Good morning Miss Nellie!> he says brightly, waving. The Piloswine studies him behind the fur covering her eyes, then snorts.

<Martin. Out hunting again? Who's that behind you? If that's the sneaky little twit that keeps trying to steal my berries, you tell her—>

<Oh no, it's not Morana! She's off trying to steal from Trainers again! This is my other sister, Glint! Say hi to Miss Nellie, Glint, > he says, twisting around to shoo his sister out from behind him.

<Uh, hi, > she says quietly, waving a little. Nellie snorts again.

<So Biton wasn't crazy. Well, he is, but not about this, I suppose. But still, you make sure that Morana knows to stay away from my family! That goes for you too, Martin, and the rest of your family. I let you hunt around here because you're good kids, but you get too close to my children...> The Piloswine swings her tusks vines at a dead branch hanging by the entrance of her cave and snaps it in two.

<Of course, Miss Nellie! Have a good day!> Martin brightly waves and starts walking away.

<Yeah... what he said.> Glint scampers after her brother.

The Piloswine chuckles. <You too, kids. Don't get yourselves into trouble and make me and Asher go fine you.> She turns and lumbers back into her cave to attend her cubs.

As they near the woods, Glint taps Martin's shoulder. <Um, why do you three have friendships with Pokemon that could kill you if they want?> she asks.

<Oh, you mean Miss Nellie and Miss Stella and Asher? Well, we wouldn't, but we got in a lot of trouble when we were wandering all over the place looking for you, and they were all nice to us. Also, we always knew Asher. You'll meet him at some point, I'm sure.>

Don't be too sure about that, brat. That just means more work for me.

<Shut up, Narrator. Anyway, how did Miss Nellie help you?> Glint asks, looking over her shoulder in the direction of the Piloswine's cave.

<There was this giant storm that we got caught in, and we couldn't find our way back home, > Martin says, bending down to pick up a rock poking out of the snow. He brushes it off with one paw, then places it back down. <We would probably freeze to death—we do have limits as to how cold we can get—but we found Miss Nellie's cave. We went in to get shelter, and she decided not to kill us. Probably because even Biton looked really pathetic.> Martin stops at a tree and looks over it for something Glint can't see, then nods and moves on. <So yeah, she took pity on us and let us stay there until we could move on. And then we found out that she lives really close to the best hunting spot, so we kept being friends with her. So long as we stay away from her cubs, she lets us hunt here and protects us from predators.>

<Huh. What about Miss Stella?> Glint asks. She stumbles over a branch half-sticking out of the snow and Martin grabs her arm to keep her from falling.

<Careful. Miss Stella met us when we trying to climb the mountain. She saved us from being eaten by an Absol, and told us to get off her mountain before we died. We agreed—well, Morana and I agreed, and Biton could hardly argue with an Abomasnow—and she helped us get down safely. And then the next time we tried to go up, she helped us reach the top and get down.> Martin slips behind a boulder, dragging Glint with him, and puts down the shield.

<What about—>

Martin shakes his head, motioning for her to be quiet. <Ask Morana when we get home.>

Glint sighs but otherwise shuts up—how these morons manage to get her to do that when I, a great Narrator, can never is a mystery that needs to be solved. The two remain motionless as the birds start singing again. When a Starly lands too close to the boulder and starts scratching at the snow around the roots of a tree, Martin is on it in a flash, neatly cutting its throat. Glint scrambles up over the boulder and slides down next to him. Martin wipes his claws on the tree bark, then looks up towards the branches.

<I'd like to thank the family of this Starly for the life of it, and offer my condolences for your loss. My sister and I will leave you be now.> He picks up the Starly and hands it to Glint as he puts up a Reflect screen to block the wind.

<Hey, Martin? Why do you do that every time you make a kill?> Glint asks, struggling to keep up and not trip now that her arms are occupied.

<What, wipe my claws? Well, I don't want them to be bloody, do I? And it marks the tree that I made a kill in so I don't hunt from the same family every time. Well, that's what it's supposed to do, anyway, > Martin slows down a bit to accommodate his sister.

<No, I mean talk to the Starly.>

<Oh, that. Well, I did just kill someone's kid. I figure its family deserves something. Biton sometimes leaves berries. Morana thinks we're both stupid.> Martin glances back at the woods. <I just hope they don't take it as me mocking them. A Staraptor attack would probably not go well for us.>

<Yeah, I guess you're right, > Glint murmurs thoughtfully.

<C'mon, we gotta hurry up. Biton's gonna bite my head off if you're late getting home.> Martin grabs the Starly under one arm and starts hurrying. Glint scrambles after him.

Aaaand that's it. I'll be going now. Go get that kid back in here so she can take over again. I've got some stuff to do.

The little boy's mother comes over to reprimand him, but Clockwise and the Rotom are more interested in the Pokemon.

<Ooh! Beedrill!> Clockwise says, hopping up and down.

The Rotom lets out an excited whirr.

<Huh? No, you're not a Beedrill. That's a Beedrill. You're a Fan Rotom. Most definitely not a Beedrill, > Clockwise corrects, giving him a stern look.
