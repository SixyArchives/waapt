Tower of Mastery - Shalur City

Valorie was entering the tower of mastery when...

"Whoa!" a girl's voice called out and Valorie was simultaneously knocked off her feet and caught in a fluid motion before she had a chance to react as someone bigger than her ran or more accurately skated into her.

The person quickly came to a stop a few feet away and began easing Valorie back upright.

"Sorry about that. I guess I need to be more careful around that door! Are you okay?" none other than Korrina asked with a smile, unable to help herself after seeing the bewildered-but-otherwise-fine look on Valorie's face. Gurkinn chuckled quietly from a distance away, declining to comment.

"It's... It's alright." a startled shortcake replied, regaining her composure.

"I was just on my way to the gym. Are you sure you're alright..? Even your arm?" Korrina proceeded to ask, overtly friendly in tone. This was a nice girl Valorie appreciated nice things.

"Yeah... Um—Are you Korrina, the Shalour City Gym Leader?" Valorie asked, suspicious for obvious reasons.

"That's me..! Listen you're a trainer, right? Why don't you come down to the gym with me? Maybe I can help you and put on a show at the same time!" Korrina said in a knowing manner.

"Well... alright." Valorie replied slightly sheepishly. Korrina decidedly did not leave the handicapped girl in the dust, taking her time getting to the gym for once.

...

Standing on the gym battlefield floor, Valorie released her Dragonite. Korrina's eyes widened in a moment's shock as she internally remarked that this was not a pokemon any 'normal' little kid could've possibly gotten ahold of. She was also pretty sure it wasn't supposed to be that humongous—Was it? That entire family of pokemon was one of the rarest on the planet, so it wasn't like she had something to compare to.

She was certain the pokemon had the raw strength; now she just needed to make sure Valorie had the skill to command it. You never knew whose parents might give their kids strong pokemon without teaching them how to use them first!

...

With a strong type advantage and plenty of skill to back it up there hadn't really been any way for Korrina to recover from the start of the battle. The gym leader merely smiled in satisfaction however.

"There's not a lot left I could teach you!~" she remarked pleasantly, closing the distance between the two of them and offering Valorie her hand to shake. "Thanks for surprising me with a really tough fight. I feel like my Pokemon really gave it their all." she continued.

Valorie beamed in response, a bit of admiration in her eyes. Korrina handled loss with a great deal of grace, the likes of which she definitely could not. If that had been Valorie, she probably would've been giving someone the death glare right about then... or worse!

"You're an excellent trainer, and so it's my honor to give you this badge. I bet you'll be rollin' in these soon~" she continued. Valorie would find that the rumble badge had found it's way into her palm.

The shortcake frowned however. "Well... that's the thing. I'm not taking the pokemon league challenge." Valorie responded. This once again took Korrina by momentary surprise. Silently puzzling over this, she nodded in affirmation.
I dropped out of school to become a pokemon master, but then they pulled me right back in to get my doctorate!
