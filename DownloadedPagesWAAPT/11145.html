Ignis (Gaston's Logs)

Gaston: Chikiko opened the gate that separated the Upper Ring and the Lower Ring of Ignis and what I saw... well... it changed me.

As the gates push open, Gaston is greeted by the pleasent imagry of a nation's people seemingly struggling to get by.

Chikiko: Th-this way sir...

Gaston: I was taken aback by the suffering of the people in the city. Most of the people in the lower ring... where we entered from the Upper Ring.... was ammased with great poverty. Beggards on the street clamouring about for a simple grain dropped by a Pidove, refugees from neighbouring nations, particularly Puglis, setting up shanty towns with what little they have, stalls selling overripe foods and a general sense of misery everywhere. The way they looked at me, with large, dead eys, waiting for a sound, a sign, a voice... something. There was nothing here that was pleasent in the slightest.

The middle ring, which was located furthur up, was not much better. While the sad state of poverty that the lower ring projected was not as obvious here, there was still an omnious prescense of misery all around us. As we walked through the city, were were always watched, in awe and in terror. The frozen, haunted looks of these broken people... I couldn't take it...

Gaston: Chikiko...

The maid turns around and faces Gaston.

Chikiko: Yes master Gaston?

Gaston: When my father spoke to me about his own travels through Ransei, in particular the nation of Ignis, he spoke so highly of it. That the city was essentially embellished in gold, and that the people were amoungst the happiest, liveliest and most loyal of all kingdoms he'd seen. I walk around here and I see none of that of which my father talked about. What happened to Ignis?

Chikiko sighs.

Chikiko: It's a long story, and not a particularly happy one. It's hard to belive things are better now than they were back then...

Ignis Palace (Earlier this Morning).

Ryuku streches and yawns in the morning sun.

Ryuku: Rise and shine! It's going to be another shining day in the land of fir-

Ryuku looks over to his bedside table and notices a note, bound together by ribbon and a hairpin.

Ryuku: ...Oh? What's this?

Ryuku removes the hairpin, unties the ribbon and unscrolls the note.

My Dearest Ryuku

Pleased do not be alarmed by what you are about to read, for I did not merely leave in spite of you my dear. No, I left because I need to take a pilgrimage around Ransei, as well as train in preperation for the upcoming storm that is brewing to the north of our country. We are about to enter dire times my dear, and it's best that we split for this time so I can get stronger, and you can learn how to run the country bestowed upon you by your father. It's about time you did anyway. On top of that, I have to reclaim something that was stolen from me unlawfully.

Besides, I will return in the fullness of time, my dearest Ryuku. Do not think that I left for some selfish reason. I left so I, and in turn, you can better serve the great nation of Ignis.

Regards, Keiko "Izumi" Atakashi

PS: Please don't burn down the nation while I'm away, I would like to come back to something, my dear~

Ryuku stares at the letter, tears welling up in his eye as he re-reads the letter over and over again.

Assistant: Sir? There is a meeting between your generals soon. Are you going to attend or shall I call the mistre-

Ryuku: Non... No... I'll be there... Just, I need some... breathing time.

Assistant: As you wish sir -leaves-

Ryuku re-reads the letter over and over again, stunned in his sadness.

Somewhere in Ransei...

A hooded, obviously feminine figure is Walking the Earth. With a Panpour on her shoulder and a behemoth trailing behind her.

Izumi: And such, it begins.
