-she exhales smoke-

Alys: That is, if you believed a word of it.

Honore: Yes. That one time with Gerard-

Alys: -when you said at dinner that you were over the moon with love, and that you planned to marry him one day? Yes, and you also lied to me the next day, when you said that Michel just drove you back from the party because Gerard's car broke down. -she grins-

I saw the Listerine on the counter, Honore. You spilled a bit.

-Honore blanches-

Alys: It is true that people do age gracefully, and that people can be very secure in older age. You are not one of them. You desperately cling to beauty you lost a decade ago, holding the image of the rich socialite when all you really want to do is go back to the days when all anyone expected from you was your parent's money and your mouth.

-Honore practically snarls. Alys chuckles-

Alys: Now, now. You're thinking you're a good mother, yes? You've done good things for your children, yes? I suppose you have, but they still don't tell you things, do they?

Honore: They-
