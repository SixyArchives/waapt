Sea of Kyogre - Several hundred miles south of the Orange Islands

<"How much further is it?">

<"Will you stop asking? I keep telling you, it's miles away!">

-Two Pokémon, a Gyarados and a Ponyta, are surfing across the ocean, the Ponyta riding on the Gyrados' back as he swims through the calm waters.-

Gyarados: <For once in your life, can't you be patient, please?>

Ponyta: <Well, I'm sorry, but I can't stand it out here! It's boring, it's wet, and the only Pokémon I have to talk to is...> -Shudders.- <You.>

Gyarados: <And what makes you think I enjoy having to put up with you incessant whining? If anything, it makes me want to get there faster so that I can finally be rid of you!>

Ponyta: <Well, it seems that we are in agreement then. For the rest of this trip, I won't talk to you!>

Gyarados: <And I won't talk to you either!>

Ponyta: <Fine!>

Gyarados: <Fine!>

-The two turn away from each other, remaining silent. This goes on for several minutes as the Gyarados surfs onwards. Until...-

Ponyta: -Sighs.- <Look, I'm sorry. It's not your fault that it's so far between the Orange Islands and [REDACTED]. I shouldn't have been so snappy.>

Gyarados: <Oh, don't say that. You have every right to be snappy! We're in the middle of the ocean, which is pretty much the worst place for a fire type like you to be. No wonder you feel that way.>

Ponyta: <...Alright, we're both sorry then. Agree?>

Gyarados: -Smiles and nods.- <Agree.>

Ponyta: <Cool.> -Glances upwards.- <Huh? Why is it cloudy all of a sudden?>

Gyarados: <Hmm?>

-The Water type glances up, finding that the sky has indeed become overcast. What's worrying is that the gathered layer of clouds are a deep gray. Storm clouds.-

Gyarados: <Oh no. Looks like it's going to rain.>

Ponyta: <RAIN?! Yuck! I don't want to sit out in the rain! Do something!>

Gyarados: <Alright, alright! Chill already...>

-Lacking anything else to cover both of them up with, The sea serpent Pokémon takes a deep breath and blows a Dive Bubble around the two, just as the heavens open and it starts poring with rain.-

Gyarados: <There, is that better?>

Ponyta: <...I suppose so.> -Glances skyward.- <It's weird, though. Wasn't the weather supposed to be clear for the next week?>

Gyarados: <That's what the local Castform told me. Perhaps he got it wrong?>

Ponyta: <Perhaps.> -Sighs.- <You can still surf in this weather, right?>

Gyarados: <Just as long as things don't get too mu-ARGH!>

-The Gyarados is nearly swamped by a HUGE wave that seemed to have come from nowhere. Thankfully, the water type manages to avoid being submerged by it.-

Ponyta: -Clings on for dear life.- <NEEEEEEEEEEIIIIIIGH! WHERE DID THAT WAVE COME FROM?!>

Gyarados: <I don't know! The sea's just become really rough all of a sudden!>

-A crack of thunder sounds, the Ponyta nearly falling off the Gyarados in shock.-

Ponyta: <A STORM! WE'RE IN A STORM! WHAT ARE WE GOING TO DO?!>

Gyarados: <Hang on!>

-The Gyarados dives under the water, swimming down a few meters to avoid the rough waves and rain on the surface. Although it's now much easier for him to swim, the lack of sunlight renders things incredibly dark, making it much harder to see where he's going.-

Gyarados: <Phew...That's better.>

Ponyta: -Shivering.- <For you, perhaps. But all this water, all around me...I can't handle it...>

-The Ponyta covers his head with his hoofs, his fiery mane growing dim from sadness. The Gyarados frowns, concerned for his friend.-

Gyarados: <Don't worry, it won't be for long, with any luck, this storm should clear up soon.>

Ponyta: -Sighs.- <At least it can't get any worse than this...>

<"RAAAAAAAAAAAAAAGGGGGGGGGHHHHHHHHHHHH">

Ponyta: <...Spoke too soon...What was that?!>

Gyarados: <I dunno', but it didn't sound friendly...>

-The sea serpent scans his surroundings, trying his best to make out anything in the murky surroundings. Soon, he spots a dark shape nearby.

A HUGE dark shape.-

Ponyta: -Pokes his out from behind the Gyarados' back.- <Wha...What is that?!>

Gyarados: <It's hard to tell...but it doesn't look like any other Pokemon I've seen before.>

Ponyta: <Do...Do you think it could have been the source of that roar just now?>

Gyarados: <Quite possibly.>

Ponyta: <Then I don't want to be anywhere near it then. Quick, swim for it!>

-The Gyarados nods, then swims away.

...Or at least, he would, if he wasn't being held in place by some strange force.-

Gyarados: <What?! I can't move!>

Ponyta: <Neigh! That's not the only problem! Look!>

-The Ponyta points a hoof to the large shape, which is now swimming straight towards them! Now that it was closer, the two were able to make out some features, in spite of their murky surroundings.-

Ponyta: <What is that thing?! It looks like...a bird!>

Gyarados: <Really? It looks more like a Dragon to me!>

Ponyta: <What?!> -Blinks.- <Argh, this isn't the time to argue! Do something! ANYYTHING!>

Gyarados: <Alright, hold on!>

-Taking a deep breath, the Gyarados fires a huge torrent of water out of his mouth and at the approaching creature, in the vague hope that it would break the strange hold the Gyarados was caught in.

But to both his and his passenger's utter horror, the attack barely phased it. In fact, this only served to make the creature angrier.-

<"RAAAAAAAAAAAAAAGGGGGGGGGHHHHHHHHHHHH">

-Enraged, the creature opens it's mouth wide, a ball of energy beginning to form in it's gaping jaws.-

Ponyta: <WHAT'S IT DOING?! IS THAT A HYPER BEAM?!>

Gyarados: <Whatever! I just don't want to be on the other end of it!>

Ponyta: <THEN SWIM!>

Gyarados: <I CAN'T!>

-To their utter horror, the shadowy Pokémon finishes charging the attack...Then fires it straight at them.-

Gyarados: -Gulps.-

Ponyta: <...It was nice knowing you buddy...>

-The Hyper Beam strikes the Gyarados with such force that it knocks him and his passanger flying straight up, out of the water and up into the air, sending the two flying over the horizon.-

Both: <WAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA-AAAAAAAAAAAAA!!!>

-The minor distraction delt with, the creature continues onward towards the Orange Islands...

...And his son.-
