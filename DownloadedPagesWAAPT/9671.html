Herbert: Perhaps it might help... but I get the feeling that dredging it all up at this point would only bring back more bad memories. Nevertheless, I'll tell you the abridged version.

-he sighs, and composes himself for a moment before beginning-

Herbert: You probably all know by now that I was raised by my father, a commander in Team Galactic. I never saw the significance of this, as a kid. All I knew was that Father was always away working, but every so often he'd come back home with a bunch of Pokemon. He'd never tell me where they came from, and he'd always say never to get attached to them, but I never questioned any of it. I was just happy for the company. He never mentioned what his job was - only that he was working for the "greater good". I always thought he ran a Pokemon sanctuary or something.

I didn't really have many friends as a kid. The one girl I actually got to know, Alice, was much smarter than I was. She figured out something was fishy about the whole affair, but I was far too pig-headed to listen to her. So on my ninth birthday, she decided to make me see sense. With the help of an old family friend, she snuck into the labs beneath the Eterna Galactic building, and managed to convince me to tag along.

You have to remember at this point that I was nine years old. I idolised my father and worshiped the very ground upon which he walked. For the longest time, he had brought home Pokemon for me to, a lonely and isolated child, to befriend. And then, when I saw what was happening down there... the things that Galactic did to some of those Pokemon... that had nothing to do with any "greater good". The experiments they were conducting... I saw Pokemon in pain, suffering, begging for an end to it all... everything I had believed about my father and his team was a lie. And then he... he...

-he holds his head in his hands-

Herbert: ...I'm sorry, that's all I can manage. Hopefully that explains why I don't trust scientists, and why I've been having these breakdowns over the last couple of days.
