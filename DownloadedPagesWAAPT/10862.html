Isolde's Cave

-despite her considerable tolerance to the cold and her warm jacket, the freezing atmosphere of the cave is beginning to get to Crewe. The ancient Froslass has spent this time regaling her with story after story, though often she hardly seems to notice her guest, going on as if to herself. This is fortunate, as Crewe has long stopped responding with more than an occasional grunt or nod-

Isolde: <... and so diligent! He spent all his time working, working so hard for the ghost lord... you know, sometimes I thought... I thought maybe we shouldn't be working for that man. But of course, I would never question Amhlaidh!>

-Crewe gives a curt nod, shivering. Isolde doesn't notice-

Isolde: <He had this silly habit, every day he'd go all the way around the citadel and make sure the guards were alert. If they weren't, I got to spook them! Sometimes Amhlaidh would even smack them with his sword, if they were bad enough. I don't know why he bothered, though. He could probably have just spent all the time standing right next to the ghost lord, and no one would be able to do anything!>

I-Isold-d-de?

-she seems to blink and remember there is someone else in the cave. She doesn't seem pleased to be interrupted-

Isolde: <What is it?>

N-not that th-this isn't int-teresting and all, b-but I-I n-need t-to g-go.

-she starts to slowly climb to her feet, muscles cramped and sore-

-Isolde's eyes widen in apparent panic-

Isolde: <No, dear, you can't leave! I have more stories to tell!>

Th-that's g-great, and I'd l-love t-to hear them, b-but m-my P-Pokemon are w-waiting, and—

Isolde: <You have Pokemon? What are they?>

A-A S-Sneasel and a-a P-Pidgeot.

Isolde: <A Sneasel... I never knew many Sneasel... Kori had already evolved by the time I met Amhlaidh...>

Th-that's g-great. I-I r-really n-need to g-go—

-she turns to leave the cave, and jumps as she finds Isolde floating right in front of her, a desperate look in her eye-

Isolde: <You can't go yet! I haven't spoken to anyone in so long! I haven't been able to speak about Amhlaidh! I need you to stay and listen! Please!>

-Crewe takes a half-step back, one hand reaching for her slingshot, but she takes a deep breath and drops her hand-

O-ok-kay. I-I c-can st-stay f-for a l-little while l-longer, b-but th-than I have to g-go.

-Isolde seems to relax and ushers Crewe farther back into the cave. Crewe goes along quietly, though she shivers and stumbles once or twice. She takes a seat against the wall again, huddling up as best as she can. Isolde goes back to seemingly ignoring her, though she now stays mostly between Crewe and the entrance to the cave-

Isolde: <Now, where was I... Oh! Yes, Amhlaidh and his insistence on keeping the guards on their toes. He was just so dutiful, always serving. But he always made sure that Kori and I were comfortable. We had to do our job too, of course, but he never worked us too hard, and we always had some time to ourselves... Of course, I was always at Amhlaidh's side, no matter the time.>

-Crewe hugs her knees tighter to her chest and starts to doze off as Isolde continues to talk-
