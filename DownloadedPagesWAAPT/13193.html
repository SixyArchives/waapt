-A Druddigon is resting when he is roused by the sound of scratches at a door-

<???: “Rrgh! For crying out loud, I thought we put a stepladder here!”>

<Karst: “... What the-?”>

-And the door swings open with an Archen latched onto the knob, who hops off and pushes the door closed behind him-

<Karst: “... What are you doing here?”>

<“... I heard you were almost ambulatory. I figured that since I’m heading off the island soon, there was something I needed to do with you.”>

-Starting to get apprehensive-

-... What do you want?-

<Karst: “... Okay… What would that be?”>

-And is interrupted by the avian form hopping onto the medward bed peering into the Druddigon’s eyes-

<“I want to have a talk…”>

-Oh great… Again?-

<Karst: “... Look, I already went through this with Tangent, we don’t see eye to eye, alr-”>

-The small bird gives what can perhaps be described as a frustrated growl-

<“Karst, this isn’t some dry difference in opinion! Those mons you left us for would have killed us if they had the chance!

How on earth did you ever conclude that that was a good idea-?!”>

-The bird’s tirade is cut off by the sound of the door opening-

<“Dammit.”>

-And the sound of figures entering the room-

Tagg: "... On my end, I don’t particularly appreciate my mercy getting shat upon by you joining up with those cultural genocidal bastards!"

-Did you just wake me up to put me something that you could have just asked your friend about? Leave me alone, seriously!-

-Growling-

<Karst: “Excuse me for putting stock in blood ties! Or are you insinuating that those don’t matter to you?”>

<Muddy: You don’t betray those who fed and healed you when next to no one would for them. When was the last time you even saw your clan?>

<Karst: “Half past an eternity ago, because you never ‘‘let’’ me see them!”>

-Looking away-

<“... You never asked.”>

-Huh?!-

-In an aggravated tone-

<Karst: “Say what?”>

<“I’m not trying to be cruel… But you legitimately never told us about this. You didn’t tell us that they still were important to you or that you were homesick-”>

-I- I- But I shouldn’t have to tell you that!-

-And what are the odds you would have listened anyways?-

<Karst: “I haven’t seen home in years! Do you honestly need to be a genius to figure that out?!”>

Tagg: "Some species don’t put much stock in family ties, others simply don’t want to see their old homes again. Considering your past, it’s possible you wouldn’t want to see them again out of pure shame for your past actions."

-... Get. Out.-

-The Druddigon curls away on the medward bed-

<Karst: “Thanks for assuming.”>

<“... With all due respect… But you’re not exactly making things any less murky for us.”>

<Karst: “...”>

<“Karst, if you had told us that you were uncomfortable out there, we would have tried to arrange something! Don’t you think that Draca felt uncomfortable out there? Or Garchomp? If it bugged you that much, why didn’t you bring it up with them?”>

<Karst: “Let me sleep.”>

<“Why did you really join those mons, Karst? I mean, picking up sticks and abruptly changing camp? Family loyalty alone doesn’t do that! What was your cause behi-?”>

<Karst: “I said let me sleep!”>

<“...”>

-A certain Salamence decides to speak up-

<Draca: Dovah are supposed to be loyal to their comrades, Karst. Your clan asking you to assist in subjugating everyone else, including your friends, doesn’t exactly count as that.>

<Karst: *growl* “What kind of teacher did you have that encouraged you to side with wandering friends over the ones who would have taught you how to even say 'Dovah' to begin with?”>

-Glares-

<Draca: When I was young I used to think like that, but there’s the family you’re born with, and then there’s the family you choose. Did you not think some of my siblings or even my parents have likely joined in our clan head’s mad crusade against other species and that I may have to fight them. I made my choice to stick with my friends no matter what, have you?>

-Leering back-

<Karst: “I’ll believe it when I see it.

Seriously, why are you all even up at this hour?!”>

-Sighing-

<“... If you must know, it’s because we’re headed home. And the trip length is such that our best odds of getting in unnoticed is if we leave before the sun rises.”>

-And hops off the bed-

<“... Originally, I was hoping that this would be a smaller meeting… But… I must say that Dr. Tagg and the others perhaps poked at a few matters that I would not have thought of.

I was going to say that I would try to find a way to break things softly to everyone else… Since we are friends… Or at least, were. But… Given that it seems word got out anyways… It might not be a promise that I can reasonably keep anymore.”>

-And reaches the doorway-

<“I know that I can’t control you… For your own sake… Try to be honest with yourself about what you’re really after here.

After all, there is a chance that you might not have an opportunity to be honest with me in the future.

… The others will find something useful to put you up to when you’ve recovered.”>

<Karst: “What’s that supposed to-?”>

<“You said that you’re fighting for your clan, yes? … Unfortunately, since you so insist, we will have to treat you accordingly.

Anyhow, you raised a decent point earlier… Get some rest. You are still rather weak right now.”>

-And departs, shaking head-

Tagg: "I suppose we should be making preparations for our return to Kanto then."

-And also departs-

-The room quickly empties, leaving behind a Druddigon, and perhaps a few sundry mons roused from their sleep who attempt to nod back off, grumbling about the disruption-

-... Is… This… Really what I wanted?-

-... I mean… I know that I wanted to have a purpose beyond looking out for my own hide all the time, but…-

-And shakes head-

-It must be worth it… I mean, fighting for one’s own is a purpose…-

-... Right?-

-But, alas, there is no response to the question… Which prompts the Druddigon to drift off to uneasy sleep, if with a gnawing feeling of uncomfortable loneliness and isolation that might be unwelcomely familiar-
