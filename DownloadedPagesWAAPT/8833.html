Somewhere on the Airship

Eskay finds Skye

Eskay: Hey, Skye.

Skye: <Hey, what's up?>

Eskay: I was just wondering if... you'd help me out with something.

Skye: <Oh? With what?>

Eskay: Well... I was going to try and figure out what the whole gold-voice thing really did...

Skye: <Oh...>

Eskay: Yeah... I wanted to find out so I can really know when to use it... I want to keep my promise to Psyche.

Skye: <I see... Well, I'm not exactly the most familiar with it... Spectrum might be a better mon to ask...>

Eskay: I know she's more familiar with it... That's exactly why I didn't go to her. She's had it her entire life; I'm not sure if she'd quite recognize the difference as easily...

Skye: <...Good point... But still, I think we can start with her. If not by directly asking her.>

Eskay: That's a good idea, actually... Let's see... Her voice... it seems to change color depending on what she's saying...

Skye: <...Are you sure it's that? It seems more like how she's saying it rather than the words she says.>

Eskay: ...That actually sounds a bit more accurate... Like it's linked to her emotions...

Skye: <...Maybe that's what your gold voice is for?>

Eskay: Perhaps...

He thinks about when he first used the voice. When he tricked a man into giving him a watch.

Eskay: ...Maybe that was it...

Skye notices the gold voice. She also notices Eskay was feeling really guilty about... something.

Skye: <There's nothing to be guilty about... You're trying to keep a promise, not find ways around it...>

Eskay: No, that's not why I'm- Wait, you noticed my guilt... Hmm... Skye, close your eyes for a second. Don't look at my face. Focus only on my voice.

Skye: <...I take it you have some idea? All right...>

She closes her eyes. Eskay starts thinking about his mom; how it's been so tough managing both his own life and Alli's for the past five years. Keeping those thoughts in his mind, he straightens up, and removes all inflection from his voice, giving no audible hint as to what he was thinking.

Eskay: ...The quick brown Eevee jumps over the lazy Growlithe.

Skye probably would have laughed at this incredibly random sentence... if not for the inexplicable sadness she suddenly started feeling.

Skye: <...Oh, wow...>

She opens her eyes.

Eskay: ...What happened when I said that?

Skye: <...Somehow, that ended up sounding incredibly... depressive...>

Eskay: I figured that was the case... I think I know what it does now.

Skye: <Oh?>

She then figures it out, just before Eskay says it.

Eskay: ...I think it's pretty much pure emotion.

Skye: <Given off as an audible color...>

Eskay: Yeah...

He pulls Skye into a hug.

Eskay: Thank you, Skye...

Skye hugs back, knowing he truly meant it.
