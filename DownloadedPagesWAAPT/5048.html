Fortree City: Pokemon Center

With a yawn, Rex awoke, rubbing his eyes and sitting up in the bed. It had been rather fortunate that he was able to get a room at the Pokemon Center after the grievous misunderstanding about him starting town fires. However, news soon spread that it was the machinations of a rogue Skitty and thus his name was cleared within the hour. Of course, people still eyed him warily due to him being a member of the J-Team. That was rather...disheartening.

Rex: I suppose the least I can do is try to make a good impression wherever I go from now on.

He shook his head, wondering how a group so widely feared ended up with a fanclub. Then, he looked around the room, observing his Pokemon to see how they'd spent the night. Pierce was curled up with Rascal on a floor mat, the two finally having had a chance to bond more without Blossom, Ichtacya, Aqua, or Maekrite around to divert the young Eevee's attention.

To their right were Grey and Sulfur, both asleep on a large pillow. Surprisingly, Sulfur had ended up having a rather interesting talent: he could apparently cook! As a result, their team was able to enjoy a homemade dinner, not made by him, , something his Pokemon were very grateful for...it's not like his cooking skills were that bad anyway... Of course, the meal had been slightly ruined once Sulfur had started to digest his own food, releasing several noxious gases... Oh well. They'd work on that next time. As for the tiny dinosaur, Rex made a note to monitor how much he'd grown. If he was old enough, it might be time to get a Pokeball for him.

Fang and Breezy were missing at the moment, though Rex was unworried. Knowing them, they'd probably gone to the center kitchen as a result of his mother instilling scheduled food hours into their minds. That wasn't to say that Rex didn't feed them frequently himself, though sometimes he would eat later were something capturing his attention. In any case, a Pokemon Center would be less panicky about a Raticate in a kitchen than a hotel, so there was no cause for alarm there. And surely Breezy would manage to be the voice of reason should any problems arise.

His final Pokemon, Spathi, was also awake. The dragon Pokemon stared out the window, looking out at the lush forests of the city. He noticed an Altaria fly past, pawing at the window suddenly. Then he frowned as if noticing something and went back to looking. Rex moved closer, catching the Salamence's attention, and he turned to his trainer with an inquisitive look.

Spathi: <Hey, where's Salamence?>

Rex: Good morning to you too. And Salamence? I'm not sure. ...Why? You got a crush?

Spathi: <...Do you want me to bite you?>

Rex: ...E-erm...I'm sure you have your reasons for looking for her.

Grumbling to himself, the Salamence turned back to the window, continuing to look around for the Altaria. Shrugging, and deciding to avoid any unfortunate property damage that might occur later, Rex wandered over and lifted the window open, the warm breeze drifting into the room.

Rex: If you want to go look for her, feel free.

The Salamence immediately jumped into the space, taking off to search for the object of his affections interest. With a sigh, Rex turned away from the window, preparing to shower and get ready for his day. However, a noise alerted him that something was at the window again, the boy turning to see his Salamence had returned.

Spathi: <...err...thanks. ...and good morning.>

Then he flew off again, Rex smiling a little and turning back to his task. He grabbed for a pair of fresh clothes, a face towel, and a bar of soap, stepping into the small personal bathroom. After several moments, the sound of shower water flowing filled the room, a few of his Pokemon stirring from the noise. As a result, they were able to hear the PA Announcement that occurred shortly afterwards.

P.A. System: Attention, trainers. The Global Trade Station building in Lilycove has now been completed. Trainers seeking trades may now communicate wirelessly, transferring their Pokemon to people all across the world! Several smaller terminals will soon be added to other Hoenn Centers in the future. Thank you.

Be back later, guys.
