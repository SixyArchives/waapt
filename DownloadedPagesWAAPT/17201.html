Factory Floor  1:45 until Cannon Fire

The elevator came to a halt, Luke and Unite stepping off as they gazed up at their new surroundings.

Luke: Wasn't expecting this...

They now found themselves on what appeared to be a vast factory floor, dominated by a series of large machines clanking, groaning and whirling away as they completed their tasks autonomously.

Unite: <What the heck is he making down here?>

Luke shrugged, just as clueless.

Luke: Weapons?

Unite shook his head.

Unite: <Pokémon don't need weapons!>

Luke: ...Farfetch'd, Cubone and Marowak.

Unite: <Most Pokémon don't need weapons! It's gotta' be something else. Look here...>

He lead his trainer over to a series of large storage tanks. They were made of transparent glass so they could see their contents  various coloured liquids.

<What could this stuff be?>

Luke: Uh, toxic chemicals? Mutagen? Liquid explosives? There's got to be some clue around here somewhere.

Unite nodded, then pointed a wing at the production lines.

Unite: <Let's follow the machinery. Whatever they're making down here, they're churning them out en-mass at the other end, I bet.>

1:35 Until Cannon Fire

Further investigation revealed that there were actually two production lines at work there.

The first was the more complex, as various machines worked delicately to produce what appeared to be small computers contained within metal sphere about the size of a football.

Unite: <But footballs aren't round!>

Luke: Fine, Soccer balls.

Unite: <Aaaah.>

The other assembly line was more unorthodox. Some sort of strange powder was being mixed with a chemical of some sort. It then merged with the finished first production line, where he liquid it was produced was poured onto the soccer ball computers.

After a brief trip into a heat chamber, the finished product was revealed  a computer encased in a sphere of some sort of coloured gel.

It was all very confusing.

Luke: They're making computers...in Jelly?

Unite: <Jello.>

Luke waved a hand dismissively.

Luke: Whatever.

The two made their way over to the very end of the line, where the Jelly Spheres were being packed away into large metal crates. A few finished specimens were also just lying around, of which Luke and Unite took a closer look at.

Unite: <Alright, we just need to think of a reason why someone would be making computers stuck in gel.>

Luke: Oh, I've got it. The gel is explosive and the computer inside is actually the triggering mechanism!

Unite: <Plastic explosive, then?>

The bird gazed back at the machinery, seemingly unconvinced.

<Why produce it en-mass, though? In fact, why devote precious space off your airship to a factory to even make it when you could just buy in bulk from an arms dealer or something?>

Luke: He doesn't want to get a third party involved? Doesn't explain why he would need so much though.

Luke shook his head, dismissing the idea.

No, it's got to be something else...
