Charicific Valley - Johto

The thing about Charizard is that they love nothing better than to challenge strong opponents to battle. Especially others of their kind. So in the Charicific Valley - where a large population of Charmander line members made their home - a fight breaking out between two Charizard was nothing unusual.

Today, however, was different.

One of the Charizard had decided to challenge a particularly smarmy member of his line to a battle. Nothing unusual about that, you say. But it wasn't another Charizard that he had challenged. She was a Charmeleon.

And she was, at that point, kicking his tail rather thoroughly.

He was really beginning to regret insulting her grass lizard friend now.

With a loud roar, the Charizard in question unleashed a ball of draconic energy from his gaping more, aiming at his currently ground-bound target. But she was having none of that.

Opening her own jaws wide, the Charmeleon exhaled a plume of intense flame at the sphere, the Dragon Pulse exploding on contact with the flames, obscuring the battlefield with smoke.

<"OOOOH!">

<"OH, COME ON?! Why does every attack cause an explosion?! I can't see a thing with all this smoke!">

<"Keep quiet! We're trying to watch!">

Naturally, a battle between a Charmeleon and a Charizard had attracted quite a crowd. While most of the spectators were Charizard, a few of the braver wild Pokémon from nearby had cautiously gathered nearby to watch, not wanting to venture too close least one of the Charizards decided to flash cook them for lunch.

Luckily for them, the spectators were far too engorssed in watching a Charmeleon utterly dominate a Charizard on the battlefield, despite the latter being fully evolved and possessing the ability to fly.

Wild Charizard — <Darn it, whelp! Have you ne respect for your elders?!>

Taking a different approach, the Charizard dived at the Charmeleon, attempting to strike her with his outstretched wings. He was surprised however, that even though she could easily see his attack coming, his opponent simply held her ground, as he closed the distance towards her.

Something that came as a shock to the onlookers.

Wild Charizard #1 — <WHAT ARE YOU DOING?! MOVE!>

Wild Charizard #2 — <Is she trying to get herself killed?>

Wild Charizard #3 — <Wow...is she always like this in battle?>

The Wild Charizard glanced at the Pokémon sitting next to him, who also happened to be the only member of the crowd who wasn't a Charizard. In fact, he was pretty much one of the last kinds of Pokémon that you would expect to see in a valley populated by powerful fire type Pokémon.

Hearing himself being addressed, the wood-gecko Pokémon glanced at the Charizard sitting next to him, briefly uncovering his eyes, having obscured them with his hands due to not being able to watch what was about to happen next out of fright.

Treeo the Sceptile♂ — Sighs. <All the time...>

Back on the field of battle, the Charizard was just about to knock his opponent with his wings, when at the last possible second, the Charmeleon lept into the air...then came back down again, now on the Charizard's back.

Wild Charizard — <What the-?!>

Shocked at such a gutsy move the Charizard surged into a steep climb in an attempt to dislodge his unexpected passenger. Taken by surprise, the Charmeleon fell off, but managed to grab hold of her foe's tail, the gathered crowd gasping in shock at the near miss.

Wild Charizard #1 — <HOLY MUK! THAT WAS AWESOME!>

Wild Charizard #2 — <Awesome? More like insane...>

Wild Charziard #3 — <Y'know, constant stress reduces your lifespan...>

Treeo — <Not helping.>

Before she could even let out a sigh of relief, the Charmeleon suddenly found her world spinning. Literally. Her opponent had leveled out and was now trying to dislodge her by preforming a series of barrel rolls. Since a fall from this height would be disasterous, she gug her claws into the Charizard's thick scales and clung on for dear life, desperately trying to keep her grip, as well as her lunch from earlier, held down.

Wild Charizard — <You stubborn little whelp! Let go already!>

The Charmeleon sneered back, fed up with being refereed to as a whelp.

Comet the Charmeleon♀ — <For your information Urk, I'm most likely the oldest of anymon here!>

She wasn't quite sure on that, as the circumstances of her birth had been...well, different from most other Pokémon.

Wild Charizard — <Well you can't hold on Hurk forever!>

The Charmeleon grinned, eyeing her opponets face as it began to turn a sickly shade of green.

Comet — <Neither ca- URP! Oh, Cresselia...>

As she swallowed to keep the contents of her stomach from going everywhere, Comet felt herself slow as the world stopped spinning. Or rather, her opponent stopped barrel rolling, the Charizard hovering weakly in mid-air as he struggled to stay aloft, his face now a putrid green color.

Wild Charizard #2 — <Is he alright?>

Wild Charizard #4 — <He's almost as green as the Sceptile!>

Treeo — <Hey!>

The Charizard covered his mouth, trying in vain to keep the the Tauros he had eaten for breakfast from coming out the wrong end.

But it was already too late.

Wild Charizard — <BLURGH!!!>

The Wild Charizard used Gastro Acid!

To make matters even worse, most of the half-digested remains had been accidentally sprayed at the spectators, causing a panic as most of the gathered Charizard attempt to avoided being showered with vomit. Some simply ran for it, others flew, one particularity well trained even threw up a Protect Shield.

Wild Charizard #1 — <Oh, YUCK!>

Wild Charizard #2 — <NO! Now I'll have to have another bath this month!>

Wild Charizard #3 — <Ew...are you alright?>

Treeo nodded in response, brushing some of the vomit off of himself. Thankfully it was nothing a quick dip in some water wouldn't be able to fix.

Treeo — <It's okay. I've seen much worse than that, trust me.>

The Sceptile was thankful that Comet's opponent wasn't as big an eater as a certain Dragonite he was friends with.

The act of being sick, as well as the injuries he has sustained in battle having taken his tole, the Charizard plopped weakly to the ground, groaning as he clutched his still turbulent stomach. Letting go off his tail, Comet pulled herself up, only to stumble, still dizzy from all the spinning.

Comet — <I really should have thought things through some more. Urk...>

She then slowly made her way over to the front of her downed opponent, who was too busy focusing on keeping last night's dinner down to pay much attention.

<Urk...give up?>

The Charizard nodded vigorously in response.

Wild Charizard — <I give, I give! I won't insult your friend again! Now please! Somemon bet me an Oran Berry...>

Wild Charizard #1 — <You heard him, peoplemons! Comet is the winner!>

<"YAY!">

<"WHOO!">

<"Okay...who's taking a bath first?">

<"You should, you smell.">

<"What? No way! You got hit worse!">

<"If anyone's taking a bath first, it's you!">

<"Not without a fight I'm not!">

<"Oh, is that a challenge?">

Wanting to avoid another scuffle, Treeo quickly makes his way over to Comet, giving the Charmeleon a concerned look.

Treeo — <...You didn't have to do that for me...>

The Charmeleon sighs in response, crossing her arms.

Comet — <Well, I would have let you do it, but I didn't want to see you getting burnt to a crisp just for sticking up for yourself.>

Treeo — <But I don't want you to get yourself beaten up just to stick up for me! It's not worth the hassle.>

Comet frowns at this, displeased at Treeo's line of thinking.

Comet — <Hey, you're always worth the hassle. How many times do I have to tell you that?>

Treeo says nothing, instead choosing to look down and stare at his feet, looking awkward.

Treeo — <...Sorry, I didn't mean to offend you...>

The Charmeleon frowns, slightly saddened at the fact that she had made her friend uncomfortable. Trying to cheer him up, she reaches out and pats his scaly hide gently with her claw.

Comet — <It's okay. It's just...I like sticking up for you. And not just because I get to beat jerks up.>

She casts a glance over to the Charizard, who is getting assistance from another Charizard in the form of some Oran Berries. Unfortunately, he's having a hard time keeping them down.

Treeo — <Hey, I like sticking up for you, too. Not that you need it most of the time, but if you were ever in a situation like that, I'd gladly fight for you. And...well, you would probably know how I feel...>

Comet — <...Yeah, I guess I don't know what that's like.>

Comet lowers her head.

<Guess I should be the one saying sorry, then.>

Treeo, however, simply lowers his head and gives the Charmeleon a friendly nuzzle.

Treeo — <It's fine. And...I like that you think I'm worth fighting for.>

The Sceptile smiles awkwardly, and would most likely be blushing heavily...if geckos could blush.

<Urm...I really should go and wash. I smell really bad right now.>

Comet — <You've got that right. Pee-yow, you stink!>

With a smirk, Comet quickly clasps her nostrils shut with her claw, Treeo giving her an unamused look in return.

Treeo — <Not funny...>
