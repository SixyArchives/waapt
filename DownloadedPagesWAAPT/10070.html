Last time on WAAPT...

About twenty minutes of wandering later, and Comet hadn't seen anything apart from the odd rock or two here and there. It was as if she was the only Pokémon alive for miles, and it was creeping her out.

Just a little bit.

Comet: <Jeez, just how long am I supposed to look until I find my fear?>

Pausing for a moment, the Charmeleon put her arms on her hips, huffing in annoyance.

<Trial of Courage? Trial of Patience would have been more fitting.>

Indeed, it seemed more as if her patience was being tested rather than her bravery. Still, there wasn't anything that could be done about it, so she decided to keep looking.

But as she started to continue onward, she stopped as she caught sight of what looked like some shadowy force just on the edge of her vision. Quickly, the Charmeleon whirled around to try and spot it...but there was nothing there.

<What was that?>

Slightly on edge, Comet scanned her surroundings for any sign of life, but found nothing. Odd. The barren surroundings offered little in the way of hiding places, so by all accounts, she should have found something by now.

Perhaps she had imagined it? Was her mind starting to play tricks on her?

Suddenly she caught a glimpse of the shadow in the corner of her eye. Swinging around once more, Comet was once again met with nothing.

<Darn it, where are you?!>

Now a little more nervous, she spun around, trying to find her mysterious stalker, when...

???: <Looking for me?>

She whirled around, only to come face to face with a Charizard.

But it was no ordinary Charizard, as it was completely black - as if it were made out of the shadows themselves - save for a pair of red, glowing eyes.

Comet paused at the sight of the shadowy Pokémon, somewhat surprised.

Comet: <...I expected worse.>

Shadow Wastes - Five Years Ago...

Comet: <I suppose you're my fear, then?>

A positively evil chuckle escaped from the Shadow Charizard's jaws.

???: <You could say that. But I don't represent your fear, Kindle...>

Bearing it's teeth, the shadow smirked.

<I'm your own doubts, given form.>

Comet: <My...doubts?>

Comet was confused by this revelation. Where was this coming from? She wasn't doubting herself at all right now. Well, not by much. She was sort of hoping that she wouldn't have to fight this Charizard, but that would most likely be wishful thinking.

Shadow!Comet: <Yes, I am the doubt the stirs within you. The doubt that you may not complete this challenge, that you may never become a Dream Guard...>

The shadow leaned forward, bearing down on Comet.

<And the doubt that you won't make your parents proud.>

Unconvinced, Comet waved her claw in a gesture of dismissal.

Comet: <Pfft. I don't know what you're talking about. If I doubted myself that much, I wouldn't have made it this far, would I?>

Shadow!Comet: <But how can you be sure this was down to your own skill, and not simply lucky flukes?>

This took Comet off guard. But she quickly reassured herself that it was skill and not luck that had carried her this far. Lutalo had said so himself that she was one of the best Dreamweavers he had ever seen!

Comet: <You're just trying to psyche me out, aren't you? Well I'm not falling for it!>

Shadow!Comet: <But it's not me trying to psyche you out. It's you. I am the doubt inside of you. Even if you deny my existence, I am there. Gnawing away at the back of your mind endlessly. Deny me all you want. But sooner or later, you're going to crack.>

Annoyed, Comet growled. She had a trial to complete, and she didn't want to waste time just hanging around and yapping about doubt or whatever nonsense this Charizard was going on about.

Comet: <Just shut up and fight me so that I can finish this thing already!>

Shadow!Comet: <...Suit yourself.>
