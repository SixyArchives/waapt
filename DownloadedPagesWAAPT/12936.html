Li'l Punk

Anville Town

-Crewe leaves the woods and looks around-

Maul? I'm ready to go. Didn't die, even.

A clearing in the woods

-Hecate returns to the clearing with a solemn look on her face. As she leaves the trees, a pair of twin identical Zorua break away from a group and scamper over to her. She smiles and stoops to pet their ears-

Hecate: <Hello, my dears.>

Vonda: <Hi Mama!>

Jetta: <Where'd you go, Mama?>

Hecate: <I had to take care of something very important. Now, can you tell me where you big brother is?>

Vonda: <Oh! Oh!>

Jetta: <Swift is with Starlight.>

Vonda: <Jetta! I wanted to tell her!>

Jetta: <Yeah, well, I told her first!>

Hecate: <Now, now. Could you go fetch him?>

Vonda: <I'll get him first!>

Jetta: <No, I'll get him first!>

-the two go running off, squabbling. Hecate watches them go, smiling faintly. Her smile fades as a familiar scent comes up behind her-

Rosaleen: <So, meeting humans in the middle of our territory, Hecate? That doesn't seem like something the tribe would approve of if they knew>

-Hecate whirls around, grabbing the other Zoroark by the throat and pinning her to a nearby tree-

Hecate: <If you intend to use this in some manner of pitiful ploy to gain power against me, Rosaleen, I highly suggest you reconsider.>

-Rosaleen sneers, grabbing at Hecate's claws-

Rosaleen: -gasping for breath- <I thought you had outgrown humans>

Hecate: <I assure you, I have no intention of returning to humanity.>

Rosaleen: <Why not kill the girl?>

Hecate: < That is none of your concern, Rosaleen.>

Rosaleen: <I think concern of the tribe if chief is meeting humans>

-Hecate growls and presses her up harder against the tree. Rosaleen yelps-

Hecate: <The girl will never return here. She has proven herself before to be of no danger to the tribe, and has in fact shown herself to be a an ally to us. Nevertheless, you may rest assured she will never return here.>

-Rosaleen is no longer able to speak, pawing desperately at Hecate's hold. Hecate drops her-

Rosaleen: <No human ally>

Hecate: <You have a closed mind, Rosaleen. You never could accept anything you weren't told by your parents. Or my brother, I suppose.>

Rosaleen: <See what tribe thinks>

-Hecate snorts and backhands her-

Hecate: <Oh, I doubt it. You know, my dear, you aren't thought of very highly among our tribes mates. Should you attempt to lower their opinion of me through slander, I don't really think they'll believe you, or look kindly on you at all. And should they find that you are, perhaps, mistaken Well. Tell me, what do you think would happen, my dear?>

Rosaleen: <>

Hecate: <Hm? I'm afraid I didn't hear you, dear.>

Rosaleen: <Don't talk down to me.>

Hecate: <I will talk any way I like to you, Rosaleen. You are not the chief any longer. Your attempts to usurp my position are occasionally amusing, so I tolerate them, but remember that I am in charge here, and you have no more worth than the dirt beneath my feet unless I say you do.>

Rosaleen: <>

Hecate: <Now run along, dear. I have some work to be done, and you have ceased to be amusing.>

-Rosaleen scampers off. Hecate turns to see Swift looking at her-

Swift: < Problems, Mother?>

Hecate: <Rosaleen being Rosaleen, dear, nothing to worry about.>

Swift: <I worry you're too hard on her, Mother. She was your brother's best friend.>

Hecate: <That is hardly a reason to be kinder to her, Swift, and you know it. Regardless, she is a conniving little weasel intent on stealing my position from me once again.>

Swift: < If you say so, Mother. What did you want me for?>

Hecate: <You can return to your Starlight soon, don't worry, dear. Will you walk with me a moment?>

Swift: -blushing under his fur- <We—we weren't—>

Hecate: -smiling- <Oh, hush, dear. I have nothing against the girl. She's very pretty, you know.>

Swift: <Mother!>

Hecate: <Though I hear she's excellent with Illusion so good, you can never tell she's using it, and they say she can make herself look like anything>

Swift: <You said you wanted to walk, Mother?!>

Hecate: <Ah, yes. Come, dear.>

-she starts off through the woods. Swift follows alongside her-

Hecate: <Do you remember, some years ago, when I came home with a human girl?>

Swift: <Yes>

Hecate: <Do you remember who she was?>

Swift: <She was your Trainer, right? And then she released you and you came back and challenged Rosaleen for command of the tribe again, and won.>

Hecate: <Yes, she was my Trainer.>

Swift: <She betrayed you.>

Hecate: < Yes, so I thought.>

Swift: < Mother?>

Hecate: <That girl came to speak with me earlier.>

-Swift stops short-

Swift: <Wh-what?!>

Hecate: <Keep walking, dear.>

-he hurries to catch up with her again-

Swift: <She came after you? Did she try to catch you? Did you fight her off?>

Hecate: <Don't be silly. She knows better than to try and catch me again. I daresay I left quite the impression on the girl. I'll be surprised if she touches another Zoroark again.>

Swift: <Then what did she want?>

Hecate: < To apologize.>

Swift: <Did you>

Hecate: <Accept it? Oh, no. Apologizing was the cruelest thing she's ever done to me.>

-Swift looks confused-

Hecate: <My dear she has challenged me. She has challenged one of my fiercest beliefs, and she knew that and still came looking for me.>

Swift: <You don't seem very upset, Mother.>

Hecate: <Because, Swift, I can handle challenges. Crewe is no more of a threat than Rosaleen is. Does Rosaleen upset me?>

Swift: <She certainly seems to make you angry.>

Hecate: <Oh, she is an irritation, to be sure, but she has been dealt with and continues to be dealt with. Crewe's challenge may be slightly different, but the principle is the same. I will not allow this little girl to cow me with her words any more than I will allow Rosaleen to usurp my position with her whatever fool plot she is currently working on.>

Swift: < As long as you won't let it get to you too badly, Mother>

-Hecate smiles and kisses his forehead-

Hecate: <I promise. Now run along and get back to your Starlight. She is a lovely girl, you know.>

-Swift hugs her-

Swift: <I know, Mother.>

Hecate: <Oh, and Swift? There will be a small white bag under the turntable in the town outside the woods. Take some fighters with her and go retrieve it. The items inside will be passed around to hunting parties as they go out. They are to prevent Trainers from kidnapping you.>

-Swift blinks in surprise, but nods-

Swift: <Yes, Mother.>

-she smiles-

Hecate: <Good. Now, go on.>

-he nods and darts back to camp-

-Hecate watches him go, then follows him back more slowly, a small smile on her face-
