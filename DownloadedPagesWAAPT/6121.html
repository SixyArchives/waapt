PEFE HQ - AU

-A certain Flygon with a bad wing is currently undergoing physiotherapy to attempt to fly again-

WHUMP!

<AU!Speck: "Oog...">

-Which isn't making much progress-

Medical Ward!Intern: "You okay there, big guy? That landing looked a bit hard..."

<Gligar!Vlad: "You made it further that time, Speck!">

<AU!Speck: "M-Maybe I should just give up... I probably don't deserve to fly again anyways...">

-Oh, he's doing this again... Can't say that I really blame him, it's painful to have something that you used to be able to take for granted snatched away from you. All in all, I guess I got lucky... I didn't get anything major messed up, didn't get a bone ripped out of me by an upset midget lizard, and was able to move around a bit about a week after I got pulled in here... If with a bit of difficulty-

-Watching from aside-

<Buckley: "Hmm... What did you think of his choice?">

<Manasa: "He's a grown mon, I'm sure that he understood what was going on when he made his decision...">

-Hard to believe that someone actually wanted him... Or any of us, for that matter... One of the things that I learned quickly when I was pressed into that Arceus-awful army was that we were utter scum. Even the guys who were supposedly on our side treated us that way... It was kinda telling when we were the only ones that didn't ever get a victory parade in Castellia... Even when "that pansy gambler" and "that profligate lizard" and their troops got them. Apparently Scolipede and Liepard's wiseguys in the propaganda department came to the conclusion that the damage that parading us around in public and celebrating getting some place plowed into the ground would have caused outweighed any intimidation value that we provided. Never mind that we were the ones who were expected to get the job done if no one else could... Or for that matter, that it was common knowledge amongst other grunts that the only time that getting reassigned to Lanius' army was ideal was when your other option was being executed-

<Gligar!Vlad: "But you're making good progress, Speck! Won't you try again?">

<AU!Speck: "... A-Alright... But I'm not sure if anything will come out of it...">

-One of the things that I was taught by my mother was that there's apparently a 'Hunter's Code'... That you can kill and steal to fill your stomach, but only if you take just what you need... And you don't revel in it... One of the things that I was taught by other mons under Scolipede and Liepard's service is that you can apparently tell a bit about the heart of the mon that lead you to join by what they have you do...

... In that case, I'm fairly certain that the mons that made me join didn't have hearts-

-A certain Flygon attempts to fly again... And makes it a little further-

WHAM!

-And crashes again-

<AU!Speck: "Ow...">

-"Violence sets mons free in ways you cannot understand..." Maybe, but they're all in ways that you'd never want to understand... It sets you free from fear of not being able to see the next sunrise, from fear of having to decide amongst you and a group of others that you barely know who's going to be thrown under the bus to make up for not doing well enough to please your superiors... And... It enslaves you in ways too... It makes you feel worthless about yourself, getting up each day and looking and seeing your face in a puddle, and instead of being able to hold your crest up high and say "that's a hunter", "that's a strong, majestic mon", you find yourself saying "that's a butcher", "a slaver", "a mon whose mother would have smashed her egg if she ever knew it would have turned into that"-

Medical Ward!Intern: "... That's probably enough for today... Here, let me help you up..."

-Being helped to stand up, bit dazed, chittering-

<AU!Speck: "O-Okay...">

-Worst of all, it provides an easy out... It undermines your hope and your resolve to stand up for what's right... Or at least, it did for me... There was always some group that mutinied during every campaign, usually filled with fresh guys who didn't want to be used as cannon fodder... They usually didn't last long... If they were lucky, nine out of every ten of them would be able to learn to never repeat that mistake again. The one time the mutineers won... During that siege of that tower, after Lanius was incapacitated, they were hung out to dry by the two mons that they swore loyalty to during the whole thing... When all they wanted and asked for was to be able to serve them while being lead by someone who would allow them to be able to hold their heads up high. Supposedly, the week Lanius returned to lead his army, one out of every 20 in the service didn't live past that week... And then there's the stories of what happened to those who surrendered or defected... If some vindictive human or mon who had lost everything thanks to your comrades didn't open up your neck with a blade, there was a good chance that an infiltrator from your former side would come and do the job for him... Because you had let yourself get taken alive. Never mind the fact that a good chunk of our enemies had a technique developed over centuries to do just that. It was something that kept me up for the first few nights I was here... With odds like that, suddenly it becomes a lot easier to not worry about what happens to the people and the mons you're fighting-

-A certain Druddigon is watching Speck and Gligar!Vlad leave-

<Karst: "(*sigh*)">

-And is approached by another-

<Nathan: "... Something wrong? You seem rather down...">

<Karst: "... Where on earth did you come from?">

<Nathan: "Well, hey, I like to explore... So I've gotten a pretty good feel for this place... So what's eating you?">

<Karst: "... Nothing, I just have a bit on my mind...">

-I guess that's part of the reason why I don't really get these people and mons here... They're nice and look after us well, yeah, but why? Why do they see anything in us that they find worthy?

... And whatever it is that they see, why can't I see it?-

Night, and don't count on these things being regular occurrences.
