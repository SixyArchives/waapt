PEFE HQ - Past

Luke: "ENTIRE ENEMY TEAM IS BABIES!"

A certain trainer had spent much of his time playing a classed based multiplayer shooter on his PC in his module at the PEFE HQ. Despite all the hours sunk into the game, he was far from proficient, as demonstrated when he was literally blown to bits a few seconds later by another player.

"OOH, THIS IS BAD!"

But before he could retaliate, he heard a knock at his front door. Grumbling at having to disconnect (You can't pause online games, and going AFK would likely result in several deaths), he got up and went to answer the door. Upon opening it to an initially empty hallway, he looked around in confusion, until he spotted a small box lying at the foot of the door.

"...?"

Curiously, he picked it up, only to notice that it had a tag attached to it. Perhaps it was a late Christmas present?

Wondering who it was from, Luke read the tag...and his heart skipped a beat.

"To Luke, merry Christmas."'
-Skye.

Just as it seemed he was getting over his failed potential relationship with the Skarmory, something came along to remind Luke of how painfully awkward it had all been for him. His attempts to court her as a Feraligatr, that desperate deceleration of love he had sent to her just as she had decided that she was interested in...other things...

Part of him wanted to curl up in a ball on the carpet and sob his eyes out for the rest of the night, but it was overpowered by the fact that he had a Christmas present to open.

Forgetting about his relationship issues for a second, he eagerly opened the package...and pulled out what appeared to be a stylish yellow scarf.

Wait...a stylish yellow scarf just like the one Lake wore, which in turn, was technically the same as the one Skye wore.

He was honestly shocked at how...thoughtful this was. In fact, it was one of the most thoughtful gifts he had received that Christmas. It definitely beat those six pairs of fresh underwear his Aunt had sent to him. Or that awful Christmas jumper his other Aunt had sent to him that played tinkly Christmas music. Because that was certainly something that he would get a lot of use out throughout the year.

Still... as he wrapped the scarf around his neck, he thought to himself that perhaps his relationship with Skye hadn't turned out to be such a disaster after all?

He continued to pounder this as he went back to his PC, hopefully to not die so much in this next game.

Route 43 - Present Day

A flock of Mareep and the occasional Flaaffy grazed cheerfully on the fresh grass, their fluffy coats keeping them warm from the chilly winter air the blew through the mountains of Johto.

However, while one would expect them to be watched over by an Ampharos or two, they had instead been temporarily left in the care of a certain Feraligatr and Dragonite, who were keeping a close eye on them from some rocks nearby.

Stratus: <Heh, we should do this more often! We're helping other Pokémon without even having to lift a claw!>

Smirking to himself, Stratus reclined back on the rock, resting his head on his hands.

<It doesn't get much better than this...>

Lake, however, seemed to differ on this.

Lake: <I'm not so sure.>

Without lifting his head, Stratus turned his head to look at him.

Stratus: <What do you mean by that?>

Lake: <Oh, well this may sound a bit crazy, but...>

He pauses for a second, somewhat hesitant.

<...I sorta' miss the crazy stuff sometimes.>

Stratus: <Wait, what?!>

The Dragon sat bolt upright, aghast at what his son has just said.

<I...but...why?! Why would you miss getting lost in alternate worlds with no TV or Ice Cream and almost getting killed by a mad computer? And then almost getting killed by another mad computer? Or going on vacation and then suddenly, Delta Pokémon! Or->

Lake: <Whoa, whoa! Calm down!>

The Dragonite stopped, taking Lake's advice by inhaling deeply, the deep breath relaxing him somewhat.

Stratus: <...Sorry, got carried away there. But I would like to know what's going on here.>

Raising his paws, he crosses his arms, giving Lake a look of scrutiny.

<Because I'm not seeing your logic here.>

Lake: <Alright, maybe I should have worded it better. You see, it's not the life and death situations that I miss, it's the excitement, you know?>

Stratus: <Excitement?>

Lake: <Yeah. The excitement of adventure. Of challenges. The excitement you can't find in everyday life. Think about it, what have we done recently that's been exciting?>

Stratus: <Um...are you forgetting on Monday, when we used Bubble Shield to roll down that hill? Does that not count?>

Lake: <It did...until you were sick...while you were still in your bubble...>

Becoming awkward upon the mention of that somewhat traumatic event, Stratus nodded as he rubbed the back of his head with a paw.

Stratus: <Couldn't get out of that thing fast enough. Yuck.>

Lake: <I'm looking for something more adventurous and less disgusting than that, though.>

Shifting around on the rock, Lake turned to gaze at the distant mountains to the north that lay beyond the Lake of Rage.

<I wanna' go somewhere new, see exotic places, meet new types of Pokémon. A proper adventure like that. Johto's nice, but I've kinda' seen it all already.>

Stratus: <...Ooh, I get it now. You wanna' go and see the world, huh?>

The Feraligatr gazed back at the Dragonite, nodding in affirmation.

Lake: <Pretty much.>

Stratus: <So...that's pretty much a vacation, right?>

Lake: <I guess it is, yeah.>

The Dragonite suddenly grinned, now much more keen on the idea.

Stratus: <Well a vacation is just going someplace else to relax! So...>

Raising his paw, Stratus clenched it, preforming an excited fist-pump.

<I'm game!>
