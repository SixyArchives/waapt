Spectra - Around 600 years ago

-Lucanus has grown tired of sitting and moping on the castle ramparts, and has decided to take a walk around the city proper for the first time. It isn't long before he finds himself in the poorer quarters of the kingdom, and he glances around at the barely-standing houses and impoverished townsfolk, most of whom scatter upon his approach.-

Lucanus: Dear Arceus... the poverty and squalor here is beyond anything I could have imagined. During times of famine in Pugilis - another of my many failures - life grew hard, but not to this extent... the Ghost Lord must be aware of the conditions in which his people live. Or does he simply choose to ignore them?

-He rounds a corner and is immediately hurtled into by a hurried-looking villager. The villager remains upright, but Lucanus is not so lucky - the weight of his armour sends him toppling backwards into the muddy street. A look of horror spreads across the woman's face.-

Villager: Oh dear Giratina... m-my apologies, milord, I'm afraid I d-didn't notice your coming...

Lucanus: ...

-Lucanus glares at the clearly terrified villager for a moment, then gives a small sigh.-

Lucanus: ...No. I will not allow myself to abuse my power any longer. Despite the many evils I have committed, there is still a chance that I may be able to bring some good into this world.

-He heaves himself to his feet and smiles at the woman.-

Lucanus: No need to apologise, my dear lady. It was an honest mistake, and as much my own fault as yours. Shall we both agree to continue as though this whole affair never transpired?

-The villager does not look comforted by Lucanus' words, however - in fact, she starts to back away as though terrified that Lucanus will seize the opportunity to pounce. Lucanus gives her a confused look, then glances down at his purple breastplate.-

Lucanus: ...Of course. The armour. In Pugilis it merely drew derision, but here it commands fear. I can only thank Arceus that she has yet to realise just who I am... otherwise, it would not merely be the armour that inspires such terror...

-He sighs, then holds up his hands.-

Lucanus: ...Whilst I am aware that my word may not mean much, I can promise you that I have no intention of causing you harm. As you can see, I am unarmed. Even if I were, I would like to believe myself to be above administering punishment for such petty misdemeanors...

-Again, Lucanus' efforts do not appear to comfort the villager, though she does stop backing away.-

Lucanus: My apologies, I am not choosing my words particularly well. Perhaps it would make things easier if we were to start off with introductions. My name is Lucanus Cain. Yours?

Villager: K-Kohaku...

Lucanus: I'm sorry, I don't think I caught that.

Villager: My n-name's Kohaku...

Lucanus: Kohaku? I can't say it's a name I'm familiar with. Then again, I am not a native of Spectra, or even Ransei. I suppose you live around these parts?

-Kohaku nods.-

Lucanus: I see. Well, I suppose I had better offer you some recompense for my earlier rudeness. Unfortunately I do not have any gold on me, but I'm certain there is something else I could offer...

-He thinks for a moment, then pulls a Berry out of a pouch on his belt. Upon noticing Kohaku's eyes light up when she sees the Berry, however, he pauses.-

Lucanus: ...In fact, you might as well take the whole pouch. Arceus knows you and your fellow people will need it more than I do.

-He hands her the pouch.-

Kohaku: T-thank you...

Lucanus: No need. After all, it is the least I can do to atone for my sins...

-With that, Lucanus walks off, leaving Kohaku clutching the pouch of Berries and looking rather confused.-
