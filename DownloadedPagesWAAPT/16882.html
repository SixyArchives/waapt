An abandoned warehouse of some description, outside the space-time continuum as we know it

(Five mons are currently located here. On the ground are Vattenfallet, Snowy, and a thoroughly terrified Spalje. All around them, tarpaulins have been flung over machines of varying dimensions, from roughly the size of an adult human to monstrous walls of fabric and black metal that scrape the ceiling of the warehouse. Two red birdmons of considerable strength, who helped move these devices into their current positions in the first place, can occasionally be seen flying/leaping from tarp to tarp.)

Vattenfallet: <So, Spalje. Care to guess what purpose these machines serve?>

Spalje: (gulp) <Uh... they're exercise machines, right?>

Vattenfallet: <Correct. And every single one is the work of Snowy here, aren't they, dear?>

Snowy: (nuzzles Vatt) <Aye! My career is finally takin' off!>

(Spalje glances at the two-foot-tall Sneasel, then at one particularly monstrous mass of titanium in the corner of the room.)

Spalje: <How.>

Snowy: <Ah! Trade secret, that is.> ;3

Vattenfallet: <Now then, Spalje. Shall we begin?>

Spalje: <I don't have an actual choice in the matter, do I?>

Vattenfallet: <Again, your assumptions are 100% correct.>

Spalje: <This must be how prisoners of war feel...>

(Svavel and Tungvikt take all of seconds to haul out the first device, which stands roughly seven feet tall beneath its tarpaulin. Once the tarp comes loose, the device is revealed to resemble a cross-trainer for quadrupeds, but suspended in the air from a hinge that appears capable of turning a full 360 degrees.)

Vattenfallet: <Now, all you have to do for this first exercise is strap yourself into those foot pads and move as though jogging briskly. Does that sound simple enough?>

Spalje: <Hey, if they're all this cushy, I dunno what I was so scared of now!> :D

(Cue eeeeevil smirk from the birdmons, contemplative face from the Vaporeon and sheepish, almost guilty expression from the Sneasel. By the time Spalje notices these faces, however, he's already strapped in and taking his first tentative "steps".)

Spalje: <Okay, now I'm worried again. What are you->

(His speech dissolves into a panicked yelp as Tungvikt suddenly leaps forward and headbutts a switch on the side, causing the hinge holding the device to grow roughly twenty feet in height and start swinging it around like one of those pirate ship rides at a theme park.)

Vattenfallet: <Tungvikt! We were supposed to be saving up that feature until he seemed confident enough to handle it!>

Tungvikt: <BUT MIGHTY WRESTLER WAS BORED!>

Svavel: <Besides, it's not like he's going to lose weight if we just coddle the shit out of him, is it now?>

(Spalje, meanwhile, can't tell whether the voices he's hearing are real, or hallucinated, or whether or not he's alive or dead. All he knows is that Arceus Christ he's never been so dizzy.)

(Several minutes later, after a hurried search for the button that shuts the cross-trainer of death down...)

Spalje: <I'm gonna dieeeee~> :S

Vattenfallet: <...and in light of the aforementioned incident, we shall save up these machines until Spalje has gained confidence in his exercise regimes through alternative methods. Is that clear?>

Svavel: <You just don't appreciate the joy of watching morons squirm.> >v>
