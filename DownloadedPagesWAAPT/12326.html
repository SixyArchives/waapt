Titan's Lab

-There is a flash as Herbert's armband is removed and he reverts back to human.-

Herbert: ...Alright, here goes nothing... I just hope this doesn't turn out to be something else I regret later...

-He turns to Titan, now visibly struggling to maintain the Shadow illusion - not that it makes much of a difference, since by this point Titan is too emotionally torn up to notice. He certainly doesn't hear the Sneasel scurrying around above, even if Herbert does.-

Titan: Of c-course... you don't even recognise me now, do you? I... I'm sorry, Lucius, I can't say that enough...

Herbert: ...If he doesn't quit crying soon, am I allowed to start beating some sense into him?

Titan: You... you have to forgive me, son, I... I thought I could stop them from going this far...

-Herbert sighs, braces himself, and drops the illusionary Shadow Aura.-

Herbert: Did you really? Because I didn't see you making many efforts to stop them.

-The sudden change in emotional feedback causes Titan to stumble out of his chair, staggering backwards until he hits the rear wall of the office.-

Herbert: So, you'd sit back and let the people you work for Shadow your own son, would you? Dear Arceus, you're worse than Lyuri.

Titan: But... you shouldn't... how are you-

Herbert: I'm surprised I managed to get this far, actually. Guess I'm just lucky that the veteran Auric can't identify a simple illusion, huh?

-He strides forward, grabbing the stumbling Titan by the throat despite his diminutive stature.-

Titan: So you're not actually... oh, thank Arceus. You have to understand that I would never let Cipher do something like that to you, Lucius, there are no circumstances in which I-

Herbert: Really? Because you've never raised an objection up until now. You never objected to Cipher sending hit squads after me and the rest of the team. You never objected to having my friend and mentor Shadowed. You never objected to your boss' fucked-up little minion having a Shadow Pokemon beat me until I lay broken and bleeding in the street. Have you ever raised a finger to protect me at any point during these last five years? Of course not. You're far too busy plotting how best to brainwash Pokemon and murder children to care about the wellbeing of your only son. If it wasn't for the fact that I've figured out how to fend for myself over the years, you'd have nothing more than an urn on the mantlepiece to continue the family!

Titan: ...It hardly excuses my actions, but you've proven yourself quite capable of-

Herbert: Shut up. I'm not finished yet.

-He releases his grip on Titan's collar, letting him slump to the floor, then turns and starts pacing up and down the room.-

Herbert: I haven't even begun to describe what a replusive, despicable scumbag you are, as if the fact that you work for Cipher didn't already illustrate that well enough. Wasn't Galactic enough, or do you have to join a new evil organisation every few months to keep yourself motivated? You've killed, lied, stolen, worked with multiple terrorist groups, and yet you still carry yourself with an air of self-righteousness. The levels of delusion required to convince yourself that you're one of the good guys are so deeply pathetic it's almost pitiable.

Titan: I... I have never held such delusions. I've never been a good person, Lucius, and whilst I believe that my goals are benevolent the means I use to accomplish them are a necessary-

Herbert: Let me guess - a necessary evil? I've heard enough about those from the people who're supposed to be on our side! The only reason the evil is necessary in the first place is because you made that decision! You think you're so much better than Alexiel, that you can lord over him from a smug position of moral superiority because you don't go the extra mile to be a complete asshole, when really you're just as bad as each other! Next time you think that what you're doing is justified just because you're not as much of an dickhead as he is, remember that both of you were perfectly willing to kidnap, blackmail and brainwash teenage girls into doing your dirty work!

Titan: ...Europa - no, Crewe - was something of a unique case. I did what I could to-

Herbert: For fuck's sake, would you cut that out! Stop pretending that everything you do is suddenly beyond petty concepts of morality because it'll result in some better world or whatever it is that Cyrus wanted! You're working towards a greater good that nobody wanted or asked for! I know the concept might be entirely alien to whatever circuitry you have in place of a functioning human brain, but some of us are fighting for this world because we prefer it to the emotionally barren hellhole you'd replace it with!

-Titan falls silent and clenches his fists as Herbert continues to pace furiously up and down the office.-

Herbert: I'll be the first to admit that this world's a pretty shitty place at times. Orre's proof of that. Over the last few months, I've been abandoned by nearly everyone I ever considered to be a friend, betrayed by the very people I was trying to help, and survived several attempts on my life - most of which you were complicit in, I have to add. And yet I still think this flawed, broken, imperfect world that you hate so much is worth fighting for. Why? Because if there's even one friend I have left by the end of this, one person to pat me on the shoulder and tell me that it's all over, then it'll all have been worth it.

-He rounds on Titan, tears of fustration beginning to well in his eyes.-

Herbert: But of course, you wouldn't understand that, would you? I don't know what happened, when it happened, or even if anything happened at all, but life screwed you over one day and you never quite got over that. You retreated inside yourself, became this sad, empty, broken shell of a man determined to lash out at the world in any way you could. I wish things could go back to the way they used to be - when we actually used to be an Arceusdamned family - but that's never going to happen, is it? You're too stubborn and pigheaded to give upon something you've spent all this time working towards, even if it does turn out to be pointless and destructive. You just want to watch the world burn. To get your own back against the unfair nature of existence itself. You're not interested in the good of the world, or humanity, or any of that guff - no, you just want to tear down reality just to see if you can. You just want to end it all and bring everyone else down with-

Titan: SILENCE!

-Titan charges an Aura Sphere and launches it at Herbert, sending him flying across the room and against the wall. He groans slightly as Titan's expression shifts from one of anger to one of horror, then gets to his feet, still glaring and blinking back tears.-

Herbert: ...And you can't even be bothered to try and defend yourself with reasonable debate, so you just resort to blowing me across the room instead. As if I needed more proof that even you don't believe your own drivel.

Titan: ...Son, I-

Herbert: No. You don't get to call me that. You don't ever get to call me that. Forget being your son, I'm ashamed of the fact that whatever base organisms we're descended from crawled out of the same primordial ooze.

-He snatches the armband from Psyche and puts it on, transformimg into an Abra once again before blipping away.-

Titan: Lucius...

-Titan simply stares at the spot where Herbert used to be for several moments, before heaving himself upright, slumping down at his desk, and resting his head in his hands once again.-
