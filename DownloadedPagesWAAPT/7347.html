Good Guy Germany

Efrafa (Timeskip)

It's been around two weeks since Laurel received his post from the General and his scouting routes. He was shown around the colony and saw how things were done here, and it was awful. The nidos had no freedom when they were to go for silflay, there were allocated times at which the lower caste and females nido could go out for silflay. Hraka was not passed freely, but had to be passed into pits which would be filled later by the Oswlafa so that no elil were drawn to the colony. Nidos could not explore their area outside of the area marked by the Oswlafa, or they will be attacked by the scouts. The Nidorina could not dig new burrows freely until they had permission from the Owslafa, which was rare as many of them weren't even allowed to talk to the General, let alone ask for permission for a new dig. This caused the runs to be overcrowded with Nidos, who lived too close to eachother in no privacy. On top of that, no-one was allowed to leave to warren. Those who disobeyed this rule were either mauled or killed by the Oswlafa scouts sent after them.

The Owslafa was not any better. While the higher cast Nidoran males and Nidorinos got the better food, the better runs and the respect of the inhabitants, the training was gruelling. Each day, there would be massive fights between Oswlafa members to prove their strength and ability. An Oswlafa member was expected to be strong, swift and silent, deadly for the kill. They were expected not to flee from elil, but fight them. Any natural instinct of fear and flight had been bred out of them underneath the tyrant General's rule. They were expected to go out on their scouting route for days, with minimal food or water, to report on anything out of the ordinary in the area. Those who failed to meet these requirements were harmed and ostracized, until Inle showed his face.

There was nothing natural about life in Efrafa. Everything was controlled, in some way or form, by the council. It was a totalitarian hellhole.

While Laurel acted out his part within the colony, he looked for signs of rebellion within the system. He was brought to attention a lovely young doe known as Nildro-hain, who hated her supressed existence within Efrafa and was looking for any opportunity to escape. She was even brave enough to ask the General himself for her freedom. Laurel admired Nildro-hain's bravery and willpower and often talked to her in secret. Talking to her about Hazel-Rah's plans and asking her to rally up any other unsatisfied does for the eventual breakout.

Laurel also made the acquaintance of a nido named Blackavar, a buck with darker shade of purple fur than your run-off-the-mill Nido. He, and a few other does, attempted to escape from Efrafa. While the does did not make it far from the colony, Blackavar, who once was a member of the Owslafa, did. However, he was eventually captured by the faster and stronger scouts and taken back to the colony to be punished. When he returned, the Oswlafa gathered all the bucks and does into the main tunnel to witness the spectacle about to take place. Once everyone was gathered, they shred Blackavar's ears to pieces and beat him to near-death. The message was clear:

If you try to escape, this will be you.

Laurel had talked many times to Blackavar over the weeks. Blackavar admitted to Laurel that if he saw another chance to escape, he would. So Laurel made up his mind that he would take Blackavar with him once the breakout was finally organised.

Laurel kept in contact with the others at the mountain foot via secret meetings with Skree, who had evolved during the time Laurel had spent with Efrafa. During these meetings, he found that his group is doing fine on the fringes, eating fresh mountain grass and telling stories about El-ahriarah. He also found out that the group has discovered a small trail through the mountainside, which they will make their escape from. However, the trail is incredibly narrow, and a false step could spell doom for the unfortunate one, but it was a risk worth taking.

Laurel scratched at the soft earth underneath his paws and lied down. Earlier today, Nildro-hain claimed that she had organised a group of 12 does -including her- that are willing to risk their lives for freedom. Laurel smiled at her and said that everything was in place now.

All they had to do was wait.

  • Silflay: "Above-ground food/feed"
  • Hraka: "Waste, Droppings"
  • Owslafa: The Owsla (council) of Efrafa
  • Elil: "Enemy", creatures that prey upon Nidos
  • Nildro-hain: Lit. "Blackbirds Song"
  • El-ahriarah: "Prince-Thousand-Enemy" lit. "The Prince with 1000 enemies", he is a folk hero in traditional Lapin tales.
