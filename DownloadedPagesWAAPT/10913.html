Holon Ruins

-Dune is still searching for his missing Phanpy and crown, though he has happened to start his search in a completely wrong direction. Meanwhile...-

Dune's Mindscape

-The Command Center is abuzz with activity as crew members talk to themselves about the exchange with the other Orrian yesterday. The Captain taps his pointer stick against the table to silence the group.-

The Captain: Alright everyone, I know we're all excited about our conversation with Pi yesterday, and that we all would like to try and help him, but we need to get a few things straight before we get too tangled up in conjecture.

-He hits a few buttons and two holograms, one of Dune and one of Pi, appear above the table.-

The Captain: I'll let the Scribe handle this part.

The Scribe: Thank you, sir. From our previous conversations with Pi, I can draw that, like Dune, is a native of Orre. Not only that, but something in his past has caused him to develop a vendetta against Cipher, a criminal organization that we too have had run-ins with in the past.

-The points of similarity appear between the two figures as they are mentioned.-

The Scribe: Now, the Strategist and the Lorekeeper both helped me with this next part. While I did mention that the two did have a mutual enemy in Cipher, Pi appears to have had taken it from "oppose them because they are criminals" to "oppose them because they are". This suggests extreme emotional trauma associated with the criminal group.

The Engineer: You still haven't explained why we're going over all of this.

The Captain: We're going over this because I don't want it getting into anyone's processes that Pi could just do the same thing we did with the Criminal. Despite our initial similarities, Pi's mindset is notably different than ours. Also..

-The Captain pushes some different buttons, and a display of two question marks shows.-

The Captain: It appears our two "demons" are connected, but not the same thing. We know that the Criminal was a brute, who crushed minds with raw force. While we don't know much about Pi's, his appears before him as a hallucination. As discussed with the Lorekeeper, this suggests a trickster mentality, one where it uses mind games to weaken its prey's resolve.

The Doctor: So... what can we do?

The Captain: Really... nothing, without knowing more. All we can do right now is keep him in our thoughts.

My availability over the next few days will be iffy, as I'm heading back up to my soon-to-be college for orientation, and the signal strength coming and going will be a crap shoot.
