-One quick trip across Holon later, and Herbert is currently waiting in Coulombia, dressed in his characteristically ridiculous desert outfit. Alice is buying the ferry tickets nearby, still in her usual winterwear. Falchion and Bishop are waiting by Herbert's side as he impatiently checks his Poketch.-

Herbert: Come on, the line for tickets can't be that long...

Falchion: <Aren't you waiting for some of your friends to show up?>

Herbert: Why would I? I know some of the other guys are interested in taking down Cipher as well, but like Sakura said, none of them want to work with me... I mean, hopefully I'll be bumping into some of them whilst I'm there, but aside from that I expect I'll be working on my own most of the time.

Falchion: <Let me get this straight. You're planning on taking down a major part of a regionwide crime syndicate by yourself, with no support other than a team of five Pokemon, two of which aren't even fully evolved.>

Herbert: Well... yeah, pretty much.

Falchion: <Permission to speak freely, sir?>

Herbert: Granted.

Falcion: <That's insane.>

Herbert: I never said it would be easy. But what else are we supposed to do? They've already taken my friend and my starter. How do we know they're going to stop there? Should we just sit back and let Cipher walk all over us?

Falchion: <You would've been perfectly fine with that a few months ago.>

Herbert: ...Things have changed since then.

Falchion: <Apparently so, especially since you're now cocky enough to throw yourself at a freaking paramilitary organisation. You know those guys use guns, right?>

Herbert: I know. I've had experience with Cipher before.

Falchion: <Then that should be enough to tell you that you're way out of your depth. Remember that you're a sixteen-year-old kid going up against adults with guns and your only advantage is the ability to make your hands glow. How much can you really do with Aura, anyway? Aside from smack me around because you're feeling bored or unhinged.>

Herbert: If you're telling me that I need a teacher, then I don't. Duke's gone and Tagg's got enough on his hands without taking me on as a student. I know the basics, I can teach myself from here on out. Look, do you want to come with me or not? Because you're free to head back to Challenger's Cave.

-Falchion shrugs.-

Falchion: <Like I said, I'm in this for the duration unless Edge's ghost tells me to go back to stealing mattresses. I'd just rather not be sent on a suicide mission.>

Herbert: Duly noted. Now quit bellyaching and fall back in line.

-Falchion nods, and heads off to talk with the rest of Herbert's team. Herbert looks over at Bishop, who has remained silent throughout the entire conversation.-

Herbert: You okay, Bish?

Bishop: <...I would be lying if I said that I was. But I am ready for our journey, at the least.>

Herbert: Good. Remind me exactly what we're up against, again?

Bishop: <Our first order of business will most likely be... well, the retrieval of Kirk. I believe the Cipher operative who snagged him is known as Verax. Alice has encountered him before. From there, we will need to investigate just who Alexiel is and attempt to rescue Duke from his custody. Whilst we are in the region, we should also attempt to track down Titan and recover Crewe's pendant from a scientist named Aelia.>

Herbert: ...Okay, now I'm beginning to see Falchion's point. That's a tall order for us to take care of alone...

Bishop: <Hopefully we will have the J-Team's assistance with at least some of those points.>

Herbert: You sound very calm about all of this.

Bishop: <I am making an additional effort to maintain my composure. I cannot afford to let it slip for even a moment whilst we are in Orre.>

-Herbert raises an eyebrow at Bishop, then gives him a quick pat before returning to waiting. He checks his Holon Transceiver again, smirks at Zeal, Jacob and Pi's messages, then turns it off and puts it in his bag as Alice approaches.-

Alice: Here are the tickets. Now then, we should be going now.
