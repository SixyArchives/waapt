-it's not really the Library anymore. There aren't any books to be seen anywhere! The door leads to something of a steam tunnel. No longer painted blue, the stone wall is a dull, oppressive grey, with hissing steam emanating from the rusty, clanging pipes that line the tunnel-

-he says this sarcastically, but in all honesty the loud industry of the surroundings comforts him. The big, heavy iron door locked as it closed, so disregarding serious temporal jiggerypokery the Wolf wouldn't be invading right this minute. The loud hiss was omnipresent, and it cleansed him. Something like sitting in a sauna while listening to an avant-garde percussion ensemble-

Joseph: Hmm? What's this?

-the pipes and steam seemed to abruptly end around a curve in the tunnel, replaced by natural light-

Joseph: Well, either I've found the hallway to heaven, or there's just some opening...

-he could be forgiven for assuming that he had found the hallway to heaven when he enters the rose garden. It's a greenhouse as big as an airplane hangar, the sky the purest blue as Joseph looks up. Joseph: Hmm! Looks like we're in another green wo-ROSES!
