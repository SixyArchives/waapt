He looked around and saw an ice cream stand nearby. On such a warm day, the thought of ice cream was very tempting. So Lair wandered over there. Looking at the display, he only saw one chocolate-covered vanilla ice cream bar.

"Good morning, young sir, " said the honest-looking merchant. "You look like you need some ice cream. I'd gladly sell it to you for a thousand."

"A thousand?! Isn't that a tad steep."

"Normally, I'd agree with you, " replied that veracious-looking storekeeper. "But unfortunately, I only have a single bar left."

"Why? Has there been a run on ice cream or something?"

The ethical-looking vendor leaned forward and spoke in a loud stage whisper. "Normally, yes. But there is a gang of lactose-intolerant fire pokemon rampaging the local area. They're hijacking all of the convoys coming into town and melting the ice cream. It's horrible, just horrible."

"That... seems a tad implausible, " stated Lair.

"Look at this face, " said the ingenuous-appearing consigner as he wiped away a single tear. "Does this look like the face of a liar?"

"I guess not."

"Exactly! However, I like you kid and you look like you need some cooling down. I'll make you a deal. I'll sell it to you for a mere five hundred, " offered the equitable businessman.

Lair had to think about it. Five hundred is a lot for an ice cream bar. It didn't even look that fancy, like made from contented miltanks or something. However, it was very hot and it didn't make too big of a dent in his budget.

"Okay. I'll talk it."

"Good choice, young sir!" exclaimed the trustworthy salesman. He took the ice cream bar out the refridgerated display case and handed it to Lair. Lair left the ice cream stand and went back to Glares, content with his slightly over-priced ice cream.

The virtuous ice cream dealer just shook his head as he reached into the cooler beneath the display case filled with boxes of ice cream bars. "Gets them everytime..."
