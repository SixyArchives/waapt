Almer - Hoenn Sector - Garrison

-A certain Scrafty is mirroring the activities of a certain Torterra-

<Commander!Metagross: "How has the hunt for those mainland spies been going?">

<Grif: "Hunt for- Oh right... Uh... Well, it's been thorough...">

<Commander!Metagross: "Mmph. I see that it must truly be confidence-inspiring if a mon that I'd specifically have problems reading was sent to deliver the report...">

<Grif: "... That's not why I'm here...">

<Commander!Metagross: "... Then why are you?">

-And is handed a letter and starts reading-

To Whom it May Concern in the Imperial Garrison at Almer:

I am aware that with each passing generation from humanity's end, the decrees of my throne seem to hold less and less resonance with the mons of our land, but I implore you to remember the bonds upon which Hoenn forged itself as a remnant of the old world and relay my instructions onto your subordinates.

Hoenn's future is very much an open question at the moment. Parliament has remained as intransigent and unproductive as it was in my days as a mere Bagon. And now the very principalities that nominally swear allegiance to my throne are on the cusp of raising arms against each other and elevating their local rulers over that of my throne's rightful claim.

I cannot pretend my ancestors have always been able to ensure unity amongst their subjects, indeed, there are periods of thousands of moons in the old world in which the counterparts of Hoenn's present principalities openly bore arms against each other. But even in the midst of all of that, they preserved a unified identity that transcended their local squabbles. Something that I fear my realm is quickly losing.

What I ask you to do is not an errand for my throne... But rather an errand for the very heart and soul of Hoenn. Things in Hoenn cannot persist as they are without change. We must provide ourselves opportunities to reaffirm our common identity as a continuation of the old world... And... As paradoxically as it may seem, I believe that the key to this may be to look outwards beyond our borders.

I will be arriving in Almer in order to meet with the one who proclaims himself 'Emperor of the Ancients'. We will be discussing matters of trade and diplomacy... Though it is my hope that their 'Ancient' moniker is not purely for show, and that perhaps they too remember facets of the old world that we have forgotten. Do everything in your power to ensure that our portion of those islands is peaceable and fitting for a reception between monarchs.

Sincerely, Emperor Uriel Septim the [n]th of Hoenn
