Sea of Kyogre - A few hundred miles closer to the Orange Islands

-It wasn't much further now. If he kept swimming at the rate he was going, Lugia would reach the Orange Islands by tomorrow. And then he could start looking for his son...and make whoever was responsible for taking him pay.

As the legendary began to think of how wonderful Areoblasting the ones responsible for this would be, a foreign presence entered his mind.-

Jello: <Um...hello?>

Lugia: Get out. I don't feel like talking.

Jello: <Oh! But listen, please! It's about your son!>

-Lugia was about to eject whoever had decided to invade him mind, but stopped upon the mention of his son.-

Lugia: ...What do you know about my son? TELL ME!

Jello: <Eek! Pleo's safe! We have him right here on Lightning Island! Honest!>

Lugia: Hmm, Why should I believe you? And how can I be certain that you're not the one repsonisble for his suffering?

Jello: <Um! Oh, how about you speak to him for yourself, let me just link his mind to ours...>

-A few seconds pass, then Lugia feels another, much more familiar presence enter his mind.-

Pleo: <...Dad?>

Lugia: ...! Pleo? Are you alright?

Pleo: <I'm fine right now! Well, the weather made it a bit hard to get here, but apart from that, I'm okay!>

Lugia: Oh. What happened, Pleo? I sensed that you were in trouble a short time ago. Why was that? And are you safe now?

Pleo: <Well, you see...>

-Pleo explains the events of the past few weeks to Lugia. From Tempest almost kidnapping him, to how the J-Team saved him and looked after him, how the Delta's had managed to kidnap him, and how he had been rescued again.-

Pleo: <So you see? I'm okay now! and I've got some really nice peoplemons looking after me. So, could you please not blow up the Orange Islands? I don't want you to hurt anyone...>

Lugia: ...Oh, Pleo. I'm so sorry. It's my fault this happened. I should have been there for you instead of just leaving you like that where anymon could get you.

Pleo: <No, Dad, it's fine! Sure, I really missed you. But if you hadn't left me, I would have never met King Nautilus and The Reef Pokémon and everyone else. But...Dad? Why did you leave me? Didn't you want to be with me anymore?>

Lugia: No! No no, it's not like that at all, Pleo! I love you. and I wanted to stay with you, but...I was needed elsewhere. And I wasn't sure if it would be safe or not, so I left you with King Nautilus and the Pokémon of the Reef because I thought they would be able to keep you safe. But it seems I was wrong.

It's clear to me now. I was never meant to be a father...

Pleo: <No, Dad! Don't say that! I...I love you...>

Lugia: ...I love you too, Pleo.

Pleo: <Dad...Can you come home now, please? I really miss you...>

Lugia: ...Yes, Pleo. I will.

Pleo: <Really?>

Lugia: I promise you, Pleo, I'll be there within the nest day or two. Where are you?

Pleo: <We're all at Shamouti Island...Home.>

Lugia: All right then. You wait there, and I will be there by Saturday.

Pleo: <You promise?>

Lugia: Yeah, and if I'm not...I'll get you all the Kelp you can eat. How does that sound?

Pleo: <That sounds awesome. Oh, but Dad?>

Lugia: What is it, Pleo?

Pleo: <Could you get rid of this nasty storm? Me and my friends had a lot of trouble getting here...>

Lugia: Oh. I'm sorry. I'll clear that up right away, okay?

Pleo: <Okay.>

Jello: <Er, I hate to be a bother, but keeping this channel open is rather tiring...>

Pleo: <Sorry, Jello. I guess I should go now, then.>

Lugia: Okay. I'll see you soon, son.

Pleo: <See you, Dad.>

-As the two presences leave his mind, Lugia begins to swim with added vigor, determined to get to the Oragane Islands and see his son again. Now in a much happier mood, the skies above him clear, the dreadful weather finally turning sunny again for the first time in days.

Like the weather, for Lugia, things just brighter.-
