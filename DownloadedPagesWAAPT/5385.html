 134615 Hobgoblin, Sat, 4th Feb '12 1:09:51 PM from the Internet Get RP Mod
Super Trainer 2 Hugh
Kim: Well, there's lots of places. In terms of similarity, it's probably a bit closer to Hoenn, though there are a lot of Pokemon that live in Sinnoh there as well.

There's a large, forested area called [Vientown] in the south of Almia, which is where I first worked when I was an Area Ranger. I also went to the nearby Ranger School across the lake bridge. My hometown of [Chicole Village] is to the further south. It's a bit of an agricultural community; my mom and dad do farm work, and sometimes my Pokemon and my little sister help. Around this area is also the [Nabiki Beach]. It's a beach where Rangers are said to meet their first partners, which held true for me since I met Blossom there.

In the center of Almia is [Pueltown]. It's an industrial area and a large fishing company operates in the harbor there as well. The place also has a small problem with Voltorb rolling around and blowing things up... To the north of the city is the [   Altru Building   ] and [Altru Park]. There was actually an incident with Altru Inc. and Darkrai in the past...but let's not get into that now. There was also a [Shaymin] sighting near here once.

The [Sea of Wailord] and the neighboring [Puel Sea] are to the east of Pueltown, and sometimes we Rangers make visits there to ensure that there are no oil spills or any other kinds of hazardous occurrences that could threaten the species there. Just as a fun fact, Rangers can't take most of their partners underwater. Aqua is probably the only one of mine who could swim with me.

To the north is the [Hia Valley], in which you can find the Shiver Camp. It's a site set up by a team of researchers to look into the various Ice-types that live out there. A friend of mine has a father who works there. Further north you'll find [Almia Castle], a frozen castle that houses the remains of an old kingdom guarded by a Lucario. There's a bit of a legend to it...but I won't go into that now. I ended up meeting Youko on a mission there, incidentally. [Dialga] was also reported being seen near there, but I can't really verify that claim.

To the north-east is [Boyleland], appropriately named for its volcanoes. They usually have an Ice Festival to cool the village down at certain times of the year, usually involving a Glalie and a Froslass. There's also a Volcano Cave which houses a myriad of Pokemon, from Torkoal to Muk, Aggron, Kangaskhan, and even Pikachu! There was also a Snover that turned up there once somehow, but one of our Rangers managed to rescue it. A Heatran guards this cave.

To the south-east is [Haruba Village], a desert community. There is a town among an oasis here, and in the past we were required to bring Blastoise to replenish the water supply. Nowadays, the inhabitants breed their own. North from the village is the Hippowdown Temple, which houses two Legendary Pokemon: Cresselia, its guardian, and Regigigas. We weren't even aware of Regigigas' existence there until one of our other Rangers brought the other members of the Regi Family to the temple on a hunch; it was quite the discovery! There were also rumors of [Palkia] being seen around, but, again, I can't verify that.

And finally, in northern Almia there is the [Ranger Union], the headquarters for Pokemon Rangers. Chairperson Erma is the head of the Union, in addition to Prof. Hastings in R&D. This is also the base of operations for [Top Rangers], like myself. Being a Top Ranger means that you are given a more powerful styler than Area Rangers, and are able to assign yourself your own missions. My current mission is to assist with any possible catastrophes in the "Battle Regions" (Kanto, Johto, Hoenn, etc.), as we call them in Almia.

There are also several caves, valleys, forest routes, and things like that. Most notable...well, for me anyway, would be the [Vien Forest]. During an incident with Team Dim Sun (and yes, they are as smart as the name suggests), the forest was burned down, leaving a lot of Pokemon injured or without homes. I was just a little girl at the time, but I wanted to do what I could for the Pokemon who were suffering. So, one day, I headed over to the forest to see what I could do. As fate would have it, a Top Ranger was there, along with a Celebi. Using its power, they were able to restore the forest to its former glory. The Pokemon were so happy, as were the townspeople. *smiles* It was that event, seeing how much of a difference you could make to people's lives that made me want to be a Pokemon Ranger.

...

...

...

Oh... *blushes* I went on a really long monologue. *sweatdrops* Please forgive me.

Rayquaza: <Wait, wait, wait...> Frowns. <...So the prize for that contest was the Jade Orb?>

Stratus: Nods. <Well, it was embedded in the trophy, but yeah.>

Rayquaza: <So, who ended up winning it?>

Stratus: <I don't know. We left before the tournament was over, so we don't know who won it.>

Rayquaza grumbles under his breath at this, then lets out a sigh.

Rayquaza: <Oh well...it can't be in the wrong hands, otherwise somebody would have tried to have summon me by now.>

He then turns his attention back to Stratus, eager to hear what he has to say next.

<What happened next, then?>

The Dragonite pauses to think for a few moment, then shrugs.

Stratus: <To be honest? I don't really know. We haven't spoken to anyone else much since Lilycove, apart from our friend Tracer and his Pokémon. We only just re-joined the rest of the group yesterday, and we haven't had the chance to catch up yet.>

Rayquaza: <Oh...so you're a bit left out, then?>

Stratus: Nods. <You could say that.>

Rayquaza: <Ah...>

The Dragon glances wistfully down at the island of Mossdeep far below.

<...I know how that feels...>

Stratus: Blinks. <What do you mean by that?>

Rayquaza gazes back at Stratus, a rather lonely look on his face.

Rayquaza: <Since I was created eons ago, I've lived up here in clouds, keeping watch over the sky, just as Groudon protects the land, and Kyogre defends the seas. So I stay up here, ensuring that the air, and the world below, is kept safe from anyone or anything that dares to intrude.

But...>

The Dragon gazes down at the land and sea spread below them.

<...I've never been able to spend much time in my counterparts domains. Because unlike mine...many people and Pokémon make their homes there, whereas I'm the only living creature for miles in mine.>

Rayquaza gazes back at Stratus.

<Few, if any other beings can survive up here. I was only able to come and talk to you because you're as low down as I'm allowed to go. Which is pretty high for you, no?>

The Dragonite nods in response.

<Yeah...So, I don't get the chance to talk to many other Pokémon, so it gets pretty lonely sometimes...>

Rayquaza gazes downwards again.

<There's this whole beautiful planet down there filled with all sorts of people and Pokémon, yet sometimes...I feel likes I'm just an observer sitting on the sidelines. Watching as the world goes by, but never being able to interact with it...>

The Dragon falls silent, raising a claw to rub something out of his eyes, leading Stratus to wonder if he had nearly started crying for a second there.

<...And that makes me sad sometimes...>
