Above Route 6

| Zeta has decided to take her party out of the vicinity of the City, as she wants to be farther away from the population when she explores the North Desert.

| She quickly slips into the state of mind necessary to reach that place.

The Land of the People

| Moving quickly so as to give herself no chance to hesitate or turn back, Zeta strides towards the North Desert, and the grim skies and withered sands.

Northern Desert

| She stumbles along the nearly nonexistent path, growing more and more uneasy with each step she takes.

| The sands around her are disturbingly uniform.

| They're tinged a pale, almost sickly gray. They are unchanging in color and texture, resembling fine, powdery dust more than sand.

| The heavy winds blow this pseudo-sand up into the air.

| This uniformity, this bland, yet utterly cruel, harsh sameness is, in an inexplicable way, far more disturbing than the ever-changing sand in the Desert of Madness.

| At long last, her legs weak, her mouth dry, her nerves twitching, Zeta reaches the end of the path.

| In front of her stands a raised platform, made out of the petrified stump of a mindbogglingly huge tree.

| She climbs up on it.

| The figure here is the one that looks the most like her.

| She sits on a throne, made out of billowing clouds of shadow, ethereal yet solid. It slowly shifts and morphs. Zeta can see faces emerge from the shadows, silently screaming, before being subsumed back into the dark mass of the throne.

| The figure is dressed in a black, high-collared, tight shirt. She lounges on the throne casually, legs hanging off one end, elbow propping her head up on the other.

| She has a sneer on her face as Zeta, trembling and tense, approaches.

Zeta?: Hah. Finally showed up I see. Took your own sweet time about it, too. I was beginning to think you weren't gonna show.

Zeta: Well, you're a lot less formal than your sisters.

Zeta?: Damn straight.

Zeta: What's your name?

Zeta?: Girl, you know next to nothing. Nada. Zip, zilch. Zero. Why would I bother to give my name to an ignorant little girl like you?

Zeta: |taken aback| Fine. What shall I call you then?

Zeta?: Hmm. Good question. Lemme see...

| Zeta? sits and thinks for a long time.

Zeta?: Call me Stver.

Zeta: Stver. What's the name of this place.

Stver: Whaddya think it's called?

| Zeta pauses, obviously hesitant.

Zeta: The Desert of Shadow.

Stver: DING DING DING! Somebody give this numskull a prize, she's figured out the smallest fraction of knowledge about the world.

Zeta: ...

Zeta: Will you tell me what the keys are?

Stver: Aww. You were just starting to get slightly tolerable, too! No. Figure it out yourself. Actually, first, just get out of my sight. I'm bored with you now.

Zeta: Fine.

| And the shadows rise up to pull her down. They are thicker, stronger, more tangible, more disturbing here than any of the other three Deserts.

| She is gone.

Stver: Stupid little bitch.

| And then she fades out of existence.

| Then the platform.

| Then the sand.

| Then the path.

| Then the storm.

| Then the sky.

| At last, the only thing remaining is the throne made of shadows, billowing against the nothingness.

| After a long pause, it too phases out of existence.
