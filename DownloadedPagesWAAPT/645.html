Celestial Tower

-The Elgyem to be known as Portales is nervously holding onto the Eldest Beheeyem's arm. Eventually they arrive at the floor just before they reach the top of the Tower, where the Chandelure and Beheeyem agreed to meet. Elgyem, soon finding his friend Lampent, starts to bolt in her direction, but is stopped by the Eldest Beheeyem. He understands, and looks away while the Beheeyem leads him to the Chandelure.-

Chandelure: You're early.

Beheeyem: As are you.

Chandelure: Alright then... Well?

Beheeyem: Well what? You are the one with the concerns, not I.

Chandelure: Fine. I want to know if your 'Littlest Elgyem' has been messing with my daughter's memories.

Lampent: But I already told you mom! He's not doing anything!

Chandelure: But I don't know that! How can I know that that Elgyem hasn't done anything to you!? For all I know he could have—

Beheeyem: Miss Chandelure. I can assure you that we will not go anywhere unless you can trust your own offspring's word. If she says that Elgyem has done nothing, then he has done nothing.

Chandelure: I can't trust creatures that don't even belong here! If it weren't for the other Chandelure allowing you to live in this Tower you wouldn't have invaded and I could live my life in peace!

Lampent: Mother!

Beheeyem: We have done no harm to you! We live in the top of the tower and you live at the bottom of the tower—that was our agreement! We have kept to the rules you gave us and have done almost nothing to interact with you!

Chandelure: Then why has your Elgyem brainwashed my daughter!?

Beheeyem: He did not brainwash your daughter! He has merely befriended her!

Lampent: She's right, mom! I swear, me and Elgyem are just friends! He hasn't—

Chandelure: Quiet Lampent! I'm going to beat this space-butt to a pulp!

-The argument between the three escalates into shouting. Elgyem, not knowing what to do, remains silent and hides behind the Eldest Beheeyem. Eventually, he hears threats of a Shadow Ball and, fearing for his life, makes a run for it.

Lampent and Beheeyem chase him down, hoping to calm him down, while the Chandelure chases the Beheeyem, expecting a fight. The Elgyem keeps flying away, going further and further down the Tower, until eventually he bumps into something.-

Elgyem: Ow... -he holds his head and looks up at what he hit. Then he realizes he hit a trainer-

Trainer: Hey there little guy! My name's Reginald, and...

-The Elgyem scrambles over to a tombstone and stares fearfully up at the trainer-

Trainer: Oh? You're afraid of me? Huh! Well, don't worry! I'll help you become brave in no time! -activates an Ultra Ball- Pokeball, go!

-Elgyem starts scrambling away from the pokeball for a few seconds, before he gets sucked into the Ultra Ball. Inside the Ultra Ball, he tries to break out, but due to him being about an inch and a half smaller than the average Elgyem, he can't make it out, and he is captured.

Reginald picks up his pokeball and places it in a special pocket labeled 'Gifts', and prepares to give the pokeballs to random trainers who don't have them. Pokemon theft has become slightly more prominent after the Team Plasma incident, and this is his way of countering that. With a triumphant whistle, he walks out of the tower and begins searching for a trainer...-

Chandelure: ... Lampent?

-Lampent floats motionless above the place where Elgyem was caught.-

Chandelure: <Lampent, sweetie, let's go h—>

Lampent: <Shut up, mom. Just... just shut up.>

Chandelure: <Lampent... Lampent you can always talk to me about it...>

Lampent: <Mom, just... just shut up okay? Mom, you told me not to come crying to you when my best friend in the entire world got caught, so... so I'm respecting your wishes and I'm not going to come crying to you about how my best friend got caught.> -her voice begins to crack-

Chandelure: <Lampent, I—>

Lampent: <Go away.>

-The Chandelure eventually complies, looking back at her daughter constantly as she floats down the stairs. The Eldest Beheeyem teleports away, leaving the Lampent alone. She absentmindedly wonders if Fire Types can even cry in the first place.

And then she finally realizes that Elgyem isn't hiding behind a tombstone anymore, and cries.-
