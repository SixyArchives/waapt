Unite: <Wait, are you...texting?>

Unite: <What the-?>

And then, with a final lurch, a small ball of light emerged from the device, floating up into the air and hovering briefly before shifting into the form of an hummingbird composed of abstract shapes.

Ad Min the Porygon Z: BEEP! Start-up complete. Good afternoon, sir! How may I help?

Unite: <Oh, I forgot about you...>

Luke: “Ad, I need you-”

He pointed at the control panel.

“To hack this control panel and shut down the power core so I can grab that fancy looking stone in there.”

He then pointed at the Key Stone inside the core chamber.

“You up for it? Be warned, they'll be someone else on the other end that'll try and kick you out.”

Hovering in mid-air, the digital hummingbird spent a few seconds running some potential scenarios through his C.P.U.

Ad Min: My official documentation advises against the use of my model as a hacking tool. But on the other wing...it also advises against cracking my O.S and installing homebrew software upgrades.

Luke sighed.

Luke: “You don't have to tell me, Ad. I knew I was voiding your warranty when I used that Dubious Disk on you. I decided it was worth the risk.”

Ad Min: I'm not stating it was a bad thing, just...

Do know that you'll have to pay for a replacement if things go wrong?

Unite: <Wow. Do all computers think they're expendable like that?>

Ad Min: Affirmative. Running program: easyhackr.exe.

Bridge - 1:12 to Cannon Fire

An alarm sounded as a large message appeared on the display screen.

Foreign Program detected! Hacking in progress!

Sinclair raised a metaphorical eyebrow as he turned to back to the security feed of the Gun Deck. How were the human and the bird possibly hacking their systems?

The answer was that the human appeared to have summoned some sort of digital bird. The name of it escaped him for the moment, but Sinclair knew that – in recent times – the humans had advanced enough to produce a fully artificial Pokémon. Mainly used for working with computer systems, but competent hackers in a pinch.

But he was better.

Taking one of his claws, he plugged it into a slot under the control panel and closed his eyes as Sinclair accessed the computer systems directly...

Gun Deck – 1:11 to Cannon Fire

Ad Min: Hacking 90% complete. Shutting down Wave Motion Cannon Power core...

Off to the side, the electrical arcs that were holding the Key Stone in place began to weaken. However, as he continued his job, Ad Min suddenly jerked violently, taking Luke and Unite by surprise.

Unite: <Whoa! Did you hit a fire-wall?>

Ad Min: You were correct! There's another presence in here with me. He's interfacing with me and...

The robotic Pokémon faltered, dumbstruck by what he was feeling.

By the coder! This mind, it's so vast! I...I feel as if I'm a mere light bulb trying to outshine a star! It's...too much for my RAM to process!

The bird winced as he tried his hardest to complete his task while fighting against the presence on the other side.

Oh, it's on purpose! He's merging my...subroutines with his own to cause...a memory leak...urgh! Systems...slowing to...unacceptable levels! Shut...down...ini...ment!

Luke: “There has to be something we can do! Quick! Open the task manager! Control + Alt + Delete! CONTROL + ALT + DELETE!”

Unite: <Is that helping?! WHAT DOES IT EVEN MEAN?!>

Ad Min continued to wince, seemingly in pain as he tried his hardest to finish hacking the door with what little memory he had left.

Ad Min: 98%...99%...

His virtual body flickered worryingly, on the verge of shutting down.

...100%...SEGMENTATION FAULT.

As the beams died away, the bird drooped in mid-air, then destabilized. The anti-gravity projector that had been holding him aloft shutting off and allowing his component parts to fall to the floor, de-activated. Not as well versed with computers as Luke was, Unite peered down at the pieces, more than a little horrified.

Unite: <Is...is he dead?>

Luke shook his head.

Luke: “No, no! He's fine. We just need to turn him on again.”

Turning to Luke, he gave the trainer an annoyed look.

Unite: <Really? With all that fuss, he sounded like he was legit dying!>

Luke: “He's fine, I'm sure. Now quick!”

He pointed at the now silent core, the Key Stone that was held in place by the electrical field having fallen to the floor of the chamber.

“Get that thing out of there before whatever kicked Ad Min out turns it back on!”

With a smirk, Unite held up a wing, his feathers taking on a familiar metallic sheen.

Unite: <With pleasure!>

Bridge – 1:09 to Cannon Fire

As he pulled himself back to reality, the Metagross smirked. The so called “Porygon-Z” had held on stubbornly, but the sheer enormity of Sinclair's accumulated memories – stretching back thousands of years – had been too much for the human construct to handle. Even if it had manage to shut the Wave Motion Cannon's reactor core off, it was just a simple matter of turning it back...

ALERT! ALERT! WAVE MOTION CANNON CORE BREACHED!

Sinclair: <What?!>

Gazing at the screen, the Metagross found that the Braviary had shattered the thick glass that held the keystone core in place, allowing the human to gingerly pull it out, leaving their leverage against Goldenrod City powerless.

<No! NO!!!>

Gun Deck – Cannon Fire suspended

Luke found a Key Stone!

The trainer held the Key Stone carefully in his hands. It was still the form of an unrefined ore chunk, but once properly forged, there would be just enough to make a proper key stone for him.

Unite: <How does it feel? It's not burning your hands, is it?>

Luke: “This stone...It feels powerful just holding it. Chills down my spine, man.”

Unite: <Huh. Okay, we did what we had to do. Mission Accomplished! Let's get out of here before->

DING!

The two turned to the back wall as the elevator opened to reveal it's occupants: A few of the False Pokémon from upstairs.
