Chords of Steel

Holon City

-Bishop has now dismissed himself from the PEFE branch, and is now wandering the city, alone and looking distinctly morose. He only begins to brighten when he hears a voice call out to him from a nearby rooftop.-

Kirk: <Hey, Bish!>

-Kirk waves to Bishop, then hops down from the roof and lands in front of him.-

Kirk: <So, those scientists finally managed to get Samus and that Sneasel girl fixed, did they?>

Bishop: <Indeed they did...>

-Bishop sighs, and Kirk tilts his head at the Scyther.-

Kirk: <Something wrong?>

Bishop: <Not particularly, no... at least, it is not an issue where others are at fault. Rather, it is something that has been beginning to bother me for a while, and I feel that I should discuss the problem before it manifests itself again. Would you mind if I were to share the matter with you?>

Kirk: <Er... no, not at all. After all, you've helped me out with my problems loads of times, I figure it's only fair for me to try and help you out at some point. What's bothering you?>

Bishop: <Well...>

-He sighs, and looks down at his feet.-

Bishop: <...I am concerned about what will happen to me should we return to Orre.>

-Kirk tilts his head in confusion.-

Kirk: <...What, are you worried about being Shadowed again?>

Bishop: <No, that is not the crux of the issue... though I will admit that I am terrified of meeting such a fate again. It is the thought of what will happen to my psyche even without Cipher's intervention that gives me cause for concern.>

Kirk: <...I don't follow.>

Bishop: <Allow me to elaborate. There was a situation at the laboratory yesterday prior to my departure - you will forgive me if I omit the details, since it is not an event I particularly wish to recount - and it caused me to lose my temper. I grew angry, and used threats of violence. I came to my senses soon afterwards, thankfully, and provided a woefully inadequate apology... but not only am I deeply ashamed of myself, this event has also caused me to wonder what will happen should I return to an environment where such violent urges are all but actively encouraged.>

-He starts to pace up and down.-

Bishop: <For the entirety of the last year, I have been attempting to suppress base instincts such as these. I have always maintained that diplomacy, not violence, is the optimal way of solving problems, and I have done my best to maintain a pleasant and affable demeanour. And yet if such a minor event can drive me to a level of anger where I am tempted to forsake my every effort to be civilised and controlled, if the restraints I put on myself are really so weak... then what will happen to me when we return to a land with no restraints whatsoever?>

-He pauses, then gives another sigh and looks back up at Kirk, who shrugs.-

Kirk: <I wouldn't worry about it, if I were you.>

Bishop: <...I beg your pardon?>

Kirk: <Well, you've always been able to keep your temper under control until now, haven't you? You only ever get mad at me when I actually deserve it. And whatever happened must've been pretty damn infuriating if it managed to make you of all people lose your cool.>

Bishop: <Still, I should apologise again once I get the chance... my response to her remarks was both disproportionate and->

Kirk: <Bish, you don't go apologising to people when they deliberately go out of their way to wind you up! If I were in that situation, I'd show them who they were dealing with by kicking their teeth in. That'd teach them a lesson.>

Bishop: <Yes, but that's your default response to most situations.>

Kirk: <Look, nobody thinks that you have anger management issues. If anything, your problem is that you're too polite. You're always calm and well-mannered and treat everyone well, even when they're walking all over you. One of these days you're going to have to learn to stand up for yourself, Bish. Not everyone is as infuriatingly nice as you are.>

Bishop: <...Whilst you may have a point, I would rather allow myself to be walked over than deliberately cause conflict and confrontation.>

Kirk: <And you've just managed to summarise the issue in a single sentence. Anyway, if this is bothering you so much, then why not just avoid ever returning to Orre? I know I don't have any plans to revisit that Arceusforsaken desert.>

Bishop: <It's... well, you see, some of the other members of the J-Team have discussed the possibility of visiting the region in order to conclude our business with the Cipher-sponsored scientists, and I fear that these plans may soon become a reality.>

Kirk: <And who said you had to follow them there, exactly?>

Bishop: <Well... no-one. But it is undoubtedly what Herbert would've done, and so I intend on following in the group's footsteps wherever they go.>

Kirk: <Why are you still so concerned about "what Herbert would've done"? Herbert's gone. You're your own mon now, Bishop. You're free to go wherever you want.>

-Bishop stares at Kirk in almost-horrified disbelief.-

Bishop: <...Kirk, you and I both know that is not true. We are still trained Pokemon. Once Herbert returns, we will be->

Kirk: <If Herbert returns, you mean. Face it, Bishop, he's been gone for almost two months. He's probably dead by now.>

Bishop: <KIRK!>

Kirk: <What? He's a frail fifteen-year-old kid - well, sixteen-year-old by now - who's disappeared off to who knows where with no Pokemon and no supplies. I wouldn't give him a week. And that's assuming that the flash didn't drop him straight into Stark Mountain or just simply disintegrate him.>

Bishop: <But Maul says->

Kirk: <Yeah, Maul says he's fine. But how do we know Maul isn't just pulling that fact out of his ass? For all we know this is all just his idea of a sick joke. Look, if Herbert was still alive, then surely he'd have found some way to get in contact with us over the last two months.>

Bishop: <...>

Kirk: <Look, I know you liked the kid, and I did as well. But it's time to move on. We're free, Bishop. It's up to you to decide where you go from here.>

-With that, Kirk turns and walks away.-
