Rex: But the othe- ... *decides to just go along with things* Well... I've been with my starter, Rascal, for a long while. You see, it was back when I was five years old that I first met him. See, I wasn't really too familiar with all of the sports games that the other children in my neighborhood were used to playing, so I was always left out. So I'd hang out at the Game Corner and watch the slot machines. This lady gave me some coins once and let me play. ...I was really bad at it. *recalls memories of crying like a baby and some of the other patrons giving him some of their coins just to shut him up so they could go back to playing* But eventually, I...err, won enough coins.

Flashback: Goldenrod City: Game Corner, a long time ago

Little Rex wandered up to the counter, looking at the various prizes on the wall. His eyes shone with wonder as he bit his lip, trying to decide what to get. On the right counter there were some strange items that looked like CDs with different colors. He wondered what they were for. Under those was a shelf filled with various items. There was a scarf, some kind of clock...thingie, and two different kinds of magnifrying glasses.

The woman at the counter smiled down at him, watching him stare at the prizes. "Oh, you probably don't want these," she said, a motherly look on her face. "These are for trainers. They won't be much fun to use if you don't have a Pokemon."

"But...I dun have a Pogeyman..." Rex whined, looking sad. He had all these coins, but couldn't do anything with them. He looked like he was going to cry again, his lip trembling a little.

"Oh, don't cry," the woman cooed. "We have some rare Pokemon as prizes." The boy blinked the tears out of his eyes, looking up at her. "You do?" he asked, looking hopeful.

She gestured to the shelf behind her, nine Pokeballs behind her. In three were those funny Pokemon that he would see around the city teleporting places. They always looked like they were asleep and were really weird. Two others had a cute armadillo looking Pokemon, and three others had purple, wiggling snakes. But the last one...

"Oh, you want this one?" The woman asked, picking up the ball. "It's the last one we have in stock. It's called Eevee. It's a long way from home, so I'm sure it'd be happy to have a friend." The little boy blinked and stared into the ball, locking eyes with the puppy-like creature. "Ee...vee?"

He placed his coins on the counter so that his hands were free and took the ball, letting the small creature out. The brown Pokemon looked at him curiously, its eyes locked on his face. "Hewwo..." Rex lisped, smiling and reaching out his hand. "Do you...want to be my fwiend?"

The Eevee stepped forward and sniffed his hand, looking up at him curiously. Suddenly, it pounced onto his chest, giving his cheek several playful licks. The little boy giggled happily, petting the Pokemon. "Oh...cut it out...you wascal..."

End Flashback

Rex: Fortunately, I grew out of the lisp, so I don't sound like Elmer Fudd anymore.
