455089 Eskay646th May 2015 04:57:41 PM from Hoenn Get RP Mod , Relationship Status: Longing for my OTP
Musical and stuff

Fortree Pokemon Center

Vierr was bored. Incredibly, insanely, impossibly bored.

Back in Holon City, he got bored incredibly easily. Before the J-Team came around, he rarely ever got any chances to visit other places; his dad, who had come to Holon from just north of Kalos, was a scientist researching the mutative properties of the environment. He was never home.

On the other hand, his mom was at home frequently, but she never had any urges to travel. In fact, she had a fear of traveling beyond her home city. Considering Holon City was a safe zone otherwise surrounded by killer cats and a forest notorious for leading those who enter it astray, one couldn't blame her. But for a guy like Vierr, staying cooped up in one city for years was painful.

One thing he was fond of was folklore and mythology. You'd be surprised how much he reads, but whatever book he might have in his hands, nine times out of ten, it had to do with myths and legends. That's what initially attracted him to stray into the forest for the first time, in fact — after discovering his Psychic powers and learning that he could use them to understand Pokemon, he eavesdropped on a conversation about StarClan, by some of the Clan mons patrolling near the city borders. Although he never would admit it now, those cats are the reason his chosen patron Legendary happens to be Mew — the "Starkeeper".

Even before that, though, his mom told him stories about who she claimed was their ancestor. A person, gifted with the powers of Pokemon by a Legendary he'd managed to free from confinement. With these powers — the Auric Spectrum, incidentally — he rose up to become a king, or as his subjects thought, a god amongst mortals. He taught his people how to harness this power on their own, although no student of his managed to learn any more than just one element. Not even his children — a son and a daughter — inherited any of his abilities. However, according to Vierr's mother, he knew his unique combined powers would show up again, after many, many generations of being lost. These powers, she told him, were proof that Vierr himself was destined for greatness. Or something like that.

Of course, that was the version made for kids. The happy ending version. As Vierr became more detached from the people he was stuck with for years, he looked into the story on his own time. As it turns out, the full story involved the king becoming a heartless dictator, only ever concerned with himself. Needless to say, Vierr wasn't happy to find that little detail out.

Anyway, one of the king's most talented students grew weary of his selfish deeds. They tricked him into drinking from a poisoned cup. Although he quickly purged most of the poison from his body using his incredible psychic power and promptly executed the traitor, the poison prevented any of his kin from inheriting his abilities. In fact, he ended up stabbing his first son when he found out he had no Auric potential.

Now, anyone who kills their own firstborn child is bound to have his regrets. In fact, upon his daughter's birth, he realized he'd been wrong in blaming his son, when it was really all the poison's fault. In memory of his lost son, he kept a locket with the son's image embedded in it. Unfortunately, as the years went on, he lost the ability to use his powers one by one — first his Dark Obscura, then Ghost, then Aura. At this point, he had become so bitter and paranoid that he didn't want to let his throne be passed down to his powerless daughter, so he instead decided to pass on his crown to the one student of his that he knew he could trust — a Psychic.

Of course, Vierr knew this was all folklore. Even when the Ghost Lord spread the rumors of pan-Auric power as a prize for uniting Ransei, the story of the king was mostly considered little more than a fairy tale. Besides, if he was really descended from this king, his abilities would be more than they actually were; years of believing the story and trying to stay inspired that he could fend for himself without companionship by Pokemon eventually jaded him.

But it never killed his love of lore. In fact, at this very moment, he's reading about Hoenn's legends, right there in the lobby of the Pokemon Center. He doesn't even seem to mind the Sableye rummaging through his bag.
