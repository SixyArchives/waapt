435725 PippingFool25th Dec 2014 08:27:54 PM from Sydney, The Vivid City Get RP Mod , Relationship Status: Getting away with murder
Less than Three

Kalos, sometime before Festivus celebrations

It's a rather slow and relaxed day at the Al-Gala S.A Headquarters in Lumoise City. Most of the salarymen and white-collar workers have cleared out their office spaces and taken leave to spend time with their families for whatever festivities they celebrating during this wintery time of year, though there are still a few staff lingering about doing odd jobs. However, for one particular vertically challenged lady briskly walking down the beige and pewter hallways of the 23rd floor, it just seems like an average work day.

Cleo: Granduncle has summoned me to his quarters. He must have information of the utmost importance to tell me, though I wish it was after Christmas celebration so I could spend time with my family without having to worry about future business prospects...

Cleo stops behind a large and grandiose cedarwood door. She tentatively dancers her fingers on the elaborate door knob, but instead of entering it right away, she leans forward and presses her right ear on the door.

???: I believe we had a deal, ustaaz*. Our bid was unmatched during the whole auction

There is a small amount of static and mumbling that can be heard.

???: Al-Gala wishes to branch out. Hoteliering will always be our main business, but that doesn't preclude going into other business...

There is a very short chuckling sound.

???: Grande. I knew you would see it my way. Bring over the paperwork tomorrow morning we can at last finalise the sale of-

Cleo knocks on the door Before pressing her ear up against the door again.

???: We'll continue this talk later. Ma'a as-salāmah**

The sound of a phone clicking back into place can be heard, as the deep voice in the room bellows out to Cleo:

???: You may enter.

Cleo slowly turns the knob on her door as she pokes her head into the room.

Cleo: Khalu***? You wished to talk to me?

The man behind the desk turns around to face his grand niece and gives her a warm, hearty smile.

Grand-Uncle Ibrahiem: There you are, my darling morning and evening star.

The man is not very tall, only reaching about 5'1 in height, though half of that has to do with age shrinking you down from the massive height of 5'3. Though what he lacks for in size, he makes up for in mass, as he is a very round individual with a belly like a big beach ball and a plump face with two rosy cheeks. His brown eyes are like the shiny carapaces of a beetle, the teeth of his jolly smile are stained yellow, either years of drinking strong teas and coffees or from years of chewing tobacco (probably a combination of both), and his hairline is spotted with a few strands of jet black amongst the greys and the whites. His rotund body is clothed in white robes so pristine it's almost blinding (obviously he pays his dry cleaners well).

Cleo enters the room and closes the door behind her. She walks over to her granduncle, who gives her a big, warm hug.

Cleo: Hello Granduncle. I am good, better than I have been...

Ibrahiem gently caresses Cleo on her cheek.

Ibrahiem: I know what it's like to lose a loved one, my child. But you are as strong as you are clever. Some wounds never fully heal, but they do get better with time.

Cleo smiles solemnly

Cleo: Thank you khalu.

There is a short pause.

Cleo: Is there any particular reason you summoned me here khalu?

Ibrahiem smiles back at Cleo before looking out his window.

Ibrahiem: Indeed, my little lotus blossom. You see, the recently acquired strains in Hoenn (how fortunate that a group of rapscallions made it easy for us to get a foothold in there at a most opportune time~) are starting to experience a slight dip in confidence due to rumours circulating around about an impending disaster. My job for you, my child, is to go an manage some of our hotels in Hoenn for a time until the uncertainty blows away with the changing winds. Are you up for that challenge, my dear Cleo?

Cleo stands up straight and bows.

Cleo: I will take you up on the challenge, khalu. When should I be departing for Slateport?

Ibrahiem gives another warm smile to his grandniece.

Ibrahiem: After the winter celebrations and holidays are over. I wouldn't want to pry you away from celebrations with our family. I know how much it means for the both of us.

Cleo: Will you be there khalu?

Ibrahiem: I will make an effort. It's one of the few years I am in kalos for the festivities and Maryyam would be very... ornery if I didn't make an effort to celebrate with my favourite branch of the family.

Ibrahiem gives a small laugh as he rustles Cleo's hair, who smiles in return.

Cleo: Well I would be happy to have your company khalu

The two smile and laugh at each other once more before sitting down on two large and comfortable chairs. Cleo ponders for a while, before turning to her elderly Granduncle.

Cleo: Khalu?

Ibrahiem raises an eyebrow, but doesn't turn from his paperwork.

Ibrahiem: Hmm?

Cleo: Hoenn... That's where the Mauville Corporation started right? They were a pretty big energy and development company back in the day - you even did work with them khalu - whatever happened to them? You never did tell me you know...

Ibrahiem sighs and turns around, twiddling his thumbs in his hands.

Ibrahiem: Mauville, eh?

He stares out the window again.

Ibrahiem: Mauville were an... interesting... company to work with. To give credit where it's due, they were always on the forefront of development - and always had lofty and visionary ideas for the future - but I cannot recall anyone who worked at that place who was ever happy. Their drive to be the top energy producer in Hoenn lead them to doing a lot of dirty, underhanded tactics. Their rivalry with Devon had their run their enterprises like sweatshops and their idealistic future communities were far to lofty and grand to ever make a reality and that bankrupted them - after a series of scandals levied them out of public favour. Now we businessmen stare the bones of what was once a great corporation as warning signs for what could happen to us.

Cleo: But uncle, we aren't exactly the... cleanest... company out there. Are you sure what happened to Mauville. Co won't happen to us?

Ibrahiem looks at Cleo and laughs.

Ibrahiem: You have nothing to worry about my lotus blossom. What will happen to Mauville will never happen to us.

His brows furrow.

Ibrahiem: I've made sure of it.

Clao: ...I see.

Cleo stares out of the window with her uncle.

Ibrahiem: Call you father and tell him that we'll be home for the holidays. It's time that both of us took a rest.

Cleo: I like that idea uncle. I'll do that.

Ibrahiem: Even big business needs to sleep. Sometimes.

Both share another laugh as Cleo bids her uncle farewell and leaves the room to call her family. Ibrahiem takes out a tin of chewing tobacco and begins to chew on it - contemplating on the conversation he and his grandniece just shared.
Translations:

*Ustaaz = Sir
**Ma'a as-salāmah = Goodbye
***Khalu = Uncle
