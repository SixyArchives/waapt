UTINNI!

Hooks Ship

Kabutops: [Yar! I never be surrenderin' to...]

As the the pokemon began to protest, but Penny made a counter-argument by pressing her stingers as close as she could against the pokemon's throat without actually attempting to puncture his hide.

Kabutops: [Though, 'Never' is bein' such a strong word...]

Penny: [A wise decision.]

The Beedrill turned, still keeping her stingers pointed at the pokemon she had forced into surrender as she attempted to contact Jack.

Penny: [Puny human of the capture transport mechanism!]

Jack, hearing his Beedrill speak, let out a heavy sigh. Runt was currently being carried in his arms as he moved to the pokemon's position, already regretting actually responding to the pokemon's 'title' for him.

Jack: "What?"

Penny: [I have subdued this prisoner. He is now my minion! I order you to capture him and make it official!]

Jack glared at the Beedrill, then glanced at the Kabutops she had "captured", then glared at Penny again.

Jack: "I'm your frikken trainer, you stupid Beedrill! You don't get to order me to do anything! Especially not when you resort to calling me a 'puny human' like all I'm good for is carrying around Pokeballs."

Penny glared at Jack, then attempted to rephrase her words.

Penny: [I request and recommend you officially capture my new minion.]

Jack: "He's not your minion, Penny."

Penny: [Is too.]

Looking to avoid making the petty argument worse, Jack turned his attention to the Beedrill's prisoner. He had to admit - not that he'd do it out loud, that Penny may have had a good point about capturing the pokemon, even looking past the fact that he was a pirate and Jack's incredibly lax capture efforts.

For the Pokemon she had 'captured' was a Kabutops. A highly rare specimen that was typically considered to be extinct. Whats more, it was half water-type, which even with it's scythe-like limbs would seriously help fill a gap in his team he'd recently been lamenting the absence of.

Jack: "So you surrendered to her?"

Kabutops: [Aye. She be bestin' me and leave me with nary another option.]

Runt: (Telepathically) Oh joy. He even talks like a pirate.

That would be another case in favor of capturing the creature. The whole pirate them made an already rare pokemon even more of an oddity, what with the eyepatch covering his right eye, the double belt that ran both around his waist and from left shoulder to right hip, the ragged pants, and odd-looking boots. Truly he was a sight that he was believing mostly because he was seeing it.

Jack: "So you've agreed to join us?"

Kabutops: [Yar, that be true.]

Jack: "You have a name?"

Kabutops: [Aye! They be callin' me 'Lefty Mc Stabbington'.]

Jack: "What? For real?"

Lefty Mc Stabbington: [Aye! See, because I be relyin' on me left eye to see, and me arms I be usin' to be stabbin' things. Which I be very much enjoyin'.]

Jack hesitated as he considered the situation, then shrugged and decided he'd just go with it. In order to ensure the capture would take place as quickly as possible, he brought out one of the many Net Balls he still had in reserve from Penny's capture attempt, then let it drop onto 'Lefty'.

Jack: "Well, I did figure I could use a water type..."

In a flash, the net ball absorbed Lefty in a blur of light, and sure enough, the pokeball hardly even wiggled before it confirmed the pokemon's capture.

Jack: "Welcome to the team, 'Lefty'."

Runt: (Telepathically) I really hope this isn't something you regret later.

Jack: "Yeah, me too."
