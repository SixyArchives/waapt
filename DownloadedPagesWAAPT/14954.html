Less than Three

Snowbelle (present time)

Zilah is sitting at the same table at the same cafe as the one where she had her encounter with Alex and Mikki a few days back. It seems that she is almost on the verge of tears at this point.

Zilah: ...-Damned brother of mine. Why does he always have to go off on these dangerous jobs that Cleo asks him to? Can't he just learn to say no? Of course, he's much to kind and gentle to say "no", the duffer...

Zilah looks down at her Holo Caster and sighs, seemingly resigned to the fact that Lawrence is missing.

Zilah: Best telling maman and papa now, probably file a missing persons report while I'm at it. Though I'm sure that won't draw any unnecessary attention...

Despite saying this. Zilah herself doesn't seem all that motivated to call her parents. Idly fiddling with the empt glass on the table.

Not far away from the cafe, however, a slightly dazed and confused Lawrence is moving about. Trying to see if he can find anyone familiar faces. It seems that his time at Slenderhouse has left him quite shaken to say the least.

Lawrence: -Softly- Ung... Erm Hello? Any one around?

Lawrence turns towards the direction of the cafe, and promptly does a Double Take as he spots his sister.

Lawrence: ZILAH?

Lawrence's statement of slight shock was loud enough to catch Zilah's attention. She lazily looks over to her brother before performing a Double Take of her own.

Zilah: LAWRENCE!?

Before Zilah could react, Lawrence was already embracing her in a tight hug. Seems that his time away has made him slightly more protective of the people he cares about.

Lawrence: Don't worry Zi, I'm back now. Everything is going to be okay, I promise.

Zilah: Oh Lawrence. I'm just glad you're here. And alive for that matter.

Lawrence: And I intend on stating that way.

The two siblings continue on the hugs on the hugs for quite some time, with Zilah being oddly affectionate towards her elder brother who she normally belittles and teases.

After the hugs, though, Zilah punches Lawrence clean in the stomach. Causing Lawrence to lurch forward and make an undignified sound in response.

Zilah: Lauf nicht wieder so weg, du Arsch! You had me worried to bits, you did!

Lawrence: -After regaining his breath- Good grief Zilah! Punching your older brother in the stomach after he's been missing for a week Really now!?

Zilah: Just be thankful I didn't tell maman and papa that you went missing or otherwise there would've been hell to pay. Beat Where did you run off to anyway?

Lawrence: ...I had somewhat of an existential crisis.

Zilah: Oh really now? I bet you and Al could have long faux-philosophical discussions about it. Since he seems to have one every other month.

Lawrence: -Sigh- Zi, you really do need to curtail the belittling of your siblings, especially your younger ones.

Zilah: No can do, it is my duty as the eldest sister of the family

Lawrence rolls his eyes, but the pain in his stomach sharpens and he sits back down at the table, waiting for the pain to subside.

Zilah -Placing her fingers over her mouth- I think I hit you a little to hard Lawrence...

Lawrence: You think?
