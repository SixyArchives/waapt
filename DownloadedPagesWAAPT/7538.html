I don't know if this post is an ending or not, but it's something.

Somewhere near Azalea Town - Johto

On the outskirts of the swampland just off the Route network between Azalea and Goldenrod, a Dragonite swoops down and lands carefully on the edge of the tree-line, his Feraligatr passenger stepping off carefully.

Gazing ahead at the thick, marshy forest ahead, the aforementioned Pokémon gulped nervously, a tell that didn't escape his Dragonite companion.

Stratus: <What's up? You didn't get airsick, did you?>

He shook his head, even though his stomach was tying itself up in knots in fear at that point.

Lake: <No, it's not that. I'm just...nervous.>

This caused the Dragonite to give him a quizzical look.

Stratus: <Nervous? What have you got to be nervous about?>

Lake frowned as he began to twiddle his claws awkwardly, shifting his gaze at the forest to glance up at a rather interesting cloud in the sky.

Lake: <Well, - if I'm right and they are here - I'm about to meet my family, who I haven't seen for ten years. That's a really long time to go without seeing somemon.>

The reptilian Pokémon frowned at the dragon, a frightened look in his eyes.

<What if they don't remember me? What if they do, but they've all moved on with their lives? What if...what if they don't like me?>

In an attempt to reassure his friend, Stratus flaps over to Lake, giving him a reassuring smile and a gentle pat on the back.

Stratus: <I don't see why you would need to worry about any of that.>

Lake couldn't help but look doubtful.

Lake: <And how can you be so sure?>

The Dragonite simply responded with a cheerful smile.

Stratus: <Because I don't see how anymon could ever forget about you, buddy. I most certainly wouldn't, you're just way too memorable.>

The Feraligatr simply blinked in response.

Lake: <But me and you have been together a lot longer than I spent with my family, I didn't have as much time to leave an impression on them, and even then...>

He gestures a claw at his own body.

<I was just a little Totodile back then who barely knew anything about the world. Look at me now - I've evolved twice since then on the outside, and after all I've been through, I bet I barely resemble that hatchling they all might remember.>

Stratus:' <...Hmm.>

The Dragonite raised a paw to rub the scales on his chin thoughtfully.

<You raise some good points, but...>

He raises his arms and shrugs.

<Does it really matter? I bet your family are gonna' be so pleased to see you again that they won't care what you're like now! Plus it would be kinda' silly to expect to not see a Pokémon in ten years and expect them to not have changed in either body or mind.>

Lake took a few seconds to reflect on this, before nodding in agreement. He had to admit that what Stratus was saying made sense.

Lake: <Okay...but...what if they don't approve of me?>

The Dragonite frowned, tilting his head quizzically.

Stratus: <What do you mean?>

Lake: <What if they don't approve of the life choices I've made? What if they don't like that I have - or rather, had - a trainer? What if they don't like how human I act? What if...they don't like that my mate is a Dragon or that I'm associated with Ho-Oh or->

Stratus: <Whoa, whoa!>

The Dragonite waves his paws, motioning for Lake to stop.

<Calm down! You're just getting yourself all worked up!>

Lake opens his mouth to protest, but the Dragonite raises a paw and gently closes it shut, silencing him.

<Just relax, okay? I find it hard to believe to disapprove of any of that. I mean...if I was your Dad, don't you think I would be pretty pleased to not only have my long lost son return, but to find out that he's a done so many amazing things in his life?>

The Dragonite takes his paw off Lake's snout, allowing him to answer.

Lake: <...But we don't know that for sure. We have no idea what any of them are like. And...>

He glances nervously at the swamp nearby.

<...I'm not sure if I have the guts to find out.>

The cream colored Dragon frowns.

Stratus: <Even after all you've been through?>

The Feraligatr nods, glancing back at the him.

Lake: <Saving the world is easy. But family? Family is terrifying!>

Taking a moment to think back on his own family, Stratus was reminded of the frighting visage of his own father, the viscous, disapproving Gyarados who's approval he had never been able to earn, leading him to exile him away from his home and the rest of his family.

He couldn't help but shiver at the thought.

Stratus: <Urk...you don't have to tell me.>

Calming himself down by thinking of something else - namely the fresh Magikarp he had packed for lunch - the Dragonite turned his attention back to Lake.

<Look. You're gonna' be fine, trust me.>

Lake responds with a look akin to that of a young Pokémon visiting the Pokémon Center for their first set of shots - wondering how they could not possibly worry about something that had been described in a way that seemed obviously painful.

Lake: <I dunno'. Maybe we should come back another day. After all, I really wanted to do this with Nadia, she's not back yet and I promised we would do it together. Besides, I think Luke wanted to do something with me later and->

Pausing to let out a deep sigh, the Dragonite marched forward, grabbing Lake's arm and pulling the Feraligatr along with him with some effort.

<Hey! I'm not ready yet!>

Stratus: <Yeah, well putting this off isn't going to help! So we're gonna' go in here and get this over with, okay?>

He was about to object again...but stopped, choosing to give the Dragonite a pitiful look instead.

Lake: <Since when did I become such a Scaredy-Skitty?>

Stratus: <Because you're right - family is terrifying.>
