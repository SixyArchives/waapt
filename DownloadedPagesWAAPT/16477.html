Less than Three

Secluded Area in Vien Forest

Salvador is once again in a distant area in the Vien Forest, down on his knees in prayer. However this time, it appears he is not alone as a figure cloaked by the shadowsof the trees is by his side. After a minute of silent prayer, Salvador looks up at the opening in the canopy at the moon and the stars filtering their light in the forest night.

Salvador: Yveltal give me strength, what am I to do? I am torn between what I have percieved and what I wish to do, is it really a futile task considering the past and future have been revealed to me since I was young?

At the moment, what looks like a Clock Punk prosthetic arm and gently rubs Salvador.

???: You are torn between fate and will, my child. An issue that plagued your father for many a moon all those years ago.

Salvador: Oh what do I do? I wish to save him from despair, but fate does not deceive when revealed...

???: What if it's possible that your thoughts and visions are being influenced by... something else? As if a demon is influencing your mind to deceive you...

Salvador makes a noticeable sound of distress as he clutches his head.

Salvador: I want nothing to do with demons, I've had enough of them! I know how they ate away my father 'till he was nothing more that a breathing corpse and I wish not to repeat those same mistakes, but are we cursed to forever be consumed by sin?

???: ...

The figure turns Salvador's head towards their own, removing the hat and the mask that adorn his face.

???: Salvador, I believe you can rise and ascend from the faults of the past generations. Your father taught you well. After all, he wanted you to go farther than he ever could. If anyone can change things, it's you my child.

Salvador: Thank you, I would be lost without your wisdom [REDACTED].

???: (Chuckling) That is what I am here for. To guide you in your father's place.

The figure looks up at the sky. Salvador joins him.

???: I see a lot of similarities with between you and him. But yet, lots of differences too. Love is one of the most powerful forces we know of, and it transcends even life and death.

The figure looks back at Salvador

???: As you know too well, my child.

Salvador looks back to the figure, smiles, and resumes stargazing.

Salvador: ...Tell me about the stars again...

The figure nods knowingly, and starts going over the stars and constellations in the sky. Salvador in retaliation, lies back and listens to his partner go on about the celestial skies. A nostalgic yet solemn smile marking his face illuminated by the moon's faint light.
