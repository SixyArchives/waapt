133236 Crewe29th Jan 2012 02:28:24 PM from Gravity Falls, Oregon Get RP Mod
Li'l Punk

Several years ago, in a small suburb of Veilstone...

A young girl, about four or five years old, sits cross-legged on a carpet inside a well-lit living room. On her shoulder perches a young Pidgey, and she's surrounded by wrapping paper. Books and other various gifts surround her, and on the table in the kitchen lays a birthday cake, waiting to be eaten. This girl has short, dirty blonde hair—the same color as her mother's, who stands at her shoulder with a Pidgeot by her side, and bright blue eyes, which match those of both her parents, but are trained on only one of them—her father, who stands in front of her. The family's pet Nidorina is behind him, sniffing curiously at what he holds behind his back.

"C'mon, Dad!" the girl complains, bobbing up and down with impatience. "What's my last present?"

"Well..." the man kneels down in front of her, his hands still behind his back. "This one's from Nicole."

Before the girl can wonder what kind of present comes from a Nidorina, her father has placed a tiny, baby Nidoran-M on her lap. A green ribbon is tied around his neck in a massive bow, and he makes soft mewling sounds, pawing at her lap and sniffing. The girl gasps and picks him up softly, then giggles and kisses his nose.

"He's so cute!" she squeals softly. The Pidgey on her shoulder leans over and makes an inquiring chirping sound. The Nidoran turns towards him and makes a "rawr" sounds.

"Oh, Cole likes him!" the girl says happily, as the Pidgey nudges the Nidoran with his beak before settling back onto her shoulder.

Placing the Nidoran back onto her lap, the girl pats the Nicole as the Nidorina pads over to sniff at her baby.

"What do you want to call him, sweetie?" the girls mom asks, leaning down over her shoulder.

"Oh! Um..." the girl looks down at the baby. "How 'bout... Ion! 'Cause he's little and purple!"

Her parents glance at each other, perplexed, but say nothing. Her dad places a hand on her shoulder.

"Well, when little Ion grows up into a big Nidoking... You can go anywhere."

As the little girl stares in awe at the baby, her mother kisses her on the top of her head.

"Happy birthday, Ashley."

Present Day, Hearthome

The girl, now about fourteen, stands in the outskirts of town. Her dirty blonde hair now reaches her shoulder blades, and is tied up in a braid. By her side stands a Nidoking and a Pidgeot, both looking relaxed. She absently scratches the Pidgeot between his wings, watching people move through the streets idly. Behind her, a Fearow nests on the fence, and a Scyther stands rigid beside him.

"Y'know, guys..." the girl says, breaking the silence. "You're awesome. You know that right?"

The Pidgeot makes a trill that almost sounds like a chuckle and affectionately nudges the girl's shoulder. The Nidoking shifts from one foot to the other and murmurs under his breath. The Fearow hunches his shoulders and frowns, while the Scyther remains unresponsive. The girl turns to them.

"No, really. I mean it. All the work we've done, in my book, it's all thanks to you. Yes, even you, Domino."

The Fearow looks startled to be addressed and hastily tries to compose himself, nearly falling from the fence. The girl moves over to him and pats his beak, then scratches the Scyther between his horns. "And you, Six."

The Pidgeot hops over to her and nudges her, chirping urgently.

"Yes, yes, I know we should move on, Cole..." she says, stroking his crest. "I just wanted to let you know how important you guys are to me. After all, today's the anniversary of the day I got Ja—Where's James?"

The four Pokemon all look around, then start making noises varying from chirps to hisses to rawrs.

The girl sighs. "He's gone missing again. This is getting ridiculous. C'mon, guys, let's split up and look for him."

With the comfort of long practice, Cole and Domino take off into the air and fly in opposite directions. Six darts into the woods. The girl climbs onto Ion's shoulder and he starts walking off, both calling out James's name.
