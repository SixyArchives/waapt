 62569 Crewe, Mon, 27th Jun '11 6:31:26 PM from Johto Get RP Mod
All right, I'll do this before my dad makes me go to bed, because I can't use my iPod because my mom took it on a college tour with her despite it only having my soundtrack music and a few songs. Sorry. I already dealt with this earlier.

-Yesterday-

-walks into the Pokemon Center after the match and unbuckles my Pokeball belt. I hand the nurse Hecate's Pokeball-

Would you mind just doing this one first?

Nurse: Sure.

-she heals Hecate and hands her ball back to me. I hand her the other five-

I'll be back in a minute to pick these up.

-holding Hecate's ball tightly, I walk back outside and take a deep breath before releasing her-

Hecate: <Joy. I figured this would—>

Shut up, Hecate.

Hecate: -eyes widening slightly- <You—>

-sarcastically- Yes. I dare tell the almighty Hecate to shut up. Just like you tell me to shut up every single time you enter a battle field.

Hecate: <Oh, this is about me not letting a child tell me what to do.>

Partly, yes. I'm sick of you ignoring all commands in battle.

Hecate: <I don't need your commands. I'm an excellent battler.>

There's more to battling than just strength, Hecate! You may be a powerful Pokemon, but have you noticed that every time you enter the arena you're knocked out because you're too busy telling me to stop giving you orders?

Hecate: <Well excuse me for not wanting to be a slave.>

-clenches and unclenches my fists-

It's not slavery, Hecate! It's supposed to be a partnership. As in, working together! And if you remember correctly, you agreed to join my team!

Hecate: <I was promised a chance to become stronger! I was trying to to make the best of the situation! I don't recall agreeing to be caught! You came after me.>

Oh? Let me refresh your memory. I only had Lydia and Weavile with me, along with Anom's team, against your entire pack. They were fighting for their lives across the clearing. You shouted something that I didn't understand at that point, and then you attacked me. Remember that? You charged me. The only thing I had to defend myself with was an Ultra Ball.

Hecate: <Regardless. You didn't release me directly afterwards.>

Partly because you were dangerous and a menace to everyone around you, and partly because you agreed to join my team. But this isn't just about you not following orders.

Hecate: <Then enlighten me. What else is this about?>

You. Just... you. Pretty much everyone dislikes you to some degree, and with good reason!

Hecate: -gives an exaggerated sigh- <This is about that Yanmega, isn't it?>

No! Well, not for me. I doubt anyone is ready to forgive and forget yet, but that isn't the only problem! The problem is you! You're nasty, arrogant, unpleasant to be around, and you look down on everyone and everything! And I'm sick and tired of it! You won't even use your ability because you think it's below you!

Hecate: <It is below me. What is there that's good enough for me to try to look like?>

How about every single other one of my Pokemon? They're better battlers than you are, treat everyone better, and are generally just better Pokemon than you could ever hope to be!

Hecate: -eyes narrowed- <I could destroy any one of those fools in battle.>

-sarcastically-

Oh, really? Just like you beat that "lummox" earlier?

Hecate: -laughs dryly- <Ah, now we're getting to the point. This is about the battle.>

-takes a few steps forward until I'm nose to nose with her, angry-

You lost the match for me! She had a single Pokemon left. You had the power to beat it. And then you decided you were too high and mighty to bother attacking, and was knocked out in one shot! I was counting on you! My entire team told me not to use you. I wanted to give you a chance. But I've been giving you chance after chance since I caught you, and you've blown every single one!

Hecate: -growls- <So what? You'll release me? Box me? Because I lost a single match?>

This wasn't just any match! It's not like this is some gym, where if you lose you can come back as often as you like and ask for a rematch! This is a tournament! I don't know why I was invited to this thing, but this was my chance to prove that I am a good trainer, and it was right for me to run away years ago to become one! And you've ruined it, because you're too stuck up to realize that maybe you aren't the most powerful Pokemon to ever walk the Earth, and a trainer can help you in battle! And I swear if you keep leering at me like that I am going to slap you.

Hecate: -looks somewhat taken aback- <I—>

Shut up. Just shut up. I'm sick and tired of you. Your attitude, your disobedience, everything about you.

Hecate: <... You said nearly everyone dislikes me. I assume that includes you.>

-glares at her for a minute, then takes a step back, leaving about a foot of space between us-

While I could never hate a Pokemon that's battled for me, however badly it turned out... Yes. I dislike you.

Hecate: -softly- <So you're releasing me?>

-looks at the empty Pokeball in my hand-

I'm giving you a choice.

-tosses the Pokeball towards her. It lands at her feet, unharmed-

I'm going to get the Pokemon I can trust from the Pokemon Center. I'll return in five minutes. You can either destroy the Pokeball and release yourself, and we'll never have to see each other again, or you can recall yourself.

-pauses for a second, then looks her in the eye-

If you recall yourself, you're making a promise. No more idiotic disobedience, no more arrogance, no more disappearing randomly and forcing me to hunt you down, no more of the old Hecate. This is your last chance.

-turns around-

I'll see you in five minutes. Maybe.

-walks into the Pokemon Center-
