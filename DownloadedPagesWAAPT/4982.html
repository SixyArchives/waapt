 124540 Crewe, Wed, 28th Dec '11 12:41:41 PM from Johto Get RP Mod
After brief conversation with a certain young Kadabra, and a few mis-teleportations, Hecate finally stands where she thought she would never again stand—in front of the imposing forest she is once again preparing to call her home. Taking a deep breath and shaking her hair back, she enters the dark trees.

Taking the shortest route she can remember, and hearing nearly inaudible pawsteps shadowing her the entire way, she soon finds herself in a familiar clearing. Straightening up in the center, she waits patiently for the Zoroark to appear out of the shadows. Several leer and growl, and a few give silently accusing looks. Finally, a young female steps forward. Hecate looks her over slowly.

<Ah, Rosaleen... So I suppose you became leader? I must admit myself surprised.> One corner of her mouth twitches in a brief half-smile.

The other Zoroark bristles.

<What do you want, Hecate? Where's your Trainer?> She spits out the word like a curse, and smirks at the older Zoroark's flinch.

<The human is gone, > Hecate snarls. <I have returned for my rightful place as leader.>

<Rightful?> Rosaleen screeches, circling Hecate. She darts in with a Night Slash, which Hecate whirls to the side to avoid. The younger Zoroark resumes pacing as if nothing had happened. <You're weak, Hecate!>

<If you truly believe so, then says so with your claws, not your lips. Though you never were good at either, my dear Rosaleen, > Hecate snarls, flexing her own claws.

Rosaleen cackles again, and this time several of the watching Zoroark join in. <Oh, no one doubts your battling skills, Hecate. But you're still weak, in your heart and your mind.> She fires a Focus Blast, which Hecate stops with a Protect screen. <You looked after those runts>—this mention of her adoptive children elicits another snarl from Hecate—<Not to mention this is the second time you've apparently been spurned by a Trainer!>

More of the surrounding Zoroark break into laughter, and a few chime in to the conversation.

<Fool you once, Hecate!>

<Go back to the humans!>

<You won't betray us again!>

<Yeah, we've learned from your mistakes, even if you haven't!>

<Hush, please, my friends. I believe this a challenge for leadership, and your interference is not appreciated.> A quiet voice cuts through the crowd, and Hecate's eyes immediately shoot to its source, spotting an elderly male, who gives her a small salute.

<Thank you, Darwin...> she says calmly, turning her eyes back to Rosaleen. <This was a different circumstance; I joined the team willingly, and for a while I believed... Regardless. I have learned that even the best humans are truly evil. I will not make the mistake again.>

Rosaleen throws her head and laughs again. <You said "best", Hecate!>

<ENOUGH!>

Rosaleen's laughter is quickly cut off by a large Focus Blast that she has to dive out of the way to avoid. She looks up, her eyes wide as a bristling, snarling Hecate advances on her.

<Rosaleen, I am challenging you for the leadership of the pack. And that challenge is carried out with claws, not voice... As you seem to be constantly forgetting.>

<That's not the only thing the outcome of the challenge is based on!> Rosaleen gasps, scrambling to her feet.

<No...> Hecate begins circling the younger Zoroark now, snarling. <But as we are both... former... leaders, the pack has already accepted both of us at some point, and we each have a member to vouch for our competence.>

<Oh? Who will vouch for the old bleeding heart?> Rosaleen snarls, regaining her composure.

The elderly Zoroark steps forward.

<I will vouch for Hecate. She led us well for years; and she will not abandon us again, I am sure.>

Hecate smiles and gives him a sweeping bow.

<Once more you earn my gratitude, Darwin.>

He salutes her. <Please, take it as a testament to our longstanding friendship, my dear.>

Hecate smiles once more, then turns to Rosaleen.

<And yours?> she says. <Preferably one to match Darwin's status as elder.>

Rosaleen turns to the watching Zoroark expectantly. <Well?>

There's a pause and some shuffling as they mutter amongst themselves. Finally, another Zoroark with gray streaks in his mane steps forward.

<Rosaleen has led us well in your absence, Hecate, > he says. Rosaleen smirks at Hecate.

<So your father is the only one to step forward for you, Rosaleen? Well, Corbin is certainly an elder...>

More muttering sweeps through the watching Zoroark as they try to figure out if this is meant as an insult or compliment, until finally Corbin raises one hand, giving both Zoroark in the center of the clearing a level gaze.

<Both of you have a pack member to vouch for your competence as leader. Very well, then...>

Darwin steps forward to stand besides Corbin and finishes his statement.

<... Let the challenge begin.>

BATTLE!

Zoroark Leader ROSALEEN vs. Former Zoroark Leader HECATE
