Hermit Jarod's House

"...Spit it out."

"Rex! Spit it out /right now/!"

A newly traumatized Valorie was spit onto the ground, the impact sending a wave of pain shooting through one of her arms. The tiny girl attempted to push herself to her knees and failed, her right arm refusing to support weight. She grabbed at the ground with her left, still shivering from the shock.

"D-Damn... I thought I heard a girl scream, but I didn't think—Hey, heeey... Hold up now." the man said, attempting to be gentle with the small, clearly slightly hysterical girl. "He's just not used to people. You're fine..." he continued, prompting Valorie to prop herself up and scoot a bit away again, shifting onto her butt and wiping her eyes clear of saliva so that she could get a good look at the strange man talking to her.

He was a middle aged man with a hell of a beard. His clothes very primitive; She could tell he'd made them himself, including the hat they shadowed his eyes constantly. As the overwhelming stench of dragon halitosis began to fade she noticed that he probably hadn't taken a bath in some time either. She shuddered, attempting to lift her right arm and just sort of whimpering as a shot of pain ran through her.

The man cringed. "...Alright, that doesn't look good. Here, let's get you inside and dried off." he remarked somewhat sadly, moving to help Valorie up and lead her inside.

...

Valorie finished thoroughly drying herself off with a towel, a thoroughly unamused frown on her face. She /would/ have appreciated a nice, thorough bath right about then... however a part of her feared what she might find there.

"So how are you feeling?" the man asked from nearby.

"...My arm really hurts." Valorie responded in a lackluster tone.

The man sighed and began working with some cloth. "I'll fix you up something." he assured her, attempting to keep her calm. "It's been a long time since I've had company here." he remarked idly as he worked. Valorie declined to comment. She was, admittedly, not happy about nearly becoming pokefood for hid pokemon. ...Not happy at all. The man came back with a crudely fashioned sling for Valorie's arm and began helping her put it on, ignoring Valorie's whining as he moved her arm for her. "Juuuust a moment... There. Now doesn't that feel better already?" he asked half-heartedly. Valorie shrugged, equally half-hearted.

"...Listen—Uh. I don't feel comfortable with you running of in the night. You might not get spit back up next time a pokemon goes at you, and I'd hate for a lovely little lady like you to spend the night in some monster's belly." the man began in a lackluster tone, averting his eyes. "I know Rex kind of roughed you up back there, but I want you to stay here tonight. Now that he knows you're not food he won't bother ya' anymore. Maybe I can grind you up something to help take te pain away... What's your name?"

Valorie looked up at the man. "Valorie." she replied simply, still not quite on anything resembling buddy buddy terms with him. As much as she was glad he hadn't let Tyrantrum eat her, she did kind of blame him for her being attacked in the first place.

The man smiled down at her. "The name's Jarod; I haven't really had company over for a long time, so I apologize for the mess. ...I'd offer you the bed, but I don't think you'd appreciate that." he remarked, earning raised brows from Valorie in response.

"Nice." Shu added sarcastically, causing the man's face to pale slightly.

"...That yours?" he asked, clearly unnerved by the ghostly echo of a man's voice he'd heard. Valorie nodded. "He's mine... I think he saved my life back there." Valorie responded, starting to drop her grudge. Staying mad took energy!

"Why did your Tyrantrum try to eat me?" she quickly added, pointedly pouting at Jarod. The man was put on the spot. He wasn't used to this pouting children thing. It was so... difficult to resist! He just wanted to turn that frown upside down right then!

"Buh—...Well, he keeps me nice n' safe. Y'see, we didn't think we'd ever really have any visitors out here, let alone little girls..." the man hurried to explain.

"...I'm not that little..." Valorie protested lamely. She attempted to fold her arms, only to realize that this very much hurt and take a rain check on that action.

"Aaah I didn't mean it like that. You're just so...—Nevermind. Point is, I'm what yeh call a hermit. A... long time ago I chose to live away from the rest of society, you're quite a long ways from home by the way, and I just didn't think anyone'd come across my humble home is all. That n' Rex doesn't like listening to me. He's got a mind of his own, he does." Jarod went on to explain, nodding in satisfaction at his own words.
