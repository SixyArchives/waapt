It was fine. She'd be in much more perilous situations than this. Like that time she had gotten a whole flock of Murkrow after her, and she only managed to survive by jumping down an open manhole into the sewers. Where she was then almost eaten by a Muk.

She was confident, at least, that she would come out of this not smelling like human defecation for a week.

It wasn't like she had a choice anyway, as she rubbed her quietly grumbling stomach. Sure, the sun's rays could sustain her, but without actual food, only for so long. And food was becoming hard to come by.

Do or die, as they say. And given the choice, it was always do.

Quietly as she could, she crept over to the mound of food. Which was easy for her, as lizards didn't make much noise when moving.

Raticate: <...Snrk!>

She froze suddenly as the Raticate brushed her whiskers in her sleep, then rolled over onto her side.

<...Zzz...>

With her heart now beating a mile a minute, the little lizard continued creeping towards the food, closer and closer...until she was finally next to it. If she had been capable of drooling, her mouth would be salivating at the veritable riches of half eaten food lying before her. French fries, half eaten burgers, bits of candy. To most people, it was a disgusting mess. To her, it was an all-you-could eat buffet.

Guarded by a Pokémon with fangs that would likely decapitate her with a single bite.

Swings and roundabouts, eh?

But she'd done well so far. She just had to grab enough food so as to keep her going for a while without waking the rat Pokémon up.

Carefully, the lizard Pokémon grabbed a few morals of food, keeping one eye on other Pokémon as she did so. Some fries, a small bit of a burger, a handful of jellybeans that looked fresh. Wait...was that a candy bar?

It was rather deep in the pile, though. Carefully, she grabbed hold of the chocolate with both hands, and slowly slid it out, like one would remove a block from a perilously stacked Jenga tower. Luckily, it was soon free without trouble.

Now with enough food to keep her from dying any time soon, the lizard turned around and crept away, luck seemingly on her side that day.

The truth was, it wasn't. As that bar of chocolate had been load bearing...

SPLAT!

<SQUEAK!>

Frozen in her tracks, she turned around to find a collapsed pile of food, and a very angry Raticate bearing her fangs at her.

Wild Helioptile (Lv.27): <...Florp.>
