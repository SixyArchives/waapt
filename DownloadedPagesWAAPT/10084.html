Shadow Wastes - Five Years Ago...

No matter what she tried, Comet just couldn't land a hit on the shadowy mockery of a real Charizard. Her Embers were either easier dodged or nullified by a Protect shield. Attempting to get physical lead to her being grabbed and slashed by it's claws, thrown off to the side hard, or a Flamethrower right to the face.

As much as she really really didn't want to admit it, it was looking like Comet wouldn't be able to defeat it in battle.

Charmelon!Comet: //////////

<Eurgh...>

Breathing heavily, Comet clutched her side, wincing in pain from all of the fierce attacks she had taken from her so called fear. While the Charizard in question had barely taken any damage at all. It was now simply staring at her with a smug look.

Shadow!Comet: //////////

<Do you feel it now? The doubt inside of you?>

She didn't bother to respond, deciding to stare at the ground instead. Taking this as a sign of weakness, the Charizard chuckled evilly.

<You think you're so tough, don't you? Well it's clear that underneath it all, you're utterly pathetic.>

Comet winced again, not from the pain that the Charizard had inflicted with it's claws, but with it's words.

<I was right. You're just a worthless little whelp, aren't you? Not cut out for the Dream Guard at all.>

The words stung like a Beedrill, constantly jabbing at the raw flesh of her psyche. As much as she wanted to deny it all, to believe that she was tougher than this...she just couldn't.

<Look at you. You've let everyone down. How do you think your friends and family are going to feel when you tell them that brave little Kindle failed her last exam? They're going to be so crushed.>

Plodding over to her, the Charizard craned it's neck down so that they were face to face.

<And it's all. Your. Fault.>

Comet glared back at the Charizard, but then looked away, shutting her eyes tight to try and avoid any tears from leaking out. Not that she had much luck.

Now satisfied with it's work, the Charizard turned around and began to walk away, but stopped, turning back to face Comet briefly.

<Try an easier career next time. Like cooking. Perfect for a worthless whelp like you who couldn't win a fight to save her life.>

Snickering evilly to itself, the Shadow Charizard dissolved into the darkness, leaving Comet alone in the middle of the broken land.

Shadow Wastes - Five Years Ago...

The Charizard was right. So right. Comet had been boasting to everyone about how she was going to be on the Dream Guard for certain. She had promised so much, and now? She had totally failed to deliver on her promises.

How would she face Latalo? Her friends? Her parents, even? What would they say when she told them that she had failed right at the end?

Quivering in fright, Comet buried her head in her claws. She couldn't do it. She wouldn't be able to face their shock, their sadness, their disappointment. She just wanted to run. Run away from it all. Get away where nobody could find her. Where she wouldn't let anyone down.

...Maybe she could do that.

She remembered what her Mother had told her once. Of a magical set of doors that lead to another world entirely. Where the Dreamers that crossed over to their world when they slept came from. A world whose rules were consistent and logical, rather than bending to those who were strongest.

To Comet, who wasn't at all thinking clearly at that point, it sounded just fresh start she needed.

Wiping a few tears from her eyes with the back of her arm, Comet conjured a portal to these doors, paused for a moment to glance back the way she came, back to where Latolo was surely waiting, with a sorrowful expression on her face. With a sad sigh, she turned around, and jumped through the portal.

The Doors of Dawn - Five Years Ago...

The chilling winds of the wastes gave way to the gentle warmth of the morning sun as she emerged on the other side of the portal, finding herself in front of a magnificent pair of doors, built into a large golden sun made of amber crystal, gently lit by a blue-orange sky stuck in an endless sunrise.

Comet always liked sunrises, and witnessing one - even in what was the middle of the day for her - calmed her somewhat, allowing a brief sense of rationality to emerge.

Was running away really the right idea? Was she really willing to throw her life away just from the harsh words of her own sense of doubt given form?

But what was left for her here in the Dream World? All she had wanted was to join the Dream Guard, but that desire had fallen apart in her hands just moments before. She had no idea where to go from here.

Whereas the gates beyond lead to a new dawn - a chance to start over. To leave her shattered dreams behind - and everyone she had disappointed behind.

Maybe it was a bit extreme. The logical side of her was practically screaming at her to turn around, to go back and explain what had happened, to do anything but to throw her life away in one go.

But if her logical side had won that day, then there would be no story to tell.

Steeling herself, Comet cautiously approached the gates, only to be halted by the two Pokémon guards.

Sunburn the Combusken: <Hold. If you pass through here, you will never be able to return to this world outside of your dreams.>

Solain the Solrock: <Are you sure you wish to pass through, young Charmeleon?>

One last chance. Would she stay or would she go?

She hesitated just briefly, knowing that she may never see her friends, her family ever again after stepping through the doors. For a moment, she entertained the thought of going home...

But the terrible thought of their disappointment stabbed her like an icy knife, the cold feeling of fear chilling her to the bone.

There was no going back.

Comet: <Yes. I'm sure.>
