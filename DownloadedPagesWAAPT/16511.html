Pueltown Hotel, Alan's Bathroom

Alan opened the green backpack.

There are many steps to completing the look.

He had this process memorized.

Starts with the shoes. Black high-tops, red along the sides.

He thought to himself as he went step by step, putting on each individual piece as he came to it.

Black socks. Red jeans with a black belt.

He worked from the bottom up.

Now above the waist. Black shirt with a red pleather-lined jacket. As tight as it looks, it allows for mobility.

He drew out a long, red scarf. He stared at it.

First time I've had a partner in a while.

He closed his eyes as he wrapped it around himself. It felt familiar, comforting.

He looked deeply into the mirror, at the blue eyes that only reminded him of the past. He removed a pair of colored contacts.

These weren't brown. These were a bright red.

He stared again in the mirror. For all anyone knew, these could've been the eyes he'd had since birth.

He covered his hair under a red beanie, which reached down to his eyebrows.

The Scarlet Rogue.
