-A flickering hologram raises its hand.-

-The Captain turns his gaze to a man to his left, his face almost completely obscured by the lens flare of his enormous, shiny glasses.-

The Scribe: Hm. No. Nothing.

The Strategist: What if we're dealing with a group of highly organized Pokémon? That would explain the lack of human scent.

The Engineer: They'd need one hell of a personality to keep a group that big together. Plus, why would a group of Pokémon run off with Hildr? She's just a Phanpy.

The Lorekeeper: Do not forget, the Crown is missing as well. Pokémon stealing things is far from unheard of, citing the tails of the Ninety Nine Zoroark, and its quite possible that Hildr caught sight of the thief and ran in pursuit.

The Captain: So, that's the prevailing theory. However, we don't know enough about this group to come up with an operating procedure for when we make contact.

-The Captain hits a button on his console.-

The Captain: Now, the thieves are apparently heading north, which means...

-A map of Holon comes up, with three lines going north, ending in different points, but all starting at Joulant Town.-

The Captain: We need to think of where they're going next. Here, we have our route so far. Here we have the route Hildr made obvious. This one is the route we can confirm the thieves took.

-A mess of lines pop up where the thief-line stops.-

The Captain: So, theories? Wild guesses? Radical hearsay?

-The group murmurs for a moment while the Lorekeeper stays silent.-

The Lorekeeper: ... I think we all know where they're going.

-The hologram points a flickering finger at one particular line. Its terminus reads, "Holon Ruins". A figure at the end of the table, who had been silent the whole time, stands up. He is decked in a black military jumpsuit with orange highlights.-

Lucanus: Erm... well, of course. She is most amicable company, and I find her to be rather pleasant to be around. And as a diplomatic partner, she has been most helpful by allowing Illusio to support-

Revan: ~You know perfectly well what I mean, you bloody imbecile! I've been looking after you since you crawled out of your mother, I know that you're not as stupid as you look - though it's a pretty damn convincing act, I have to say! Now, if you don't mind, I'm going to ask one last time, nice and loud, to get the question through your thick, brainless skull! DO YOU HAVE FEELINGS FOR AIURA?~

Lucanus: Um...

Revan: ~Well?~

Lucanus: Yes.

Revan: ~What was that?~

Lucanus: Yes.

Revan: ~Speak up, for Arceus' sake! I can't hear you!~

Lucanus: Yes! The answer's yes, alright? I confess I might have been harbouring certain... feelings.

Revan: ~You don't have to explain that to me. I know perfectly well that you haven't just been harbouring them, you've been dry-docking them, refitting them, giving them a good coat of paint and scraping the barnacles off their bottom.
