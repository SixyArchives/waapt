Dragonspiral Moat - Dragonite Caves

One shift later, and Stratus and Nacre had taken their case to Alto to see if they could part the knowledge of using bubbles to protect oneself onto the Dragonites of Dragonspiral.

Alto: <So if I understand this correctly. You know a move...>

Stratus: <Yes.>

Alto: <Like Substitute.>

Stratus: <Yes.>

Alto: <But instead you blow a bubble to protect yourself?>

Stratus: <It's just what it sounds like.>

The elder Dragonite stared at the two for a few seconds, leaving them somewhat awkward.

Alto: <...This has to be a joke.>

Nacre: <But it isn't, sir! I've seen it for myself! It's foolproof! Bulletproof! Everything proof!>

Sighing, Alto crossed his arms, an unamused expression on his face.

Alto: <Forgive me if I don't quite believe you. After all, there's a reason the tower our Gods call home is built from bricks and mortar. Not soap bubbles.>

Stratus: <Aww, come on! Don't you trust me, sir?>

Stratus dropped to his knees, assuming the begging position. Finding it somewhat pathetic, Alto sighed.

Alto: <It's not that I have no trust in you, Stratus - after all, you did somehow find a way to keep everymon in the cave well fed with little time or effort - but that doesn't mean I'm just going to follow you blindly. On anything but culinary matters, at least.>

Since Stratus had introduced the Ancient Dragon to the miracle that was pizza, his tastebuds would go to the ends of the Earth and back again for him. But the rest of him was not quite so trusting.

Nacre: <Well how about we give you a demo then? Show just how good it can be?>

Alto: <If you're capable of showing me yourselves, drakes...>

He sat back in his rocky chair, interested.

<By all means.>

Nacre: <Very well. Stratus! DO THE BUBBLE THING!>

Stratus: <YES MA'AM!>

Inhaling, Stratus once again blew himself a bubble to hide inside.

Stratus did the bubble th- uh, used Bubble Shield!

Leaning forward in his seat, Alto rubbed his chin in interest at the sight of Stratus enclosed completely in a bubble.

Alto: <..Alright. I'll admit I'm somewhat interested.>

He frowned.

<But you do look rather ridiculous. How would any foe take us seriously if we approached surrounded in bug green bubbles?>

Glancing down, Stratus fiddled with his claws nervously.

Stratus: <If it helps, different Pokémon make different colours...>

Nacre: <Hey! We haven't got to the good part yet!>

Flapping her wings, Nacre flew up into the air, put some distance between her and the bubbled Stratus...

<Just watch this!>

Stratus: <Huh? Wait a minute! Are you using Dragon Rush? Because I think that's a->

Nacre used Dragon Rush!

<EEP!>

Nacre shot forward and rammed the bubbled Stratus hard, launching him backwards...

<WAAAAAAAHHHHH!!!>

Until he hit the wall, bouncing off it and rebounding in a different direction. Alto and Nacre watched, rather perturbed, as he pinballed around the room.

Alto: <Is this supposed to happen?>

Nacre: <To be fair, this isn't as likely to happen in a more open space.>

Stratus: <SOMEBODY STOP THIS THING!> @_@

The Dragonite kept rebounding until he hit a rather sharp stalagmite on the floor of the cave. Bursting his bubble and dropping the Dragon to a floor, leaving him dazed, but otherwise unharmed.

<...I'm okay...>

Alto turned his attention back to Nacre, rather unimpressed by this display.

Alto: <Bit of a design flaw, no?>

Nacre: <But you saw it take that Dragon Rush, no? And you know how powerful that attack is!>

He had to admit, she had a point there. Dragon Rush was one of the most powerful of Dragon moves. So if it could take that...

Alto: <...I suppose it wouldn't hurt to give it a try. It never hurts to have a wide variety of moves, after all.>

Rather weakly, Stratus raised his arm from across the room and gave a claws-up.

Stratus: <...Cool...>
