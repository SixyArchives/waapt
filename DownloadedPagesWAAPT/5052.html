Fortree City: Pokemon Center

The sound of flowing water ceased, steam wafting out from under the bathroom door. The muffled sound of a door sliding open came from the bathroom, followed by the padding of wet footsteps. From several more moments there was only silence, until Rex emerged at last, fully clothed.

Rex: ...Oh, hey, guys. I didn't mean to wake you.

He wandered over to Rascal and Pierce giving them both a pat on their heads. The two Eeveelutions purred happily, the younger one pawing at his hand gently before pouncing onto his father. Rex smiled, watching them both play around before wandering over to Grey.

Rex: Morning, Grey.

Grey: <Salutations, my homo sapien comrade.>

Rex: *blink*

Nodding awkwardly, Rex looked around for his last teammate, finding him over on the table by his Pokegear.

Rex: Hey, you aren't trying to eat that, are you, Sulfur?

The Gulpin turned to him, holding up the device to his trainer.

Sulfur: <You have some messages. o3o>

Rex: Oh, I do? Well, thank you for letting me know.

Picking up the device, Rex browsed through his folder, checking his inbox for anything important.

Rex: Let's see...Bugsy caught a Skorupi, one of Whitney's Trainer's Meowth just had a litter, Lyra wants more screentime, mom says I should pick up Yuki, and Felix says that Dahlia could use some air too...and to call an ambulance for him?

Blinking at that, Rex strapped the Pokegear to his wrist, shrugging in confusion.

Rex: Well, if we're going to be picking up two new members, I guess I'd better find Fang and Breezy so we can get it sorted out who will leave and who will stay.

His team nodded, each of them rising and stretching to prepare for the day. Then, letting their trainer lead the way, they headed for the Center's kitchen.

Fortree City: Treetops

The upper path of Fortree City was peaceful, a few Tailow chirping, some Wurmple roaming, and a Skitty mewling. A few citizens went about their way here and there, purchasing a few items from some stores and chatting with their neighbors.

However, no one seemed to be willing to go near the Secret Base Shop due to the odd tendency for people to trip and go sprawling over the bridge and into the bushes below upon exiting. The shopkeeper was very distraught, unsure of what to do. Inspection of the bridge showed no holes, and the structure of it was set up well enough that it wasn't a loose vine causing the tumbles either. So what could it be?

Of course, one creature knew. And it was the one causing the accident.

Kecleon: <Kekekeke; stupid humans~>

The chameleon Pokemon snickered to himself, delighting at all of the mischief he was causing. It sure was funny watching them trip over themselves, even if they did end up kicking him in the process. Still, it was worth it seeing them look all goofy afterwards. And best of all, he didn't even get in trouble for it!

He was just congratulating himself for his achievement when he noticed someone else walking closer: a girl in red clothing. Stifling his chortles, he sat quietly, waiting for her to get closer and inevitably go falling over the edge. Hehe...this would be great.

Girl: ... ... ...Alright, the spot should be right about here.

The Kecleon blinked in confusion, looking over at her. What was she doing? Was she another person inspecting the bridge?

The girl smirked a little, turning to the spot in front of the shop. Then, she put her fingers to her lips and gave a shrill whistle. The Kecleon blinked in confusion, wondering just what she was doing.

Then it suddenly began to rain, the water beating down on everything. The Kecleon grumbled at the feel of the rain beating down on his skin, finding it very uncomfortable. He'd probably have to put off his tripping act for a while until it died down. What a dra-

Girl: I see you.

The Kecleon blinked, noticing that the girl was looking directly at him. Looking down at himself in alarm, he noted that his shape was made completely visible in the rain, beads of water dripping off of his form.

Kecleon: <...Uh oh.>

Grinning, the girl pointed a red device on her wrist at the Kecleon, launching a little disc forward at him. As it spun around, little blue lines of energy began to circle him, a strange feeling welling up inside of him. Unsure of what the item was, the Kecleon tried to flee, only to find that his legs would not move. Looking down, he noticed that the water on his body had begun to freeze over, inhibiting his movement.

Girl: It's no use. Glaze's assist won't let you get away.

Unsure of what the girl was talking about and not really caring, the Kecleon struggled and groaned against the ice, trying feebly to escape. Then there was a sudden flash of light, the Kecleon's camouflage melting away. He felt...oddly at peace, no longer trying to escape. He noted absently that the rain had stopped and that the ice around his legs was also gone. The girl smiled, walking over to him and patting his head.

Girl: Now, be a good Pokemon and go home, okay? No more pranking.

Nodding agreeably, the Kecleon about-faced and dashed for the trees, heading home to his family. As he left, the disc flashed again, a Minun appearing next to the device. From some nearby bushes, a Lopunny and Vaporeon also emerged, the rabbit Pokemon's long ears making her aware of everything that had happened on the bridge.

Clover: <Mission success?>

Kim: Yep; now let's let the shopkeeper know what was going on.
