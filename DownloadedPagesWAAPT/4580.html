Mikan Island: Pokemon Center - Rental Room

Wordlessly, Rex entered his room, not even bothering to turn on the lights. He shut the door gently behind him, kicking off his shoes and crawling atop the bed. With some simple adjustments, he curled up under the covers, detaching his belt and Pokeballs and setting them on the desk next to the bed. He pressed each of the buttons, releasing his Pokemon into the room.

Rex: Feel free to hang out. I'm turning in early.

Without another word, Rex turned over in bed, covering his head with a pillow and slowly but surely drifting off to sleep.

Mikan Island: Pokemon Center - Rental Room, some odd hours later

Rex: ...

Rex found himself awake some time later, staring up at the ceiling with a grimace on his features. He sat up in bed, looking around to see what his Pokemon were up to. At the foot of the bed, Rascal and Aqua were curled up, as they usually tended to do. Gaea was sleeping in the middle of the floor; Rex wasn't really sure how she found cold stone comfortable to sleep on, but whatever made her happy. Krystal was curled up on a plush chair in a corner of the room, snoring lightly as her illusory abilities made the air ripple around her. Zippy was laying next to a wall, her lightning-bolt tail resting on the nearby wall socket; mentally, Rex made a note to move her later before she caused a black-out.

And Alfonse was sitting next to him, staring straight ahead, his wings folded in front of him.

Rex: ...Is something wrong?

Alfonse: <I could ask you the same.>

Rex: ...I'm fine. Nothing is wrong.

Alfonse: <...>

Alfonse tilted his head in Rex's direction, giving as much of a smile as something with a beak could do.

Alfonse: <It's almost impossible to lie to an empath, you know?>

Rex: ...

Alfonse: <You're still upset about earlier today?>

Rex: No... ... ...yes... ... ...

Rex sighed, running a hand through his hair in agitation.

Rex: I can understand Silent going to hang out with his Pokemon; that's fine, I do the same thing too. But it's just...

Alfonse: <You thought he had ignored you?>

Rex nodded, his shoulder slumping. His face seem wearier than usual, stripped of its usual happiness and zeal.

Rex: I hate being ignored... In all honesty, Alfonse, when I heard that everyone left us...I felt hurt. I mean, I can understand that I'm new and that I don't really fit in yet...but it wasn't the first time that I'd been left behind.

Alfonse: <You are referring to the smoothie bar?>

Rex: ...Not just that.

He drew his legs to his chest, looking down at the sheets sadly.

Rex: After I was attacked by the Sharpedo...Lian said that she ran into Star. I thought that she would at least be worried about how I was doing, but she wasn't at the hospital when I woke up. I thought that after I helped her with the whole Shinx thing and found her Pokemon...

Alfonse: <I'm sure it's not personal, Rex...>

Rex: I know...but it still makes me feel horrible. And...that's not all...

Alfonse: <?>

Rex: I made the suggestion to Luke to take Pleo...the baby Lugia, back to his father. I thought we'd go together, since I wanted to see Moltres...but I got left behind then too.

Alfonse: <But you warded off those Deltas that attacked the island.>

Rex: Well...good did come out of it...but it's still kind of disappointing that I didn't get to meet it...

Rex sighed, leaning back against the bedrest and staring up at the ceiling.

Rex: I don't even know if I'm worthy of their attention, in all honesty. They're a bunch of champions and world-savers. I can't even get eight badges from my home region...

Alfonse: <...So, what will you do?>

Rex: I don't know. I honestly don't know, Alfonse. They are nice people...but I feel like I'm just not in their league.
