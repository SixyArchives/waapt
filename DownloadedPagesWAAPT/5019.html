Bravely Fashionaaabluh

Ever Grande City - Cliff

A gentle breeze blew through the mountains of Ever Grande, the flowers and shrubs shaking gently as they were caressed by the sky's tender kiss. On land, a few Zigzagoon scurried across the ground, seeking shelter from the cool winds. In the water, several Magikarp huddled around the city's waterfall, making valiant attempts to climb it. It was a well-known legend among both man and mon that a Magikarp who could climb a waterfall would grow into a powerful Gyarados; as a result, several of the creatures were frequently found around the falls, and tonight was no exception. However, tonight they had a witness to their deeds.

Sidney: Not bad! Not bad at all, little guy!

There was a strum of an electric guitar, a mostly bald man dressed like a rock-star standing at the top of the perilous cliffs. As another Magikarp tried and failed, he strummed the guitar again, the chord resonating throughout the night air.

Sidney: Try again, little man! You can do it!

He ran his fingers along the strings, the silence in the air broken by the sudden stream of rock music.

Sidney: They said that Magikarp was a pathetic excuse of a Pokemon! They said that it was too weak to survive! But they also said that I would never be anything!

He began to work himself into a frenzy, his playing getting more and more energetic.

Sidney: But here I am! An Elite! One of the greats! Even if I am the lowest ranked, that's more than most other trainers can say!

He clenched his eyes shut, his hand flying across the strings.

Sidney: Let's show 'em all, Magikarp! Let's shatter all of those illusions they have about us!

???:    Talking to yourself again?   

Sidney's stopped mid-note, the hairs on the back of his neck rising as he felt a presence behind him. He was fairly sure he felt the temperature drop a few degrees, though it may have just been his lack of a jacket that caused him to think so. He turned, setting his guitar to the ground as he faced the woman behind him.

Sidney: Oh...hey, Pheebs. You gave me a start.

Phoebe: Oh, surely you're not scared of little old me. I thought you Dark-type users loved the night and all of its    surprises   ~

The girl stalked forward, her skirt (or whatever the hell that thing was supposed to be; damned if anyone else wore it) barely moving despite the motions and the breeze. For that matter, it didn't even seem like her footsteps were making noise. Just how on Earth did she do tha-

Phoebe: My eyes are up here, Sidney.

Feeling his cheeks heat despite himself, he tore his gaze up to her amused expression.

Sidney: Err...sorry about that.

Truth be told, Phoebe always kind of made him uncomfortable. Not just because she managed to outrank him despite his blatant type advantage, something that he still got flak about, but because she was so darned...creepy. Attractive, definitely. Absolutely...but creepy all the same. As if reading his thoughts, Phoebe's smile widened, and, unless he was mistaken, there was a strange glint in her eyes for a moment.

Phoebe: It's fine. I assume you're just happy to have some company.

She came over to the cliffside, looking down at the splashing Pokemon curiously. Yet another of the fish fell from the falls, another flopping up to take its place.

Phoebe: Hmm...I always saw Magikarp as a heavy metal kind of Pokemon, myself.

He didn't chuckle, the girl frowning that her joke didn't go over so well. She turned to face him fully, placing a hand on her hip.

Phoebe: You could stand to ease up a little, Sid. Anyway, I'm going to visit my grandparents at Mt. Pyre. I thought I'd tell you since you're seeing Wallace when you head back to the League tonight.

It boggled his mind that Phoebe liked visiting that place so much. Sure, she had family there and all...but why in the hell would you live on a mountain filled with ghosts and gravestones? Just the thought of it made him queasy, something he noted with some distate; this girl was seriously costing him points on the manliness scale.

Phoebe: So, you'll tell him for me...won't you?

Sidney: Ah...yeah. Sure thing, Pheebs.

That strange smile of hers returned again, the girl stepping a bit closer to him. She placed a gentle hand on his shoulder, a small chill running up his spine.

Phoebe: Thanks, Sid. I knew I could count on you.

She stepped past him, making her way back in the direction of the League building. No doubt she was heading for Victory Road. He sighed, bending down to pick up his guitar when her voice called out to him once again.

Phoebe: Oh, and I'd suggest wearing something warmer, Sidney. You'll catch your    death    of cold.

He turned to look in her direction, but all that remained was a barren field, the Dark "Master" finding himself alone.

Sidney: ...

Picking up his guitar again, he gave it a few experimental strums and resumed his song, shaking his head as his thoughts drifted to his teammate.

Sidney: ...crazy ass girl.
