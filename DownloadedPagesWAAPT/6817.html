PEFE Exterior

I wasn't sure whether to feel threatened by this structure, or to be awed at just what those silly little humans could accomplish when they put their minds to it.

The building in question was a large dome approximately three miles in diameter, it's huge presence dominating the surrounding landscape, the huge form able to be seen from miles away both on the ground, and from the air. It's stark white metal exterior shone in the mid-day sun as it towered over the surroundings.

It was one of the rare few things in this world that made me feel small. Almost humbled, in fact.

Almost.

It wasn't like those obnoxious "Skyscrapers" that the humans liked to build in their cities. Tall ugly structures of glass and concrete built seemingly to intrude on my domain. As if they have any right to it!

However, the few times I had try to show them that they had no place venturing that high into my territory, I had received a stern warning from my superiors that I was forbidden to turn my Hyper Beam on such offensive structures. Even if I had been merciful enough to wait until they had left it unattended before knocking them down as effortlessly as a young Bagon would knock over a pile of pebbles.

Even if I wasn't allowed to stimmy their efforts of expanding towards the clouds, I still think that if whomever had created the humans had wanted them to fly, they would have given them wings!

Humans with wings. What a completely ridiculous thought.

As I snaked my way around the dome's exterior, I failed to catch site of an entrance, or even a window or any sort of hole to peer through to see into the interior.

Just what could have been inside of it? What would they have needed to have built such a massive structure to contain? It seemed enormous enough to cover an entire city! Could that have been what was inside? And if so, why?

All this pondering was beginning to make my head throb, and I still couldn't find a mean of entry.

I was starting to get frustrated. I had been meaning to visit this "P.E.F.E" as my friend Stratus referred to it to inquire to his whereabouts, and that simply could not be done without finding a way inside.

My patience was beginning to wear thin, and still having not found a way inside, I came to the conclusion that I would have to force my way inside somehow. Obviously, there would be some property damage, but if the humans could build something as vast as this in the first place, I doubted that patching a hole in the roof would be a huge issue for them.

With a challenging roar, I flew back a fair distance up and away from the dome, then spun around, and began to dive towards it, boosting my body to an incredible speed.

Rayquaza used Extremespeed!

The distance between myself and the dome closed rapidly as I flew downward faster and faster, determined to punch clean through the metal outer layer and into the interior. Reaching maximum speed, I gritted my teeth, a determined expression on my face as I preprepared to plow through the dome...

WHAM!!!

...Only for to crash face-first into the hard metal, the plate buckling under the force of my attack, but failing to give way.

It's not very effective.

Screeching in pain, I raised my head, feeling a tickled of blood leak from my nose, my snout bruised from impact. Had I been a lesser Pokémon, I most likely would have shattered several bones upon impact.

With an irate growl, I raised a claw to rub my throbbing nose. Although my body was only slightly injured, my pride was badly wounded. I had never come across a human building that had remained fully intact after just a single strike or ranged assault.

Even though I could probably eventually tear my way inside with a sustained assault of Physical and Ranged attacks, for the first time in my long existence, I felt like the humans had bested me.

Well played, humanity. But as you know, I never back away from a challenge...
