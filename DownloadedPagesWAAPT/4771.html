Rex's Mindscape

Rex awoke in daze, his world spinning around him. He pressed a hand to his forehead, trying to stave off the imminent headache that would no doubt arise. He pulled himself to his feet wearily, wobbling slightly back and forth as he did so.

Rex: Nnnh... what happened...? Where am...!

Taking in his surroundings, Rex stared in surprise.

Rex: But, how can I be...

Could he have fainted after Crewe expelled him? ...No, that seemed unlikely. He doubted that he'd be unconscious as a result of being unconscious. And it seemed rather unlikely that his mother would just grab him and take him back here.

Rex: So...this is my...what did they call it...mindscape?

???: You always were brilliant at figuring things out.

Rex: !!!

The air around Rex felt colder, the boy protectively wrapping his arms around himself as if he knew what was coming. The features of the room he was in prior flickered out of existence, leaving him standing amidst a black void.

???: Yet you are incapable of practical application.

Rex took a step back, his eyes wide with fear. He shook his head, trying to tell himself that this was merely an illusion. It was just a part of his own mind. It had to be...

Rex: No... no, just go away...you're not real...you're not here. You can't be...

???: Believe me, I'd rather not be here to witness such a stunning failure of a man...no, a CHILD such as you.

At that comment, Rex glared, hatred welling up inside of him.

Rex: Shut up! You don't know anything about me! You have no right to say anything about who I am!

???: No right?

From out of the darkness stepped a man. He wore a white dress shirt, a black tie hanging from around his neck. His pants and shoes were black, both equally as formal. But perhaps his most notable feature was his hair, equally as spiky and brown as Rex's. He gave the boy a harsh glare, his next words slow and deliberate.

Rex's Father: And who are you to tell me what rights I have?
