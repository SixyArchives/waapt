215431 Crewe21st Dec 2012 07:20:53 PM from Gravity Falls, Oregon Get RP Mod
Li'l Punk

AU, a secluded valley

A large Salamence sits on his haunches on a cliff, looking down austerely at the swooping and diving squads of Altaria and Flygon. Presently, a younger, female, more brightly colored Salamence treads up the incline towards him. She pauses a short distance away and stands for a moment, as if waiting for some reaction. When the older Salamence makes no sign of acknowledgement, she clears her throat and speaks.

Salamence ♀: <Uncle Austerium?>

The other Dragon chuckles and shifts, looking over his shoulder towards her and opening one wing to gesture her forward.

Austerium: <I was wondering when you'd speak up, Stormfly. And what have I told you about referring to me as uncle? I am your General, first and foremost.>

The female Salamence, evidently named Stormfly, closes the rest of the distance between them and sits beside the older male.

Stormfly: <Oh, uncle, you know we don't really have to be so formal until it really gets serious.>

Austerium laughs dryly and gestures with his tail towards the Pokemon drilling in the valley below.

Austerium: <My dear Stormfly, it is very serious already.>

Stormfly: <Fie, uncle. This is child's play. Besides, I notice you don't use my title, either. So, there.>

Austerium draws himself up and gives her a playfully stern look.

Austerium: <Well, then, Colonel Stormfly. How fare your troops?>

The younger Salamence seems pleased to talk about it, and mimics her uncle, doing her best to look as serious as possible.

Stormfly: <They do very well, General Austerium! My Salamence and Flygonhave learned their formations by heart and have been spending much of their time lately in sparring matches between squads, to practice improvisation. My Aerodactyl and Charizard keep up well enough, though sometimes the other troops look down on them. It's not good for moral, but my Captain Cole is a real marvel, he keeps order among them. Lieutenant Opaline—>

Austerium gives her a hard look and her words die in her throat.

Austerium: <That Charizard is still an officer?>

Stormfly's tail sweeps the grass behind her uneasily, and her neck spikes flatten slightly.

Stormfly: <General, Captain Cole is the best and brightest of my soldiers. He's ever so loyal, the Council could never have any reason to complain of him, and the troops love him...>

Austerium shakes his head slowly, looking disappointed.

Austerium: <Colonel, Charizard are not permitted to be officers. You know that.>

Stormfly: <But, Uncle—>

Austerium: <No. This will be the end of the matter, you hear? Lieutenant Opaline—she's a Flygon, is she not? A good soldier. Promote her, perhaps.>

Stormfly: <Opaline is a good soldier, but she doesn't have the same respect as—>

Austerium: <Then find someone else. But Cole can not be an officer.>

Stormfly droops.

Stormfly: <Yes, General.>

The two watch the drilling Pokemon below for a brief time. Austerium appears lost in thought, while Stormfly watches the weaving Aerodactyl sadly, perhaps contemplating the other non-Dragons she'd managed to sneak into a position without being noticed. Eventually, Austerium speaks again.

Austerium: <Where are my other colonels? Those are Featherstone's troops below, but where is Belinda?>

Stormfly: <Drilling, I think, General. Across the valley.>

Austerium sweeps his tail through the grass, his wings opening and closing.

Austerium: <Good, good... Tell me, Stormfly, how are your Pipers coming along?>

Stormfly starts; he'd remembered her Pipers? She stared at him in surprise for a few moments. Then, realizing he was likely expecting an answer, speaks quickly.

Stormfly: <The Pipers are coming along better than I ever hoped. They might not be ready by our first movement, though. They are rather young...>

Austerium: <A shame, they might bolster morale among the more... reluctant of the troops. Perhaps later on.>

Stormfly: <Oh, yes, they'll be ready soon, sir. The dears really are excellent.>

Austerium nods slowly, then turns his eyes from the wheeling Dragons below to the mountains on the horizon.

Austerium: <Belinda had best have her soldiers ready. When our lords sound the command and the airships fly, we will need to fly with them. Are you ready, Stormfly?>

Stormfly: <Yes, Uncle, I'm ready for anything.>

Austerium gives her a warm look, then gets to his feet.

Austerium: <I hope you are. Well, I'd best see to my own soldiers. Attend to your Pokemon, Colonel. And remember about that Charizard.>

He pauses.

Austerium: <Send Cole to my den later on. I'd like to speak with him. Any other officers that aren't of the proper breeding, as well. Good bye, Colonel. Be prepared.>

With this, he gets to his feet and opens his wings, beating them a few times before leaping off the cliff and swooping down and around the cliff, banking hard and hugging the rock. Stormfly watches him go, then ruffles her wings uncomfortably and walks down the trail she came, frowning as she considers the looming conversations with several of her trusted officers before her.
