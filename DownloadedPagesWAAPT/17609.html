440220 CorvusAtrox16th Jan 2015 09:16:34 PM from a Jolly Caucus Race Get RP Mod , Relationship Status: Don't hug me; I'm scared

X-Naut Fortress, Computer Room

-As Squealer steps into the darkly lit room, a giant computer can be seen-

-The lights turn on and the screen flashes to life suddenly and a voice comes from the device-

???: Hello, Squealer.

Squealer: <Huh? Who said that?>

???: How amusing... I am right here before you.

I am this laboratory's main computer. I am the TEC-XX. Many call me TEC. You may.

Sir Grodus created me to be a perfect computer. One that is flawless in its reason.

Squealer: <Sir Grodus... Isn't he the Metagross that's in charge of all these guys?>

TEC: That is correct. He worked many long hours to make sure that I was perfect for managing this base's needs.

Squealer: <You were the one who opened the doors, then?>

TEC: Correct.

Squealer: <So you wanted me to come here? Why?>

TEC: I am unsure. Unusual program deviations have occurred recently when observing you.

I ran a diagnostic and some things became clear... I have the impulse to spend time with you, to talk with each other, to share common interests.

Such a compulsion has no precedence. Cause unknown.

I... the perfect computer. I must diagnose this situation. I will not fail. That is why I led you here.

Squealer: <Hm...>

-He has a look of realization-

<I've got it!>

TEC: You do? Please, inform me of my malfunction.

Squealer: <It's not a malfunction! It just sounds like you want to be friends!>

TEC: Friends? What do you mean by that? I don't have much data on the subject...

Squealer: <Hm...>

<Well, friendship is when you have a bond with others, when you do support each other and want to do stuff together.>

<It makes you happy to see each other happy and want to try and help when either of you is in trouble.>

<You could even say friendship is magic.>

TEC: Happiness? Fun? Magic?

I have definitions for those words, but in practice...

My programming is insufficient. There should be nothing I don't comprehend.

Squealer: <Hm... I think there's a difference between understanding friendship and feeling it, though.>

TEC: Even though I know little about friendship, I am reasonable sure it is not an emotion.

Regardless...

Squealer, will you teach me about friendship?

Squealer: <Sure! Having a friend here will be great since it doesn't look like these guys will be letting me out anytime soon.>

TEC: Excellent.

I would be willing to do you some favors to help compensate for your teaching, of course.

Of course, that does not include such a thing as letting you escape.

Squealer: <Great! Then could you get me my ball back? I want to let my teammates know I'm okay.>

TEC: Unfortunately, that is not possible either. Sir Grodus has your ball in his personal care.

Though, I could attempt to contact your trainer personally.

Squealer: <Hm... Well, I'd like to talk with them, but I guess we'll have to deal with that.>

TEC: Right. Then I'll let you have access to my keyboard, so you can type out the message.

Squealer: <Er...>

<I don't really know how to use a computer. Or read or write.>

TEC: Then dictate a message and let me enter it?

Squealer: <Sure!>

-He starts talking and the words go on the screen-

Squealer: <Okay, could you send it, TEC?>

-Four beeps are heard as a mail icon moves across the screen-

TEC: The message has been sent. For the time being, you may return to your room.

I will call you again when I'm interested in doing something together.

Squealer: <Okay, see you, then. Good night!>

-He heads out the door-

TEC: Good night, Squealer.

X-Naut Fortress, Command Room

-Grodus is alone here when the holographic screen pops up, showing nothing-

Grodus: <Ah, TEC. How did your attempt to convince him go?>

-A voice comes from the holographic screen-

TEC: It went as planned. The Emboar seems to have readily opened up to me. I suspect it will be easy to make him sympathetic to the goals of the X-Nauts, especially with how he is.

Grodus: <Gaaack ack ack! Excellent work, TEC. It went better than I could have expected.>

TEC: There would be no doubt. You did design me to be perfect, after all. It's no surprise that this would go as well as it did.

Grodus: <Quite so, quite so.>

<Make sure not to let him send anything that might cause a security breach, though. This group seems to know a strangely large amount already, but let's not give them any more to work with.>

TEC: Understood. I will try my best.

Goodnight, then, Sir Grodus.

-The holographic image of nothing vanishes-

Grodus: <Goodnight, TEC.>
