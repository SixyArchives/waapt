Coyote: My my, the doll with the parasite, the Fox-who-is-no-longer, and the robot in a fleshy body.

-He taps his claws together with glee.-

Coyote: This will do magnificently.

-With all three minds deep in slumber, not a single care in the world of their physical bodies, Coyote is free to snip the connections between the physical and the mental. Coyote: That's one...

-His next target was the doll. He went in close and tapped a connection, only to have the mind recoil in pain. Coyote stopped.-

Coyote: ... Riiiiight. How could I forget.

-He ponders a moment before an wondrous~ idea dawned on him. He sets the Not!Fox's mind down for a moment and pulls out the Obscura he reacquired from his old friend Renard. He coats his hand in the odd, tainted Obscura, and moves to cut a connection again. This time, the connection is severed with ease.-

Coyote: Oh, how clever of me~

-The mind comes free with even greater ease - the mind must truly care little for the shell it is forced to inhabit. Again, however, Coyote tends to view things in a strange light. He places the Not!Fox's mind in the now-empty slot, the poor body accepting the replacement with jealous hunger.-

Coyote: One more...

-The last mind did not want to be separated from its body, though. It wanted to stay put, but in it's dreamtime bliss, it did not defend itself well. The mind was heavy with thoughts, and difficult to move as he shifted it over and placed the doll's mind in it's place.-

Coyote: Oof! This one's heavy in a metaphorical and a physical sense.

-He rolls the heavy mind over to the empty socket he started out with, where it takes some convincing to set in right. Looking back at his work, Coyote nods to himself. Now all he had to do was wait... and watch the fun~-
