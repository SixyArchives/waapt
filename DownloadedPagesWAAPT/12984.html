Lumiose City - Kalos

Aline was rollerskating through the city, smiling and daydreaming about the things she wants to do once she goes in a journey. All the sights, people and Pokémon she had yet to see were making her burst with excitement. Unfortunately, while daydreaming she completely missed a tree in her way and hit it face first, her face sliding down the tree as she fell to the ground.

A bit dazed as her eyes spiraled and she came back to the real world, she eventually recovers and hits herself in the head lightly as a small punishment for her lack of attention, a goofy smile on her face making it clear that she wasn't very hurt. She then goes back to skating, this time paying full attention to her her surroundings.

Eventually she came to her stop: The Galette stand! She and the lady who sold them were very familiar with each other, as she was a regular there. As soon as she stopped there, the shopkeeper smiled and waved at her

"Hi Aline! How many do you want today?" she asked with a very friendly disposition

Aline then holds her sign with her arm by pressing it against her torso so that she could show her two open hands to the shop owner

"Ten huh? Is it two for you, your Pokémon and your parents?"

Aline then shakes her head with a smile

The shop owner raises an eyebrow "Hm? Don't tell me it's all for you."

She nods in response, smiling all the way

The shopkeeper blinks a few times "E-Eh...?"

Aline then starts giggling, though there wasn't any sound coming from her, she then shows her signs, which read "Joking~"

"... Oh." said the shopkeeper, giggling a bit "So, was my first guess right?"

Aline then nods, a big smile on her face.

"All righty then!"

After paying for a bag full of galettes and getting a quick "see you", Aline starts heading back home with a very cheerful disposition until it stops as she goes past an alley and suddenly hears some whimpering. She stops in her tracks and backtracks a bit as she looks into the dark alley, hearing some whimpering, the girl suddenly found herself conflicted on whether she should go check what was going on, or to continue on her way due to a dark alley in the middle of the night being dangerous, at least according to her mother.

As she heard more whimpering, an expression of worry made itself clear on her face, but at the same time she was afraid of what would happen if she went there.
