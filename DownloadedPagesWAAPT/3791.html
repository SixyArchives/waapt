They are watched from afar by a group of guards, as well as by some of their partners sitting near some bushes-

-One of the females tries to sort our which one to take between a Mint Berry and a PRZCureBerry, when she stops to think for a moment. After that she turns to another Nidorina who lives by the next burrow-

Ametsa — <Hey... Inka?>

Inka — <...> — focused on counting her berries.

Ametsa (pats her friend's head) — <...Inka?>

-Inka turns to her and blinks, then tilts her head-

Ametsa — <Something wrong?>

Inka — <No, no... I was just counting how many Gold Berries we have in store for the rest of the year.> — points to the last two Gold Berry trees in the forest. — <Why? Something happened?>

Ametsa — <Well... I'm not sure.> — rubs paws — <You are gonna think I'm crazy...>

-Inka hugs her neighbor-

Inka — <The things you say. So?>

Ametsa (breathes) — <Since one or two days ago I feel like there's something missing... somewhere.> — sees Inka staring at her oddly — <I don't know what I mean by that, I just feel like it's something... obvious.>

-Another female listens to the conversation-

Eulalia — <What, your brain went on vacation, like, again?> — snickers.

-Laughs from the group, which are stopped by a dead-serious glare from Inka-

Inka — <Perhaps you misplaced something?>

Ametsa — <I don't know... but I know that I'd realize what it is if I just saw it, I swear!> — clenches her fist.

-The two chat for a bit longer until the time comes for the group to separate, each Nidorina to return to their own burrows-

Ametsa — <See you tomorrow for the Xatu public service, Inka?> — points to the mountains to the east — <I hear Calino is going to open a herb trading store!>

-Inka lets a faint smile draw on her muzzle-

Inka — <Really? I hope her daughters help her with the business...>

Ametsa — <Seeing you tomorrow then, bye!>

-turns to her mate-

Ametsa's mate — <♥?>

Ametsa — <♥!>

-With help of their mates who come in to the edge of the clearing, the other Nidorina take the collected berries and depart to their burrows. One by one they are gone, until in the end there's only Inka and the berry trees-

Inka — <...> — sighs.

-looks around for a while, then picks her berries-

-She waits for a little longer, then returns to her burrow alone. However, before leaving the clearing she stands under a tree, and looks around looking for any trace of something missing-

-Squints, trying to distinguish the features of the forest in the dark night-

Inka — <...Isn't this night the third of the cycle of the Glowing Inlé? Hmmm...> — blinks — <I wish Silent had taught me those "astronomy" things too...>

-sighs, then walks away alone-
