Gateon Port, Hospital

-Herbert starts to stir awake, having only managed to snatch a couple of hours of nightmare-wracked sleep the previous night. He opens his eyes, and immediately starts upon seeing Alice sitting by his bedside.-

Herbert: Gah! You could at least have given me some sort of warning...

Alice: I could say the same for you, Lucius. Carol appears to be the only member of the team that is aware that you are currently hospitalised. You really should call more often, you know.

Herbert: Yeah, probably... anyway, I guess you're wondering what happened, aren't you?

Alice: No, actually. Bishop explained the situation to me, using Bella here as an interpreter.

-She gestures to the Scyther and the Exeggutor in the corner of the room, who both nod at Herbert before resuming their own conversation.-

Alice: I have to say, I cannot see any situation in which a high-ranking Cipher officer who is affiliated with your father taking an interest in you would be beneficial. However, I will try to provide some measure of assistance by gathering whatever information I can on Cipher, since I gather that you are determined to continue your mission.

Herbert: Thanks for the help... is it okay if I cut this catchup session a little short, though? I kind of want to talk to Bishop alone...

-Bishop stops talking upon Herbert's mention of him and starts to look rather anxious, but Alice simply nods.-

Alice: Very well. I will take my leave, then, since I have business of my own to attend to. There is no doubt that we will both be largely occupied over the course of the next few weeks, but do try to call when you can. Let us hope for your speedy recovery and relative safety.

-She stands up, recalls Belladonna, and leaves. Herbert looks over at Bishop.-

Bishop: <...I assume you wish to ask about what transpired in the alley?>

Herbert: I'm afraid so. I didn't see much, but from what I overheard you were rather-

Bishop: <My sincerest apologies, but I... well, I was not ready to face a Shadow Pokemon again, as you quite clearly saw. But when he started to... you see, something... I reverted to base instincts in order to protect you. It is not something I am proud of, considering that I was once in Duke's position myself. I would like to assure you that such an event will never happen again, but...>

-He trails off awkwardly. Herbert pats him with his good arm.-

Herbert: You know, you don't have to do this. I could always send you back to Challenger's Cave, get someone-

Bishop: <No! I mean... that is not what I want at all. I want to be able to protect you. Kirk saved you from me once, I was hoping I could save you from Duke in the same manner... evidently I was mistaken. I failed...>

Herbert: You didn't fail, Bishop.

Bishop: <Really? Because from where I was standing it looked as though you would have died had your masked assailant not called off the attack. And my intervention did nothing to help.>

Herbert: You tried to help, though. And like I've been saying since I got here, that's the only thing that matters.

Bishop: <Attempts at assistance do not save lives. And besides, even if my help is all you wanted, you clearly have issues with my rather extreme means of providing it...>

-Herbert pats again.-

Herbert: Look, I know this is difficult. Just... try to tone it down, and do your best to keep the team together. I can't ask for anything more from you.

Bishop: <If you say so...>

-Herbert gives a small sigh, and then goes back to staring at the ceiling whilst idly wondering if he should try calling anyone with his X-Transceiver.-
