Marie and Ray's room

Whistling to herself, Ray pulled open the over door, and put a dish inside, filled with cake batter. She then got up, and brushed some hair back behind her ear, and took off the oven mitts and hung them along with her apron.

She then looked at a photo frame on the table, and picked it up. She gently touched it, and smiled to herself. The photo was of her and Marie, back at their house in Fiore. Ray had been trying to make the cake herself, but had gotten it all over her, so Marie had decided to make it on her own, like she usually does. She and Marie were standing side by side, with white cream on their noses and cheeks, grinning at the camera, her arm around Marie's shoulder.

The door heard a knickotty, a bit of a knockitty, and a hint of a knack, as Locke came into the room. He was grinning, and quickly gave Ray a big huggle."Happy birthday, Ray!"

Ray grinned back at him, having put the photo down, face up. She gave a bit of a surprised "yeep!" at the hug, but gave him a tight one nevertheless, keeping her hands away from his right shoulder."Thankee, Locke! Odd, you're never much of a hugger."

Locke gave her a smile, which looked like he was trying to brace past some pain."Well, I really feel like you should be happy today, that's all." He glanced at the photo, and his smile drooped sadly. Ray looked where he was looking."Still no word?"

Locke shook his head."But she won't miss the party, I'm sure of it. Speaking of which, I'll go get some guests!"

He ran off, and shut the door just as Ray was yelling "That's okay, you don't have to-" "THUD!" Ray shook her head, and started chuckling.
