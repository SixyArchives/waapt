Kirk: ...There's still time, isn't there? They're probably still back in Holon City... I could just ExtremeSpeed back there and... and...

-He shakes his head.-

Kirk: No. I can't go back. Not now. I can't go back to a dead trainer and a team that probably hates me for walking out on them. I need freedom. Freedom and a fresh start. Somewhere new, where I can start over again. Somewhere I can be strong, for once.

-He sighs.-

Kirk: I just need to take care of a couple of loose ends first...

-He stands up and starts pacing up and down nervously, his paw curled over the fragment of Challenge Rock hanging around his neck.-

Kirk: How am I supposed to get a message to her, though? I can't just go and deliver it myself, she'd try to stop me. And knowing her, she'd probably succeed. I could try sending her a letter, but I'm not sure how I'd get it to her... can she even read? I don't think I ever got around to asking... there's a lot of things I never got around to saying to her, honestly...

-His grip tightens.-

Kirk: ...I need to do this, though. Things just weren't going to work out between us. Time to put the past behind me and move on.

-He quickly darts into the back of a stationary shop, helps himself to a pen and a piece of paper, and starts writing out a quick letter.-

Eowyn,

I don't know if you'll get this message, or even if you'll understand it if you do, but I'm leaving. It's something I've been thinking of doing for a while now... I feel like I'm being held back when I'm serving beneath a human, and now that my trainer's dead I have a chance to experience life by myself for once. Without anyone stopping me from reaching my true potential.

I'm sorry our relationship had to end like this... but after thinking about it for a while, I don't know if we could have made it work anyway. So don't come looking for me. I'll miss you, Eowyn, but... this is something I have to do on my own.

Kirk

-Almost the moment he finishes scribbling out his letter, he hears one of the ships in town sound its horn as a boarding call.-

Dockworker: Oi, you lot! Finish getting that cargo on board! We'll be leaving for Kalos in just ten minutes!

Kirk: <...Kalos? Never heard of that place before... well, it has to better than this dump. Then again, pretty much anywhere's better than this dump.>

-He starts hurriedly glancing around the town, searching for a way to deliver his message before the ship leaves, when he notices a Fire/Flying Delta Fearow preening itself nearby.-

Kirk: <...That'll do. Hey, bird-brain!>

Fearow: <Huh?>

-Kirk shoves the letter into the Fearow's talons.-

Kirk: <Take this to a Lucario named Eowyn, okay? Her trainer's an Obscuric, she shouldn't be too hard to find. You'd better make sure she gets it, because I'm leaving this dreary little island in just a few minutes. You got that?>

-Before the Fearow can respond, the ship's boarding horn blows again.-

Kirk: <Never mind. Just make sure that message gets to her!>

-He ExtremeSpeeds away to find a place to stow away on the ship, leaving the Fearow to stare blankly at the message in its talons.-

Fearow: <...>

-After a moment, he burns the note with an Incinerate.-

Fearow: <He could've just said "please".>
