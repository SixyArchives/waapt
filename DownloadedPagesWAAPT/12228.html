Mt. Coronet, Unknown Time

A figure clad in a black trench coat trudged up the mountain. The wind howled and roared but didn't deter this man from his path. He could've just used his Pokemon to reach the top quicker but he had left them all in the care of a friend. Save for one. This one was neither his strongest, nor his longest known friend. Yet out of all of his companions this one was by far the most loyal. And the most childish.

"On second thought maybe I should've brought AF with us." The man said as he looked up towards his destination, shielding his eyes from the oncoming flurry of ice with his arm. The Arcanine at his side grumbled.

<You think?>

"Onwards Sparkers."

The man and his dog continued the long journey up the mountain. Neither were quite sure what they would find at it's summit. The only thing stranger was the circumstances as to how they ended up here.

Sometime Last Week

Des woke up to a chilly breeze blowing over his body. He slowly opened his eyes...and found himself naked, handcuffed to a bed, in a rundown motel room with a certain redheaded cat burglar sleeping soundly next to him. If Des could face palm he would.

I can't believe I let her get the better of me again! Interpol will have my head for sure.

Sparkers pawed at the door from the outside.

<Des? Are you in there?>

"Um...no."

<Wait, you're with her again are you?>

"Well...depends on who her is..."

The door shook and rattled as Sparkers repeatedly 'facedoored' himself. Another chilly breeze came in through the window causing Des to shiver.

"She probably left that window open just to spite me."

Des could easily get out of the handcuffs if he wanted to. He was no stranger to escape artistry. But his chivalrous side got the better of him and decided to let the lady have her victory. For now.

As soon as she wakes up and leaves through the door, I'm givin' her a 5 minute headstart. Then I'm comin' after he in full force!

Another breeze entered the room, this one carrying not only a whole new set of chills for Des but also a peculiar piece of paper. It landed almost meticulously onto his chest. Upon it was a simple message.

"Spear Pillar. Bring Swords."

"There's only one other person I know who can send messages like this. But it doesn't appear to be in his handwriting." Des reasoned to himself. "My god this guy's writin' is terrible. Looks like Torchic scratches."

Des looked to the open window where the note had come from. Wondering what the universe had in store for him this time.
