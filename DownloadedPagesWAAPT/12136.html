A ship, off the coast of Ransei - Around 600 years ago

After saying their goodbyes to the few people in Ransei still willing to hear them and one last meeting with old friends in Avia, Lucanus and Aiura are stood on the deck of a small merchant vessel, watching the region slowly disappear from view.

Eventually, Lucanus speaks. ...Is it unusual that I feel more emotional leaving Ransei than I did when first leaving my native Kalos?

Sinnoh - Around 599 years and 6 months ago

Aiura, after a long period of labor, is resting peacefully. The nurse holds the child in her arms, as Lucanus walks up.

Lucanus: May I hold her?

Nurse: Of course, but

Lucanus: But?

Nurse: Well, sir, its a boy, not a girl.

Lucanus: Hmm. It seems her powers of prediction have failed her for once. Interesting.

Aiura stirs. She likes you, Lucanus.

Lucanus: How can you tell? Also, our babys not a girl.

Aiura furrows her brow.

Aiura: Really? Interesting. Those mental patterns usually indicate femininity

Lucanus shrugs. Youre the one with psychic abilities. I will take your word for it. So, what should we name him?

Aiura thinks for a moment. The baby seems to want to be called Stormageddon, the Dark One.

Lucanus: ...We are not naming our son Stormageddon."

Aiura crosses her arms. Well why not?

Lucanus: Because that is quite frankly moronic.

Aiura: You hear that? Daddy says youre moronic. The baby says that youre moronic too, dear.

Lucanus gives an exasperated sigh. There is always the possibility that you are psionically holding a mental conversation with a newborn baby, but I think youre pulling my leg. Were naming him Lucas.

Aiura sighs. Fine. Lucas it is. The baby coos. Hes still requesting to be called Stormageddon.

Lucanus: Tell young Lucas that he should learn to respect his elders decisions.

Aiura grabs the baby from him and rocks him. Shh Interesting Hes already got nicknames for us. Im mama, and youre not mama. Oh, and thats unfortunate Everyone else are peons.

Lucanus gives another sigh. Id rather we keep the number of megalomaniacal overlords in the family at one, thank you very much.

She smirks. Im sure its just a phase, love. You know how children are.

Sinnoh - Around 595 years ago

Aiura is watching little Stormaggedon Lucas chase around his toddler siblings, Lilliana and Elspeth.

Aiura sits down next to Lucanus. Arent our children wonderful?

Lucanus smiles. Indeed they are. I will confess that doubted my capabilities as a father at first, but well, between the two of us we appear to have done a half-decent job.

Aiura hugs him and smiles. I for one never doubted you. There was a period there that I was a bit nervous about your head, but then again, were all a little screwed up sometimes.

Lucanus: Thank you, dear that faith was a large part of what kept me going through some of my darker periods. But those times are thankfully long behind us now. No more power politics, or warfare, or intrigue - no, here it is just us living a regular life as a regular family. I only hope my past does not catch up with us here

Aiura grins. How could it? As long as no kooky knife-wielding maniacs with a grudge bust in, we should be fine.

Lucanus: I suppose you are correct. It has been five years now, after all, and nothing has disturbed our peace as of yet. With any luck, that state of affairs should continue.

Aiura: Indeed, you worry too much. Im supposed to be the paranoid one, love.

Lucanus: Perhaps. Then again, until recently I had plenty to worry about. Thankfully, the number of concerns in my life has now been reduced to just four.

He gives a small smile, kisses Aiura on the cheek, then looks over at Lucas again. Children! Dinner will be in a couple of minutes. Get yourselves inside, would you?

The twins rush inside and wash up, but Lucas dawdles a bit.

Aiura: Something wrong, Lucas?

Lucas just shrugs. Then go inside and get washed up, dear, will you? He nods and walks in.

Aiura sighs. Im worried about him, darling.

Lucanus: I know he always seems to be so quiet. Ive only ever seen him open up around Lillianna and Elspeth. Perhaps we should try to talk to him?

Aiura nods. Probably would be wise. I dont even know where I would begin, though

Lucanus: Maybe I should be the one to start the conversation. He could simply be in need of a father-son bonding moment or somesuch. No offense meant, of course.

Aiura shakes her head. Something tells me thats not such a good idea. Youre certainly welcome to try, though.

Lucanus thinks for a moment. I know that the suggestion is terribly impolite, but would it be possible to use your psychic abilities to gain some measure of insight into the situation? At the very least, it may give me something to work with.

Aiura sighs. I really dont want to do that. But I understand the idea. Ill try.

She walks into the house and sees Lucas washing his hands. She listens to his thoughts for a little while.

After a bit, Lucas turns around. Mama, are you looking in my head again? I thought you said that was rude

Aiura blinks. Er Yes?

Lucass lip quivers. Mama, have I done something wrong? Do you not trust me?

Aiura sighs. No, darling. Your father and I are just worried about you, thats all. You know you can talk to us about anything, right?

Lucas blinks. Yes? And?

Aiura furrows her brow. O...K Well, go get your sisters, dinners done.

Lucas nods. Yes, mama. He runs off.

Aiura goes to get Lucanus. Well, I dont think Im ever going to stop being surprised by parenthood.

Lucanus raises an eyebrow at her in return. What do you mean?

Well, for one, I was reading Lucass mind, and not only did he notice, but he shut me down in the middle of doing so. I think I need to have a word with Uncle Jace on how much hes teaching Lucas.

Lucanus: I suppose such a level of ability is to be expected from the son of a psychic and an Auric.

He gives a small smile of pride, despite himself. Did you learn anything during the brief period that you were well, looking around, as it were?

She wavers a bit. Yeeeees? And no. Yes, because Im ninety percent sure I was right five years ago. No, because lets be honest, is that ever a surprise?

Lucanus: ...Again, I dont think I quite understand.

Aiura thinks of the best way to put this. Im not sure if I do, either. But, I will say, I think Lucas is a girl. Seeing Lucanuss expression, she continues. Not physically, of course, weve both changed enough diapers for that to be the case. But mentally.

Lucanus continues to stare at her with a look somewhere between confusion and disbelief.

Aiura blinks. Yeah Thats about what Im thinking, too.

Lucanus: You have been mistaken on occasions before now, dear

Aiura snickers. Thats funny. Me? Wrong? Youre hilarious, darling.

Lucanus gives a rather hollow smile, which fails to convey any real amusement. I believe I need some time to think about things. And possibly have a conversation with our son afterwards.

Aiura nods. Indeed
