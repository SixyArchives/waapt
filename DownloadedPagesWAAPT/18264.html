Joseph: ...well, I suppose this is the Sibyl's house, then?

-it' a perfectly ordinary looking house. It's two stories, comes with shuttered window shades, and is painted a pleasant light blue-it could have come from any nineteenth-century town. The atmosphere speaks otherwise, however; as quaint and pretty as it looks, it's in the middle of a large field on a foggy day, the Nordic air biting and unpleasant. It gives a disquieting effect, like a 50's era malt shop in the middle of the Black Plague-

Joseph: She'd better have some good bloody answers, then...

-the door is ajar, and he enters. Apparently, it's a library-there are shelves and shelves of books, the musty, dusty smell of the leatherbound objects somewhat comforting in conjunction with the crackling fire on the other end of the room-

Joseph: Hmmm...good to see ol' Sibyl trying to make me feel at HRRRRK!

-he clamps a hand over his nose as the stench becomes overpowering. Overwhelming the smell of dust and smoke is a putrid, rotting smell of old carrion. Perhaps Sibyl forgot to cure herring before its expiration...

Joseph: Hmmph...I'd have preferred mothballs.

-sniffing despite himself, Joseph determines the source of the smell-that long, thin thing laying on the red hardwood floor around the fireplace-

Joseph: Well, what do we have h-

-squelch. He pauses, in the manner of one stepping in dog droppings and not wanting to look down. He slowly lifts his boot-clad foot and inspects it...-

Joseph: ...

-he looks down at his footprint. Simple hardwood, colored beige, surrounded by red-

Joseph: ...oh no...

-he becomes gravely aware of exactly what that long thin thing is, and the location of its three companions-

Joseph: No no no no no...

-Joseph makes a frantic, panicked run for the space between two bookshelves, while the Sibyl's killer steps out from in between two others and licks his lips. His teeth are still stained-
