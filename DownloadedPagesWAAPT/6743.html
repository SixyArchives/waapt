Treasure Town

The moments flashed before Ken's mind again. And again. And again.

The surge of emotions flooding through him from his comrades. The rage from the people protecting him. The fear from the person they were against. The malice and trickery emanating from... Her.

It froze his entire body, stiff as a board... Useless. And worse than that, a detriment to those around him... Eowyn was risking her own neck to protect him when he flinched.

The feeling had left Ken blank, a cold chill down his back... That without the courage to step up, this would happen again.

Once Krom and the others were healed up in town, Ken was left to wander the streets of Treasure Town, alone. He planned to join back up with them later, of course, but not before seeing what could be done. Trailing his scarf close behind, Ken looked around... But for a while, found nothing but stalls that would be able to help anyone but him.

Eventually, the little green mon took a breather by the lower area of town. After speaking to a friendly Chansey (and finding it helped by caring for eggs, ) Ken felt like he had no where else he could really go. That is, until he heard the sound of bone on rock.

Curious, Ken quietly stepped up to the dojo and leaned inside for a peak. Inside, the Psychic type discovered a Marowak pounding on the wall, widening it and aiming to expand. The fire flickered across the walls, driving back the darkness, before Marowak wiped it's skull and noticed Ken out of the corner of it's eye.

"Well, don't just stand there shaking like a leaf! Come on in!" The trainer gave a light chuckle, tossing it's bone over it's shoulder with smiling eyes.

Ken nearly shrunk away at this, but relented and walked inside, a little bit nervous and holding his hands together. "Uh... G-Greetings! My name is Ken, and... I'm sorry, I don't even know why I'm here-" Ken was about to briskly turn away and run off, but found his little legs running to nowhere as a bone had locked him into place over his shoulder.

"Hmm... You're still a fresh one... Not much in the way of battle experience, at least not on your own." The Marowak judged, measuring Ken's strength as he was running and his reaction time. Neither were very good as it stood. The Ralts was much too stiff in his movements, unable to really react as he needed to, yet.

Marowak spoke again. "If you want to get stronger, you're going to have to earn it." Ken slowly stopped trying to run upon hearing these words, the light of the flames dancing over his eyes.

"...How?" He asked slowly, even though as he turned to the pits below, he already had an idea.

"Simple... You go down there and train until you're stronger." Marowak's reply was short, effective, and pointing out the obvious. "I've got ten different mazes down there, each one filled to the brim with adventurers just like you, training to be the best they can be. It's not to the death, though, I've taken care of that." Motioning to the stairwell down, branching off into ten different routes, each route Marowak motioned to was decorated with a different image. "You're a Psychic type, so I'll be sending you into the Psychic/Fighting maze. Shouldn't be that tough, all in all. I'm just going to have to ask that you hand over your scarf before you go in. Next time, hand it over to Kangaskhan Storage."

Ken nodded, handing over his scarf slowly. Marowak looked it over slowly, before putting it to the side. "Alright, head on in." The trainer told the Ralts with a nod, before getting back to work on his digging.

Nodding gently, Ken walked into the darkness, trying to keep his feet from briskly turning around and running again. Climbing down the stairwell, Ken finally reached the door he was told to go into. A giant image was on the door, the left side appearing to be a fist and the right an eye, the two split into each other down the middle. With a gentle push, Ken entered the dark door and walked inside, unsure of what he'd find. What he found, however, was the door slamming shut behind him, before a trapdoor opened up beneath his feet. With a surprised yelp, Ken found himself sliding down into a deep cavern, swallowing him up before spitting him out onto a cold, harsh dirt floor.

Ken lay flat on the ground for a minute, groaning lowly about the pain, the surprise, and how it was going to be hard to get out, before he began to feel something. A presence in the darkness... A calm mind that seemed ready to explode into anger, and merely needed a trigger.

Fumbling around in the pitch dark, Ken remembered the words Marowak had told him; this was a dojo, and the other mons here were merely training, the same as him. Moreover, he didn't need to worry about killing or being killed... Though he didn't explain how.

To Ken, this meant one thing. Someone else out there was going to be attacking him with no remorse, and Ken had to beat him first. Using the emotions he felt as a focal point, Ken waited until the other being was in range in dead silence, before blasting forth Confusion at that point. The cry of a Mankey yelled forth, a wave of frustration piercing forth as Ken silently continued to attack from a distance, not giving away his position. Within a minute of the Mankey running about, hopelessly trying to find it's opponent, Ken's Confusions finally laid the Mankey down, KO'd. Before Ken could go to make sure his foe was alright, unknown to the Ralts a little blue orb rose out of the ground, an Escape orb. Activated, the Mankey vanished from the maze in an instant.

What appeared instead was a wave of emotions from other mons, just coming into Ken's range. Some were excited, some concerned, some uncaring, and most just hungry for battle. All were rushing to where the Mankey's last cries could be heard, eager for a fight.

Dozens of mons rushed into the pitch black room, Ken quickly hiding off to the side and sidestepping the lot of them. Hoping they would all fight amongst themselves, Ken planned to simply be able to pick them off one by one, feeling more at home since he believed himself the only one who could see.

That is, until a Monferno came barreling around the corner, tail ablaze and blasting forth fire, lighting up the entire room and revealing the combatants to each other. Ken was barely able to escape out of the room before the blood-crazed gladiators began to tear into each other, free from actual concern for each others' well being.

Ken, from a hidden dark area was able to blast off Confusion at a couple of the Fighting types, but more often than not the only real fighting was among the better fighting types. Just before the Monferno went down for the count, the last Fighting type left standing let out a low sigh, the calm face of a Medicham, before vanishing into the darkness.

Well, mostly vanishing. Ken could still sense the controlled bloodlust coming off that mon, and it wasn't hard to do. What was more worrying, though, was that the Medicham was sensing Ken in return, and given it's level was likely far above Ken in strength. Quickly, Ken dashed off into the darkness, trying to keep himself from running headlong into a wall.

For five seconds exactly, despite knowing where Ken was, the Medicham stood still. When those seconds were up, it dashed after the little Ralts. It was playing with Ken, despite already having to fight tooth and nail against several tough foes and being weakened.

Ken's heart was racing now. Stumbling through the dark as fast as he could, trying to find somewhere, anywhere he could hide, the green Mon realized all too soon that he and the Medicham were the only combatants left on the floor. Almost tripping to the dirt-covered ground, leaning against a wall and peering back through the darkness, only to find the silent rage of the Medicham screaming behind him, Ken could only close his eyes slowly as the Dual Fighting/Psychic type bared down on him, it's fist clenched and trailing fire. Right then, Ken wished he could be somewhere... Anywhere other than right there.

And in a moment's flash of blue light, he was. Across the room, to be precise.

Ken's eyes opened suddenly to see the Medicham's Fire Punch crushing right into the wall where Ken was standing just a moment ago, stunned he'd somehow used Teleport. The Medicham was stunned as well, as Ken could sense a bit of frustration leaking through it's composure.

Maybe it was the fear of this more powerful mon pumping adrenaline into his body. Maybe it was just his newfound experience kicking in. Maybe it was dumb luck. However it happened, though, it happened. An idea came into Ken's mind in that instant. He couldn't fight the Medicham toe-to-toe, obviously. But Ken seemed to have superior agility, at least through his moves. Immediately, Ken began to use Double Teams, one after another. Trying to focus all of his thoughts through each one, in order to confuse Medicham's psychic powers, Ken's clones numbered half a dozen.

Then a dozen.

Then two dozen, if not more. Every single one running circles around trying to dodge Medicham's movements and confuse it. Most every time, Medicham's Fire Punches would hit nothing but air, as Ken got into the spirit of things and began to mock the Medicham with snickers, tongue-waggling, and the occasional "Nyah-nyah!" Whenever Ken felt safe, he'd blast forth a Confusion onto the weakened mon, just to push it closer to the edge of exhaustion. Besides that exhaustion, however, Ken could feel the Medicham's frustration growing deeper and more powerful with every taunt, becoming a sheer rage.

Letting out a scream, the Medicham yelled out with clenched fists, <"STOP MOCKING ME!!!"> before freezing in place, it's eyes glowing. Suddenly, Ken's mind was read as where he was going to go was revealed. The Medicham let out a little smirk, looking at the whirling Ralts surrounding it, before leaping into the air for a High Jump Kick where it knew Ken would be. Ken, unable to move without confirming to the Medicham where he was and likely blowing the illusion, was left to watch in silent fear as the Medicham's knee flew forward in the air, it's eyes blazing with fury...

Until those eyes blinked.

In that one instant, Ken used Teleport one more time, vanishing into his crowd of Double Team Ralts again, and leaving Medicham to keep going and crash headlong into the dirt, twisting and turning before being left in a pile. Still conscious, the Medicham tried to raise it's hands... Before Ken shot it down in one last blast of Confusion. The Medicham's head finally fell to the ground, before being ported out by an Escape Orb. Ken had won.

As his Double team clones dissipated one by one, Ken was left with an elated sense of joy and confidence. Pumping his tiny fist into the sky, Ken felt a surge of power throughout his body in the darkness, like nothing else he'd felt.

Then, his body began to glow softly, before shining in a bright white light. Before Ken had even realized what had happened, he felt taller, stronger and more agile. His body felt stronger than before by a lot, and Ken was left with a wide smile on his face as the ladder back up to the surface was finally dropped down to Ken.

As the fire's light flickered across his face once more, Ken lept out of the darkness feeling like a new mon. "Woo! That was fantastic! I mean, that Medicham was pretty scary, he was all, 'I'm the strongest, ' and I was all 'Aaaah!' and he was all 'Grrrrrrrraaaaaah!' And I was all 'Double team!' And he was all 'Why... Can't... I... Hit you?!?' and I was all 'Can't touch this!' and I beat him! That was AMAZING!" As if on cue, Ken spun in place, pumping his fist to the sky once more.

The Marowak, on the other hand, was now stifling a giggle. "Oh... Yeah... Heheh... Then tell me... Do you feel... Manlier?" Marowak asked while handing Ken back his scarf.

"Oh, definitely! I mean, I feel like I can fight harder, better, faster, stronger... Like I'm a whole different person!" Ken responded while twirling his scarf around his neck without a care, having not even glanced down at his body.

Marowak just gave a laugh, before returning to it's digging. "In that case... Have a good day, young one! Just, uh... Remember to check out yourself in the water's edge!"

Ken wasn't sure what Marowak meant by that. Right then, he just felt like his body was insanely stronger, so he should go out to see his new muscles in action. Clambering out into the sun, Ken dashed out past Chansey's daycare to the river, looking down at his face.

His female face.

And thinner form.

And skirt.

And so he screamed.

"AAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAA- HHHHHHHHHHHHH!"

It was then that the newly evolved Kirlia promptly passed out, the shock of his new feminine form combined with exhaustion turning out to be too much for him.
