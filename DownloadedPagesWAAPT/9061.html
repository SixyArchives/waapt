The most unique type

Rustboro

Hikari was sitting in her room in the Pokémon Center, looking at a map of the region while drinking some hot chocolate, it was a fairly cold day in Hoenn at the moment, so she was was wearing a coat, pants and gloves, she was thinking of where to go next, all the while wondering what Sakura and Lyuri were up to, when suddenly a certain Buneary bursted into the room.

“Hikari! Hikari! Somebody sent something for you!” she said, carrying a package with her and with an excited look in her face.

It was about them that her parents came in, each holding a package of their own, all of them looked the same though, so maybe the other two were for someone else.

“There are these too, they look like they both have the same thing inside.” said Blade, not being able to read what was said on the package, and thus, not knowing those were for someone else.

“Maybe these are for someone else, dear.” said Rei, glancing at Blade with a small smile.

“Wow, that’s a lot of gifts for one day.” said Hikari, surprised with all the packages, she then took a closer look at them and saw that two of them were for Sakura and Lyuri “Oh, two of them are for the Rieses.” she commented, she then picked the package Ami was holding and started to open it.

“See?” said Rei, with a giggle.

“Soooo… We have to deliver these to them later?” asked Ami, looking at the remaining ones with curiosity

“Seems like it, why they couldn’t simply send these ones to them in the first place is beyond me.” said Blade, putting the package he was carrying on top of Hikari’s bed.

As the family chatted, Hikari was opening the package and once it was open, she saw a purple, heart-shaped pillow with “Luck” stitched in silver, along with a piece of a paper, curious, she decided to read it.

I've bested many villains From regions far a wide I've beaten many baddies From justice they cannot hide But at the end of the day When they're locked away And I get a moment to myself I stop and reminisce About all those from whom I have received help

Sometimes people come up to me Sometimes these people ask "Sir, why do you do the things you do? Why have you chosen this path?" Many answers come to mind But only one answer rings true I tell them "Once upon a time, there were once these three angels that I knew They rescued me from darkness, They brought me into the light These three guardian angels are the reasons why I fight".

Happy St. Valentines Sakura, Lyuri and Hikari Hope, Courage and Luck -your friend, Des

As she read the poem, Hikari could only give a small smile “Figures he would give me something for Valentine’s Day.” she said, looking at the pillow for a second before going back to the poem “It needed more rhymes though, but I like the message.”

Curious, Hikari decided to open the other packages, she first took the package Rei was holding, all the while giving her a smile.

“Why don’t you and Blade enjoy Valentine’s Day together, it’s just once in a year you know.”

“Oh! Of course! Come on, dear!” said Rei, quickly holding Blade’s arm and dragging him along with her.

“Wait, mommy!” said Ami, soon following them outside the room.

Now alone in the room, Hikari opened the other packages and saw exactly what she expected upon reading the poem: A heart-shaped pillow with “Hope” written on it, and another one with “Courage” written instead, the color for Sakura’s was unsurprising, pink with gold writing, Heart of Gold anyone? Lyuri’s being blue was weird, but the bronze writing was fittingly enough considering her favorite color.

She then placed the pillows next to each other and stared at them for a while, each package had its own copy of the poem, but what intrigued her most was the color of the writing.

“Lyuri’s bronze, I’m silver and Sakura’s gold… Des… Are you trying to tell us something...?” she muttered to herself, curious about the “message” that she thought was hidden on those hearts, however she soon dismissed it “No… Des isn’t that clever, it must be a coincidence.”

She then started to put the gifts back in place, she would need to wrap up both of them again, but nobody would know that she peeked at it anyway, she left the pillows on her bed and started to head outside, however, before leaving the room she glanced at the pillows again.

“Quite the interesting coincidence though, it’s almost unbelievable… Happy Valentine’s Day, Des.” she said, with a smile as she finally left.

The poem was still on Hikari’s head, she already had it memorized and couldn’t stop thinking about it, as she headed outside the Pokémon Center with her arms folded and looking down, she was trying to interpret the poem, find any hidden meaning in it, because she felt that there was one, finally, she finally realized everything.

“Like any good poem, there are many ways on interpreting it; yours in special is making me think a lot Des… This one is hard, because there a lot of factors you have to understand before trying, fortunately, I do. And here’s my take at interpreting this poem:”

Bronze, Silver and Gold. Courage, Luck and Hope Those are the three guardian angels, But two of them don’t exist; you're just deluding yourself There's just one angel An angel of Courage, Luck and Hope, that watched over you. That angel also gave you Luck so that you would meet, That angel gave you the Courage needed to face the light, And finally, that angel gave you the Hope you needed to keep going, There was only one angel, that one was the responsible for saving you, nobody else was. The true angel… Is the one with the Heart of Gold.

Hikari then went to Rustboro’s streets, taking a quick look at the sky and then taking her glasses off to clean them, all the while having a smile on her face and looking somewhat proud.

“After all, gold is the noblest of all metals.”
