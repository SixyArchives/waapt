The man chuckled darkly, grinning wickedly.

“You see, my plan was quite simple. Our Delta research at Holon is considered quite…questionable by several people, so much so that visionaries like me who wish to study the Delta transformation effects are incapable of doing so. Protection laws and all that on natural habitats in Holon.” He snorted at the very thought.

“So, I thought I’d arrange a little visit to another region, round up some Pokémon there, and then bring them back to Holon. Kanto, being the nearest, was my destination of choice. Of course, upon hearing that some Deltas had been sighted in the Orange Islands, I had to abandon my plan and make way here. I wanted to see how they reacted in the wild, you see?”

<Why do bad guys always feel the need to monologue?> Clover sighed.

“Anyway, I could not find all of the escapees, but I did find one. It was rather easy to enlist the help of, considering how it seemed solely focused on food. After retrieving it, I continued my initial plan of gathering test subjects, these Rattata happened to be nesting on the island we are on now. I created that statute that the savage there smashed as a means of keeping people from coming to the island, spreading rumors of a horrible Pokemon eating monster.”

He smiled nastily, concluding his story. “And there you have it; the entire plan laid bare before you.”

“Wow…” Kim gasped. “That’s…that’s…”

“Yes, ” the man crowed. “It’s a very well thought out plan, wouldn’t you agree?”

“No, you misunderstand, ” she corrected. “I’m in awe because that is the DUMBEST thing I’ve ever heard.”

“…Wh-what!? You dare to moc-”

“No, be quiet. You need to understand just how stupid you’ve been.”

The scientist’s eye twitched as Kim lazily began counting off on her fingers.

“First of all, you tried to poach Pokémon. In Kanto. Y’know, the region that popularized the Pokéball? Why not just catch the Pokémon and take them back to Holon?”

“Err…well…”

“Second, no offense to Rattata or anything, but I’d go for something a lot more rare, like a Dratini or something, if I was going to break the law.”

<She’s got a point, > one of the Rattata agreed.

<Yeah, we’re almost as common as Zubat.>

“And finally, if you were planning to stay hidden until you arranged for a vehicle to pick you up, why on Earth would you spread rumors about the island you’re staying on?” She folded her arms, giving him a look of pure condescension. “Seriously, did you get your plan from a Scooby Doo episode?”

“SILENCE!” the scientist screamed. “I can’t expect a child like you to understand the subtle nuances of my brilliance!”

“You cut down a tree, shaped it into a dragon’s face, and tried to scare off people on an island that’s deserted, ” Kim deadpanned. “You’re about as subtle as a rampaging Tauros.”

“Enough!” he glowered, his eyes brimming with hate. “I will not tolerate these insults any longer! Carnivine!”

From out of the brush came a plant-like Pokémon, its mouth similar in shape to a Venus flytrap. Upon seeing Kim and the others, it opened its jaws hungrily, a small flame burning in the back of its throat.

“It’s feeding time!”
