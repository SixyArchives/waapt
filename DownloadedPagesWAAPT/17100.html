427492 Luke99248th Nov 2014 05:12:29 PM from England Get RP Mod , Relationship Status: Is that a kind of food?

Goldenrod City - Abandoned Warehouse District

Like most cities, some of the many many properties that had been built over the years had decayed to the point where they had become usable, but not to the point where they had fallen apart completely. Now long condemned, almost all were now going through the process of being reclaimed by nature. It wasn't uncommon of Pokémon of all sorts to make their homes inside.

But there was one they all avoided, for fear of it's occupant: One of the most monstrous Raticate you'd ever see. The local feral Pokémon spoke of her in hushed tones. They said her fangs were sharp enough to gnaw through steel, and that when the local pest control sent someone to exterminate her, it was the humans who had ended up dead by the end of the day. And to add insult to injury, she had eaten their corpses afterwards.

All rumors, perhaps. But what was true, was that this Raticate was said to have horded a whole host of scraps of food she had snatched from humans in the city. Apparently more than enough to feed even a flock of Murkrow for days! Few were brave enough - or foolish enough - to attempt to snatch any off her, though.

But there was a Pokémon that was perhaps a bit of both.

She had managed to slip in through a small gap in one of the boarded up windows at the front of the decaying property. It was only just big enough to squeeze her thin form through.

Quietly, she crawled down the wall to the wooden floor, sneaking almost silently on all fours towards an old door into the front room, where the Raticate and her veritable treasure trove were said to lay.

The intruder in question was a small lizard Pokémon. Her bright yellow skin was contrasted by the deep black scales that covered her head, feet and the tip of her tail. A pair of frills hand from each side of her head, which could be fanned out for a startling display. She lacked a nose or any sort of snout, but possessed a small mouth and a pair of large, blue eyes.

Peeking carefully around the edge of the door, she eyed the interior of the room. Apart from a few broken pieces of furniture and soggy looking cardboard boxes, there was a large pile of scraps in the very center of the room - placed so the the few beams of light the shined through the window would fall on it exactly.

She had found her target. But the problem was, the Raticate was sleeping right on top of it. As if she was a dragon guarding her hoard. But of course, much less grand.

Rats. Literally.
