Locke turned to him, confused."What?"

"<Nothing! Nothing...dammit, that wasn't supposed to get through the tele-link.>"

Locke turns to the broad, stocky, Johnathan. Or was it Morgan?"Could you please move Ms.Ivy away a bit, right about" points a feet to his left."There."

The big burly man rushed to do as he said, roughly, but not unkindly pulling Ms.Ivy up to her feet."Let me go! Ah demand that yo unhand me, sir!"

And he does so, after she now stands where Locke wishes her too. He releases her, and she huffs, straightening her dress.

Locke's eyes connected with Jim's, and for a second, neither looked away. Then Jim coughed, lowered his gaze, and asked:"N-n-now what?"

Locke: "Mr Logan, (ignoring groans of possibly incorrect naming) you would say that you got hit with a blunt object, right?"

Mr Logan:"Y-yeah..."

Locke turned to Ms.Ivy, and then the others."Now, the order in which you were found here by the other were; Ivy found by Jim, and Jim found by Morgan, correct?"

"Yeah, but our names are.."

Locke steps beside Logan. There lay, beside him, behind where Ivy was laying against, a small cavern, something of a cubbyhole in the rocks. He reached inside, and after someone shimmying, pulled out a bloody baseball bat.

The others gasped, but Ivy remained frozen, unblinking. Locke gestured at her with the bloody bat."You were the first one here, and you haven't moved at all in the whole time that I've seen you, you remained in front of that hole the whole time. That means you must have known about it."

With a sudden move, he swiped one of her opera gloves off of her slender hands. She tried to snatch it back, but to no avail. Locke was taller than her.

He inspected the glove, and then turned it inside out. When he did so, they all saw that it was stained with blood.

"Or perhaps", he approached her, faces only a few inches apart;"You must have done it."
