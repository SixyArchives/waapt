118520 Crewe10th Dec 2011 07:18:54 PM from Gravity Falls, Oregon Get RP Mod
Li'l Punk
Crewe is completely unresponsive, as she's already deep in the Mindscape.

Deep In Said Mindscape

In the gym room, Battler and Trainer, looking shaken, are dealing with this the only way they know how: yelling at each other.

"This is all your fault!" Battler puts her hands on her hips and glares at Trainer.

"How can it be my fault if I'm part of Crewe and an external party caused it?! Nitwit!" Trainer glares back, looking down on the younger aspect.

"Dipstick!"

"Numskull!"

"Girls!" ??? strides over, looking stern. "Your terrible insults are not solving anything. Now, I believe you are needed." She motions with one hand towards the door to the hallway.

The two younger aspects look at each, then hurry through the door, down the hallway, and into Crewe's room, not pausing to notice that the obsidian door is open by a hair. ???, however, pauses and looks at it, pursing her lips, then continues down the hall.

Battler and Trainer pull open the last door on the left side of the hall, and run inside. The room is easily the largest in the Mindscape, square in shape. Shelves line the room floor to ceiling, and more shelves divide the room into aisles. The shelves are full of Pokeballs of all types.

In the center, sitting cross-legged on the floor, Crewe sits, holding her head in her hands. Battler and Trainer kneel down next to her, making comforting noises and patting her back. They all suddenly freeze as a loud creak is heard throughout the Mindscape.

???, with a hint of a smirk on her face, appears in the doorway.

"I believe someone would like to speak with you..." she says, stepping to the side and gesturing.

Besides the oldest aspect is a very, very young Sneasel. She's much smaller than the average Sneasel, not coming up to ???'s knees. With both arms, she clutches a worn Mew plushie to her chest. Slowly, hesitantly, she walks over to the three in the center of the room. They stare at her, frozen. Stopping in front of Crewe, the Sneasel tilts her head. Crewe slowly reaches out one hand towards her. Battler catches her breath, biting her lip. Trainer frowns, hands clenching and unclenching into fists.

The Sneasel smiles and lets go of the plushie to pat Crewe's hand gently. She points at herself.

"Glint," she whispers, still smiling. Her smile fades slightly and she points to Crewe. "Glint."

Crewe shakes her head, drawing her hand back.

"No. No, no, no. You aren't me."

Glint frowns and points again at Crewe, now more authoritatively.

"Glint."

She turns and points at the wall opposite them. The shelves and door suddenly disappear, the wall turning into a large screen. Battler, Trainer, and Crewe watch, still frozen in place, as a scene begins playing.

The picture—fuzzy, as if through the eyes of someone not wearing their glasses—shows mostly whiteness. Snow and hail swirl down, all but obscuring a few dark trees. A coldness descends on the room, causing Battler to start coughing, almost choking. Glint places a hand on her knee, and the noises stop. Crewe hugs herself, her eyes not moving from the screen.

Suddenly, the whiteness starts getting much closer, the vague image of trees suddenly replaced by pure white. A heaviness takes hold of the limbs of all in the room, causing Trainer and Battler to sag against each other. The two clutch at each other, a wild look coming into their eyes. But Crewe doesn't move, just taking a deep breath.

Suddenly, a pink flash illuminates the area, and the brief, whispering impression of a giggle winds through the room. The scene goes abruptly black, then slowly fades in again, now showing the faces, though very fuzzy, of Crewe's (adoptive) mother and father.

The screen disappears, the shelves and door replacing it. Crewe turns her deer-in-the-headlights stare on the baby Sneasel, who nods sadly and points at her.

"Glint."
