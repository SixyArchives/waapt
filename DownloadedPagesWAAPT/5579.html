And now for something completely different.

Iron Island

We had a bit of trouble getting to Iron Island, namely the fact that we were lacking visibility. Of course, this led us to only one recourse. Mainly, stealing some small motorboat and running it all the way to Iron Island. Along the way, I was once again reminded of my hatred of the cold by the wind whipping past as we propelled it north. Luckily, it was a pretty short trip, like it always is. Though part of it was punctuated with me telling Amanita to be more careful when fighting indoors, or better yet to have led the Spiritomb out before engaging them.

Iron Island hadn't changed much from the last time I was here. The wild mons (though wisped), the abandoned machinery, it was pretty much the same. I wondered if I'd run into Riley and his Lucario, who'd helped me quite a bit on my first visit. While I'm sure that if push came to shove I'd explore the depths with my Pokemon alone, as I quite often do, Riley being around made it a lot easier. It was around then that I got the most peculiar feeling. (One that I normally associated with the presence of a Tear.)

"<Ooh, a mine.>", Vanilluxe said, the icicle's voice filled with mirth at going on yet another adventure, which is something I've always enjoyed in people and Pokemon alike. "<Is it like the Wellspring Cave, Tagg?>", Audilith said, her crystals giving off flashes that I assume meant interest. "Well, it used to be one, but now it's mainly a training center for Gym Leaders. It's not as wet as over there though.", I said, mentally readying myself for what we might run into inside. Spiritomb of any variety? A particularly angry Steelix? Perhaps even the wizard who shot me in the back and brought this twilight, ready to try to test our mettle in some matter? I knew this as my mons and I walked down into the depths, my injury from a week or so ago giving a dull ache, as if to remind me of what happened that night. Whatever comes, do your worst. It won't stop us. Never has, never will.
