Iron Island, bottommost level

We reached the bottom after taking a bit of a lunch break, the meal comprised of what we still had, hunted, or outright stole. As we ate, I was kind of reminded of the game acronym that had served me well both while doing research and during the Battle of Celadon itself. S.T.A.L.K.E.R. Scavengers, Trespassers, Adventurers, Loners, Killers, Explorers, and Robbers. Squeaky clean and/or nice, no. Will it help you survive in the wilds and in a warzone? Yes.

We looked around and surprisingly there the Tear was, sitting by it's lonesome on a pedastal of sorts. I walked over and touched it, my body once again host to that strange feeling of both warm and cold at the same time. "Well, that's another one down.", I said, my body having glowed pink for a short time, "Let's get out of here." It was then I felt a rumble coming from the ground. "Well, that can't be good", I thought as my mons and I took up combat positions.

Soon enough, the head of a Steelix emerged from the earth, the rest of the metal worm's 30 foot long body coming out soon after as the Iron "Snake" (A misnomer, both morphs are worms as stated in an article of my colleague's.) started looking around. Nudi started shaking and said, "<I hope that he doesn't see us.>" I had to agree, seeing as that a fight was something we'd like to avoid, even if I had several mons capable of taking the worm down in no time if necessary. From what I could tell though, the Steelix was indeed probably not a Soul, since he happened to be in wisp form. Either way, with our job done here, we decided to leave the cave posthaste.
