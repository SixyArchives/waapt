Desert Resort.

| Pi and Zeta are camping out.

| Zeta has once more entered the meditative state, accessing the Land of the People.

| After experimenting during the nights she spent in Village Bridge, Zeta has discovered the following:

| That the Land of the People is an eldritch location, existing only in the metaphysical, and representing her mind.

| That each of the Four Sisters are different parts, or Aspects, of herself.

| That each of the keys allow her to see different Aspects of the Land of the People itself.

| That one is the City of the Torch, in which prophesy might be found.

| That one is the Jungle of the Spear, in which memories might be recalled.

| That one is the Mountains of the Veil, in which clarity might be achieved.

| Now, at last, she has one last experiment to perform.

The Land of the People: The Desert of the Empty Hand

| Zeta, upon arriving, reaches out to grasp the torch.

| As the ripples spread, signalling a shift in Aspect, she grabs hold of the spear.

| This sends out further ripples, augmenting those created by the torch.

| Within the same moment, she dons the veil.

| As the third set of ripples oscillate out into the world, they begin to form a feedback loop. They waver more and more violently, as well as faster and faster.

| Soon, it reaches a point where Zeta finds herself compelled to close her eyes, or be shaken to pieces.

| At last, she can feel a sense of stillness.

| She opens her eyes.

The Land of the People: The Void of the All

| She is standing in a white nothingness. There is no apparent delineation between the solid mass she is standing on and the gaseous matter in which she can move.

| The only features she can see are doors. Thousands upon thousands of doors, stretching off as far as the eye can see.

| They vary in size, in shape, in material, in color, in age.

Zeta: What...?

Seal: They are entry ways to others' minds.

Zeta: Really?

Seal: Yes.

| Zeta notices another detail.

| Each and every one of the doors are chained shut, with each chain being closed by a padlock.

| The chains and padlocks vary as much as the doors do.

| Most of them are of laughably large size.

Zeta: And the locks?

Seal: Those represent your connection to the person whose mind each door represents. The smaller the lock, the closer they are to you. After all, you can't be allowed to just stroll on in to the mind of a complete stranger!

| Only one of the doors has no lock or chain at all.

| Zeta approaches it.

| It appears to be made of what she could only describe as solidified light.

| Above the door, inscribed into the frame is the name "Pi Cuspis Warden."

Zeta: And... this is Pi's mind?

Seal: Yes. After all, he trusts you with his very heart and soul.

| Zeta is struck speechless.

| She reaches out, as if to open the door. But then she hesitates, and pulls away.

Zeta: I need to think about this. The sheer implications of these doors are staggering.

Seal: Do as you wish.

Seal: One last warning about this place:

Seal: Entering the Void of the All is extremely taxing, on your body, your mind, and your soul. It should never be done lightly. Use it only if your situation is most dire, and even then, pause to give the idea some thought, before following through.

Zeta: I understand.

| She releases the torch, spear, and veil, leaving the Land of the People altogether.

Desert Resort

| Zeta opens her eyes. She can feel the strain that the Seal described, in the form of a powerful mental, physical, and emotional fatigue. Seeking comfort, she moves over to where Pi lies, sleeping, and lays down next to him.

| The effort of moving burns what little strength she has left, and she falls into deep, blessed, dreamless sleep.
