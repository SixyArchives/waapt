A house in Twinleaf Town

-a female Corphish with a rag tied about her neck like a cape bustles about a sink, blowing soft bubbles at dirty dishes while a woman working beside her dries and stacks them. After a while, the woman picks up the dishes and puts them away, and then walks towards into the living room. The Corphish hops down from the counter and bustles after her, waving her claws about-

Woman: And Donna, I think the upstairs windows could use cleaning, dont you? And then we can finish with the gardening.

-she sits down in a chair by a coffee table and flips through a magazine. The Corphish climbs up onto the table using a seemingly custom-made step ladder. She begins blowing bubbles onto the table and pulls the rag from around her neck to dry. As she works, a stray bubble accidentally knocks over a framed picture of a young girl holding a Piplup. The woman starts at the sound and glares at the Corphish-

Woman: Donna! Be more careful, would you?

-the Corphish jumps and nods, then carefully picks up the picture. The woman sighs and pats her-

Woman: Im sorry, Donna, you know I couldnt manage without you. Its the date, thats all.

-the Corphish bobs her head and makes sympathetic noises, then gets back to cleaning the table. The woman looks at the picture and sighs again-

Woman: Donna, would you bring me the note again? I think I can manage to clean up my own mess for a few minutes. You make things too easy for me, honestly.

-the Corphish pats the womans hand with her claw, then climbs down and scuttles off-

Woman: Be careful on the stairs, Donna, theyre still a bit wet!

-she picks up the picture and looks at it, sighing again. Suddenly, theres a knock at the door. The woman looks up, then puts the picture back down on the table and gets up-

Woman: Coming, coming!

-she goes over to the door and opens it, to reveal a rather familiar teenage girl, looking somewhat scrawny and disheveled, with a young Sneasel perched on her shoulder and a sleeping Absol kit in her arms. She gives the woman an awkward smile-

Uh hi, Mom. Happy Mothers Day.

-the woman stands frozen for a moment, staring at the girl-

Woman: H Helen?

-the girls blinks, then runs her free hand through her hair-

Geez, been a while since Ive been called that. I have too many names nowadays.

Sneasel: <And theyre all pretty dumb.>

Shut up, Darek. So, uh, Mom can I come in?

-the woman seems to come to herself and takes a step to the side to open up the doorway-

Woman: Of course, Helen! Come right in!

Thanks, Mom.

-the two walk in and somewhat awkwardly move to the coffee table, where they sit down across from each other. Crewe shifts the sleeping kit to her lap-

Mom: So, Helen what brings you here?

Well, yknow, its Mothers Day, and I figured I havent been here in a while, and I havent talked to you since I sent you that letter did you ever get it, by the way?

Mom:Oh, yes. I got your letter.

Did you believe it?

Mom: Well, you you certainly look like youve been living in the wilderness for a while.

Yeah

-the two sit awkwardly for a time. The Sneasel fidgets on her shoulder, and the Absol kit yawns-

Mom: So, Helen who are your friends?

Oh! Well, this here is Darek. Hes kinda like my little brother.

Darek: waves a paw cheerfully- <Hiya! You dont look like Crewe at all.>

Shut up, Darek. And, uh, this baby is called Pumba. His dad is a really good guy, but he asked me to find a good home for him. And I, uh, I gave up on raising baby Pokemon a while ago. Except Darek. Im kinda stuck with him.

Darek: <Hey! I am not a baby! I can do stuff that would blow your mind.>

Yeah, yeah, youre super awesome amazing sauce, I get it. Anyway, Mom, I was I was wondering I mean, I know you dont, well

-she trails off, looking down at the Absol kit and petting him. Her mother looks down at her hands in her lap-

-at that moment, Donna comes scuttling down the hallway, a letter clutched gently in her claws. She blinks at the stranger in her living room-

Donna: <Well now, whats all this, whats all this?>

Mom: Oh! Donna, youve found it! Set it down on the table, Id like you to meet someone.

Donna: <Yesm, though I must say this old thing has gotten a bit dusty, and I just cleaned the table.>

-she scuttles over and climbs onto the coffee table, setting down the letter and turning to face Crewe and the two Pokemon. Darek peers at her curiously, Crewe looks rather confounded, and Pumba slumbers on peacefully-

Whabuhwho

Donna: addressing Darek- <Youre a dusty little mongrel, arent you? Keep your muddy paws off the carpet, if you please. Would you like something to eat?>

Darek: <I am not dusty! Ive been in Crewes bag most of the trip here, fixing all the holes. What kinda food dyou have?>

Mom, youyou have a Pokemon?!

Mom: Well, yes, I do. Helen, dear, this is Donna, my helper. Some time after you left, I decided well, I decided that I could use a little help around the house, and Mrs. Brownyou know Mrs. Brown, across the streetshe convinced me that a Water type might be tidy enough to keep around, to help out with the chores. I was going to get a Piplup, you know, but I remembered that yours was terribly clumsy sometimeswhatever happened to that Piplup of yours?

-Crewe looks vaguely guilty-

Uh

Mom:  Anyway, I looked into smaller Water Pokemon, and I found Donna here at a local pet shop. Shes been a real help around the house, and always such a pleasure.

-Donna scuttles over and pats her hand with her claw-

Donna: <So kind you are, dear. Now, little weasel child, whos your friend?>

Darek: <Oh, this sCrewe. Shes like, your persons kid or sometin. Apparently its complicated, cause Crewe wont tell me whats up whenever I ask. She snaps sometin bout a Mew and a cave and sall weird.>

Donna: < Well then. Any child of this house is welcome! I get something to eat. Be right back, dear.>

-she pats Crewes mom again and scuttles off towards the kitchen-

Mom: Oh! Now wheres she going off to?

She said she was gonna get something to eat. So, uh, Mom youre okay with Pokemon?

Mom: Well perhaps I was a bit hasty when I outlawed them from the house. But, you know, your father

Yeah, I know.

Darek: <What— >

Shut up, Darek.

-he pouts-

So, Mom would you could you look after Pumba?

-they both look down at the sleeping Absol, who yawns-

Mom: He is somewhat furry, and hes sure to shed everywhere. And when he grows up, hell be a real handful, I bet.

Yeah, maybe, but Absol are real smart, and hell be as loyal a friend as you could ever want.

Mom: He is rather cute, isnt he?

-she sighs-

Mom: At this rate, Ill be a regular Trainer, wont I?

-Crewe hops up, snatching Pumba up to her chest-

Mom! Thats great!

Mom: Dont get too excited, dear. Im not about to start fights with them anytime soon.

Thats fine!

-she offers the sleeping Pumba to her mom, who gingerly takes hold of the kit and cradles it, quickly looking more comfortable as he snuggles into her arms. She looks down at him warmly-

Mom: Ill have to find a bed for him. What do Absol eat?

Berries should be okay, I think. If there are any Skitty or whatever around town, they can probably teach him to hunt when he gets older.

Darek: <I could have taught him to hunt if you would let him hang around with us.>

No, Darek.

Mom: What? What did he say? This speaking with Pokemon ability of yours is extremely odd, Helen.

He didnt really say anything important. And yeah, it is a little weird, I guess.

Mom: Yes, well how long are you in town?

Not that long I have a few promises that need keeping. Im kind of on the trail of someone.

Mom: Oh, dear.

No, nothing like that! Im not a police officer or whatever. Im looking for someone for a friend. Shes proving a little difficult to track down. Shes nearby, though, Im sure of it. I was hoping to find her today.

Mom: Oh. So youll be leaving, then.

-Crewe scratches at her arm awkwardy-

Yeah

Mom: clears her throat and sets Pumba down on her chair- Well, then. Ill have Donna make you a sandwich. How long has it been since youve had civilized food?

Uh a pretty long time, actually. Thanks, Mom.

Mom: Oh, its no trouble.

-the two stand awkwardly for a moment, then Crewe steps forward and hugs her-

Im Im sorry, Mom. For everything. I love you.

Mom: hugs her as well- So am I, Helen. Youre always welcome home. I love you, too.

-they stand like this for a few moments, Darek slipping into Crewes backpack. After a bit, Donna scuttles out carrying a tray of cheese and crackers in her claws-

Mom: steps back- Oh, Donna, just in time! Could you make Helen a sandwich, please? She needs to leave quickly, but she cant go without tasting your cooking.

Donna: <Leaving already? Seems a bit rude to me! And to her own mother. Yes, yes, Ill make a sandwich. I daresay she could stand to stay a bit longer, with all the worry shes put you through!>

-she scuttles off, leaving the tray on the table. Darek quickly descends upon it and starts eating-

-after a while, Donna returns with a sandwich, and Crewe picks up the Sneasel, who takes his place on his shoulder-

Well Im off. I gotta find the Spitfire.

Darek: <I still dont like the fire part.>

Bye, Mom.

Mom: Good-bye, Helen. Come back sometime.

I Ill try. Happy Mothers Day, Mom.

Mom: Donna, I hate to ask it of you, but would you put the letter away? I dont need to read it anymore.

-the Corphish sighs, scuttles over to the table, and picks up the letter and her rag. She begins scuttling off towards the stairs-

Mom: calling after her- Ill be up in a few minutes to clean the windows!

-she bends up and picks up the tray of cracker crumbs, pausing to run a hand over the frame of the picture, then turns and walks into the kitchen, pausing to pat Pumba as she passes. The Absol yawns and stretches, then curls up again, still sleeping. Smiling and humming softly to herself, she leaves the room-
