169114 MindGamer17th Jun 2012 08:10:41 PM from In your mind. Obviously. Get RP Mod
Help... My hand's stuck.

The Hospital

Ken: Nnngh... What... Why... Why did you... Go...

Inside Ken's dream, 12 years ago in Lavaridge Town

...A hazy fog descends across Ken's dreamscape as for a brief time, he is brought back to his human form. Brought back across the 12 years it's been since that day. Brought back across the dimensions unknown... To remember one event from his childhood.

The vision wasn't clear. Ken's own memory was flawed at best, staring out from a child's eyes at a world he could not comprehend at the time. 5 years old, and clutching the hand of his mother, the bright and fresh-faced Nurse Joy of Lavaridge. Hidden behind the counter, he'd watch on as his mother would happily accept any and all Pokemon, regardless of their condition, and admit them to be healed. Some were easy to help, using the Machine to revitalize their bodies. Others weren't so easy, requiring the personal touch of the Nurse herself.

In any case, Ken would toddle around the Pokemon Center day in, day out, protected by his mother's Chansey. He'd always see trainers of all kinds walking into the town, from the young to the old, male, female, and from all across the world. But... Even as he saw the trainers of the world, it was a rare event when he saw the one trainer he wanted to meet most.

His father.

As his mother told him often, Ken's father was a trainer. One traveling the globe and seeking to become the very best. But by the time he had reached Lavaridge, Ken's father was a young man. Perhaps it was timing or fate, but one way or another Ken was born less than a year after his father first breezed into Lavaridge. According to his mother, it was a miracle he'd come back to see his child born at all... But it was enough for them both.

Ken's mother didn't argue too much over his father needing to return to a life in the world. After all, he was a trainer. That didn't stop her from forcing him into marriage with a smile, though they both were happy. Of course, this was merely stories Ken was being told since his youth about his father. The only thing that Ken had faith in with his father, though, was that he'd come back every year for his birthday. One day out of the year Ken's father swore he'd do whatever it took to get back there for.

It was enough for Ken.

Then Ken's vision returned to the Lavaridge of 12 years ago, as Ken joyfully stepped out into the warm summer heat, parading around in the grass. Today was the day, the day his father would come back again. To play with him, teach him some new trick, or take him flying. Ken didn't care what... As long as he had the chance to be with him as much as possible. Pushing back his short black hair out of his eyes, Ken smiled up at the perfect blue sky above, waiting.

From that sky, a shadow over the sun, hidden from Ken suddenly formed. The shadow of a Bird Pokemon. Ken's eyes lit up with joy. He was finally back home.

Ken's memory from that point on became fuzzy once more. The time spent playing with his father seemed to blend together, a wonderful feeling.

That feeling was suddenly cut as the seemingly endless daylight fell, and the sky glistened with stars. Bedtime was called, Ken was brought back into the Center for sleep, and he knew his father would be gone in the morning.

But for once, Ken was old enough not to collapse from exhaustion instantly. In a rare moment of rebellion, the young boy clad only in fire red pajamas clambered out of bed, and snuck around the center slowly, looking for his father.

That was the first time he heard either his mother or his father yell in anger.

His memory of the argument was again confused. The very idea that his mother and father were upset with each other... Didn't make sense. He had no idea what they were really arguing about at the time... Though he could figure it out later. Shocked, horrified and scared of his parents' angry faces, Ken fled to the safety of his bed, and cuddled up with his little Growlithe plushie... Hiding from the anger.

When he woke up, Ken's mother seemed perfectly normal at first. His father had left like always, and his mother had made pancakes with Combee Honey. Ken wondered if the previous night was just a bad dream... But he soon found out it wasn't. Very quickly, Ken was being chastised and punished for not being completely respectful to his elders, to his superiors, even his equals. Even if Ken couldn't understand why yet, he was being taught to be obedient and respectful above all else. His mother also began teaching Ken far more about the Pokemon Center, and getting him to learn how to help out around the Center. Training him to become focused on medicine.

By the year's end, the little boy was dutifully rushing about trying to please his mother and be as respectful as he could, even when he had no idea where he was going... Or why. The only thing he thought might give him some clarity was his father... And he was far away, already on another journey.

Hospital

Ken awoke with a start, leaning up in his bed before gazing about at his new friends all around. It was just a dream. A memory. Slowly, Ken's mind began to remember all that had happened... The castle. The dojo. The Medicham. ...And now his new body. Looking down on his new feminine form, all Ken could do right then was give a low sigh, closing his eyes and hugging his legs for a second. Wishing he could be hugging his plushie right then. Or his mother. Or his father. Someone.
