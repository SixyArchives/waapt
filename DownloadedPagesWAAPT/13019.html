Lucy smiles upon seeing her. ~Psyche, darling, welcome to... Actually I'm not sure where "here" is, but here.~

Aiura blinks and looks over. "Lu... Lucille, is that you?" She rushes over and gives her oldest daughter a hug.

Lucy tears up. ~Mother? Wait... Does this mean I'm dead?~

Psyche blinks. ~Dead? I certainly hope not. That means someone disturbed my trance, and they're going to be a grease stain on the grass of Lostlorn Forest when the Gothitelle find them.~

Aiura smiles. "No, dears, you aren't dead. Merely sleeping."

Psyche glares at Aiura. ~You're one of those irksome aspects in my head. I don't need any paranoid ramblings, or-~

Aiura shakes her head. "No, dear, not an aspect. It's about time one of my descendants became self-aware enough to enter mindscapes. It's been almost 200 years since the last one. Dear child, I am Aiura Cain, former warlord of Illusio. And you, dear child, are the first descendant that's actualized as Psychic in a long, long time. Aurics, sure, several, but Psychics are the proud, the few!"

Psyche holds a hand up. ~Save it. I'm sorry, Lady Aiura, but I was attempting to reconstruct my mind library after a psychic onslaught a couple of weeks ago. It's high time I returned to doing so.~

Lucy sets a hand upon her shoulder. ~Please stay. For a little while at least. It's not very often three generations of Psychics, 600 years removed get to have a conversation.~

Psyche sighs. ~Fine. Ten minutes. Then, dearest ancestors, I shall have to take my leave.~

Aiura smiles. "Cheeky. I like this one. Lucanus handles all of the Aurics, but he tells me they're all megalomaniacal, spineless brats, or a combination of the two."

Psyche counts on her fingers. ~That would sum up every Auric I know except my mother.~

Aiura's smile droops. "A female Auric? That's interesting. Most Aurics in our line have been male. Lucanus didn't tell me about her."

Lucy shrugs. ~Maybe Father didn't know.~

Aiura narrows her eyes. "Maybe... I shall definitely have to ask him at some point."
