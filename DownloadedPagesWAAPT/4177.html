After a few awkward moments Hecate passes by glaring at a few of the sulkier Zoroarks, a small path clears and an elderly and frail Zoroark leads a small knot of young Zoroark and Zorua towards Crewe and Hecate. Crewe studies them carefully, frowning. Besides the evidence of malnutrition many of the pack have, they are all undersized at the best and downright runty at worst. The oldest, and one of the smallest of the evolved, breaks out of the group and runs over.

Hecate visibly relaxes and the two embrace, the young Zoroark burying his face in her shoulder.

<Mother!> he cries happily.

<Swift,> Hecate murmurs back, gently stroking his head. <Have you been taking care of your siblings?>

<Yes, Mother,> he says, breaking the embrace and stepping back. The rest of the children run over, and Hecate spends a few minutes greeting them. She picks up two of the Zoruas and turns towards Crewe, holding out the babies.

<Children, I'd like you to meet someone,> she says softly. Crewe hesitantly steps forwards and accepts the two. They sniff her, then happily settle down in her arms.

"Hi, there," she whispers, gently petting one of their heads.

<This is Vonda,> Hecate says, laying a paw on one of their heads. <The other is Jetta. They are twins. Their parents were killed just before our battle here.>

Crewe nods slowly, smiling down at the young Pokemon. After a few moments, she sets them on the ground and they run back towards their siblings. Hecate introduces the other children. There are three Zoroark in all, and five Zoruas. All are orphans. The oldest, Swift, had his father die before he was even born, and his mother died while he was an egg. The two youngest are Vonda and Jetta, the twins.

After a little while, Hecate says good-bye to her family, and nods at the elderly Zoroark that brought them.

<Take care of them, Darwin.>

He smiles to her and touches his forehead with one paw in a salute. <Of course, Chief.>

Darwin and the children leave, a few of them turning to wave, and Swift holding the twins and speaking softly to them. Hecate raises one paw and holds it there for a short while after they disappear, then drops it and whirls around.

<We're leaving,> she says shortly to Crewe, then turns back to the pack. <I do not know who is now in charge. But let them know that their position is not in danger... or at least, not from me.>

A few pack members shift uncomfortably, but Hecate ignores them and strides back towards Anville Town. Crewe hurries after her, feeling the accusing stares of the Zoroark pack on the back of her neck even after they've disappeared from view.

On the train ride back to Nimbasa, Crewe doesn't say anything but watches Hecate carefully. The Zoroark sits with her paws resting neatly on her lap, staring out the window with an intense expression. Once they reach the station, they exit the train, but Crewe grabs her arm and pulls her into the corner, speaking in a low voice.

"None of them are yours," she says. Hecate nods once.

"You take care of them, and call them your own children." Hecate nods again.

"... Why? This is not the Hecate I know." Crewe narrows her eyes and looks at Hecate intently. The Zoroark meets her eyes briefly, then breaks the eye contact, looking just above Crewe's head.

<You know me as humans have made me, including yourself. That—>she jerks her head to the side. <—is how Zoroarks have made me.> She pauses for a moment. <The children were abandoned. They likely would have died, or been killed. Food is not plentiful for most, and children need to be looked after and defended.> She stops once more, and Crewe starts thinking she's done talking before she keeps talking. <I have also been abandoned. And I would not wish that fate on any. Least of all children of my own kind.>

She fixes Crewe with a sharp glare. <Is that a good enough answer for you? I am going to find my... team mates... and prepare to leave.>

She whirls around and starts walking away, then looks over her shoulder. <I will not be welcome there from now on. Do not expect to see my children again. But you can trust Darwin to take care of them. He is... a very dear friend of mine, and of theirs.>

With that, she continues walking. Crewe hurries after her.
