Schrodinger's Date - Happening to Come Some Unspecified Time After the Last Entry in the Prior Notebook

<Lardna:

We finally have radio contact with the outside world again. If not all places. Currently we have channels open with PEFE branches in Goldenrod, Cerulean, Slateport, and Icirrus. Most others appear to still be down at the moment.

Here's everything that we know about what's out there so far:

- Johto

The fighting here's apparently over, and there's a transitional government overseeing martial law in Johto at the moment. The local infrastructure appears to be thoroughly shot on all fronts in many places, a number of places have no electricity or water barring localized generators and wells. What we learned about the Goldenrod Colony from the floatilla is very much true, and the floatilla that we ran into is apparently but a drop in a draconic bucket of mons fleeing Johto in all directions. There've apparently been calls in some circles to extend the expulsion order to the rest of Johto.

Mercifully, there's some decent souls that have raised some loud protests over the whole idea. When I heard who some of they were, I think that my lower beak made physical contact with the floor. The main opponents to the expansion of the expulsion order are apparently Generals "Cinder, " "Lake, " and "Nadia". Nadia? The little Bagon I left with Luke? She's a general now?

If I ever meet her again, I'll have to ask her about what happened out there.

- Kanto

The local occupiers were overthrown just a couple weeks ago. So things are still very much up in the air at the moment. The infrastructure situation appears to be similar to Johto. The government there's attempting to re-establish contact with other regions and locate the head of state and any next of kin. The transitional civic government is hastily trying to sort things out in order to put normal cops on the beat again.

Thankfully, there's no reports of large-scale massacres like there are from Johto. And apparently the revolt that overthrew the occupation started from a mutiny of local draconic mons who decided that they had enough. What on earth happened there? Slightly concerning blurb I heard over the channel suggests that the government may be entertaining plans to deport all draconic mons that can't prove that they were born in Kanto. By Lugia, I hope that that's just a rumor...

- Hoenn

Things here seem the brightest. Local infrastructure appears to be mostly intact with the very visible exception of electricity and fuel distribution. Which as you can imagine, makes getting from Point A to Point B annoying for anyone who can't fly.

The local grunts are apparently humored fairly well relative to other regions, seeing as they're more or less local laughingstocks. The Hoennese Civil Government has apparently been pushing to shut its borders to immigration from any further dragon mons though. Especially after word of the flow of refugees from Johto became more widely known.

- Unova

Things on this front sound... Chaotic, to say the least. Dragonspiral Tower apparently started out on the side of the revolt, but changed sides within days of the bombs going off. Considering who lives there, it would make sense that the locals would have an abrupt change of heart (and if they wore them, they'd likely need an equally abrupt change of pants). They apparently took over Icirrus, seeing as the rest of Unova was being swallowed up by the revolt.

Unlike the other regions we've heard about, there seem to be two main internal factions here that don't seem to get along (not counting Resh and Zek's lackeys). Apparently, Qoriik's goons were rather untypical in terms of demography, the only other faction that made as extensive of use of non-Dragon Type "dragons" is a faction led mostly by Hydreigon that's headquartered in Opelucid. Supposedly, they got desperate enough for recruits that they wound up causing a noticeable decline in the Sandile and Scraggy line populations in the Desert Kingdom thanks to all the mercenary postings, and they're allowing non-draconic mons to serve provided that they have some sort of relation with a "dragon" mon. The other camp is apparently more typical, composed almost entirely of dragon-typed "dragons". They appear to be lead mostly by Haxorus and headquartered in Driftveil. (Go figure why they don't get along!)

Descriptions of on-the-ground conditions are similarly all over the board. The Haxorus are apparently using the same shtick of "mass internal deportations" that happened in Johto and Kanto. The Hydreigon... Surprisingly seem to be attempting to make as few waves as possible. Infrastructure in areas controlled by them is said to be very intact, and there appears to be decent freedom of movement. Icirrus is the little island of neutrality in all of this, they were apparently cut from the power grid, though Zekrom appears to have been doing part-time work as a municipal generator.

The matter of who's fighting who is similarly confusing. There's some sort of resistance that's opposed to both factions, and the two camps more or less got into open hostilities with each other last week. Also, I must be hearing things, but apparently two of the highest ranking resistance leaders are Mike and Richard (the annoying fox, not Tagg). How on earth did that happen?

Oh, almost forgot to mention. The entire north of Unova's apparently been buried under some freaky cold snap. Official word from Icirrus is that "the gods have other ways of voicing their displeasure", whatever that means.

- Sinnoh

The big wildcard at the moment. We literally know nothing about what's going on up there.

Tangent's calling us for some sort of contingency meeting first thing tomorrow morning. I wonder what's going on?

>

<Moogle: "Eh? What did this 'Tangent' find?">

The Next Entry

<Lardna: "Let's see here..."

Went over morning debriefing today with Tangent, and he pointed out something that I probably should have thought more about. Namely, that thanks to fate, we might just happen to be the most influential group of mons on the face of this planet.

Namely, we control knowledge of the workings of a food distribution system that at once holds the potential to keep what was once human society from unraveling at the seams in spite of the new hurdles in transportation and communication. And at the same time, a potential tool with which to help mons trying to overthrow their local region's occupation to coalesce.

Gummis.

Tangent proposed that we use our knowledge of these things. Parts. Schematics. Recipes. In order to try and control what's going on out there beyond this rock more. So that more mons don't have to go through what those mons from the derelicts did. He's been organizing us into groups, to attempt to handle different regions.

Initially, I was going to sign up for the group responsible for Kanto... But I thought that perhaps it might be for the best to join the group headed for Unova. They're still under the burden of occupation, after all...

And... It's what Tagg would have wanted to do if he were here.
