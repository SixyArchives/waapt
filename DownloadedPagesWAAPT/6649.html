<Lardna:

Talked with some of the floatilla mons from yesterday. Things are still fuzzy, but from what we can gather, they're from Johto, and for a lack of a better term, things have been getting unpleasant there. The Dratini line members appear to be predominantly from the Dragons' Den and its surroundings. A lot of them seem rather loath to talk about what happened, but whatever it was, it sounds like a bloodbath.

A lot of the younger mons appear to be rather badly shaken from the whole ordeal of being forced to leave. Some of them simply don't have parents, they're either dead or separated with no foreseeable way of making contact with them in the future. Kammy spent the better part of today trying to cheer a group of them up. We spotted a small number of non-draconic mons in their midst... They're mostly friends, family, trainers and the like. A lot of them seem rather bitter, apparently they were hoping that they'd somehow be able to prevent this from happening to their respective draconic acquaintances...

I can't help but worry about Richard (no, not the annoying Zoroark). He set off months ago on his own. Something about him changed after DS died. He blamed himself for what happened, and became increasingly adamant on going off to "fix things"... I hope he is alright. Wherever he is.

The Entry After That

<Lardna:

After talking with some of the floatilla mons, we're starting to piece more and more of what happened to them, though the overall picture remains hazy. From what we've heard, apparently the guys who had sided with the invaders had been put on the defensive, but had dug themselves in very deeply even after some of their counterparts had surrendered. Faced with a potentially long and ugly fight, the colony was apparently given the order to give all draconic mons its members and their allies encountered two options, regardless of who or where they were:

Permanent exile from Johto, or death.

From what we've been able to piece together, the colony didn't really have that much success at first. The times they actually carried out their threat, they apparently did so at great cost. But the raids just kept coming and coming... And eventually, there was apparently a raid on the Dragons' Den. Right where the local center of power was.

That one wasn't so unsuccessful. Apparently, the place was cut off from the rest of the outside world in the middle of a diversionary raid on Blackthorn, and Inka herself put forth the ultimatum to the den. And that any mons who wanted to challenge it would have to step over her corpse mano e mano to do so. All of the takers died, and the Dragon's Den's leadership was more or less wiped out before the others were cowed into submission. A group that was dead set on staying put in their ancestral home got their wish. Their bodies were left to bleed out into the lake there.

There's conflicting reports to whether or not the Charizific Valley was subjected to the same treatment, but the matter's probably irrelevant. After that, there weren't many dragons who dared to pass up on that "exile" option. The same choice was apparently given to all draconic mons in Nido-controlled territory away from active battlezones. There's mons here that were forced from Goldenrod, from Azalea, from Violet.

I knew that that damned book was trouble, but I never knew that it would lead those little rabbits to do something like this. May the gods have mercy on Silent, I can't imagine that he'll be sleeping well for a while.
