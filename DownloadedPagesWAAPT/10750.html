Luke had recruited help in the form of the only one of his Pokémon who still knew Strength, a certain Sandslash of his.

Quin: <I wish we could have met under better circumstances, kid. Oof, you're a heavy one.>

From the Shieldon's point of few - being carried by some strange mammalian Pokémon that she had never seen before, it was a very strange experience.

Shieldon: <It...it's fine? I dunno...>

Luke: "Thanks for your help, Quin. We're here now."

After a trip down the hall and a short ride on the elevator, the three had reached the ground floor, and we're now standing beside the main entrance. Relieved to be free of the burden, Quin placed her passenger down on the floor, gently.

Quin: <Anytime, Luke. I'll be digging around in the sand back at home. Call me if you need me again.>

And with that, the Sandslash jumped into the air, curled into a ball, then - upon landing - rolled away at high speed back the way they had came.

Of course, the Shieldon had never seen anything like it.

Shieldon: <She's really something.>

Luke: "That's Sandslash for you."

Striding over to the door, he grabbed the handle, turning back to the Shieldon.

"You ready for this?"

She nodded in return, plodding over to his feet.

Shieldon: <Go ahead.>

Luke nodded, then tugged on the handle, pulling the door open, strong sunlight streaming into the room, the Shieldon squinting in surprise at the sheer brightness.

<Ah-!>

Wondering if it could really be what she thought, the little dinosaur ran out the door...

PEFE Grounds

...And stopped just out the front door, her jaw dropping in surprise.

Shieldon: <Oh my...>

Grass! Trees! Sky! The sun! Clouds! This was more like the world she remembered! The world she had come from!

She paused to bask in it all, feeling the warm sunlight shine down on her scales, the cool breeze filling her lungs. It wasn't quite the same as it had been in the past - it didn't feel quite as warm, and the air wasn't so heavy and humid...

But it was much more comforting and familiar than those plain white boxes had been!

Speaking of which, the Shieldon turned around briefly to see just what they had been inside of, and almost jumped in shock when she saw nothing but white sheets of metal towering above and away from her further than she could see.

<We were in there?>

Having followed her outside, Luke nodded as he gazed up at the PEFE dome.

Luke: "Pretty impressive, isn't it? I can't blame you for thinking it was the whole world. It sure seems like that sometimes when you spend a long time in there."

Shieldon: <That's what it felt like to me, yeah...>

She gingerlly stepped forward down the path, taking in her surroundings, glancing up to watch a flock of silly Pidgey fly past overhead.

<I thought everything had changed...>

Luke: "You're right. A lot of things have changed. But at the same time, a lot of things have stayed the same. There's still Pokémon, for example. But now there's humans like me, too."

Shieldon: <Are there many humans?>

Luke nodded.

Luke: "Yeah. We sort of became the dominate life forms after you dinosaurs disappeared."

He frowned.

"Well, there was some malarkey with some Alakazam and Metagross in-between. That didn't end well."

Shieldon: <Wait.>

She turned back around gazing up at Luke again.

<The Dinosaurs. What happened to us? Where...did we go?>

Luke: "Well, millions of years ago, you Dinosaurs were doing fine. You ruled the world, even! But one day, this really really big asteroid fell out of the sky, and..."

He raised his hands, punching the palm of one of his hands with the other.

"It just went downhill from there. That asteroid crashing into the Earth whipped up so much dust that it blocked out the sun for years. Plants died off, the temperatures plummeted, and..."

He frowned sadly.

"And that was it for the Dinosaurs."

Looking somewhat sad again, the Shieldon sniffled.

Shieldon: <Is that...what happened to my herd?>

Luke: "Oh no, no! This all happened a long time after you and your herd were alive!"

Shieldon: <How long is how long?>

Luke: "Try twenty-five million years afterwards."

Shieldon: <...Oh...>

She breathed a sigh of relief, happy to know that her herd hadn't slowly frozen to death under an endless ashy night.

Luke: "You were lucky, though. Twenty-five million years might seem like a long time, but in the grand scheme of things, you barely just missed it."

Shieldon: <I'd hardly call twenty-five million years "barely". It's such a long time, I can't even begin to comprehend it.>

Luke: "Yeah. Me neither."
