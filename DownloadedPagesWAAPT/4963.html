124062 Crewe27th Dec 2011 10:02:09 AM from Gravity Falls, Oregon Get RP Mod
Li'l Punk
This is how the next couple days went for Crewe:

I stopped bothering her. Completely.

Yeah, I know. This is the almighty Narrator, he's lowered himself to pity to most annoying Narration subject ever? But really, I think she has enough on her mind without me screwing with her. And, to be honest? I'm kind of scared of pushing her over the edge. I mean, I know I transcend the Fourth Wall and she can't actually do anything to me (that I know of—that's a scary thought) but that night with the Pokemon? That was friggin' terrifying. I don't want to think that she might do something like that again and have it be my fault...

But anyway, you don't really care about that, do you? You want to know what actually happened. Well, here it is, free from my glorious wit:

The Streets of Fallarbor

Crewe wanders among the streets, her favorite dark green baseball cap pulled down low, the brim mostly covering her face, and her hands shoved deep into her pockets. Despite her conversation with Every, Rex, and Tangent in the Mindscape, she avoids the group. But one day, as she walks, she passes an electronic store and pauses to look at the T Vs lining the front window. A man comes on and announces some sort of promotion that she doesn't really listen to, and all of a sudden a song starts blasting from all the T Vs and speakers set up on telephone poles and roofs. The song goes like this:

http://www.youtuberepeat.com/watch?v=O0zpxC6O7nE

Crewe, taking a moment to listen to it, smiles wryly at the appropriateness, and takes another moment to wonder if it's my fault, as if I could actually affect the events in her world. Oh, sorry! Does that count as bothering her? Are you bothered?

Not particularly, but you should maybe shut up if you're afraid of bothering me.

Yeah, good idea. I'll just shut up. Ahem. Back to the story.

Crewe continues walking down the street, listening to the song as it plays on endless repeat with one ear. Absorbed in her own thoughts that fit pretty well in with the song, she doesn't even notice when a salesperson taps her on the shoulder. But when he gets tired of being ignored, and lets out a, "Miss?", well...

Crewe, startled out of her thoughts, reacts without thinking. She whirls around, eyes blazing, and lashes out with one hand.

"WHOA!" The salesman jerks back, and Crewe's hand passes just in front of his face. "Hey, what's your problem?!"

She staggers back a few feet, a wild, confused look on her face, then takes off running back towards the inn, pushing past people as if they aren't even there, the song blaring in her ears. Behind her, the salesman angrily calls the police, then lets out a "Humph!" and continues walking down the street with his nose in the air.

An Inn Across Town

Running into a different inn and booking a room, she sits on the bed and fumbles through her bag, looking for the armband, only to remember that she left it on the floor of her room in the other inn. Cursing under her breath, she checks the sky, sees that it's not night yet, and looks around for ideas. A metaphorical lightbulb lighting up over her head, she quickly hops up on the windowsill. Flashing back to hours spent in the OI, she looks for the fire escape, leaps over to it, and pulls herself over the railing.

The Roofs of Fallarbor

Climbing to the roof, she ducks behind a small wall surrounding the edge and looks over it.

Spotting several police officers talking to a few people in the streets, she starts hopping over roofs, occasionally climbing down and back up when the buildings are too far away to jump. She does this, doing her best to stay out of sight—fortunately, people seem to rarely look up—until night falls. As soon as night falls, she finds a shaft of moonlight and, in a brief shimmer, becomes a Sneasel. Confident that this is good enough to escape the police, she moves more slowly, now finding a roof devoid of small bird Pokemon.

Sitting down in the center of the roof over a closed bakery, she sits hugging her knees to her chest. With the song still blaring from every corner of the city, even with darkness, her thoughts start leaning in that direction again. Almost unconsciously, as a shaft of moonlight shifts over her, she starts phasing back and forth, eyes squeezed shut. She stays like this until dawn comes, finding her once more in Sneasel form.

Slowly getting to her feet, she climbs back down to street level.

Back On the Streets of Fallarbor

Crewe wanders among the people, hissing at anyone that shows interest in a Sneasel randomly wandering a town near a volcano. Fortunately, most of them assume she's a Trainer's, if they care at all.

Coming up in front of the inn, she stands under her window, carefully avoiding the broken glass and Pokeballs that litter the ground, and looks up. Seeing the silhouette of an Electivire pass by the window, she startles, then turns and runs.

Inside Crewe's (First) Room

Electivire pauses and takes a step back towards the window, frowning. Ian hops up on the sill beside her.

<Electivire?> he asks, tilting his head and looking up at her.

She shakes her head. <I... nothing. Come on, we need to talk to everyone.>

The two share a look, then turn and walk back towards the other Pokemon.

Under a Tree in Fallarbor

Crewe sits once more hugging her knees. She faces away from the inn, and stares right in front of her, fighting back tears and shivering slightly, despite the heat that comes from being right next to a volcano.

Outside the Inn (not by the tree)

Electivire and Ian stand before the gathered Pokemon. They all look nervous and slightly afraid, but put on a brave face. Hecate paces near the back of the group, snarling under her breath. Electivire ignores her for the time being, looking around the group.

<I don't need to tell you what's happened, or what it means,> she begins. Skywalker whimpers and draws himself into a huddle. Anakin huddles with him, murmuring soothing words under his breath. Weavile doesn't respond, still looking rather shell-shocked. Obi-Wan tries to look like he's got it together, but can't keep from trembling. Empoleon stands silently besides Weavile, trying to be her rock.

Suddenly, Electivire frowns. <Where's Nova?>

A rustling comes from the bush near them, and a certain shiny Poochyena sticks her head out.

<Nova? How do you know my Nova?> Aurelia demands, strutting out with Amberjack and Nova following behind her. The Quilava, spotting Electivire, makes to run over to her, but Aurelia blocks her way with one paw. <Stay put, my dear.>

<Nova is not yours,> Electivire says sternly, tails sparking. <You are not her Trainer, nor her mother.>

Aurelia laughs. <As if either of those cared in the least about her! Her Trainer hasn't been seen in weeks, and her mother has never appeared to me. Now, if you don't mind, I'm taking my girls and leaving.>

<No, wait!> Nova pleads, pawing at the Poochyena. <Please, Aurelia?>

She glances down at the Quilava, and her eyes soften. <Oh, very well.> Aurelia's head snaps back around to Electivire. <Well, get on with it!>

Electivire nods, adjusting her position so she can see the newcomers as well as the other Pokemon. <You say Crewe has abandoned you as well? Then perhaps you should stay... Aurelia, I believe?>

<Yes, that is my name,> Aurelia sniffs, shaking her neck fur. <And yes, our... Trainer... has done no such thing recently.>

<Well then.> Electivire turns back to her own group. <What should we do?>

They glance at each other, then Skywalker pipes up. <I want a new Trainer.>

This sparks interest in the other Pokemon, and they all begin to talk, chiming in with their assent. Even Weavile nods slowly. Only Hecate remains silent. She flexes her claws, then pushes past Skywalker and Anakin to stand before Electivire.

<I'm going home,> she growls, giving Electivire a challenging glare. Electivire returns her look, tails sparking again.

<We stay together, Hecate. We're going to find a new Trainer.>

Hecate lets out a laughing snarl. <Oh, Electivire... You, of all here, should know me better than that. You think I will accept a new Trainer?> Her eyes change, becoming more pleading. <I want to see my family again.>

The two hold each other's eyes for a long moment, no one speaking and barely even breathing. Finally, Electivire gives her a slow nod.

<Very well. I suggest you search out Robin—I believe he's with the Katie girl?—and have him teleport you.>

Hecate nods as well, giving her a small bow. <I will.> She turns to head back to the inn.

<Hecate...> Electivire says, looking after her. The Zoroark pauses, her ears angling back towards her.

<... Good luck.>

Hecate nods, then runs off. Electivire turns back to the others. <So. A new Trainer.>

Nova pipes up with, <Katie! I want to be on Cayenne's team!>

Amberjack looks excited at this. <Oh, I like Cayenne...>

Aurelia looks down at the two young Pokemon. <I stay with my girls.>

Electivire frowns, then shrugs. <Very well. Everyone else...> she begins.

<We stay together,> Ian finishes, stepping forward.

The assembled Pokemon nod, then turn to head back.

Back Under That Tree...

Crewe, having tuned out the (still playing) music, listened to the conversation going on. Getting to her feet, she chokes back tears and runs off blindly, searching for somewhere far away to go.

My fault... But I can't be a Trainer, I can't be a Trainer, I can't be a Trainer...

She stops and stares up at the volcano looming over the city.

<I didn't have a choice!> she yells desperately at it, balling up her fists. Receiving no answer, she turns and runs again, knocking several people over, and not really caring.

I'll find them, she promises silently. I'll apologize... When I'm better. When I'm better, I'll find them...

Fallarbor Pokemon Center

Effie sits rigidly upright in her seat, knuckles white as she tightly clasps her hands, staring straight in front of her. When the nurse calls her name, she leaps to her feet and in a flash is standing by the counter. The nurse smiles and hands her three Pokeballs.

"I'm afraid your Pokemon have some injuries that only time will heal," she says apologetically. "The Buizel has a sprained ankle and punctured floatation sack. The Sealeo has a sore neck... she'll likely have trouble using her ice abilities for a while. And the Marshtomp... Well, his arm is broken. We've bandaged it as best we could. I suggest you refrain from battling with them until they're healed," she adds, patting Effie's hand.

Effie nods. "I will... Thank you."

"Have a nice day!" the nurse says cheerfully, looking for the next customer. Effie moves away from the counter, clipping the Pokeballs to her belt. A few waiting people nod to her.

"Annoying music, huh?" a young man asks, jerking his head toward the streets. Effie gives him a tight smile.

"Yes, I suppose," she says, starting to move away.

"I wonder if someone with an identity crisis is listening," the man says conversationally.

Effie shrugs, and is grateful when the nurse calls "Jim!" and the young man hops to his feet, nods to Effie, and goes to collect his Pokemon. Effie leaves the Pokemon Center, one hand hovering protectively over her own Pokeballs.
