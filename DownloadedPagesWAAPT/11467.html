Terrera, the night before

-A good portion of the population near the capital has gathered in the massive chamber that was once previously used to house the Yakshan refugees some time ago. They gather in the lower tiers as they wait for Sabaku to make his appearance at the highest tier to address them.-

Mason: Didja hear? Avia's been freed again! The Crimson Lady apparently flew up on a chariot and fought the Valoran general in a bloody brawl!

Broker: I wouldn't believe everything you hear from the Gligar, Akagi...

-The crowd hushes as Sabaku steps out from one of the side tunnels. His beard has been trimmed back to it's normal length, and he's not wearing his armor - simply his loose, simple civilian clothes, just like the ones he wore the day he met Sho in the marketplace.-

Sabaku: Good evening, everyone. I've decided to speak to you all tonight because, with the help of some of my closes confidants, I've realized that I was becoming distant from you. Distant, from the people I am sworn to serve and protect. Distant, from the life's blood of Terrera.

-Due to the acoustics of the cavern, Sabaku doesn't have to raise his voice much for the whole room to hear him clearly.-

Sabaku: And becoming distant from the people is a dangerous move. On one hand, matters of State become easier to focus on, easier to... observe objectively. On the other, however... You forget why. Why you would seek to protect you borders, why you would seek prosperity and respect for all of your people, why you do not throw away lives on drives of conquest.

-The room slightly mumbles in agreement.-

Sabaku: This is a trap. One, unfortunately, that is within our very natures. To avoid this trap one must have the capability to either view oneself objectively... or have very astute, very capable friends, as well as the foresight to see the truth to their words.

-Shiko and Tetsuho both look at each other. Kioji and the Dustrunners just smile.-

Sabaku: I will not lie to you. Spectra still occupies two kingdoms at our borders - Pugilis, and Cragspur. Nixtorm's Nobunagan bid to conquer Ransei has done nothing but to weaken some of it's greatest defenders, and the Ghost Lord has taken full advantage of that fact.

-From deep inside the crowd, a woman's voice calls.-

Armorsmith: Let the Bastard of Spectra come! We'll show him just what kind of welcome we give oppressors!

-The crowd momentarily cheers in agreement before Sabaku signals for a hush.-

Sabaku: I do not doubt the capabilities of the Terreran people - we have, since the time of Old Lord Shingen, repelled every attempted invasion by a foreign power to date. However, currently, the Dragnoran government-in-exile is pushing north, and has already freed Avia of Nixtormian occupation. They now seek to retake Dragnor, then oust Yusei from the throne of his Ice Kingdom. From there, I cannot guess at their motives.

-The crowd's spirits rise, but before they go out of control, Sabaku continues.-

Sabaku: However, we must continue to be vigilant. Our allies in Aurora are also being constantly pressed by Fontaine and Greenleaf, and Yaksha is bordered with occupied Cragspur and Viperia.

-The crowd mellows, but is still in good spirits.-

Sabaku: From my original point, we are still at war. As I say that, we are also still a country, a kingdom. Continue working as you did before, putting hammer to metal or chisel to stone. Continue feeding your families and clothing your children. Because that is something that the Ghost Lord will never understand - that all of you, not the gold, not the land, not the strategic location, is what makes Terrera truly great. You, all of you, are the craftsmen, the traders, the navigators, that make Terrera more than what it first appears to be. You are all the true heart of this land... and I love you all for it.

-Sabaku respectfully bows and walks back the way he came as the crowd attempts to articulate what feelings they should be expressing.-
