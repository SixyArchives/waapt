"Uh... Hey, don't go into the Park, there's a wild swarm of-"

"Taken care of, sir. You'll need to work a bit to get them out, but they're safely caged. What was that about, anyway?" The ranger blinks in surprise, but rubs his chin at Cebu's question.

"Well, to be honest, we don't get many visitors to the Park anymore- those pokemon were supposed to be for the Bug Catching contest, but we haven't been able to hold one in a month- not even Bug Maniacs are showing up for them. A combination of natural population growth and being unable to get out properly, and, well, they went nuts. We're going to have to send them out into their native populations or we won't be able to care for them all- but since we're taking care of so many, we don't have the budget to ship all of them. It's a real problem..." Cebu shakes her head softly.

"Do you have any idea what's keeping people away? I mean, I used to come here all the time when I was in school..."

"Oh, we know exactly what the problem is," the ranger says bitterly. "It's the damn Idol Stage that opened up in Ecruteak- where the Kimono Girl Dojo used to be. People are taking the shortcut through Violet City to get there faster- they don't want to have to walk through a whole park to get their fix of cheesy music and bad dancing." Cebu tenses up, setting her jaw, before looking back up at the man.

"What happened to the Dojo anyway? That place was an institution..."

"Apparently, they got taken down by some sort of musical dojo hunters- you know, like the martial art ones, but with performance. They had some sort of performance competition to see which discipline was better, and the people of Ecruteak chose the Idols. They're the hottest thing around now- my kid adores them. Myself, I don't get it..."

Cebu sighs, and shakes the man's hand. "I don't get it either, but thank you for your time, sir." She heads out the door, waving Selene and Kata over. "We've got to get moving- I need to see what happened to my old school."
