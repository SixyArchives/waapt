Maybe I should explain to you the importance of the Baal-Rah Iyakki. According to the story, Baal-Rah was the leader of the Simisear clan a very long time ago. He was born with a very unusual and wondrous ability. He was born with ability to be able to tell if someone was telling the truth or was lying, no matter how good their facade was. Even the most convincing and manipulating Pokemon in the forest couldn't keep a lie or a truth away from Baal-Rah. This impressed the Pokemon of the forest, even his enemies would hold him in high regard and respect and even in times of war, he would be asked by the very Pokemon he was fighting to judge another Pokemon suspected of lying.

-pauses-

But he only had one demand. That he decides who carries out the punishment and what it is. Out of all the Simi tribes, the Simisears were the most violent. Nearly every punishment he issued was Death, if not that then he would issue a punishment that was a Fate Worse than Death. He became mad with power and in the end, that power destroyed him.

-Looks back up at the stars-

But that part of the tale is mostly forgotten. His ability to separate lies from truths gave him a reputation nearly on the level of a Pokemon of Legend, he is also the reason why those who grew up in the forest tell the truth, no matter how brutal it is and why we... dislike liars.
