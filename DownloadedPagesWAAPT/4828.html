Somewhere in the wilderness of not-Hoenn...

Three figures trudge through the snow, hunched over against the storm. Two hang back, arms draped over each other's shoulders, shivering. The other, the biggest, is a few yards ahead, standing slightly more erect, his head swiveling as he looks back and forth. When one of the laggers stumbles and falls, the other catches her, then calls ahead to the other.

<We have to stop now! We can't go any farther tonight! We'll die out here!>

The leader pauses and looks over his shoulder at his companions, then nods slowly. He drops back and helps support the fallen one.

<We'll shelter in the trees, > he mutters, just barely audible over the sound of the storm, even with their good hearing.

Struggling through the quickly mounting snow, the three finally find a small cave formed by a rock hanging over a small bank. The small space is just enough for the three of them to squeeze together. None of them mind; it helps keep warm. The two that lagged behind curl up and sleep almost immediately, and after a few hours spent staring out into the storm, the leader relents and sleeps alongside them.

Eventually, the storm subsides, and the weak sun breaks free. Its light is enough to illuminate three Sneasels huddled together below a rock. The one in the center, a short, scrawny female, is glaring at the largest one to her left.

<We're not going to find her.>

He doesn't answer. The other Sneasel, a thin, pointy-looking male, places a hand on her shoulder, which she shrugs off.

<She's not out here!>

He still doesn't answer, staring pointedly out into the trees. The female Sneasel jumps to her feet, nearly hitting her head on the rock, and charges into his face, eyes blazing.

<You're going to get us killed, Biton!>

This snaps him out of it. His head snaps around and his eyes meet her eyes. For a moment her gaze wavers, then steadies as she glares back at him. The other male shrinks back, looking nervously from one to the other.

<Then go home, > the bigger male growls. <Leave this to me.>

<You know very well that if we leave, we'll be killed! Besides—> the female throws her arms out to the side, baring her teeth and hissing. <We have no home anymore!>

<Guys, please...> the other male says helplessly. He's ignored by the other two.

<Then make your own home. Or stay with me, > Biton says, in the same low growl. <I don't care. But I'm going to find her.>

The female rushes at him, sending an Ice Shard his way. Before the Biton can react, however, a Reflect shield pops up between the two.

<Guys! Every time we have this argument, someone gets hurt!> The third Sneasel gets to his feet, looking irritated. <Biton, you need to be a bit more... moderate with your search attempts. Morana, you need to calm. Down. Attacking each other will only get all us killed faster.>

Morana and Biton exchange looks, both looking slightly sheepish.

<All right, Martin, I guess you have a point...> Biton glances sideways at the Reflect screen, and beyond it, the slowly calming down female Sneasel. <We can tone it down a bit. But I will keep looking. I'm going to find her, with your help or not!>

<You don't get it, Biton!> Morana yells, backing away slightly. <Of course we want to find her! She's our sister too! But it's been fourteen years since we've seen her! There's nothing to find!> She spins on her heel and runs off into the woods.

<Morana! STOP!> Martin races off after her, looking desperate.

Biton watches them go, then turns back towards the way they came, confident his siblings will be back soon.

<Oh, Glint...> he whispers, his claws sinking into the snow piled up in front of the small cleared space offered by the rock. <Where are you?>
