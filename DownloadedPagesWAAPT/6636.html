A Number of Entries Afterwards

Perhaps I haven't been keeping as good of a record as I should have... But. I'm not sure if we can do anything of value at this point. We successfully cut off Qoriik's goons' access to the space tunnels. And have been successful at talking most of them down into surrendering. (Sometimes after knocking a bit of good old fashioned sense into them first.) Karst also is finally ambulatory (though not very talkative), and a few of our own appear to be successfully getting over some nasty wounds of their own. And I've made a good friend with a Staraptor that cast her lot with the Cubone... Apparently her trainer used to call her 'Raptor'.

That's more or less where the good news ends. We still can't move around a whole lot. Qoriik's peons and the others that chased us through the space tunnels may have been taken care of, but we can't seem to shake this air support that came along after the first couple of days. They perform hit-and-run attacks normally, since they figured out that hanging around too long will leave them easy prey for the turrets. Most of the upper labs are out and out gone. The mainframe appears to be non-functional. And communications outside of HQ are utterly dead. Meaning we have no idea whether or not the group that went to Hoenn is still alive... Or mons like Mike who wound up getting chased down different tunnels than we did.

A few of the aerial raiders got downed earlier today. They're for the most part fine, if flight-challenged for the immediate future. We'll be attempting to interrogate them to figure out just what's going on outside. Not sure if we'll have any luck though. Except for that Garchomp, but he seems scared enough to just tell us things that he thinks we'll want to hear instead of the truth.

Can't help but feel as if I've seen him before... But where?

The Entry After That One

I knew that Garchomp seemed familiar. He was apparently one of the mons that got marooned after what happened to Kanto. Which would explain a lot about his behavior after being captured.

As for the matter of how it was that these punks operate, I think that I've got the matter cleared up... There's apparently seven main blighters. Qoriik is one of them, the Altaria we ran into is 'Vallok', the head honcho's some 'Alduin' mon, and there were a bunch of other dumb-sounding names that were thrown around. Hoenn's apparently taken, though there's a lot of trouble on the ground... I hope that the guys we sent there are okay. At this point, it really seems like a matter of time before humanity as we know it simply ceases to exist... We tried to figure out if any of these mons know how the bomb that went off here works, but no dice. They're as stumped as we are.

On the other hand. We pieced together from Karst and a few others how it was that they recruit mons into this whole business in the first place. Some of these mons are true believers, and seriously think that having a weakness to ice gives them a mandate to rule the world... Most of them... Seem to have been manipulated. They're told many things by their higher-ups. That if they don't chip in, they can look forward to being ostracized by their families and loved ones (there's apparently some sort of massive clan system among dragons, go figure) and treated like dirt by everyone else. A few of the mons that are captured here actually had trainers or were humans themselves, and were pressured to join in order to give their teammates and families a better existence. To keep them from being driven from their homes.

It really is sickening that for a movement that espouses the inherent nobility of dragons, that it would stoop to such methods to ensure loyalty. I don't know why the gods haven't intervened, even if some of these grunts seem rather fearful of it (a few are convinced that the likes of Palkia and Dialga will intercede on their side due to being draconic). But even if they don't, I'm not sure if the machinations of this 'Alduin' have a future.
