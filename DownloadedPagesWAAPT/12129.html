Jason nodded and replied softly.

"I'll answer both in one go. When I was born, I had to deal with growing up in living hell. The place I grew up in Saffron City was, and still is, a stewing pit of disgusting filth and horror. I have seen blood there, I've seen some of the worst in humanity there. To survive in that environment, you have to have something to hold on to, something to anchor you to reality. I chose my father, who was himself a magician in his days. With the last of his earthly power, he earned me a scholarship to the Magician's Academy in a bid to get me away from there, while he remained behind in that hellhole to stew and rot. I only carried on in that school out of my dedication to my father, as it was his wish for me to become a magician, to make something of myself, to be truly great. Thats not to say I didnt like the school, on the contrary, it was the greatest thing Ive ever experienced, it gave me a great sense of pride, knowing that during my shows, people would have the time of their lives. And then they would walk away, never remembering my name, and barely remembering my shows, no matter how fabulous. And I was okay with that, because for those few moments, those people could forget their worries and they could enjoy themselves." Jason stood up and drew his wand, spinning it between his fingers, before letting it come to rest.

"I became a magician, because to me, it means something. It symbolises my thanks and dedication to the one who created me, thanks and dedication to the one light amongst a universe of darkness, and it symbolises my desire to take that light, and expand it into something so great that no one has to feel that dark for long. Helping out against Cipher isnt just about me thinking the group is bad, its about me not being able to stand the fact that there are people in the world that spread darkness and hate and fear for selfish purposes, and that makes me sick. It goes against everything I stand for." Jason shed a Single Tear, before chuckling and pocketing his wand.

"Besides, as the Magician's Circle's representative to Orre, who would I be if I didnt help?" He blinked away the tear and sat down again, smiling sadly.

"So yes, in answer, I will join you. I will do anything it takes to stop Cipher. No one, and I repeat, no one, does the things they do and get away with it scott free. Whether its now, or later, they will pay." Jason heard the noise of footsteps, and saw a newbody enter through the doorway. He smiled through his saddened face.

"Oh, hello. Is there something I can help you with?"
