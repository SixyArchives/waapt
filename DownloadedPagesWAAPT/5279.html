"Thanks for escorting me out of that cave, Rex!" Mira grinned, beaming happily. "You're a really nice guy!" "Heh...yeah, he is pretty cute. Just don't let him near any of your valuables."

Mira simply smiled, continuing to pat her new friend excitedly. After several moments of watching them fool around, Kadabra sighed, putting his free claw to his head. <Mira, why don't you discuss your plan to him?>

"Discuss?" he asked, the Kadabra arcing a brow at the boy's deciphering of his words. Rex flushed red, realizing his error and scratching the back of his head. "Err...your psychic channel wasn't closed?"

"You're right, Kadabra! I almost forgot..." She turned to the older boy, looking up at him with wide, Lillipup eyes. "I wanted to ask you for one more thing, Rex. Do you think that you coul- Eeee! A Ponyta!"

"Eh?"

Turning around, Rex noted Aloise rapidly trotting over to his position, stopping a small distance away once she saw Mira. She eyed the other girl and her Kadabra cautiously, uncertain of their intentions.

"Don't be shy, Aloise, " Rex smiled, beckoning her over. "They're harmless."

<Oh...> Nodding, she wandered over, feeling a little unnerved by the adoring look that Mira was giving her.

"So cool! I bet you're really strong! My specialty is Pokemon with high Sp. Atk, though, but I bet that Marley wou-"

<Mira...>

"R-right!" the easily distracted girl giggled, rubbing the back of her head in embarrassment. "Sorry~" She turned back to Rex again, setting Nico down on the ground and watching him crawl back over to his trainer. "I wanted to ask if you could accompany me to my home in Hearthome City. It's not too far from here; we just need to pass through Mt. Coronet, and then we'll be there!"

"Oh...that's where we're heading next, " Rex murmured to himself. "It wouldn't be out of the way or anything, so I'm sure I could go along with it."

<Then why are you hesitating?> Aloise grumbled, nudging him with her head. <Go on; do it!>

"Ugh...why are girls so pushy?" Turning back to Mira, Rex gave a nod of agreement, the little girl letting out a cheerful whoop.

"Yay! We're going to have a ton of fun!"
