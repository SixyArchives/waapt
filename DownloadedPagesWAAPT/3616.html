-They had always told him never to go near land. The land was dangerous. There were strong Pokémon up there who would nothing more than to eat a young Lugia like himself for lunch.

But...There were also these strange creatures called humans too.

Ms. Lapras had told him a lot about humans. They were strange creatures that were completely unlike Pokémon. They weren't very strong, seemingly incapable of using any sort of moves, and spoke in strange languages completely unlike their own.

Yet...Ms. Lapras had said that while they lacked in strength, they more than made up for it in smarts. The humans had apparently made all sorts of strange machines and devices, the most strange being these weird little balls that could shrink a Pokémon down and store them inside so that they could be carried around anywhere easily.

But Ms. Lapras had said that this was why he should stay away from the humans. If they ever saw him, they would use one of those strange balls on him, and that while a lot of humans were nice, there would be a lot of bad ones who would do everything they can to get their hands on him so that they could do awful things.

So, normally, Lugia wouldn't even think of going on land.

But, that mean Feraligatr was after him. Ms. Lapras had told him once that, while Feraligatr were really fast in the water, they weren't so quick on land. And upon coming across the island, Lugia had remembered this, and swum up to the shore as quickly as he could.

Even though he had been on land with his father a few times before, it had only been for short periods, so it was still a rather alien place for him. For a start, there we're plants everywhere (Grass and trees, his father had called them.) And the few Pokémon he had spotted so far were unlike any of the ones that lived underwater.

Of course, not being able to move in three dimensional space was strange as well. His father had said that he would be able too once he could fly, but that was still a long way off yet, so he had to make do with walking.

He hadn't gotten very far in-land when he had heard voices. Most of them were Pokémon, but one of them was one like he had never heard before. Was...Was it a human?

His curiosity getting the better of him, the young legendary had followed the voices, eventually coming to a small clearing. Quickly hiding in the bushes so that whoever was there wouldn't spot them, Lugia quetly began to watch them...
