Baron Samedi

Driftveil PKMN Center

| Unable to find Zeta before night sets in, Ao heads to the Center, and nestles up under the eaves.

Above Driftveil City

| Zeta has once more returned to the sky, on Cetus's back. She enjoys it being nearly alone, far and away from the city below. It feels close to her home, the deserts of Orre; there is a similar feeling of wild nature, untamed and proud, fiercely defiant of the modern city below.

| Unable to sleep, Zeta decides to meditate, and try to visit the Land of the People again.

| She calmly sits, closes her eyes, and empties her thoughts.

| She slips away, into the shadows at the edges of the world.

The Land of the People

| Opening her eyes, Zeta is greeted by the now familiar sight of the ruins, the seal, and the floating objects.

| This visit is the one she's been dreading.

| She must either head North or West, into the harsher quadrants.

| She deliberates on which to choose for what feels like hours.

| At last, she has decided.

| She will go West, as though it may drag at her mind worryingly, it still felt the better option than the cold, dark land to the North.

Western Desert

| The path is difficult to follow, constantly shifting. It turns at random in front of her and behind her changing from one minute to the next. Now it is asphalt, then hard packed dirt, then cobblestones, then a barely discernible line in the sand. Still, she is somehow able to follow it. Zeta is glad of this; she has an uneasy feeling that stepping off the path here may be fatal.

| The sands around her shift and change as well, forming vibrant colors and fascinating pictures. Most are mere geometric shapes or scribblings, but a few form recognizable images. They involve people she knows, and herself. Some of them she recognizes as the past, events that she remembers happening. Others are unfamiliar to her.

| She ignores them and walks on. She has a similar feeling about pausing too long to watch the sands as she did about stepping off the path.

| Finally, at last, she reaches what appears to be the end of the road.

| It is a building, but of what sort is indeterminable, as it also is constantly changing form. The only consistent thing about it is the front door. In this chaotic land of flashing color and changing shapes, it alone remains steady, solid. It is a large, heavy, stone door, made of what appears to be obsidian.

| She pulls it open. It is much lighter than it looks.

| She enters the building.

| And, following a now familiar pattern, she encounters a person that might be her, but isn't.

Zeta?: Ah hahaha! So you've made it~!

| It's voice has a singsong quality to it. It is both immensely soothing and horrifyingly grating at the same time.

Zeta: Indeed. And this place is?

Zeta?: Oh, it's had many names~! More than I can count~!

Zeta: It sounds likely. Still, what is its current name.

Zeta?: At this time, in this age, it is known as the Desert of Madness.

Zeta: It fits.

Zeta?: I know, right~?

Zeta: And your name?

Zeta?: Hasn't anyone ever told you that names have power~? Why should I give my name to you~?

Zeta: For your sisters did.

Zeta?: That is a fair enough point~, but I am still unable to~.

Zeta: Why not?

Zeta?: Because, I have had more names than this place has~. I have had more names than are possible, or even impossible~.

Zeta: Even so, what is the name you use now?

Zeta?: Ah, you are a clever one, you are~. I think I'm going to like you~!

Zeta: Your name.

Zeta?: A bit pushy for my tastes, though~. We'll have to work on that~.

Zeta: Tell me. Your name.

Zeta?: Fine, fine~. I go by the name of Mania, Maniae if I'm feeling a bit plural~.

Zeta: Alright, Mania, you seem to be the most informative of your sisters that I've found yet, if in a roundabout manner.

Mania: True, true~.

Zeta: Will you tell me about the keys?

Mania: Certainly~!

Zeta: Thank you.

Mania: The keys ar-

| But Zeta has spent too long once again. As the shadows come to take her away, she can hear Mania's demented laughter. Zeta can tell that Mania stalled her on purpose, just so that she could tease her in this way.
