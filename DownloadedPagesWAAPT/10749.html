Luke's Module - Interior

Said Shieldon was currently curled up on the carpet in the middle of the front room of Luke's module, still an utter emotional wreck.

And honestly, why wouldn't she be? Everything she had know was gone forever, and in it's place was a strange, terrifying world of cold and sterile white boxes. There was no grass, no sun, and a myriad of strange, frightening creatures.

She had never felt more frightened and alone.

Sniffling in grief, the Shiledon opened her eyes briefly, just to witness a certain trainer plop a bowl of various types of leaves down in front of her.

Luke: "I brought you something to eat. I checked with the paleontology guys, and they told me this stuff was the closest thing we had to what you ate back in your time."

She stared at the admittedly rather appealing looking food, but then chose to bury her head in the floor again.

Shieldon: <I'm not hungry.>

Luke: "Come on, you must be. You haven't eaten since we brought you back yesterday. So you've really gone millions of years without eating anything at all!"

Truth be told, she actually was hungry. She had been so caught up in her grief that she had forgotten to feed herself, and now she was suffering all the more for it. After a few seconds, she breathed a resound sigh, picked herself up, then pulled a leaf out of the bowl with her mouth and began to chew it slowly.

Glad to see that they were making a little progress, Luke smiled at the little dinosaur.

"There we go. Doesn't that taste good?"

Swallowing a mouthful, the Shieldon couldn't help but admit to herself that it was good.

But now she was curious. She had seen no sign of any plant life so far in these sterile white boxes, so where had this human gotten this food?

Shieldon: <...Where did these come from?>

Luke: "Eh? Those leaves? I got them from outside."

The Shieldon blinked, turning to look at the front door, which lead out to hallway.

Shieldon: <I didn't see any plants out there...>

Luke: "No, I meant outside outside. Out the door is still in the building. You have to go further to get properly outside."

The Shieldon turned back and stared at Luke. Was what he saying true? Was there more to this new world than just endless white boxes of different sizes? And was it anything like the world she knew?

Shieldon: <...Take me there.>

Luke: "Where?"

Shieldon: <Outside. I want to go properly outside.>

Luke blinked. Were they making progress here?

Luke: "If that's what you want, sure. Let me just pick you up, and..."

Luke carefully gripped the underside of the Shieldon and lifted with both hands, intending to carry her in his arms...

"HURRRRRRRRRGH! HURRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRGH!"

...Except that Shieldon weighed fifty-seven Kilograms and thus - was impossible for him to lift. After a few more vain attempts, he stopped, panting heavily.

"Okay, I think I'm gonna' need some help here..."
