The Dreaming

The man with no name and the Gogoat are still fleeing the Wild Hunt.

However, the Gogoat is starting to tire.

<Gogoat: "Dammit... Well, we put in a good run.">

The man with no name snarls.

"No. No. I will not lose. Never again."

He looks ahead, and notices something odd in the distance. The sand appears to shimmer oddly.

"Hey. What's that?"

The Gogoat's head slowly rises while he labors for breath. Seeing the shimmering, he perks up.

<Gogoat: "We just might make it. Holy shit, we might make it!">

His speed redoubles, aided by regained hope of escape.

More arrows and long-ranged moves fly past them, missing by hairbreadths.

"What does it mean?"

<Gogoat: "That shimmer in the sand? That's a line of glass. It marks a border. The land beyond that line is the territory of something not even the Wild Hunt dares to cross."

The man with no name makes a noise of discomfort.

"Then why are we going in there?"

<Gogoat: "Because. They'll get off our backs. Plus, it won't respond immediately to our presence. We'll have time to catch a breather, plan something."

"Alright..."

The Gogoat plunges over the strip of glass.

Hot on his heals, the Wild Hunt follows, unaware of their location in the throes of their bloodlust.

"I thought you said they'd turn back!"

<Gogoat: "Turns out I was wrong!">

Dream!Terrera

Alphonse suddenly looks up.

Alphonse: "My apologies, friends, but I must go. A dark presence has entered the plot of land I've staked out for myself."

He whistles sharply, then yanks his drill-spear out of the ground and starts to walk away from the table, slowly picking up speed.

Just as he reaches a dead run, he leaps up into the air. A white and orange blur swoops down under him, and he catches it, to fly off at astounding speed.

The Dreaming

"Shit, what now, oh guide?"

<Gogoat: "Shut up. Don't be a smart-ass."

*FWOOOOOOM*

Suddenly, a ball of fire flies out of the sky, blasting some of the Wild Hunt flying.

Alphonse: "Ho HO! Back, rowdy crowd! Back I say! Leave my lands, or perish!"

He lands behind the man with no name and the Gogoat, who keeps running.

The Florges at the head of the Hunt calls for a halt, and the entire horde stops on a dime.

He stares at Alphonse, weighing his options. Alphonse bares his teeth at him.

Alphonse: "Do not test me, fae."

Deciding that it'd be in his best interest to concede, the Florges snarls, then turns about, spurring his mount off back in the direction from which they came.

Meanwhile, the man with no name and the Gogoat have made some progress, but not nearly enough. Quickly, Alphonse catches up to them.

Alphonse: "Hold! Hold, Lord of Shadow!"

The man with no name flinches at the title, then leaps off the back of the Gogoat.

"How do you know that name? Who are you?"

Alphonse: "I am Alphonse Bismarck, 60th Poet-King of Paxlia. And you, my friend, are the Sa'kage, if I'm correct."

"Yes... yes I am."

Alphonse: "Do you have a name, or are you just the Nameless?"

"I have no name. No longer, anyway."

Alphonse: "Well. Come, let's get you inside. We can talk there. Bring your friend, too."

<Gogoat: "He's not my friend. I'm merely repaying a favor I owed... a certain someone.">

Alphonse smiles, his eyes twinkling.

Alphonse: "Is that so? My mistake, then. Come along, now."

He strides forwards. The man with no name stumbles along behind him in his wake, bewildered, the Gogoat taking up the rear.
