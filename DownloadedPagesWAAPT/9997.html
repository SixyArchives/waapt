249920 luke9244th May 2013 08:50:13 AM Get RP Mod
Comet waved goodbye as Crow and his Pokémon left through the Doors of Dawn, leaving the Charizard alone with her thoughts. Sighing to herself, Comet conjured up a cloud out of Dream Mist and hopped on, dangling her legs over the side as she began to think things over.

Comet: <I want to face everyone I've left behind again, I really do. But I'm just not brave enough. They could be angry, or sad, or even disappointed. And I don't think I could bear it.>

She buried her face in her hands, distraught.

<If only I was more like my mother...>

Six Years Ago...

<MOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOM!!!>

A dozing Charizard raised her head groggily off a bed of straw as a terrified bundle of scales clutched at her neck, shaking in fright.

Charmander!Comet: <I WAS PLAYING OUTSIDE AND THEN A BIG ROCKY SNAKE...THINGY CAME OUT OF THE GROUND AND TRIED TO EAT ME!!!>

She buried her head in her mother's distinctive ashen scales, seeking safety from the aforementioned monster that had hounded her back home.

<I'm scared!>

Pulling herself out of bed, the Charizard wrapped her arms around the Charmander and gave her a warm hug, calming her down just a little bit.

Asher the Shiny Charizard: <Let me tell you a little something, Kindle.>

The Charmander glanced up at her mother curiously.

CometKindle: <What is it?>

Asher: <You know how I'm part of the Dream Guard, yeah?>

The young Comet - who was apparently named Kindle at the time - nodded.

Kindle: <Of course I do...>

The Charizard smirked.

Asher: <Well I bet you didn't know that when I took my initiation exam, I was scared half-to-death!>

Kindle blinked in a mixture of shock and surprise. Her mother? Scared?! To her young mind, the very idea sounded impossible to her.

Kindle: <But... How could you be scared? You're a Charizard! Charizard aren't scared of anything!>

Asher: <I wasn't a Charizard at the time, though! I was still a Charmeleon! And you Dad was only a Gabite. In fact, he was there on the side-lines cheering me on!>

Kindle: <Wow. I didn't know that.>

Asher smiled warmly. No pun intended.

Asher: <Your Dad was always such a sweetheart. But we're getting side-tracker here. Anyway, I was so frightened that day, that I almost ran out on the whole thing! But I didn't, and you know why, Kindle?>

Kindle: <...Why?>

Asher: <Because being brave...isn't about not being afraid.>

Surprised, the Charmander gaped in shock.

Kindle: <It's not?>

Asher: <No. Being brave means being afraid of something, and then doing it anyway. Believe me, I was once a frightened little Charmander just like you, Kindle!>

Slightly offended, Kindle scowled up at her mother.

Kindle: <I am not little!>

Amused, Asher chuckled, patting Kindle gently on the head with her claw.

Asher: <Alright, alright. You're not little. I apologise. Now as I was saying, I used to be afraid of a lot of things. In fact, sometimes? I still get scared. The idea of something bad happening to you or your Dad still leaves me frightened, or if I come across something shocking terrible while I'm out on patrol.>

Kindle: <But how do you cope, Mom?>

Asher: <It's simple, Kindle. I learned to stop being scared of getting scared, and learned to enjoy it, instead.>

Kindle: <...What?! That's crazy! How could anymon enjoy being scared?>

Asher: <You'd be surprised! Lots of Pokémon enjoy being scared! Like your Dad! You know how he likes to throw himself off of those giant crystals outside, then wait until the last second before pulling up and flying off?>

Kindle nodded, crossing her arms.

Kindle: <I still think he's always being stupid.>

Asher: <I can't help but agree with you there. But the reason he does it is because of the small chance that he might end up just a smear on the ground. That fear is what makes it exciting for him.>

Fear could make things exiting?! Her mother was just kept blowing her mind here!

Kindle: <Are you saying that being afraid and being excited are the same thing?>

Asher: <Not the same thing, but they often go claw in claw. See, whenever I get scared, Kindle, do you want to know how I feel?>

Kindle: <...Tell me.>

Asher: <Well, first my stomach ties itself in a knot not literally, don't worry, my inner flame starts burning more intensely, and my body goes all tense. But do you know what that tells me?>

Kindle: <That you're afraid?>

Smiling, Asher shook her head.

Asher: <No, Kindle. It tells me that I'm alive, that I'm truly living my life and not wasting it doing something boring like...like farming Mareep or something like that. And that's why I like being afraid sometimes. I know this is probably a lot for you to take in right now, Kindle. But maybe one day, once you're older, you'll understand what I mean.>

She was right. She still couldn't quite comprehend it. But she consigned the conversation to her memory anyway, in the hope that one day, she could come back and eventually come to understand.

Kindle: <Alright, Mom.>

With a smile, Asher gave Kindle another hug.

Asher: <That's my girl. Now, you said something about an Onix?>

She didn't know what an Onix was, but Kindle assumed her mother was talking about the giant rock snake that was still lurking around outside, just waiting for the chance to gobble her up.

Kindle: <Can you make it go away?>

With a toothy smirk, Asher held out her arms, forming a large crackling ball of Aura energy between her hands.

Asher: <I bet a good Focus Blast to the face will send him running right along.>

The Doors of Dawn - Present Day

Now six years and a change of name later, Kindle - now Comet - finally understood her mother's words.

All this time, she had treated fear as a sign of weakness, not of strength. A force that rendered her helpless in the face of strife or danger. Out of fear, she had already thrown one life away, and it had almost made her do it again.

But now she understood. Being brave wasn't about controlling fear, or not being afraid at all.

It was about embracing it.

The Charizard hopped off her cloud, which vanished into a puff of Dream Smoke, before waving her hands and conjuring another door out of the Earth.

She then paused, knowing that beyond the door was her friends - the friends she had let down. She knew they would be disappointed with her, and facing them wouldn't be easy.

KindleComet: <Okay, No more running. From now on, I'm gonna' face fear head on...>

She was feeling just as her mother had described all those years back. Her heart was pounding, her stomach was tying itself in knots thankfully still not literally, her inner fire was burning brightly. She was afraid...

...But she was alive.

<Alright guys. Here I come!>

With a mighty flap of her wings, the Charizard took to the air, flying up a short distance. Then, she suddenly swooped down towards the door, which opened up as she shot through, crossing into the same sea of sand she had lead her friends to a short while ago.

As the door closed behind her and vanished in a wisp of Dream Mist, the two guards of the Doors of Dawn exchanged a glance.

Sunburn: <Did that Charizard seem familiar to you?>

Solain the Solrock: <Don't be silly. Nobody that comes through here ever comes back again!>

Sunburn: <Hmm. Maybe it was a twin or something, then.>
