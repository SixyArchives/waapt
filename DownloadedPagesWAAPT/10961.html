Bishop: <My plan is to stop searching.>

Kirk: <We're going to find it, okay? We haven't come this far just to->

Kirk: <But it's an entrance, at least.>

-He leads the three of them into the structure. The mound consists of one large, barren room, the floor sloping downwards. At the end of the room is an unmarked stone door.-

Kirk: <Right, so this place leads underground. No suprises there.>

-Kirk heaves open the door with some difficulty, and the trio find themselves faced with a stone corridor that branches off in two different directions. A quick glance down each passage reveals that they too split off into seperate corridors. There are no markings of any kind on the walls.-

Kirk: <...Then they'd use their other eyes.>

-He starts to lead Bishop and Maul through the labyrinth.-

Pugilis - Around 600 years ago

-Lucanus looks at Herbert for a moment, his face devoid of expression.-

Lucanus: You did?

Herbert: ...Yeah, I did. I'd had enough. I was hungry, tired, and fed up with everything in Ransei trying to kill me. All as part of some adventure I never asked for and with no obvious reward at the end. So I quit. I told them that I'd had enough, and that they could finish their quest on their own. Well, you can see how well that worked out for me...

-He sighs, and looks up at Lucanus, who is still maintaining the same impassive expression.-

Herbert: You can skip giving me any reward. I don't deserve it. The others did all the work, I was just dead weight. I'm not a hero. I'm just some kid who got himself dragged into something way too big for him and couldn't handle it. So... no ceremony, please. Just send me on my way.

Lucanus: ...And let you be nearly torn apart by Scrafty again?

Herbert: Why not? I'm too weak to fight them off on my own. I'm no help to anyone. Even if I did go back to the group, I'd just slow them down... what am I supposed to do?

-Lucanus' face remains unreadable. He is silent for several moment before speaking.-

Lucanus: You can try.

Herbert: I'm sorry?

Lucanus: Some men are born great, some achieve greatness, and some have greatness thrust upon them. I believe you fall under the third category, Herbert. From what I have seen of you, you are hardly the archetypical example of a hero. But perhaps that is the point. Perhaps the point of this quest is to make you one. You are convinced of your own uselessness, but I can honestly tell you that the only way you will be useless is if you refuse to try and help.

Herbert: ...What if I don't want to be a hero, though?

Lucanus: Well, don't you?

-Herbert's silence is all the answer that Lucanus requires. He chuckles slightly.-

Lucanus: One crucial part of being an Auric is being able to read people. I can tell you want to prove yourself, Herbert. And you have been presented an opportunity to do so. I would recommend you take it.

Herbert: I... I don't know if they'll take me back, though.

Lucanus: Oh, they will. I can assure you of that much.

-Revan takes it upon himself to fill the thoughtful silence that follows.-

Revan: ~Should we be heading back to the castle now? I don't know about you two, but I'd rather we do the whole introspection thing somewhere a bit warmer.~

Lucanus: You two can head back. I think I will remain out here for a little while longer and reflect on things. Oh, and Herbert?

Herbert: Yeah?

Lucanus: Stick to your principles. Whatever situation you find yourself in, whatever threats you may face, the one thing you cannot surrender is your virtues. For if a man does not have values that we will never compromise, then he is nothing. Less than nothing.

Herbert: I... I see. Thank you, Lucanus.

Lucanus: Anytime, my boy. Revan?

-There is a blip, and Herbert and Revan are returned to the guest chambers in the castle.-

Revan: ~I'll take you back to your group in the morning. Wouldn't want them to think that you'd died a horrible and painful death, after all. That is the extent of the help I will provide, however. As incredible as it may sound, I actually find acting as a dogsbody around here preferable to having my component parts be devoured by demons.~

Herbert: Well, you're a great confidence boost.

Revan: ~It's what I'm here for, after all. And Lucius?~

Herbert: Yeah?

Revan: ~I'm not going to give you any of that "there's a hero inside of you" and "never give up" stuff. Because that's Taurosshit. Both of us can tell that you're not cut out for this. And yet, against all reason, you're here, and still alive as well. That's enough to convince me that the universe isn't done with you yet. Just stay alive, and who knows, things might just start to make sense.~

-With that, Revan leaves the room. Herbert sighs, then flops down on the bed.-

Herbert: All I want to do is go home. See everyone again. But... I guess that's not really an option, is it?

Revan: ~Nope.~

Herbert: What? How are you-

Revan: ~Psychic, remember?~
