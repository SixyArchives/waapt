Spalje: <...exercise? Months?> D:

Vattenfallet: <Yes, exercise. Yes, months. Flab does not just vanish the instant you step onto a treadmill, you know.>

Spalje: <But I can't even fit on a treadmill!>

Vattenfallet: <And that is precisely why you need help, Spalje.>

Spalje: <Why did I think this was a good idea...>

(Vatt, now in a sitting position, primes a clipboard with one front paw while somehow holding a ballpoint pen in the other. Spalje's eyes dart nervously from side to side, almost like he expects a man with a ghost mask and absurdly large knife to lunge from the nearby bushes any second now.)

Vattenfallet: <Now, first question. How balanced would you personally say your diet is?>

Spalje: <...whaddaya mean, balanced? Am I supposed to put my food on a set of scales or something?>

Vattenfallet: <Hurrrm. I'd say that's a very bad sign already, but I shall give you the benefit of the doubt.>

(Spalje looks even more worried than before now.)

Vattenfallet: <For the record, a "balanced diet" is where you eat foods from various groups with a variety of minerals in them. In that context, what would your answer be?>

Spalje: <Weeeeeellll...>

(Beat)

Spalje: <I, uh, eat chocolate and chips. Does that count as varied food groups?>

(For the record, what he just called chips, this British narrator has previously referred to as crisps. The wonders of living across the pond!)

Vattenfallet: <Hurrrrrmmmmmmmm.>

Spalje: <Arceus Christ that's a threatening sound> O_O;

Vattenfallet: <In normal circumstances, you would have several more questions to answer on the subject of the products you ingest. In this case, however, we can draw an obvious conclusion after just one question, and a question that isn't even supposed to be very informative at that. To compensate for what you are soon to experience, I shall thank you for making this process short and sweet.>

Spalje: D:

(With that done, Vatt goes off to find Snowy and commission several devices from her. In the meantime, Spalje just sits there pondering how a creature of his considerable bulk could possibly hope to hide away.)

Spalje: <Now, if only I was smaller, y'know? More... athletic...>

(He trails off as this thought reaches its logical conclusion.)
