274149 Herbert40k14th Jul 2013 08:02:54 AM from Merry Olde England Get RP Mod , Relationship Status: I-It's not like I like you, or anything!
Chords of Steel

Ergon City

-Leia, Samus, Wrex, and Elan are still asleep, though they are ready to leave for Holon City today.-

Holon Ruins, Auric Labyrinth

-Kirk is still leading Bishop and Maul through the maze of blank stone walls, talking as he goes.-

Kirk: <You see, this must be why nobody's ever found this place before. Someone's probably already found the entrance we came in through, and I'd be willing to bet that this maze links to some of the other sections of the ruins, but without an Auric you'd never be able to find your way through this place. It's an ingenious defense.>

Bishop: <So clearly they didn't want to be found.>

Kirk: <Or they only wanted to be found by other Aurics. If this place was a training centre or something, then finding it would probably be the first test.>

-They round the next corner, and find themselves in front of a large stone double door seemingly built into a large statue carved into the wall, elaborately decorated and with a separate inscription on each door.-

Kirk: <A test which we've just passed.>

-He steps closer, reading each of the inscriptions in turn.-

There is no emotion, there is peace.
There is no ignorance, there is knowledge.
There is no passion, there is serenity.
There is no chaos, there is harmony.
There is no death, there is Aura.

Peace is a lie, there is only passion.
Through passion, I gain strength.
Through strength, I gain power.
Through power, I gain victory.
Through victory, my chains are broken.
Aura shall free me.

Bishop: <They seem somewhat contradictory, don't they?

Kirk: <Maybe it's supposed to be a choice or something. I don't know. I was never that big on the whole philosophical mumbo-jumbo side of Aura anyway. Let's just get these doors open.>

-Kirk gives the doors a heavy shove, opening them with a heavy grinding noise and stepping into what may have been an antechamber of some description at one point. The ceiling appears to have partially collapsed, burying most of the room in rubble.-

Kirk: <...Well, this is great. We arrive here and find that the place has been buried under several thousand tons of rock. How are we supposed to find anything in here?>

Bishop: <At least we found something.>

Kirk: <Oh yeah, I totally told you so, by the way.>

Bishop: <Duly noted. Now come along, I can see a passage that hasn't been entirely buried yet. There might still be something worth finding in here.>

-Navigating their way through the collapsed monastery, passing through what appear to have been living chambers and sparring arenas, they come to a room which has been almost entirely overgrown by vines and other vegetation. There is a blank door with no visible handle, save for a stone panel immediately to the right, which has a large flower growing in front of it.-

Bishop: <I must say, that flower doesn't look entirely natural...>

Maul: ~That's because it's a Time Flower.~

Kirk: <Huh?>

Maul: ~Activate it. You'll see.~

Kirk: <And how exactly would I go about doing that?>

Maul: ~You're the Auric. You tell me.~

Kirk: <Remind me why I brought you along again? You're about as helpful as a beached Magikarp sometimes, Maul.>

-Kirk sighs, places a glowing paw on the Time Flower, then immediately jumps back and yelps as an image of an unfamiliar, armour-clad human emerges from the flower.-

Flower Projection: Greetings, student!

Kirk: <Gah!>

Flower Projection: If you have come this far, it means that you have completed all the trials and tribulations required to become a fully-realised Aura Guardian. Only one test remains.

Bishop: <I don't think we can communicate with it. By the looks of things, this is merely a recording.>

Kirk: <Well then, let's hear what it has to say.>

Flower Projection: Your task is to head through the door behind me into the tombs, enter the inner sanctum, and recover the artifact stored within. You will know what you are looking for when you see it. Once you have completed this task, we will grant you your rightfully-deserved status as a fully-fledged Aura Guardian. Good luck, student. When you are ready to begin, perform a Force Palm on the panel to the right of the door.

-The image fades, and Kirk and Bishop look at each other.-

Bishop: <...Do you think Lucanus would have taken this test?>

Kirk: <Maybe. Or he could be buried in these tombs. There's only one way to find out, isn't there?>

-He places his glowing paw on the panel... and nothing happens.-

Kirk: <Wait, what? Why isn't it working?>

-He tries again several more times, before snarling and attempting to break the stone door down. Again, this results in more damage done to Kirk than the door.-

Kirk: <Grr... why won't it let us in?>

Bishop: <I have a hypothesis. This place was designed for human Aurics, correct? A student could try to cheat their way past the test by using a Lucario or other such Pokemon to get past some of the obstacles. As a result, this door will likely only open for a human.>

Kirk: <How do we get through, then?>

Bishop: <Simple.>

-Bishop grins.-

Bishop: <We cheat.>

Pugilis - Around 600 years ago

-Herbert is still asleep. Lucanus, meanwhile, is wandering through the castle with Ayame, reading a letter as he goes.-

Lucanus: Hmm... Fukurou wants to meet with us, does he? Well, we would be delighted to host him here in Pugilis. Ayame, would you please head along to Yaksha and inform him of the good news?

Ayame: Sure thing, boss!
