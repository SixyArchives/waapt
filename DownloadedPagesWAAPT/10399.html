The House on the Rock, Great Hall, AU

| A general House Convocatum has been called.

| All of the members of the community, Resident and Refugee, have gathered.

| At the center of the circular Great Hall stand the two Elders, Taep and Yasu.

| In a circle around them sit the Principum, the Council of Tribal Leaders.

| Among their number is Gilt.

| Near the ceiling, floating in the shadows that gather there, the Falx, Tenebrae, observes everything that happens in the chamber below.

| The concentric circles of sitting beings indicate seniority - the refugees sit in the outermost rings, while the residents are placed nearest the center.

| On the very edge of the crowd, Del sits, singing to himself.

< Del:

Mama, we all go to hell

Mama, we all go to hell

It's really quite pleasant

Except for the smell

Mama, we all go to hell

>

<Elder Taep: Falx Tenebrae, do we have enough here for a quorum?>

| The Dusknoir near the ceiling stirs.

<Falx Tenebrae: Yes, Elder.>

| The ancient Vespiqueen next to Elder Taep raises her head.

<Elder Yasu: Then we call this Convocatum to order.>

<Elder Taep: Principus Gilt, you requested this Convocatum. Will you speak?>

| The Banette nods, and in the manner of one performing a ceremony, slowly raises his hand in a fist.

<Elder Yasu: We acknowledge the Principus Gilt. Speak.>

| He stands, and steps into the center of the circle.

<Principus Gilt: Friends! We have always been independent, ab antiquo. Why, then, have we taken in these refugees? For what purpose does this serve? Do we submit to the humans, now, merely because their shape has changed? Do we release our freedom, hard sought and hard defended, to these tyrants, now that they have been disposed?>

| From the middle section, containing the youngest of the Residents, a cry rings out.

<Various: NO!>

<Principus Gilt: NO! Certainly not. We should turn them out, turn them away, to face the Dragon hordes. We have barely maintained our peace before the event, barely managed to gather enough food to survive, barely managed to forge this community, this society, this family. The situation as it stands now cannot continue, it mustnt continue, it WILL. NOT. CONTINUE!>

<Various: NO!>

| He resumes his seat, and grins cockily at the Elders.

| The room fills with the murmurs of the crowd: some approving, some disapproving, others just trying to keep their heads down.

| Visibly straining with effort, Elder Yasu fully straightens up.

| She is so quiet as to be barely heard, yet all fall silent and hang of every word she says.

<Elder Yasu: We have discussed this before. We have voted, we have decided, and that is final. Until the situation changes significantly, this shall be final. Enough.>

| The mid section, the same that shouted out during Gilts speech, starts getting rowdy, loudly and almost violently objecting.

| Once more, the Dusknoir floating above the crowd stirs.

<Falx Tenebrae:    SILENCE.   >

| The crowd immediately falls silent once more, frightened speechless of the imposing figure.

<Elder Yasu: Thank you, Falx Tenebrae.>

| Next to her, Elder Taep clears his throat noisily.

<Elder Taep: Now that that unpleasantness is done, is there anything else anyone wishes to speak on? No? Then we call this Convocatum adjourned. Thank you.>

| With that, the crowd files out of the Great Hall, murmuring worriedly.

| Once everyone has left, the only two remaining in the chamber are Del and the Dusknoir, Falx Tenebrae.

<Del: So, youre name is Falx, then?>

<Falx Tenebrae: No. Falx is my title.>

<Del: Oh! Youre name is Tenebrae, huh? Thats a funny name. It sounds old, like, really old. Like, super duper dead language old. Its also too long. Del doesnt like it. Oh! Del knows! Dels gonna give you a nickname! Dels gonna call you... Tenae! Yeah! Youre new name is Tenae. Well, nice to meet you, Tenae. Dels name is Del.>

<Skully: And Im Skully!>

<Del: Shush, Skully, no one asked you.>

<Skully: Aww.>

<Del: Anyways, Del will see you around!>

| He scampers out of the room energetically.

| The Dusknoir sits in the air for a while. Finally, she says:

<Falx Tenae: Probably not.>
