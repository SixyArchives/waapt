288788 Herbert40k7th Sep 2013 04:01:20 PM from Merry Olde England Get RP Mod , Relationship Status: I-It's not like I like you, or anything!
Chords of Steel

Spectra - Around 600 years ago

-Lucanus is sitting in his chambers, quill in hand. In front of him is a roll of parchment, covered in his own familiar handwriting, which he has been staring blankly at for over an hour now.-

Lucanus: ...I need to stop writing these letters. It cannot be beneficial for my mental health to be so bitterly reminded of everything I have had taken from me. And it is not as though I have any reliable way of sending them. There are all too many kingdoms that will shoot a messenger Drifloon on sight...

-He sighs and closes his eyes, attempting to let his mind relax. After a moment, he becomes aware of an Aura glowing faintly outside his door.-

Lucanus: Of course. Guards. As if I needed further reminder of the fact that I am a prisoner in all but name. No doubt they are monitoring my activity, ostensibly for my own protection. In all honestly, I would prefer imprisonment to this hollow facade of freedom.

-He gets up, rolling the sheaf of parchment into a folded scroll. He is about to drop it into the bin when he focuses more closely on the Aura outside his door, and pauses, smiling slightly despite himself.-

Lucanus: ...I have to say, I was not expecting to see her here. Glad to see that her skills have not dulled over the course of Pugilis' occupation.

-Sliding the scroll of parchment somewhere within the folds of his purple robes, he steps outside, casting a glance at the guard on duty as he does. Any distinguishing features and even her gender was hidden beneath her armour, but the Aura is all Lucanus needs to identify her.-

Lucanus: Good evening, Ayame.

-She remains silent, gaze fixed forwards.-

Lucanus: How have you been? It's nice to see a friendly face here in Spectra. Well, metaphorically speaking, of course.

Ayame: ...

Lucanus: ...I take it that the memory of our last meeting is still fresh in your mind, then. My apologies. Still, if it is no great inconvenience, I would like to ask something of you.

Ayame: Sir, I'm not supposed to be fraternising with a superior whilst on duty.

-Lucanus gives a small sigh.-

Lucanus: Ayame, please. There is no point in trying to fool an Auric. I don't want to resort to-

-The rest of his sentence is cut off by Ayame quite suddenly slamming Lucanus with a rugby tackle and knocking him off his feet. Before he can compose himself, she is on top of him, eyes glinting behind her visor and a dagger pressed up against his throat.-

Lucanus: Oof!

Ayame: You even think about blowing my cover and I make sure neither one of us gets out of here alive, understood?

Lucanus: Again, my sincerest apologies, I did not mean to imply-

-He is again interrupted by a Banette floating past.-

Libitina: <What's going on over here? I heard a commotion, and figured I ought to see if you were...>

-She trails off as she notices Lucanus pinned in place by Ayame.-

Libitina: <...Ah. Him. Did he cause you any trouble?>

Ayame: He just threatened to sell us out!

Lucanus: No, that was never my intention! I merely meant to-

Ayame: Really? Because the last time we met, you were quite clear about wanting to kill me if you ever saw me in Spectran territory again.

Lucanus: That was... my judgement was impaired. Again, my sincerest apologies, but I can assure you that I have no intention of causing either of you any harm! Just... please, I merely wish to make a simple request, and then I will leave you be.

-Ayame continues to press the dagger up against his throat for a moment, expression invisible beneath her visor, before looking up at Libitina.-

Ayame: Well, what do you reckon?

Libitina: <He's telling the truth. He's too interested in saving his own miserable spine to risk pissing us off.>

Ayame: ...Alright, fine.

-She sheathes her dagger and stands up, leaving Lucanus to pick himself up and dust himself off.-

Ayame: Make it quick. I'm already in enough trouble for just talking to you in the first place.

Lucanus: Yes, yes, of course. Now, I understand why you are not inclined to assist me. After all, my last meetings with both of you did not end on good terms, and no amount of apologies can change the fact that my words and deeds on both occasions were atrocious. I am not asking you to overlook these crimes, but-

Ayame: I said make it quick! Geez, you don't half go on a bit.

Lucanus: ...Very well. Through circumstances I would rather not go into at this moment, I became involved in a resistance movement fighting against the Ghost Lord. However, due to certain... complications, several members of this movement have been-

Libitina: <Oh come on, you don't honestly expect us to believe that crap, do you?>

Lucanus: I beg your pardon?

Libitina: <About you getting involved in a resistance. We all know that you're too cowardly and power-mad to even think about standing up to the Ghost Lord.>

Lucanus: ...I will not deny that this was the case at one point in time. However, after the... incident with Celzvet, I took your words to heart and-

Libitina: <Let me ask you something. Did you ever bother to find out what happened to Sagi after what you did to Celzvet?>

Lucanus: I... I'm afraid I never heard what became of her.

Libitina: <Of course not. I didn't think you'd bother to find out what happened to the "Viperian whore". Bastard.>

Lucanus: Libitina, please, I assure you that it was not for lack of-

Libitina: <Forget it. It's Ayame you should be trying to suck up to. After all, she's the one you actually want something from.>

-Lucanus sighs, then turns back to Ayame, who has not yet bothered to raise her visor.-

Lucanus: ...As I was saying, after an incident which I would rather not elaborate on, several members of this resistance movement were captured. Two of them are being held here in the castle, awaiting execution. We are currently planning an escape, but our window of opportunity is highly limited. I was wondering if you could-

Ayame: Forget it.

Lucanus: You didn't let me finish. You would not be participating directly in the escape, merely gathering-

Ayame: I know what you meant, and my answer's still no. Even if I wanted to stick my neck on the line for you, I can't take that kind of risk. I'm probably going to get in trouble just because I'm talking to you.

-She jabs an accusing finger at him.-

Ayame: Why do you care so much about springing protestors from the dungeons, huh?

Lucanus: Well, I... I simply believe it is the right thing to do.

Ayame: Really? Because the right thing didn't seem to matter when you were quashing rioters in Pugilis.

Lucanus: It's not... they're my friends, I have to-

Ayame: Oh, so you're fine with setting your bullying thugs on nameless dissidents, but when it's your friends that get into trouble suddenly saving them is your top concern? Everything's about you, isn't it?

-Lucanus hesitates for a moment, then sighs as he realises that he has no way to answer Ayame's accusation.-

Lucanus: ...So that's a no, then?

Ayame: Yup.

Lucanus: Fair enough. Then I only have one more thing to ask of you.

-He pulls the scroll of parchment from his robes and extends it to Ayame.-

Lucanus: I do not believe it is too much of a stretch to assume that it was Aiura who sent you here. If that is indeed the case, then I would like you to deliver this to her.

-Ayame examines the scroll for a moment, then snatches it from his hands.-

Ayame: Fine. I'm probably gonna have to head back to Illusio immediately anyway, thanks to you nearly blowing my cover.

Lucanus: Understood. Thank you for doing this for me, Ayame. I am immensely-

Ayame: I'm not doing this for you. I'm doing this for Aiura. She's the one that still cares about what happens to you, not me.

Lucanus: ...Fair enough. In any case, that is all I wished to request from you. I shall be heading out for my regular walk in the villages now. Farewell... and again, thank you.

-He turns and walks away, Ayame and Libitina watching him go.-

Ayame: ...Do you reckon he's heard the news?

Libitina: <About what?>

Ayame: About... well, you know. Aiura and the baby.

Libitina: <Oh, that? Probably not. I doubt he cares, anyway.>

Ayame: ...Yeah, you're probably right. So, what are we going to tell Aiura? We didn't get to do a lot of, well, actual observing.

Libitina: <We've got what he told us, provided that any of that Tauroscrap is actually true. Plus, considering how long I worked for him, I can fill in the gaps myself if need be. I never did tell you what the Celzvet thing was all about, did I?>

Ayame: No, you didn't... but that's a story you can save for the journey back.

-With a small sigh, she starts to head back towards the castle doors.-
"Isn't violence against hair a crime, Your Honour?"
