Mt. Pyre

Phoebe strode across the cold, damp grass of the summit, nearing the shrine dedicated to the two orbs of Hoenn. To think that possession of two jewels was all that stood between their region and absolute destruction. An amusing, if disturbing thought.

It wasn't long before she reached the shrine that she'd noticed her grandparents, her grandfather smiling kindly at her as he ritualistically cleaned the two orbs. Her grandmother, on the other hand, stepped forward, appraising her granddaughter curiously.

Phoebe's Grandmother: I really wish you'd buy some shoes, dear.

Phoebe: I've never really been one for footwear. All the styles and fashions never really appealed to me.

Phoebe's Grandmother: Haha...just like your mother in that regard.

She peered closely at Phoebe, as if noticing something. The girl blinked, tilting her head at the sudden examination.

Phoebe: Something on my face, grandma?

The old woman smiled and shook her head, chuckling softly.

Phoebe's Grandmother: No no...it's not that. Now then...may I speak to my granddaughter?

Phoebe: ... ... ...

Phoebe sighed, folding her arms in irritation.

Phoebe:    We were having fun...   

Her a dark-blue light shone around her figure, radiating ghostly energies everywhere.

Then, a head poked out of Phoebe's stomach, followed by a few arms, legs, and an assortment of other limbs. With some small effort, several beings forced their way out of the Elite's body, floating behind her once they had fully emerged. The familiar forms of two Banette, a Sableye, two Dusclops, and a Dusknoir materialized behind their trainer, Phoebe's grandmother shaking her head again.

Phoebe's Grandmother: That girl seriously needs to invest in some Pokeballs.

Free of her possession, Phoebe yawned tiredly as she came down from her trance. She rubbed at her eyes and stretched her limb, getting used to using her body again after...how many days had it been since she was in control? She tended to lose track when she got caught up in her meditations.

After making sure that her body was still functional, she turned to the elderly woman with a dopey little smile, knowing full well that she was probably going to get a lecture about this.

Phoebe: Hi, grandma.
