Slateport Bay

The sound of a motorbike shut off, as Ray slipped off, a bit shakily. There were two bandages on her face, one on her forehead and another on her cheek. Turning around to the docking bay, she sighed as she saw the small white ship docked up ahead.

Slowly, she walked up, taking in the eerie calm of the sparkling water of the sea, and a slight buzzing sound coming from the ship. With a slight oh!", she realized those were actually voices, projecting out from a music player inside the ship's deck.

She giggled, and headbopped a little as she came up to the ship, in spite of the song's dark lyrics. Slowly, she knocked on the door, and upon hearing nothing, crept inside.

What she saw was an incredible mess and incredibly neat, the floor was all clean, but the bed was unmade. There was no dust on anything, but it was all cluttered in assorted places around the room. All of the books were kept safely inside the shelves, while the clothes were hanging in piles off of hooks and on the chairs. There was a...sad feel to it, Ray observed. She looked around, curiously, and came upon a photo on the desk, next to the still running laptop. It was of her, Marie, and Locke, with her and Marie blowing a noisemaker into an unamused Locke's face.

She chuckled a bit, sadly tracing Marie and Locke's face as she did so. And then, from behind her, a voice went "I dunno what's more creepy, that you snuck in here, or that I find you touching my face."

She jumped, and within a second or so had the voice pinned against the ground by the throat, and jumped back when she saw the scruffy, bearded, grinning face of Locke.

"Woah, Locke!" Ray stood shocked for a bit, and then helped Locke up hurriedly up and giving him a crushing hug."Oh, man, it's so great to see you again!"

Locke laughed a bit, grunting in pain as he was hugged."Ooof!Weary bones..are not that strong, Ray."

Shyly, Ray stepped back."Right, right." She then looked around the room, and then back at Locke. She held his his gaze for a couple of seconds, before punching him on the arm."Where've you been?!"

"Oww!" Locke, said, wincing as he nursed his arm. In doing so, Ray spotted the drink in his hand. She raised an eyebrow suspiciously. "What's in there?"

Locke took a long swig, and passed her the plastic bottle."Nothing now. A minute ago, it was milk..with some flavor." She sniffed it, and made a gagging sound."Ugh, beer. Bad memories." When she turned, Locke was sitting on the railing, staring off into the sea.

Ray came and sat down next to him on the railing, looking at him curiously. Locke looked at her the music going on in the background."I've failed a lot of people, Ray. Someone's either messing with me, or killing people for fun."

Ray pat his arm."I know it seems hard, but you'll get through it, man. You've always had."

He looked at her with this scared, sad look in his eyes, and shook his head."That's the issue, Ray. I'm always doing this." He hopped back on to the deck, and filled up his bottle again, and began swigging again."There's always a psycho, there's always death, there's always a bloody crime scene, and there's always someone I have to go up to and say 'I'm so sorry for your loss.'" He covered his face with his had, rubbing his eyes."I just..I wanted to get away for a bit." He stared at his bottle, looking at the liquid flowing, sloshing around inside."And this stuff..this makes me forget..it makes everything feel so great, so light, so..happy..." He then tossed the bottle back on to the main deck dejectedly."Guess it kept me here longer than I needed to be."

Ray gave him a good strong hug, and whispered "It's okay..we all need a break sometimes..but we need you, man. Please, come back."

Locke hugged her, sighing deeply into the embrace, and pulled back teary eyed."...Sure...Just as soon as I clean things up."

They hop off, and go inside the ship, while the boombox seemed to switch songs, now becoming: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=x7ogV49WGco

So..hey guys.:D
