Captain: We've really got to do something about this place.

-A new aspect, wearing Eskay's coat and captain's hat, is pacing along the Bridge of Dreams. In front of him is a group consisting of the rest of the aspects.-

Captain: First off, names. This whole name-by-age thing isn't really working. Too many of us, too many drastic changes in too small a time. So...

-He looks at the aspect donning the Elda mask.-

Captain: You. Guardian. Live up to your name, but don't go overboard. You're the Heart aspect, you can tell when someone's trying to break stuff, so don't go killing people that are trying to help.

15!Eskay Guardian: We cannot afford to risk—

Captain: See, that's the problem with this place. One of us goes crazy, and the rest of us go overboard trying to fix that and break something else. Just follow my directions, okay?

-Guardian shuts up and backs off.-

Captain: Good. Now that we've got that squared away, you can have this back.

-He tosses the Staff of Hunger toward Guardian, then turns to Toxicroak!Sarah.-

Captain: Prowess, deal with the Fears. You've already taken the Toxicroak Horde under your command, so deal with the others. And this time, don't leave any of them bleeding out in the river. We have a graveyard for a reason. Stick 'em all in the same general place I relocated Deimos's body to.

Toxicroak!Sarah Prowess: Hey, can you blame me? The bastard was a disgrace to humanity, so he deserved an inhumane, disgraceful death.

Captain: The fact that we have a total psychopath here scares me.

-Prowess giggles-

Prowess: You should be scared~ I'll be headed out now, 'kay, thanks, bye!

-She jumps off the side of the bridge and into the water. Captain sighs.-

Captain: Sanity, go make sure she doesn't do anything stupid.

-He gestures toward the main Sarah aspect, who crosses her arms-

Sarah Sanity: I'm not exactly fond of the way you're suddenly trying to take over.

Captain: Hate to break it to you, but it didn't seem like anything was gonna go right when you were in charge. If you're scared, take one of Hindsight's swords.

Sanity: ...Fine. Whatever.

-She leaves, annoyed. Captain looks at 14!Eskay.-

Captain: Paramour... for the love of Ho-oh, put that projector back.

-The romantic aspect in question blushes brightly, stowing away the memory projector. Captain rolls his eyes.-

Captain: Look, I get that you're still fawning over Lucius, but if you're gonna keep obsessing over memories... at least tidy up the Forget-Me-Not Shop or something. That place is a complete mess, especially after Guardian's little scuffle with Kenneth's Pokemon.

14!Eskay Paramour: Er, r-right.

-He shuffles off toward the city. Another aspect rolls his eyes.-

12!Eskay: Look, if you're just gonna throw stupid names and pointless jobs at us, we're gonna leave. At least we can keep a bit of dignity.

-He drags off 10!Eskay toward the city.-

Captain: ...There goes Cynic and Doormat. Let's see

-He looks at the three other aspects, and directs his attention to the Innovator.-

Captain: Innovator! Glad to see you made it here. I like you. You're dutiful, you've got a good name already Listen, mind fixing up the damage around the city? You're good at the whole world-making thing, no doubt you can make it at least a bit more pleasant to look at.

Innovator: I'll see what I can do.

-He walks off, fixing cracks in the pavement as he walks by them.-

Captain: All right, that leaves you two. So Migrant er, what exactly are you here for in the first place?

Mezzo!Eskay Migrant: Er insecurity?

Captain: Right, right, insecurity. Let's fix that. Go and make sure things are more secure around town. Like, check locks and stuff so that if something decides to try and break stuff, they can't kill us.

Migrant: ...Okay?

-She walks off, somewhat confused. Captain shrugs, and looks down at the final, rather short aspect.-

Captain: Okay, kid I don't really have a better name for you, so I'm just gonna keep calling you "Kid", okay, Kid?

5!Eskay Kid: Okay! What do I do?

Captain: ...I dunno, just keep the others from getting too terribly bored, I—

-And he's already running off.-

Captain: ...I guess. Well, that's that!
