After mulling over his course of action, Rex decided that it might be best to catch up with the group at another time. While he still had the intention of assisting with whatever problem seemed to be occurring within the Dream World, he still had other obligations to fulfill at the moment.

Obligations that would need to be completed at a more leisurely pace.

As such, he decided to return to the market and pick up any supplies that he would be needing for the ensuing trip. To his recollection, there was a shop that sold Moo Moo Milk. Such a treat would be appreciated by his Pokemon. Owen would likely enjoy it as well. And it'd serve as a nice reminder of his home region as well.

Stepping into the shop, already doing the math of how much a certain number of bottles would cost, he found himself in front of the stand scant moments later, placing the bills onto the counter and looking at the clerk.

Rex: This should be enough for 75 bottles, right?

The store owner gave him an odd look, but the brunette simply shrugged. Protagonists didn't need to worry about spilling, refrigerating, or carrying their inventory after all.

Unova Route 6

Since there were no Dream Rifts or any of Lyuri's Pokémon in Driftveil City, everyone had rightfully decided that it was time to move on. So they had begun their trek up Route 6 to the Chargestone Cave, and Mistralton City beyond.

There was one major problem, however. And that problem was rain.

Comet: <ARGH! STUPID RAIN! WHY DID YOU HAVE TO WAIT UNTIL WE LEFT BEFORE YOU STARTED?!>

Wet and miserable, Comet trudged up the road alongside everyone else, attempting to keep the rain of herself by hiding under her wings.

Stratus: <Hey, if it helps, I could make you a Bubble Shield, Comet.>

Stratus' kind offer, however, was met with a death glare from Comet.

Comet: <You're proposed plan to keep water off me is to surround me with more water?!>

Stratus: <But...but it's hard water!>

Now enraged, the Charizard snarled, licks of flames escaping from her throat.

Comet: <THAT'S ICE, YOU MORON!>

Stratus: <EEP!>

With Comet getting testy - and likely to start burning people in annoyance - Treeo hopped over to try and calm her down.

Treeo: <Easy, Comet. Stratus is just trying to help.>

Comet: <Rrrrr...>

Growling at the now quivering Dragonite, the Charizard paused for a moment, then let out a sigh.

<...I'm sorry. Rain always makes me miserable...>

Unite: <Oh! Chargestone cave just ahead!>

Unite - who was out in front - pointed a wing at the mouth of a cave in the mountain just ahead, an eerie blue go coming from within.

Comet didn't have to be told twice, as the Charizard broke into a sprint, rushing past the rest of the group and into the cave to escape the poring rain, much to everyone else's surprise.

Woolly: <Wow!>

Lake: <I keep forgetting just how fast she is!>

Unite: <She's got the right idea, though. Come on, let's get out of this rain! My feathers are getting soaked!>

Following Comet's example, the team headed for the cave and followed inside after her.

Chargestone Cave

While most caves were dark, Chargestone cave was one of the few that wasn't. The large amount of electrically charged stones gave everything an soft, almost eerie blue glow, just enough light to see by without getting lost in darkness.

As well as this, the other most notable feature of the cave was the large blue rocks hanging in the air, their crystalline surfaces cracking with electrical discharge as they quietly defied gravity. It was quite a strange sight.

Treeo: <Well, that's not something you see everyday.>

Unite nodded as he gazed at his surroundings, keeping an eye out for any wild Pokémon.

Unite: <That's Chargestone Cave for you. This place has such a high electrical charge that some of the rocks just...float in mid-air. It's a real strange place, and a haven for electric type Pokémon.>

Woolly: <Hey, ya' got that right! All the Electricity in the air...>

Woolly breathed a happy sigh.

<I've only been in here for about a minute, and I already feel fully recharged!>

Lake: <There's a slight problem with all this Electricity, though.>

Stratus: <What's that?>

Lake: <Well, let me take a moment to go over our types. Of course, I'm a Water Type. Comet's Fire and Flying, Treeo's Grass, Stratus is Dragon and Flying, and Unite is Normal and Flying. Do you not see the problem here?>

Comet: <That half of us are weak to Electricity?>

Comet nodded.

<Yeah, I noticed.>

Unite: <Well, duh. I'm actually kinda' nervous. Especially since I think it would be easier to find any missing Pokémon or Dream Rifts if we split up.>

Stratus: <But I thought you weren't ever supposed to split the party?>

Comet: <Oh, we'll be fine. Some of us are League Champions!>

She then pointed a claw at Lake.

<And did your forget that your son was Ho-Oh's Champion? I hardly think the Pokémon here are going to give us that much trouble, even if we do split up!>

Lake: <Even so, I think it would be best if we split into groups of two. One Pokémon being weak to electric, and one resisting it. That way, we can cover each other's weaknesses!>

Treeo: <Ooh, I like you're thinking, Lake!>

Unite: <Oh! OH! Dibs on Woolly!>

Stratus: <No fair, I wanted to go with Woolly!>

Woolly: <...Gosh, I'm popular all of a sudden...>

Treeo: <How about me and you go together, Lake? I think we'd make a good combination!>

Lake: <Sounds good to me!>

Comet, realizing that the choice of who she would go with had already been made for her, glanced over at Stratus, who responded with an awkward smile.

Stratus: <So...it's you and me, huh?>

Comet said nothing, choosing instead to glance down at the floor and shake her head.

Unite: <Okay then! We'll split up and comb this cave for any missing Pokémon or Dream Rifts, and meet up at the other end. Alright team, Move out!>

And the groups dispersed, each heading off in a different direction into the cave.
