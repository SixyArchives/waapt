Rotom-Z

Stat spread seems okay, yes.

However...

First, I would agree with Eskay that item use would have to replace the moves of both mons taking part in the Rotom-Z. There are plenty of ways for a Rotom or Porygon-Z to attack both mons in a Double Battle using only a single move, so it wouldn't be trading damage to one of the opponent's mons for the item use. In addition to this, the RP mechanics we use for battles usually preclude oneshotting. In light of this, it would be all to easy to simply Hyper/Max Potion Rotom-Z each turn, keeping its HP at full or close while dealing damage to the opponent (since even with resistance, those hits add up).

Next, there is the issue of a certain stalling move known as Protect (and really all non-damaging moves with useful effects). Since Rotom-Z is only a single mon, it would more or less gain damage immunity every other turn, since it can use Protect, followed by the other half attacking with whatever other attack it wants to use. Similar logic can be applied to Thunder Wave (knock out a mon low on health, paralyze the other to keep it from getting in the way), Taunt (negate an opponent's setup and hit them for damage), and so on.

One possible solution that I could see would be to require that both of Rotom-Z's actions each turn be the same action. So you could Hyper Potion Rotom-Z, but then you can't attack during the turn. You can have one side use Protect, but the other side needs to use it too. In exchange for this, I would remove the stipulation that attacking twice would require Rotom-Z to split its attack stat between the two moves (so yes, a double Discharge at 145 attack is a thing that is possible >:3).

Of course...that hardly makes up for both the opponent's mons focusing fire on Rotom-Z, does it? I mean, if it doesn't take out every opponent really quickly it's gonna go down pretty fast. How is that useful in battle?

I would say in this case that it probably isn't, at least not for a normally functional Rotom-Z. While the idea of Rotom-Z is a pretty cool one, I think it would be difficult beyond belief to make it both viable in battle and not broken. If it's not as useful as two mons fighting separately then it's not good enough, if it is as useful as two mons fighting separately it produces some serious balance issues.

However, in the case of Linux and Myra, it's extremely useful. From what I've gathered, Linux working alone is more or less useless due to the glitchiness causing blindness and likely some other nasty symptoms. In tandem with Myra, that's no longer the case. As such, while Rotom-Z is probably not a useful construction normally (again, explaining why similar things are not in widespread use), for Myra and Linux specifically (or Linux, anyway), it's probably quite a boon.
