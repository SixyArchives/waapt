adopting kitteh
A couple of personal thoughts on the matter of the Distribution of Ninjutsu:

Right now, we have a simply egregious disparity both in power level and in power capacity. Characters like Tagg and Fool can sun-incinerate areas as large as a city, summon Legendaries for a FATALITY, or create whole mons out of thin air, apparently. When not running a tournament policy that can be homologated to "your mon has a maximum HP of 400", there's simply not fighting something like that; but when fighting within a tournament policy, we won't be able to do much against individual level differences either because for our mons to be able to pull that level of stuff, they have to be quite powerful. On the other hand, it does make putting higher bounds on hax opponent power level easier, even if at the cost of making the ensuing battles highly predictable (not boring though).

Fortunately, we do not need a Power Creep, Power Seep (yet), we do not (for the moment) need a deleveling nor any of such things. And personally I would be against a deleveling because there is pretty much no way to hammer that in "fairly". What we do need is to actually, you know, take damage in battle and suffer the consequences. Have to use, or fail to use, the Medicine section of our item bags for something.

Of course we don't want our mons to die, and I definitively won't be asking for that (unless it helps the plot or whatever), but there is a wide assortment of Next Best Things we can use. For example, I can't remember the last time one of our mons took enough damage to be knocked out of commission for half an Arc while he goes to surgery, then rehabilitation program, then therapy or whatever. And none of our mons has performed an actually sacrificial Heroic Sacrifice ever since the AU, they are all just reduced to "I step in front of you and use Protect". All in all, we simply do not need to essentially FLAWLESS VICTORY our way into antagonist ranks.

But all this comes down to a deeper issue, one that prevents options like those actually factoring, an issue with the RP playerbase mindset that makes them "bet against the market" of character and plot development. This is the issue of Tunnel Vision, and I think it merits further, deeper discussion when enough of us can meet.

And yes, I know the universe hates me and all those who live south of the Equator. And I also know that Tracer logged in about as soon as I left. Thank you, Universe.    <_<

There is another potential solution to the overpoweredness problem, which also ties into the "Save/Defeat X" issue. For the most part, most of the problems in this RP are mostly solved by having a fight with whoever is causing the problem.
