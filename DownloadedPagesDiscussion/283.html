7069 Luke9245th Aug 2012 06:50:52 AM Get RP Mod

On the new guy

Hello, Matt. If you have a Totodile line member as your Pokform, I will love you forever. Literally. Also, what I'm about to say will help you in the long run, so sit tight.

On Newbies and Joining

It's been brought to my concern that very few people are actually making an effort to sort out the issues we have been having with newbies. Having done some thinking about it, I think I may have found the issue here.

It's not that the sign-up process isn't filtering the good from the bad, it's that after a newbie has told us what their name is, what their character is like and what sort of Pokmon they have (And hopefully what they're like, too), we then just pretty much leave them to fend for themselves.

And herein lies the problem.

Recall what happened with Musik a few weeks ago. He did some worldbuilding that violated a few things with the AU and PMD-B Sinnoh, and we were quick to assume that he was just another newbie causing trouble.

But on looking back at it, how was he even supposed to know what he did was wrong when nobody even bothered to give him a crash course in the world of WAAPT and how it works? It was akin to just tossing him in the ocean and shouting at him when he started drowning!

In other words, Musik wasn't the problem. It was us.

We've been trying to look for solutions in all the wrong places. Sure, we could require people to fill in a proper sign-up sheet, but that's not going to solve the problem if we just leave newbies to fend for themselves like this.

What we instead need to do is offer guidance.

When somebody new wants to join, we shouldn't just ask for a few details and let them loose, we should give them a crash course on WAAPT and how it works. We should spend time telling them the intricacies of this world, what they can and can't do, and give them tips on how to RP well with us, perhaps help them nail down some more details of their characters.

Then, when they're ready to actually start R Ping, we should assign them someone who's available to act as their R Ping partner for a while so that they don't have to wander around blindly for hours with nothing to do.

Granted, I'm not sure if this method would filter out the genuinely damaging players like Ori or 49, but it will make things much easier for both us and newbies.

I don't think it would be hard to make just a bit more effort on this front, so can you consider it, please?
