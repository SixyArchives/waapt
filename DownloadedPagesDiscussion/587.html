Since it's been a while, and Orre is just around the corner, I'm going to repost the Orre Subplot I made back in July.

After much cutting of fat, here the most recent write up of the detailed (but more troper-esque) version:

Title

Song of the Storm

Overview

It's gotten hot in Orre - well, even more hot. Rain has stopped falling in Agate village. Cipher is going after the Relic Stone/ Celebi connection ... again!(?) But this time Cipher's trying to get rid of Celebi, not the Relic Stone. It's up to the J-Team to stop Cipher.

Detailed Summary

Some time after arriving in Orre, someone is visited by Meloetta, and tells them that She needs the J-Team's help to save her friend, Celebi, and Agate forest. As it turns out, Cipher is going after the Relic Stone/ Celebi connection, but this time Cipher's trying to get rid of Celebi. After all, the Relic Stone is just a peace of rock without Celebi. Upon inspection, the J-team finds out what's Cipher doing: they're weaponizing Shadow Ninetales' Drought ability, with a machine that boosts the Droughts effects and preventing rain from falling, drying up irrigation systems and killing the forest to weaken Celebi!

Better get rid of that machine fast! While in raid is in progress, a Cipher operative jams the controls of the Drought Machine (or just out right disables it) and one successfully destroyed outpost/ lab later, various J-team members are scavenging the computers for useful info, but why is it still not raining? Well according to plans a personmon found, the only way to properly stop the machine was to hit the off switch before destroying the machine. Cipher command had left instructions for the machine to be sabotaged in case of a raid. Now the effects, while weaker without the Shadow Ninetales, will take a long time fade due to a power source that keeps the Drought Effect going. Cipher got smart - their plan can still work.

Experimentation with Rain Dance and Drizzle Politoed show that while rain effects stop and weaken the Drought Effect quicker, the rain effects won't stick as the Drought refreshes itself every so often. Well we can't let a few trainers and their Drought Politoed sit here for 20 years, so what to do? Well, Meloetta offers a solution; she remembers that many centuries ago she composed and choreographed a song and dance to boost the effects of weather moves. So Meloetta leads some mon and some humans in performing the song and dance. With Meloettas song the mons Rain Dance is boosted so much that the Drought Effect's power source is depleted trying to overcome the power of the boosted Rain Dance.

With that mess taken care of Agate Forest is safe again. Now the J-Team can move on to the next Cipher base.

Time Frame

A week at most

Purpose of plot

1. Getting rid of Cipher's presence in Agate
2. To Provide Meloetta and Celebi with some screen time.

A little late to the punch here (when am I ever not?), but I will say that I agree very much with Tangent from the last page. Maybe it's because I was around at the times when we had no idea what the hell we were doing aside from end goals like "stop 'M and oh, gym badges" for the most part, and a good part of the posting was just conversations between the cast that led to some fun, wacky moments (the conversation that went from "we seriously need to exchange phone numbers" to "Pentigan is a minor deity (even Raikou says so)" to "Crewe is a Soul of Silver and Pentigan might be telepathic" is a favorite of mine), but I feel like everyone plans out every step of their own plans that they want to go down in a certain arc, and anyone who wants to participate has to either sign up ahead of time, or struggle to make a difference. I get that the RP has changed a lot since those days, but improv is one of my favorite aspects of RP, and I've said this before, but I get less satisfaction out of plots that are "Go here, check X, Y, Z off the list, arc is done!" than arcs that are more like "Generic bad guy has done something heinous! Come up with a plan to stop them!", and it doesn't even have to involve bad guys. "There's been an avalanche in the mountains, the only way around them takes you weeks out of the way, and the Maxi Eary concert is TOMORROW! What do you do?" is as valid an example, if sillier. I guess it feels like everything improv nowadays is just insignificant (in terms of plot) interaction and minor shenanigans, and it's like, if you don't have every step along the way planned out, it's not gonna be considered as a plot. That may be something of an exaggeration, but it feels stifling to me.

That also ties into whoever it was that said that people spend so much time planning out their individual subplots that the main plot that is supposed to unite everyone kind of... falls along the wayside.

So... that's been a rambling post. But does anyone else see what I'm talking about, here?
