Okay, so, I had this idea sometime last night after a conversation with Verax. I think I'll just let it speak for itself.

How to Improvise, OR, How To Make My Posts a Souffle of Flavor

Welcome, everyone, to the first of what is likely to be a series of posts regarding something that I hold very dear to my heart...

Champloo: <Welsh Corgis?>

No! Well, yes, I DO hold those close to my heart, but that's not what I mean. I mean the art of improvisation!

Champloo: <That was my second guess.>

Anyway, today, my assistant and I will be discussing the basics: What is improvisation? How do I improvise? How can I use it to make my posts more interesting? How do I know I'm not going too far with the crazy? Each of these will have more dedicated posts later on, but for now, the basics.

Champloo: <And to keep things clear, we'll be doing them in that order.>

Right. So, let's begin.

What is Improvisation? How Do I Improvise?

"Improvising" is, as defined by The Other Wiki, means "the process of devising a solution to a requirement by making-do, despite absence of resources that might be expected to produce a solution". So, what does that mean for YOU?

Champloo: <Let's say you want to "make" an interaction between two characters. But, you don't have a fun or interesting place for it to take place. Instead of just ignoring the surroundings you could spice things up a bit by making up a "solution" to your "problem" of not having anything interesting to do.>

Let's say Dune is talking to Deziree. They're in the middle of... let's say Petalburg. Petalburg has very, very little in it, despite the fact that it's supposed to be a rather nice, moderately populated town. So, where would they possibly be talking? Well, this is where improvisation comes in. Let's examine the thought process of finding this "improvised solution".

1) A town the size of Petalburg would, logically, have several restaurants.

2) At least one of these restaurants is bound to be mildly upscale and family owned.

3) The restaurant would have a "special" - a dish that they take immense pride from, like a special kind of pie or a dish not native to the region.

4) The name of the restaurant will reflect the owner(s) or their theme, based on their dish.

Answering these question alone will give you a memorable place that adds to the flavor of the interaction. In fact, just writing this, I came up with this: "The Gutierrez Brother's Bar and Grill", a [Mexican] restaurant that specializes in spicy foods such as chili, fajitas, and a special quesadia filled with spiced cheese.

Champloo: <Another way of improvising is with the use of Adjectives. Those spicy little descriptors can turn a bland interaction into an amazing, memorable part of the Crowning Moment of Funny page.>

It's kinda like playing Mad Libs. Bear with me on this one. Okay, Champloo, give me three adjectives, since you're so caught up on those right now.

Champloo: <Hmmm... Flaming, rotten, and prickly.>

My, just a bundle of flowers you are today... okay, so, going by those, I came up with this:

Dune and Deziree were taking a stroll in the park when a [flaming] man came up with a [rotten] Snubbull. They asked where we could find a [prickly] place to stay.

Champloo: <I, uh, don't think that last one quite works.>

With the list of adjectives placed here and there, I just made a standard interaction memorable. Instead of just a man coming up and asking the couple for a place to stay, we have a FABULOOOOOOOUS~ man with a spoiled, pampered Snubbull accompanying. As for the "prickly" part, that could be interpreted as... oh, let's say that it means "of questionable quality", since who would want to stay at a hotel whose thread count on their sheets is so low that they "prick" you?

Champloo: <Add in a comment that he spends all of his money on "his little Snubbull Shnoodles" and you have a logical, flavorful souffle of an interaction. HAH! See what I did there?>

That'll wrap things up for today's post since I've already made it quite long. Don't forget to comment and subscribe, and I'll have another post ready for you guys tomorrow! Say bye, Champloo!

Champloo: <Remember! Snacks are okay so long as you keep them light enough to not spoil your appetite!>
