 21383 Herbert40k, Sun, 29th Jun '14 3:58:54 PM from Merry Olde England Get RP Mod Relationship Status: I-It's not like I like you, or anything!
EYE SEE YOU
Moon: I can say with absolute certainty that nothing you said or did encouraged me to leave. I realise that my earlier D!Post was made in a bit of a huff, so I've come back to try and explain things.

To clarify, I am leaving, and I don't plan on coming back. Whilst I don't want to make this an acrimonious departure, since there's still a lot I like about WAAPT and people in the community that I admire, I just can't participate in the RP anymore thanks to a combination of factors.

This depature probably seems entirely out of left field, given that I've only outlined the full extent of my issues to one person. It'd be callous of me to just up and leave without even attempting to justify why, and since I'm not planning on returning I can be entirely honest for once. So I'm going to go ahead and list my reasons for leaving/gripes with the RP. I don't expect anything to be done about them, given that I'm only one person who's leaving anyway, but I figure I owe you guys an explanation.

In no particular order, and with no particular emphasis:
  • Relationships in WAAPT are treated as immutable both in the meta and in the RP itself. Even if a couple clearly isn't working out, people are more concerned with maintaining the status quo rather than letting writers do what they want with their own characters, as was seen in the aftermath of the Psyrah breakup. The idea that a canon couple might not last forever is downright taboo, and even if they're clearly incompatible they should be regarded as just having "issues" that they need to work out. I can't get behind this sort of clinginess surrounding ships. I prefer an interesting story to watching perfect couples be lovey-dovey with each other.
  • Players not only defend but are outright proud of the fact that their characters are unrepentant mass-murderers. Someone who has a body count of hundreds and routinely gets away with murder would be the villain in most works, not a hero or protagonist. Taking another's life in WAAPT is treated with the same weight as jaywalking and petty theft - unless of course the villains do it, in which case it's unforgiveable. The fact that people (both characters and writers) are willing to brush stuff like RP!Tagg's snuff filming under the rug, and in some cases try to justify it as the right thing to do, is indictative of an endemic case of Protagonist-Centered Morality. Several of the "heroes" on the J-Team have done far worse than most of the villains and never been punished for it. I can't get invested in something where I want the people you're supposed to be rooting for to be put away.
  • Several characters are stupidly overpowered - and these are usually the very characters that shouldn't be stupidly overpowered for reasons of potential abuse. Munn, a dangerous psychotic barely clinging onto sanity (who was supposed to get less murderous but didn't) is also an invincible cyborg. RP!Tagg, a ruthless vigilante with the same regard for human life as a category 8 earthquake, can stop time and bend reality to his will. Never once is the idea that these might not be the best sort of people to wield godlike powers considered.
  • I was getting sick of writing for Lucius, and I wouldn't blame people if they were getting sick of reading him. The whole Auric Avenger/superhero thing in general was probably a pretty dumb idea, now that I think about it. It seemed cool at first, but I had nowhere to go with it and nothing to do with it, not to mention the fact that it grew to take over most of my other plotlines like an inoperable tumour. Seeing it through to its conclusion would have just resulted in page after page of repetitive angsty tripe.
  • Writers throwing their characters into situations where the only possibility is defeat and failing to realise this fact until after said defeat. In general, the power balance in WAAPT is fucked so hard it can't even walk straight anymore.

So that I don't end up completely blue-balling everybody I had plans with, you're welcome to consider all of my characters open source, assuming anybody actually wants to write for them. Hopefully people are mature enough to decide who gets who amongst themselves. Feel free to PM me if you need details about their personalities, what I had planned for them, or who I'd rather have write for them.

And I do believe that's it. I'll still be responsive to TVT PMs, and I can provide Pigdin/Skype details if people want to stay in touch. Goodbye, and here's hoping that the RP remains enjoyable even if it lost its lustre for me.
