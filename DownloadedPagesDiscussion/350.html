Sorry about that, Daisy. I was trying to interact with you, yesterday, but my computer's battery died. -pats-

As for Conquest...yeah, I'm going to have to say you're limited to one move, at least most of the time. Because Conquest battles should be conducted with multiple Pokemon on the field, in general. Since there can be up to 12 Pokemon on the field at once, the strategy is in choosing a diverse range of Pokemon to cover your weaknesses, instead of a single Pokemon with diverse moves.

That said, you can choose the move at the start of the battle, much like we do now. And one-on-one Conquest battles (which I anticipate will happen now and then) can play out similarly to modern Pokemon battles, since they're the original inspiration, at least in terms of chronology.

Hmm...speaking of moves...would anyone be especially upset if TMs didn't exist in the Conquest era? So Pokemon can't learn any of their TM moves. They can still learn their Natural movesets (duh), their Tutor movesets, their Breeding movesets (assuming their parents don't only learn them through TMs), and the move they have in Conquest, if it doesn't fit into any of the previous categories.
