KAT USED EARTHQUAKE

<Audrey: "AUGH!

AUDREY USED AURA SPHERE

KAT USED EARTHQUAKE!

AUDREY USED AURA SPHERE!

-And casts another blue orb at the Marowak-

<Kat: "Mmph. I think I'll taking a page from the past on this one.">

-And bats away the Aura Sphere with her club-

<Audrey: "... You remembered that from Sinnoh?"> ._.

<Kat: "I am a lorekeeper, Audrey. Also, you might want to brace yourself.">

KAT USED EARTHQUAKE!

-Which sends a certain presenter sprawling-

<Audrey: "O-Okay, I think that I technically have 2 or 3 hit points left, but I think that that's good enough for demonstration purposes."> @_@

-And picks herself up-

<Audrey: "Now, if that was a battle, hopefully, someone would come in to take my place... We'll say... Milo.">

<Milo: "WHAT?!"> O_O

-Smirking-

<Kat: "Well now, weasel boy... I think I'm gonna enjoy this."> >:)

<Audrey: "Now... If this were the games, Milo could probably use Aqua Jet, force Kat to take a break and move on to the next mon.">

<Milo: "Uh? Hello? She'll split my skull open with that thing if I don't get her down!"> O.O

<Audrey: "Oh come on, she wouldn't- ... Well... Maxed out attack... And proximity... Maybe it's doable...

But the point is, Milo here is still at a massive disadvantage simply walking onto the field here. With his main tools to handle this not workable. Or at least not without getting some nasty welts in the process.">

<Teala: "Er... I mean, couldn't he use Encore if he knew it? Or Foul Play?"> :?

<Audrey: "In this particular case, assuming that Milo somehow knows those, that could probably work... But remember that not every mon gets affected by elements in the same way. And that Encore could just as easily turn against Milo if Kat simply didn't set up that turn.

So yeah... There's a few things that are a little unbalanced about the current way chaining is handled in this RP right now...">

<Teala: "Wait... Where does that leave us with options, though?"> ._.

<Audrey: "Well... There's a few options... One is to flat-out change the way that chaining works...">

<Teala: "... Wouldn't that involve rewriting the rules of the universe?"> ._.

<Pepper: "It was a bit less useful, I'll say that...">

<Audrey: "For a more RP-immediate example... Remember all that cool stuff you used to be able to do with Safeguard? You know, the move that only lets you block status ailments in the games?">

<Pepper: "I still kinda miss those days..."> >_>

<Audrey: "Point is, as annoying as full-on rule re-writes are... They're really not all that jarring in the long run of things. Weirdness quickly becomes the new norm.">

<Teala: "Erm... Maybe we could do something a bit more subtle, though? I mean, that's quite a jump.">

<Audrey: "It could work... But anything subtle that still allows for chaining whenever you want to would have to require quite a bit of thought...

I mean, if chaining just lowered priority... How would my ExtremeSpeed from earlier have been treated? And how would that have helped later if Kat hadn't remembered that cheap way to block my Aura Sphere?">

<Cecil: "Maybe the stats could kick in later?">

<Milo: "That's just delaying the pain by a turn, though..."> ._.

<Teala: "Erm... Huh... Well... In some games, evasion is handled by both an 'evasiveness' and an 'aim' stat... Maybe chaining could lower one of those during a turn?">

<Lardna: "Or take away from a number of actions that you have for a turn?">

<Audrey: "As you can see, it's not a straightforward matter. But still, it's something that should be resolved sooner, rather than later, yes?

I guess at its core, the issue isn't so much that chaining's possible... After all, it has been around for forever... The issue is that you simply can't avoid chaining at every possible moment anymore without severely disadvantaging yourself. Re: Chaining

I guess the issue is then, at what point does one say whether or not antics are fair or not? I mean, I try to avoid pointing out "oh, that looks kinda hax" in battles, because there's so much variety for context, and it makes one look like a nitpicking jerk.

For instance, a gag battle can be run on rules from an entirely different game that would be broken by the standards of the Pokeverse, but still be fine in terms of a gag. It's just that we don't really have a set way of defining precedence.

Also, while I understand the desire to maintain practices that have been fair game for 2 years... I will have to say that we should probably at least keep an open mind to potentially changing things and making the rules a bit less nebulous. We will have to do something like that anyways once the inevitable G6 rule and mechanic-tweaking hits anyways, so why not also subject internal engine mechanics to the same scrutiny?
