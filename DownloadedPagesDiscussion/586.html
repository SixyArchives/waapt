Since I'm not really experience in planning arcs, all I can really add is my personal experience:

I tend to write my best by the seat of my pants. Everything about Sabaku, with maybe an exception here or there, was complete as a response to his immediate surroundings. This was mostly as a consequence of whether or not I would even participate in the Conquest arc at all.

However, without direction, my ideas tend to ramble. That's why I depend heavily on others to set up situations.

So, I'd say the most balanced approach would be to set up a general frame for the story arc to take place in, and then build up the actions of the characters around that. I understand if people would rather not freeform like that, but it just seems to work best for me personally.
