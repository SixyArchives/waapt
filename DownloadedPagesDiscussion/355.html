OK, Luke's PSA post inspired me to write something I've been planning for a bit. :P

Ransei!Umbra: Attention, all serfs, peasants,and general riffraff!

Bow the Dusknoir: And mons, too!

Ransei!Umbra: It has come to my attention that this thread is ideal for conquest... But before I get around to kidnapping your crops and burning your women...

Bow: It's the other way around, Umbra dahling.

Ransei!Umbra: Yes, right. Anyway... I feel the need to instruct you all in the art of villainy.

So You Want to Write A WAAPT    Villain   

Ransei!Umbra: First off, as a mad Spinda taught me, villains are like heroes, except smart enough that they give in to their inner dark side and embrace its power!

Bow: Basically, a hero that's given in to their negative qualities.

Ransei!Umbra: It's therefore vital that villains are like heroes in that they are actual characters. Not just something that's there just to be evil.

Bow: They can be pure evil and kick babies for fun, as long as they have an actual personality to go with that. Villains have desires and dreams too, you know!

Ransei!Umbra: Which brings me to my next point... It is pretty much impossible to pin down a "perfect" villain - there are so many different types, and everyone has their own tastes in which they like.

Bow: Some like sympathetic villains. Others like Complete Monsters. Some like Affably Evil , others like Faux Affably Evil . Some like goofy villains, others like serious villains, and yet others like villains in-between.

Ransei!Umbra: It is therefore best if you write the kind of villain you like, and not worry too much about other's tastes. Chances are, someone will have similar taste anyway.

Bow: However, for a WAAPT context, try to be careful with Complete Monsters... We've had quite a few already, with varying levels of success.

Ransei!Umbra: Also in a WAAPT context... Not every villain has to be as ambitious as I am. Too much competition that way. Smaller-scale villains can be fun!

Bow: A villain doesn't have to be a global-scale threat to be interesting.

Ransei!Umbra: Also, if you go the "tragic backstory" route... make sure it comes across as an explanation, not as an excuse. Especially if the villain does something really nasty later, which can destroy any sympathy created by said backstory.

Bow: On the other hand, if the villain has a change of heart later, an excuse is... well, more excusable.

Ransei!Umbra: Make sure your villain doesn't fall into too many of the common pitfalls, unless you're portraying them as grossly incompetent. Use the Evil Overlord List - I consult it all the time!

Bow: Try not to get too "wink wink" at the audience about it, though, that can be annoying.

Ransei!Umbra: Again, what type of villain you have depends entirely on your tastes.

Bow: And finally, no piece of writing advice applies to everyone. These are tips and guidelines, dahlings, not rules.

Ransei!Umbra: What are you talking about, Bow? All these ingrates should follow my advice like the law! >:|

Bow: If you say so, dahling.

Ransei!Umbra: Now that that's out of the way, we can get to conquering this thread... Summon the troops, Bow!

White!Arcanine!Ammy: Hey! You aren't touching this thread! -draws Thunder Edge-

Bow: We might have to delay things, Umbra dahling...

Ransei!Umbra: Grrr... Let's retreat. But I will take this thread eventually, you filthy dog!

- Exit, pursued by an Arcanine-

Disclaimer: Umbra won't be this much of a Card-Carrying Villain in-RP. :P

Also, I hope this helps anyone trying to write a villain in the future.
