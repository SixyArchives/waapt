INCOMING ARC PROPOSAL

Okay, since Chugga is doing a Xenoblade let's play, I figured I'd resuggest the idea of a Xenoblade arc.

If you need worldbuilding notes, I have a whoooooooole thing here.

There are numerous differences from my last proposal though, and those differences, as well as a few other brief points will be touched upon here. If you need the world building information, that post, Keys, or Zeal or I could help fill you in on any questions you might have. Or just go watch the let's play.

Okay so first off:

Absurdly basic arc primer:

Several J-Team members are recruited to help set right a universe in which something has gone terribly wrong and the resident Legendaries cannot act and fix it on their own. This universe bears some parallels in characters and events to their home universe but is almost entirely different in most respects, taking place on two ridiculously massive dead titans of Reshiram and a mechanical Zekrom called Bionis and Mechonis. The members recruited are dropped in four main areas upon arriving and follow and rewrite the unfolding tale of the universe's ultimate fate.

Also, a significant difference from the last proposal is the purpose of Pokémon, in this universe several Pokémon live alongside the various races, sentient and as a part of society, and most households have a mon or two or people have a mon with whom they are bonded and fight and live with. Exceptions are feral wild Pokemon, who cover the Bionis which vary wildly in shape and form and are little more than animals, but still non-feral mons exist in the wild.

This may sound similar to Conquest, in fact, you can imagine the combat and living situation and relationship between humons to be analogous to the system in Conquest. With no move limit in combat, of course. This does include the fact that humans do fight often along side their Pokemon. This is not required, however. Just a norm.

All participating members would start in one of four locations, which, without spoiling story events, I will give very general descriptions of what kinds of situations characters who start in these locations will deal with.

Location 1: Colony 9: This is where the main story starts, people dropped here will have relatively the same experience as those as the characters from the source material. Generally any type of character would work well here.

2: Colony 6: This is a bit more combat based for starters, as well they will join the main party relatively early on, but still. There will be a unique story not featured in the game that this party will go through prior to joining the arc's main plot.

3: Alcamoth: Despite being visited last in the story of all the locations, this group joins the main party third. This one will be a bit more heavy on the exposition, and a lot more slice-of-life interaction based at it's beginning.

4. Nopon Village: This is basically a comedic relief side story that goes on an outrageous series of sidequests prior to joining the arc's main plot. Expect seriousness and silliness to be found equally, as well as equal parts exposition and combat. It will be a little bit of a while before this group joins the main story.

Also in order to fit with the game mechanics of the required story battles, a small unique system would be adapted for the arc. Namely the break, topple, and daze system. In essence they'd work like status effects.

Break is knocking an enemy off balance, attacks that are precision strikes might inflict break on an enemy. Yes there will be a list of moves that can do this, but until it's needed I'm going on the assumption I can trust people who care to join the arc to use relative common sense, and ask if they're unsure.

Topple is knocking an opponent suffering from Break down and stunning them for a while, they take extra damage and are unable to attack, and all immunities and most defenses are negated while toppled.

Daze was debated about adding in, because it's mostly used for the random mook battles, which are mostly being cut out to save the hours upon hours of battles, but in the end it was thrown in. Daze is stunning a toppled opponent, which prolongs it's effect. Daze in this adaptation is a 100% chance for moves that might cause flinching to succeed when used on an opponent who is toppled or just recovered from being toppled.

Also unique hold items called Gems, which have effects that are exact equivalents to abilities canon items in Pokémon have will be somewhat abundant.

And that's it folks. Comments, complaints, or questions about anything are totally free. And really kind of desired.

To help everyone understand what they're getting into, Here some Xenoblade info that may help.

The premise of the game is that one year after a decisive victory in a war against an invading force of (apparently) sentient machines called Mechon, which lead to the Mechon's total annihilation, Shulk (the main character) -as well as the rest of the Homs (read, "humans") - find out that the Mechon were NOT wiped out. So, for personal reasons, Shulk decides to set out to truly wipe out the Mechon with a weapon called the Monado - a sword capable of piercing the Mechon's armor by itself. Shulk is accompanied on this quest by people of various races and various motives (from dept forgiveness to revenge). However, due to various plot twists, the protagonists discover that there is one common reason for all the current troubles plaguing the world - an ancient reason dating back to when time began - a time when the two Titans warred against each other.

For the WAAPT version of the story our PCs will take the place of Shulk's companions (with a few plot-relevant exceptions). note that ANY PC involved in this plot will be removed from the Original Universe for the duration of the arc mon CAN be brought over but due to how the XBC Universe would work, taking more than six could prove cumbersome.

PCs involved should be expected to At least be able to perform a support/ healer role in a scifi/ high fantasy-esque post/pre-war setting. Thanks to several plot changes the darkness of the arc has been toned down A LOT. However, this doesn't mean things will not be serious - think Okamiquest at its lightest moments.

Ask me any questions an I'll try to answer them as best as I can.
