Yet again, sorry for the double post, but something has been bugging me slightly.

I feel like we're treating our multiverse too much like an omniverse.

What's the difference, you may ask? Well, to me, a multiverse is a specific set of universes. An omniverse is pretty much EVERY POSSIBLE UNIVERSE EVER.

Now you're probably wondering why I see this as a problem. Well...

While I don't mind when people make up crazy alternate universes for gags and such, I get a bit edgy when minor spinoffs of the five "main" universes (OU, PMD-A, AU, Otherverse, and PMD-B) are mentioned, though more so the non-OU ones (Since, really, almost all the alternate 'verses shown are variations of the OU by default). I feel like having countless versions of those demeans the stuff we do in them, ESPECIALLY things like the original AU arc, or certain plots involving Stable Time Loops (Okamiquest, for example).

So while I'm not asking you all to retcon those spinoffs that already exist (I think there's only two so far), could you at least try to keep them to a minimum? And give them a good in-multiverse reason for being there? Like, Dialga having to correct some time meddling that couldn't be solved by stabilizing a time loop or something?
