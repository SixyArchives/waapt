OK, since the chat was discussing this...

Some Extra newbie tips (some of which may be upgraded into entry requirements or something like that)

Good grammar and spelling

This should be a no-brainer, yet I see people acting like it doesn't matter at all. People will IGNORE your posts if they are painful to read. So please, try and make sure you have an acceptable grasp of that.

Bring Big Ideas to Discussion or chat

OK, we have had WAY too many incidents where some new guy tried to railroad the plot with some big idea they keep secret from everyone else until it is too late. One actually SUCEEDED in derailing the plot in Hoenn. We're all sick of it. So, it is IMPERATIVE that newbies (and other players, really) bring their ideas to the Creative chat or the Discussion thread. Failing to do so will lead to consequences.

Forcing us to use a certain playstyle

OK, we know prose is used in most other R Ps. We know some sites have it as a rule for some inane reason. Some people simply prefer it or are used to it. As such, you can try prose if you realy want to...

But DON'T shove it down our throats. Most of us here prefer script for various reasons, and thinking that makes us lower-grade R Pers is an excersise in idiocy. Personally, I think prose is EXTREMELY overrated anyways. So if you do prose to try and prove you're a better R Per than everyone else here, you are deluding yourself. If its merely our of personal prefrence, that's fine, but see the grammar section.

Newbie Subplots

Genrally, they should be avoided. Why? Well, we need to learn to trust you first, make sure you aren't prone to anything drastic. (See the second section above.) Aside from that, we want to get to know your characters and how they tick. That's harder if you're doing your own vauge thing. So please... if you really want to be involved in plot, tag along with someone else.

That's all I have for now; if anyone else has similar tips, let me know.
