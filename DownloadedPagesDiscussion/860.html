Hey, so, I'm not ready to totally come back to the WAAPT community. There are still hurt feelings that I haven't entirely gotten over yet. But I am in a much better place emotionally than I was some weeks ago, and I do think I'm prepared to return to the RP in a somewhat sporadically-posting manner. The reason is mostly that I've had an idea for a plot rolling around in my head that I'd like to play out in some manner, and I dunno if it'd work in fanfic form because it's set specifically in the WAAPT world.

But this plot also happens to be something of a worldbuilding-ish thingy, so I figured I'd gauge any resistance to the idea before actually deciding if I want to go ahead and, y'know, actually do it.

Essentially, when she disappeared from the J-Team, Crewe embraced the whole Rayquaza thing and decided to do something useful with the position despite Rayquaza's extreme disinterest. So she starts using Chad the Xatu and his connections with various psychics and whatnot throughout the world to band together groups of Rayquaza followers (just mons, at least at the beginning) to be a sort of emergency response force type thing. Eventually it might branch out to include mons that don't follow Rayquaza, but at the beginning it was made up of followers.

So basically, if a problem is reported by a group in one area of the world, Crewe and Chad would organize mons from wherever else in the world that is best suited to handling the problem to go fix it. So, for example, if a fire started in some remote location in Johto, they might teleport in a group of Hoenn mons equipped with Surf/Rain Dance/the like to come fix it.

It would start and have the strongest following in Hoenn, where the largest concentration of mons that follow Rayquaza is, but would have groups of followers around the world, all linked together through a series of districts and representatives and a messenger system composed mostly of psychic types.

I've put a lot of thought into it and could type up a whole document outlining the various districts and leaders and the way things work, but that's the basic idea. The way I imagined it playing out IRP would be the organization existing in some form in present time, with flashbacks giving the story of how it formed. It could be used by anyone who wanted to use it (well, within reason, i.e. fitting within the organization's general purpose of helping those in need).

Rayquaza would be nominally involved at best. He has very little interest in the whole thing and sees it mostly as amusing, occasionally is annoyed that his chief attendant (Chad, not Crewe) devotes so much time to it, and might every so often approve of some action or another. But it wouldn't tie him down or anything, so he would still be very much open to anything anyone felt like doing with him.

I figure it would be called something along the lines of the 'Rayquaza Corps'; or maybe it's a Running Gag that no one can come up with a name for it that everybody likes. I dunno.

Anyway, I figured I'd put that out there for approval/disapproval/noncommittal.

As well, I'd like to apologize for what I said earlier. It was a terrible idea, but I was very angry and hurt and wanted to hit back. I'm still not totally over those feelings, but I'd rather not dwell on the past and I'm willing to move forward if y'all are.

All right, so if I'm reading this right... the concern is that a group of mons could suddenly and without warning be teleported somewhere they aren't wanted?

If I'm understanding you right, I think the district system should essentially solve this issue. Calls for help are put in by the representative for the district the trouble is in, and each district is handled as best suits the area; reps from different districts would have agreements and whatnot with any local powers. So if, for example, something is going down in a Nido colony, the leader may have a standing "we have trouble, we'll call you" policy with the Corps, or they might have a "stay out of our business" policy, in which case the Corps would keep out of whatever goes on there.

If a plot requires problems that do not receive help, it could either just be not reported (the mons in questions don't want the Corps involved, don't know about them, or any other reason), or else they just tell them to back off. In the case of something like a natural disaster (forest fire, earthquake, wrath of gods, whatever), the Corps would probably show up and start helping, but if it's something else and they don't know about it or are specifically told to stay out, they'll back off. They're not gonna muscle in on problems where they aren't wanted.

Meta-wise, well, I don't know what to do other than "don't call in the Corps unless the plot GM gives the OK".

It's essentially like the police IRP, but without authority and less universal; they can be called in to help if needed and/or wanted, can be assumed to be helping mons in the background when not around, but can be disregarded if they aren't wanted.
