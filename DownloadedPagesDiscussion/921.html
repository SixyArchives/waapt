How to Improve Between Characters, pt. II

And we're back!

Laharl: <Ugh.>

Flonne: <Yaaaaaay~>

Okay, so, let's say that I want to do an interaction between Laharl and Flonne. But, we're in, say, Goldenrod, and nothing's really going on. Our first step is deciding what sort of tone the interaction will have, since it's much more character-driven.

Laharl: <What do you mean by "tone"?>

I'm glad you asked! Tone refers to the overall direction, feel, and goal of the interaction. Do you want an interaction to be humorous? Serious? Off-the-walls ridiculous? Romantic?

Flonne: <Oo! Oo! I want to do the last one!>

Sure, why not?

Laharl: <BECAUSE I DON'T WANT TO!>

Welp, too bad.

Okay, for any sort of interaction, no matter the tone, you need to find yourself a location. Now, just like I mentioned last time on Dragon Ball Z, you'll likely need to make up a location that is reasonable for the area. Goldenrod, for example, has some rather hilly terrain as well as a coast to the southwest. Either of these would work for a romantic situation, but we'll use the former since it makes little sense for a pair of Rock types to want to hang around the water.

Flonne: <We don't swim well.>

Laharl: <And yet Ajax can Surf. What the hell is up with that?!>

MOVING ON, we now have Flonne and Laharl on a hilltop. Now we have to ask, "who was there and who is approaching?" Were they both arrive at the same time together? Did they meet up separately? Did one of them arrive first and the other arrived afterwards? For the sake of this one, let's go with the last one. We'll say that Laharl, tired of the sounds of the city, went up to a hilltop to get away from it all.

Laharl: <Damn straight!>

And, knowing that Laharl tends to get grumpy when he's hungry and that he's already missed lunch, Flonne decides to bring him some food.

Flonne: <Laharl's tummy rumbles more than he does!>

Laharl: <Grr... I'm only doing this because I like Champloo's food.>

Right, so anyway, we now have, on top of a place (hilltop), interaction basis (Flonne bringing Laharl's lunch), and a time (some time after lunch). Now, we have the pieces we need to make ourselves an impromptu romantic liaison! Let's see what we end up with.

Laharl: <It's not romantic, I'm just hungry!>

Champloo: <Food, romance, it's all the same thing in the end.>

How To Improv "Safely"

Hello everybody, and welcome back to How To Improv! Today, we'll be discussing boundaries. Namely, we'll be discussing which ones can be crossed and which ones really should not.

Champloo: <Does the fourth wall count as one?>

I'll be getting to that in a second. Also, this will be a rather short post, due to a rather simple answer to this quandary.

How Does One Improv Safely?

Common sense.

Really, that's all it is.

Okay, bear with me. Let's say we have two relatively straight-lace characters. Let's go with... Rill and Garuda.

Rill: <Hey.>

Garuda: <Greetings.>

These two are rather straight characters - they aren't usually goofy, ridiculous, or destructive. So, if you want to make a good, notable interaction between the two, don't force them to be any of these things. Even if you want the interaction to be humorous, deadpan humor is just as much a thing as Word Salad Humor or Straight Man Wise Guy routines.

Rill: <That isn't to say that slightly out-of-character moments aren't really all that bad, though. Say, for example, I find something that I respond to that no other thing has made me respond to quite like it before. This actually adds character traits rather than detracts from them, since that "thing" that I react to oddly would then become flagged as something unique to my character.>

Garuda: <You mean like that one popular vampire novel series? You always respond poorly whenever you see it on sale.>

Rill: <Please don't bring that up here, I really don't want to break something in the Discussion Thread.>

Really, each instance should be taken on a case-by-case basis. And if you're unsure, ask someone who's familiar with the characters as well and see what they think of it.

What Is Off-Limits?

Okay, now here's the part that's mostly negative, but for good reason. There are certain things that, even on spur-of-the-moment ideas, you either have to really think about, or just not do at all.

These include: anything involving something that is purposefully ambiguous; anything that is under the direct purview of another player; anything relating to a subject that is under scrutiny (right now, until the posting of the guide for it, new Auric and Obscuric abilities fall under this); anything relating to serious, "hot button" topics that another player or troper might feel very strongly about (basically, anything that requires a trigger warning is not proper material for an improv post).

Now, I know that this post was mostly negative, but I am merely getting this part of the series done and over with for finality's sake. Trust me, the next one will be considerably more upbeat.

Champloo: <Next time, on Dragon Ball Z, we get to talk about how originally improv'd people, items, and traits can be used as continuing aspects of the characters and world around them! See you next time, and remember: When ordering a sandwich, make sure the shop you're getting it from uses only the freshest bread! Stale bread makes a stale sandwich!>
