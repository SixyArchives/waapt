Okay, I figured I would elaborate and ask for opinions on my interpretation of Super Empathy.

To me, it has two parts, only one of which I've shown IRP.

First Part

This is the half I have shown already, the half accompanied by a gold inflection on the user's voice: Essentially, it's getting emotion across. Think a facial expression, only minus the potential misinterpretation. And like a facial expression, it can be faked to some degree; if someone is skilled enough, they can force themselves to give off whatever emotion they're feeling. This does not automatically mean instant persuasion. In some cases, it would make it even worse. For example, if someone were to try it to let Doopliss know that his latest prank will make them feel miserable, that'll only make Doopliss want to mess with them more. In the end, it depends on the personalities of both parties.

Second Part

This one's a bit more subtle, as well as more passive. It works the exact reverse way: "perfect"*
as in, a slight margin of error depending on circumstance
