I'm going to ask about this again, because I never got a definitive answer from anyone but Umbra last time I brought it up:

Due to the changes that have come with the contests, I think the idea of a Physical based counterpart to Round should be a thing. Lets just call it "Choreography" for now.

The way I see [Choreography] working is a physical, Normal type attack that makes contact with the opponent that uses synchronized movement, coordinated attacks, and/or Dance Battling. (Think Shinji and Asuka vs Israfel for a good comparison.)

Obviously, the basic mechanics of [Choreography] work identically to Round - the base power (BP) doubles if a mon uses it in conjunction with another. However - and I'm willing to forgo this mechanic - due to [Choreography] being a movement based attack, [Choreography] wouldn't be affected by Soundproof, but it would still be affected by other abilities that target physical/ contact moves.

As an optional mechanic - although I won't insist on it - [Choreography] can be used as a part of a "Round combo" without a base power penalty to either move.

Examples:
  • Round #1 is 60 BP => [Choreography] #1 is 120 BP => [Choreography] #2 is 240 BP
  • [Choreography] #1 is 60 BP => Round #1 is 120 BP => Round #2 is 240 BP
  • Round #1 is 60 BP => [Choreography] #1 is 120 BP => Round #2 is 240 BP
  • [Choreography] #1 is 60 BP => Round #1 is 120 BP => [Choreography] #2 is 240 BP
  • etc.

Thoughts on this?

In news entirely unrelated to my plot, I've come up with a new character idea! Y'know what's both adorable, hilarious and also ocassionally terrifying◊?

That's riiiight. New character time, for one of my favorite types! (I still have no ice trainer to complete my trainers-based-on-favorite-types band.)

Fairy Tale Girl Mary

Age: ??? (Claims to be 43. Looks maybe 7 or 8.)

Appearance: Imagine the stock fairy tale girl look◊ but wearing a more ornate, longer variant of the Slurpuff dress.

Background: Mary's origins are unclear but what is clear is that at some point she became entirely absorbed by her fairies, abandoning any past she ever had as she reinvented herself anew. Nobody understands where her parents might be, if she has any living parents or where she might live. Not even mind readers can crack the case of this one's origins, as she has long since deluded herself into forgetting them and replacing them with her whimsically crafted dreams.

About Mary: To say that Mary is a few bricks shy of a full load might be considered an understatement. Mary lives in a whimsical world of make believe where all things must be screened before entering. There is no fear, no doubt and no regret; there is only joy, laughter and wrath. Reality is just an opinion, one she happens to disagree with.

Not even the most imminent of threats can make Mary flinch, not because she is brave but because her pupil-free eyes do not register danger in the first place. Life is but a game and she has it all figured out. That being said, Mary's lunacy often gets in the way of her abilities as a trainer. Rarely putting thoughts into things like strategy or hard work, Mary should be grateful she does not understand shame. Because of her whimsical training methods she is more likely to catch many pokemon and train each of them very little.

None of Mary's pokemon seem to take issue with her apparent lunacy, in fact seeming to see and hear the same things she does. Supposedly Mary can understand her pokemon, however those who also understand pokemon would find that Mary's pokemon usually don't speak to her, leaving her statements dubious. Mary herself tends to enjoy living out her dreams in the real world, assigning herself and others 'roles' to fill comparable to classical stories and is normally fairly harmless. Breaking her immersion is one of the only ways to make her angry, at which point she will become uncharacteristically aggressive.

I would like Mary to be a Soul of Silver.
