On recommendation from a few folks, I'm going to bring Origa's profile back up with some additions.

Your Trainer's name: Origa

Age: 23

Gender: Female

Brief description of his/her appearance: Fairly tall- Roughly 5'7", with somewhat unmanageable brown hair, brown eyes and light skin. Commonly wearing monochromatic ensembles, they are often broken up by the pink key around her neck, which unfolds into a tiny pink braviary-themed human figure, given to her by Pent. She has fairly constant bags under her eyes from stressed, but is generally a smiley person.

Any ties to an organization(i.e Pokemon Rangers, Team Rocket, International Police, etc): Unovan Army through her father.

Region of origin: Unova, Opalucid City (The White version)

Backstory: Poor Origa. She loves pokemon to bits, really she does. The feeling just doesn't seem mutual. As a girl, her father's Arcanine and Mightyena, wonderful family pets and playmates of her brothers and sister, never even acknowledged her. When it got so bad that she nearly drowned in a boat accident from the negligence of the two, her parents decided that she should get a pokemon of her own as soon as her tenth birthday rolled around. However, that was not to be either, as the 3 starters picked out for her completely blew her off, the Oshawott she picked literally releasing itself and returning to the Juniper Lab. Out of luck, Origa decided to try and study to be a nurse, so she could at least interact with pokemon in some form. Even that, though, was a failure, as the Audino assistants, even the oldest and friendliest ones, simply disregarded her like trash on the side of the road, and the patients, no matter the ailment or problem, would wind up attacking her when she would try to treat them. Deemed unable to work with pokemon, she washed out of the program and became a simple waitress at an Opalucid Diner. Meeting Pent was a wonderful time in her life, as he was a model boyfriend, treating her well and taking her out, even lending her 2 of his own pokemon so that she could go on her own journey and maybe catch her own pokemon. And so on a journey she went, taking Verde and Scissors with her. Little did she know that the disinterest towards her in these two would fester into derision and even hate as time went on. She has arrived in Holon looking for Pent, and despite being constantly browbeat into a near-depression by the two pokemon, now practically villains in their own right to her, she remains confident that she will one day find a pokemon partner of her own.

Pokemon:

Verde the Kecleon (owned by Pentigan): Verde is the brains of the 2, and a generally unsavory customer these days. Always angry, he seems to find joy in the suffering of others, especially Origa, whose close contact over time unintentionally made him into what he is today. So much is his hate, that he will occasionally taunt an enemy into attacking him when hes right in front of Origa, and then dodge at the last minute, letting her get hit. Voiced by Bobby Moynahan.

Scissors the Krabby (owned by Pentigan): Scissors is the stronger of the two, but is fairly subservient to Verde when the two are together. He too, enjoys Origas misery, and tries to cause it whenever he can, constantly playing cruel and mean-spirited pranks on her and those around her.

While I won't question what experienced trainer in their right mind would even consider giving his/her trained mons to a new trainer, the games require you to have a Pokemon before you're even allowed to trade. This is, obviously, a mechanic so you aren't allowed to just transfer Pokemon between games, but people in the Pokemon world become trainers by growing with their Pokemon. Be it even by someone else catching your first one for you, receiving it from a professor or having it given to you as an egg, the overarching concept is the "friends in arms" sort of thought. Hence, happiness is a thing in the games. That's why Nuzlockes are so popular, bar the challenge some people pursue it for. If you want to go for this angle, I don't really mind too much, but know your options are limited by your own decision.

Name: Verax Lakirid

Rank: Peon

Age: 19

Place of Origin: Nimbasa, Unova

Affiliation: Cipher

Apperance: Verax is of average height, has cruel amber eyes and silky long hair that reaches his mid-neck, and a sharp, hawkish face. His arms are covered in scars and is fairly tan, aside from this he is unremarkable.

Bio: Verax was born in Nimbasa before moving on to visiting many places with no clearly defined purpose or reason, and eventually stumbled into Orre, where he took up with Cipher at the rather young age of fourteen and has been working with them ever since. He doesn't exactly like how Cipher operates, but enjoys the power shadowing gives him, and how as an operative he has, in practice, no restraint on how to get jobs done. He is an effective grunt, of sorts, when given a job and told to get it done, it gets done, the method of how is usually unimportant. He is however, beginning to question his role in Cipher, wondering if there should be more to his life, if the way he treats others and his Pokmon are alright. Though these are mere passing concerns, and Verax doesn't have much of a reason to quit Cipher as of this point.

Verax is a strong willed person. He's moderately intellegent, but like his physical strength it's nothing to brag about, being again, mostly average. He does however possess the unique quality of common sense, and is not afraid to use it, his competency heavily adding to his success. Verax is unforgiving, button pushy, and makes a point at getting back at anyone who crosses him in creative and subtle ways. His core quality is his tenacity, dragging on long after it would be wise to quit, before he either is forced to let go or succeeds at whatever it is he's doing.

Despite all this he has the capacity to be a warm and carming person. Before he joined Cipher, to people he knows and was comfortable around, he was fun loving and entertaining, and would always try to make people laugh when he could. Given the chance he could likely be so again.

Pokmon

None of Verax's Pokmon have names, as naming them would give them spirit, something beyond the simple tools they are to him. They also see themselves as little more than tools for him, so while they have personalities and are individuals, it rarely shows, and they rarely speak.
