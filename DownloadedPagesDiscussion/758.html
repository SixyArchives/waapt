 18939 Crewe, Mon, 10th Feb '14 1:04:21 PM from Amity Park Get RP Mod Relationship Status: Buried in snow, waiting for spring
Li'l Punk
Okay, so I figure I owe you guys an explanation as to why I was getting upset last night.

First off: I know you were trying to help. I really, really do, and I appreciate it. Please don't respond to this with "sorry, I was just trying to help". Thank you.

It's not that you guys were trying to help; it's the way you were trying to help that… well, it made things worse. And I probably overreacted, but this came at a really bad time.

Ever since Crewe quit the gym challenge, I've been… unmotivated, and wasn't having much fun doing much of anything. I started to get my mojo back… Friday. When I wrote the Cyllage gym battle, and then when I did the exhibition on Saturday. After that, I was starting to think that maybe Kalos would turn out all right after all.

But then the first reaction to Crewe's gym battle besides commenting on the gym mons turns into a conversation about how nigh impossible it'll be for her to succeed without doing something really OOC like using Flashlight or borrowing another mon? That hurts. I went from "Things will be okay after all!" to "Nope, failure is inevitable" in the course of, like, ten minutes. And it killed all joy I had in writing Crewe succeeding at her goal and simultaneously all motivation I had to get her from here to the point where she'll fail. Because I don't want her to fail.

Maybe it's just because I know what's going on in Crewe's head and why she does what she does and I'm not getting it across well enough for you guys to see it, but the way she sees the gym challenge is the very reason any suggestions that boil down to "Don't use Clockwise" just… won't work. To directly quote what I told Every:

Crewe has come to see this challenge as basically a test of whether or not she can succeed as a Trainer with Pokemon she's confident she wants to be with and that want to be with her, i.e. Clockwise. Consciously or not, she's staked pretty much her self-confidence and self-esteem on being able to succeed in this challenge with Clockwise. That's why she originally quit, because she was afraid if she lost she wouldn't be able to handle it.

She got enough confidence from the exhibition to be prepared to lose so long as they give it their best, but because she won, she's starting to think that maybe the Power of Friendship is enough to carry them through.

So using a different mon, whether it's Flashlight (who she would be horrified to battle with because he's basically Dana and the one time she battled with Dana she got her ass kicked and Crewe felt like a terrible person afterwards), borrowing a mon (defeats the whole point), or just losing... they're kind of counterproductive. I mean, she could lose. That's a possibility. But I don't think anyone wants to see yet another mental breakdown on her part.

So… yeah. Whether or not this is a good idea for character development is up in the air, but it's how she turned out. Basically, Crewe's goal was always to figure out how to be a better Trainer. Now she's found criteria: if she succeeds at the gyms, with Clockwise, she's a good Trainer. If she fails, then she's a bad Trainer and always will be, because that's the way Crewe thinks.

So, knowing that this is what's going on in her head, the discussion last night all seemed to point to "Crewe is inevitably going to fail and then we'll just have to deal with depressed Crewe again". And that killed all the joy I'd built up over the weekend, because if that's the end result, I don't want to get there. And so my muse was immediately killed deader than [insert amusing analogy here].

I overreacted, but the timing was… bad. Because it was right after Crewe's gym battle, it just made the entire retrying the gyms thing seem entirely pointless. And because it was just after I'd started getting confidence back, it just hurt worse. And because it was a discussion about a gym three gyms away, it left an entire stretch of time between now and then for me to dread because now I really don't want to ever get to the Lumiose gym.

So. Yeah. That was probably much longer and rambly-er than it needed to be, but I wanted to make sure I got everything. And I probably didn't, but I tried.

… And I don't know how to wrap this up, so I guess I'll just leave it here. Sorry for the length.
