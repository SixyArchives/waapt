It would not be a Discussion thread replacement. Filtering issues and device limits aside, the more thoughtful pace here is quite evidently valuable.

For completeness, the other points from the discussion worth noting:
  • If you have an objection to/personal problem with something somebody is planning, or a plot element, speak up as soon as you can. Having to Retcon something after the fact is very disruptive to the flow of the RP.
    • That said, when somebody does raise a problem with a plan of yours, see if you can accommodate them with a minor change to your plans first, before getting into arguments. There are a couple dozen folk in this RP, everyone will need to bend a little if we want to mesh.
  • Apparently there is some heat over longposts? Is readability helped if the scenes get split among several posts in the future? Is it okay if they get double/triple/septuple posted in that case?
  • Finally, in communication, misunderstandings are easy. Make a point of knowing what somebody else's actual opinion is, and try to take a charitable reading of any dubious posting. The only thing worse than escalating a potential fight by reacting to an insult with a bigger insult is creating a fight by reacting to a non-insult with an insult.

My votes:

Re: Chatroom: Personally, I'd like to see a two-rooms model. I'd rather the moderation be a little more loose than your post seems to be implying, though, Silent. In my opinion, the chatroom is at its best when people are talking about anything, and someone gets an idea and says "Hey guys, check it out!" I would limit the "This is what we're talking about" stuff to those occasions. When someone gets an idea for the RP, we shut up and listen. (And yes, I know that I'm hardly ever in there. I hope my vote holds equal weight, though).

Re: Longposts: Multiple posts in a row...doesn't really do much in the way of readability. As ADHD as it sounds, the beauty of this medium is that you bounce around between the different writing styles so much. Sure, if you were the last person to post, and that was almost an hour ago, either posting again, or just editing more into the last post is fine, but if it's peak hours, and we're moving relatively quickly, there really shouldn't be anything wrong with saying "Okay, I've got this really long post. I'll just break it into seven parts (which I've done internally anyway) and post it over the next 10-20 minutes."
