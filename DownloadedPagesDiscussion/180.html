On Nox Giratina plans:

To be honest, I'm still pretty iffy about it. The AU gave us more than our share in what regards to corrupted Legendaries and whatever, really. One would think that after so many problems being possesed, captured or corrupted, they'd either have learnt to secure themselves, or simply manage to become inaccessible.

And when the villains that are not a conveniently overpowered Legendary are still a conveniently superpowered mage/robot/pirate/ninja/zombie, well, it starts becoming very tiring when one tries to develop for those. Effects related to the Sliding Scale of Villain Threat (where I get the impression we've been stuck as The End of the World as We Know It for a good while, even for minor villains), start to come into effect too strongly.

Now, all this said, there's also one immediate "hold on" on this suggestion that I would hold in a meta sense: I had thought we had reached a meta agreement to try and not have the Legendaries that involved in our shenanigans? Even with a certain Mew's presence we've been doing pretty well in Hoenn. We should try to come up with... well, less "superpowered X" to fight against.

I mean, there are so many "forces to be defeated" we could be facing that don't need to be glorified numbers in the Pokédex... I once suggested taxes as a "force" for Hoenn, and while not as "Pokémon-y" as some would like, I still think it makes for a good baseline of setting development to come up with more sensible "things to be defeated".

Now, if Giratina was to try something anyway and we woukd have Nox Giratina happen somehow, ideally not in the nearby (RL) future, there is one thing that think would give us a good spin to try without needing to necessarily plan for a Boss Battle Finale. The Pokédex says that Giratina was banished for "its violence", yet the myths also suggest that it was Giratina who warned the Human With The Sword. In my opinion at least (and I admit here I'm taking a page of my own headcanon) it would make sense if Giratina was acting not as the Big Bad or anything obviously evil, but simply as a sort of post-war Cosmic Keystone - acting to balance out the score after whichever scenario backstory, and thus it is more a mechanism to be shut down than an enemy to be defeated, plotwise. Heck, we could even get to play the "evil" party of the Hostage for MacGuffin scenario without detriment.

However to deal with this idea and see how workable it is I'd need more details on when in term of both timelines would Nox Giratina be rebelling.

The trope you are looking for is not Batman Gambit but Lysistrata Gambit.

As for "fixing the situation", we do what we can but I want to remind people of two things: 1.- we are still too socially immature to use the chatroom adequately. 2.- even with the solutions we do execute and have executed, don't expect things to change overnight. Machines change with the snap of a finger. Humans and their issues don't.

Of course, (1) does not mean I'm advocating closing the chatroom - on the contrary. Perhaps it should be more open, but at the same more constrained. As someone else explained in discussion, the chatroom tends to be funnier when we are talking non-Pokémon things as well. Having the venting chatroom as an aside helps. That is also where (2) kicks in.

As a further comment, I'd say we have already paid a high price from this whole issue and that the talk that has stemmed from some RPers having left is a sign we're heading in the right direction, in terms of crisis management. YMMV can vary on that of course, I'm mostly saying "between someone leaving and us getting an alarm, or nothing happening and us still arguing in circles, I prefer the first".

Speaking about stemming, a couple of issues have come up at the private chatroom that I honestly think we should pay attention. They stem from a belief that a group of us, more or less the PEFEers, have some entitlement over the RP, just because we contribute worldbuild it. I've received more than one comment, even via PM, that "you hog the RP". Look, I'm gonna put this bluntly: you are no longer a Forum Game. You have "a world to live in". You have continuity now, and with that come consequences.

One of those consequences is that we have to either discard our previous history or live with it. When we tried to help live with it, we got flak about, example, the whole splitting PMD stuff that, go figure, Anom put us through (though to his credit, it was because he tried to help solve, just too late, a matter about the PMD that was grossly cast aside) and that has resulted in part of the recent discussion. Similarly with the matter of the end of the AU as a "verse".

But we're not trying to constrain people, we're trying to stretch possible avenues that make sense and cause us less issues in the future. Yeah, we might fail at that just like anyone, but we're doing it. If you don't, because you can't or won't, please remember you can still ask for help. I've tried to be as open as I can for character development and that kind of stuff. Tangent did wonders for the kickstarting of the TM/TA plot and he seems to be good for kickstarting stuff Tracer is a machine of generating NPCs for when we need them. But also at the same time accept that we have changed. Tagg reminds me that "Retconning RP events just because you don't like something is NOT cool", for example, with a justified level of annoyance.

But some of the reaction on this matter of style is making very hard to feel like contributing on our end as well. In particular when a longpost happens and it is met with a much less than cordial reaction from people who announce to the four winds that they won't even make the token effort of reading them. It doesn't have to be like, "right now" you know? But those posts have material, and also have continuity. Going beyond the show of disrespect, it makes us get less connected to each other, made worse because then we have to be hesitant about who to propose plots to, eventually always having to fall back to the same possé. Honestly, people act like that with our plots and then they wonder why Luke only wants to interact only with about two of us? Heck, I've been trying to interact with more people and barely managed to achieve something because everytime I reach somewhere, I might as well find a signpost that reads "the plot here is over, there's no reason to explore further, talk or let alone come back to town, ever". I feel more and more like having to RP alone, every. single. time. And R Ping alone is not R Ping. It's writing a fanfic.

So there are issues to solve there as well.

There is also the matter of ungratefulness and bluntness in fingerpointing, as well as some other associated issues, that I get when I read PMs like "you are hogging the RP" (see above) or "you chase Anom out of the chatroom and you should feel bad" (wut). I should have responded to such things two pages ago, but I'm gonna wait further until a better point in time comes; however, I'm leaving one thing for the record — anything related to Anom leaving is not dependant on us "chasing" him, if you want to call it that; he is in a condition where whatever we do or don't do he'll interpret as an attack to his persona.
