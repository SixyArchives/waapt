Okay, so I read the logs, and apparently I have some explaining to do.

First: The timing.

I felt awful about posting that on Christmas, but due to meta issues, any time I posted it would be close to Christmas, and I have a lot of stuff to jam in before the 29th, all of which hang on that one event. There's one more post to make (after Tangent finishes his Mew thing) that will be somewhat of a downer, though much less than this, and then a few more light-hearted shenanigans before wrapping up the Effie bit. There's also sending off the Pokemon and Hecate, but I'll address that later.

Next: Crewe's reaction

Admittedly, this post was originally gonna be more heartwarming and more along par with the other releasings, but the thing just wrote itself. Also it was 10:30 after the whole thing had gotten deleted and I was starting over and I was tired and frustrated. But besides that, I suppose I should explain her actions.

Crewe was wallowing in her own self-pity while walking down the street, and all of a sudden she's confronted with the Pokemon that pretty much abandoned her without warning weeks ago. While normally she'd be happy to see them, all she could think about is how they left and her anger at this; she was also being influenced by Minerva, who has wanted all her Pokemon gone for some time: that's her whole bit. And on top if all that, she hadn't given any thought to being a Trainer since the Reveal, and hasn't actualy been in contact with any of her Pokemon for even longer than that.

Basically, they came upon her at the wrong time.

And yes, yes, it's wrong to take out your anger on others, yadda yadda yadda, but seriously, when you're angry, do you really think "I shouldn't take this out on others!"? She was freaking out, and pretty mentally unstable.

And finally, unless someone has another complaint, what to do with her mons:

Wolf volunteered to take them because he wants them to be together. I really don't care who takes them, besides Hecate. I have plans for Hecate, that will hopefully end up more heartwarming (becoming leader again, being with her adoptive children), than tear-jerking, though at least some of it will be mixed...

Anyway, I'd suggest taking it up with Wolf if you want any of the 'mons. For all intents of purposes, they're his.

Silent, about Roa: Do as you like. I honestly see her as more your character than mine; whatever you think is best, I'll go with.

Well, that's that. Unless someone else has any more complaints.

Also, please note: I'm not saying Crewe's reaction was right or good or anything like that; it was an awful thing to do, and the decision was rashly made. I'm just saying that it's more justifiable (for lack of a better word) than some made it out to be.
