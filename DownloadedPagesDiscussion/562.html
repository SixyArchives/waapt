So I decided to suggest my own Media arc, perhaps unsurprisingly enough, it's a Touhou arc =P

How it Can Happen

The world of Gensokyo is a fantasy land where myth and legends that nobody believes in anymore end up, as such, there are a lot of mythologic creatures in there such as Mermaids, Werewolves, Yuki-Onnas, Vampires, you get the idea. That world exists parallel to the "real world", so it makes it pretty easy for it to exist in the WAAPT, nobody knows where exactly in the world Gensokyo is located, after all, an entire world couldn't be simply hidden like that, can it? Well, Gensokyo is a strange place where common sense is a weakness you really shouldn't think too much about it =P

The world is blocked by a barrier that doesn't let anyone go inside and also hides that world from the outside, how do the characters get there? A Reality Warper character frequently kidnapps humans to put inside Gensokyo, why would she target the J-Team? Because she spies on the culture of the outside as well and may have gotten interested in the J-Team due to having watched the TV show and deciding to let them have some "fun" inside Gensokyo.

The Characters

Initially I thought about making the characters of the Touhou series Pokémon, but then I thought that if Gensokyo is a land where things nobody believes in go, wouldn't it make sense for human-like characters to have supernatural powers that have nothing to do with Obscura/Psychic/Aura to exist there? It's a very isolated place, so it's not like the characters from there would ever come to the WAAPT world, actually, that's the entire reason for there to be a barrier in the first place =P

The Battle System

To quote the book Perfect Memento In Strict Sense, the concepts of the battle system in Gensokyo is:

That youkai can easily cause disasters.

That humans can easily resolve disasters.

That deciding victors solely based on strength is unacceptable.

That there is nothing superior to elegance and thought.

As such, while the characters may be so powerful that they're extremely overpowered, the battles aren't won by sheer power and rather by the duels that are called Spell Card battles in the game, which are basically the bullet hell that happens in the actual game. In this version, however, guess what would replaced Spell Card duels? Yep, Pokémon battles.

I thought about giving it a small twist however, in that the habitants of Gensokyo can use their own powers to give them an edge over the opponent, sorta like the Warrior Skills, but with no 1 use limitation. For example, one character has the power of creating miracles, she would make use of that powers by making so that the secondary effects of her Pokémon's moves would have a 100% chance of happening, while it would be very crippling for the trainer, it doesn't make her invincible outside of flinch hax, but even then, we can dodge moves here. The intention of this is making the battles be more like a skill test than just straight-forward battles.

Plot

I'm going with the Imperishable Night plot. There's something very wrong with the moon, but only the youkai can notice that and it makes them very nervous, seeing that, the magician Alice Margatroid works with fellow witch Marisa Kirisame into making an endless night spell so that they can have more time to see what is going on with the moon, upon meeting with the J-Team trainers (after a bit of a prologue where they get a Info Dump from Ms. Exposition Hyeda no Akyuu), they recruit them for help as well so that they're more likely in succeeding with fixing this problem.

Arc Lenght

Touhou games in general are very, very short, only having seven stages. I figured this would this a very short, light sub-arc after Conquest and Orre happen, due to how light-hearted the Touhou series is, a figured it would be a perfect candidate for a Breather Arc. Not only that, but since it's short, it wouldn't take long for the character involved in it to get back and do whatever they have to do in the current main arc without much delay. I will try to make it a bit longer though by including some Bonus Bosses that don't actually appear in the actual Imperishable Night game however, if only so that the arc can last a little longer =P.

Any thoughts on this idea?
