Apologies for doublepost, but I feel like this needs to go in its own place to make it more noticeable and avoid severe Mood Whiplash. Here we have that Team Magma character I was talking about, all set to become my main villain once Wilhelm's out of the way... if he's accepted, that is. I have a couple nagging doubts.

Before you ask, though, the elements of this character that STRONGLY tie into characters played by Pipping Fool were approved by her beforehand. Massive kudos to her for that.

Name: Muhammad ibn Bashir ibn Ibraheim al-Gala

Nickname he tends to use: Hephaestusnote Yes, the red text is necessary.

Age: Mid to late twenties; 30 at the very most

Gender: Male

Appearance: Muhammad, when out of costume, doesn't really look striking at all. If anything, he looks rather awkward. Muhammad is quite short (a hereditary trait amongst his family), with limbs that tend to bend at slightly awkward angles and a thin face with prominent cheekbones, topped off with a straggly beard and spectacles. As Hephaestus, however, the man completely reinvents his visual identity. Indeed, the primary difference between Hephaestus and Muhammad is the outfit - a long, billowing red robe, blotched red gloves, and a facial mask that looks like something from a grotesque tribal ritual, all of which covers pretty much every inch of his flesh. The mask also contains a voice filter, which mostly serves the purpose of making his rather reedy, faltering voice more intimidating, and contact lenses in the eyes that remove the need for his glasses. For the final touch, he increases his meagre height using platform shoes.

Organisation ties: Team Magma, higher-up. I'm not sure what the absolute limit for how high I'd be allowed to go is, but whatever it may be, he's in that position.

Region of Origin: Kalos

Personality: To say Muhammad is egotistical would be like saying [Scotland] is drizzly. This guy wants nothing more than to be in the spotlight, and he calculates his every action to ensure he follows its movements. That's not to say he isn't quite intelligent and good with business, because he actually is, but Muhammad overestimates his own potential by a long shot - he should control everything, and anyone else being given a say would ruin his perfect works.

However, Muhammad is intelligent enough to know that the key to maintaining power is to have your subordinates respect you, otherwise you'll end up going the same way as Marie Antoinette. As a result, he often ends up coming off as a Noble Demon - he avoids plans and actions that would cast him in a massively negative light, preferring to operate slowly and subtly and working to whip up loyalty amongst Magma's grunts. Much of his ego-stroking is also developed through his image - the Hephaestus persona was created, not to hide his identity (he couldn't care less if anyone knew his name), but to give him a striking visual style that would stick in the mind of anyone who encountered him. Even in conversation, he often comes off as a fairly reasonable man who actually makes valid points, up until you realise he's trying to weasel you into selling off your land or something.

Backstory: Originally, Muhammad was the designated heir to the company and fortune of his grandfather, Ibraheim al-Gala. From a young age, however, it became apparent that Muhammad was far too self-absorbed to properly manage a business, culminating in Grandpère Ibraheim quietly shifting his inheritance over to his great-niece and Muhammad's cousin - one Cleo de Nile. Muhammad was furious when he found out, and vowed to create his own fortune and screw over his grandfather and "thief" cousin by any means necessary.

After a long and winding trajectory through various jobs, all of which he was fired from after his superiors grew thoroughly sick of him, Muhammad ended up crawling into the ranks of Team Magma as a grunt. Originally active in the organisation during the twilight of the Maxie era, Muhammad kept to himself for the most part, knowing that getting overly absorbed in the criminal activities of the group would destroy his reputation and life for good. Once the original Magma was defeated, this tactic paid off, as Muhammad was spared arrest and quickly became a member in the new, semi-reformed Magma.

The next few years saw a slow, calculated rise as Muhammad underwent a semi-permanent transformation into Hephaestus. The grunts, who in the Maxie days had regarded him as a useless slob, now found themselves disturbingly interested in his striking visual identity and ideology that straddled the line between semi-reformed frittering and a return to the organisation's criminal grassroots. Hephaestus claimed to primarily consider "loving the land" a metaphor for supporting those who were less fortunate in society, but this was actually about screwing over big business - al-Gala Enterprises and the various de Nile fronts in particular. To date, he continues along this particular path, with his significantly more brutal section of the organisation remaining detached from most of the rest to the point that many consider it a splinter group in all but name.
