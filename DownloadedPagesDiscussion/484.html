It's...

THE HOLON ARC PRIMER!!!

Important Note!: None of this has been finalized! If you have any thoughts or concerns, please feel free to let us know either in this thread, or in the Creative Chat room!

The Plot

After following the cryptic clue from the legendary Pokémon Cresselia, the J-Team track down a group of shady scientists in the mysterious Holon Region - home to the oddly type Delta Pokémon! They soon find out that these scientist are working with Cipher to create a hax army of Pokémon capable of switching to any type at will!

It's up to the J-Team to put a stop to these Scientists before they can create their unstoppable army, leaving them to face their greatest threat of all - Cipher...

Setting

This plot takes place in the region of Holon. Located north of Kanto, the Holon region is mostly avoided by serious Pokémon Trainers due to, it's lack of an organized Pokémon League, severe periodic lightning-storms that render Pokéballs useless, and the shroud of radiation that bathes the entire region that has affected the wild Pokémon in a seriously bizarre way - by completely changing their types.

You can find out more about the Holon Region as a whole by viewing this post.

What the heck is a Delta Pokémon?

Delta Pokémon are based on a concept from the Trading Card Game. A Delta Pokémon refers to any Pokémon that has been exposed to an odd radiation - be it via scientific experiments, or a natural source - causing it to mutate and gain a type that it's species wouldn't have.

Any Pokémon can be turned into any type in this way (This would include the Fairy type. But since we know so little of the how or why of the Fairy Type yet, we most likely won't be seeing any Fairy Deltas). This is often coupled with a change in appearance to reflect their new typing. These can either be subtle - fur, scales or feathering of different colours, a slight metallic sheen of a steel hard hide - or drastic - A pair of wings, fins, body parts covered in plants, on fire, or electrified, amongst others.

As well as this, a Pokémon's natural moveset changes to match their new typing. So while an Ice Type Nidoran would likely lose most of it's Poison Type techniques, - for example - it would be able to learn quite a few Ice Types moves to compensate. And would still be just as huggable. :3

Cool! Can I catch a Delta Pokémon for my team?

Not so fast, bucko! One does not simply catch a Delta Pokémon! For a start, the lighting screw with Pokéball technology so badly that all Pokéballs (Including the ones that hold your team) simply will not work. As well as that, Delta Pokémon are barred from any and all official Pokémon League matches without going through a proper registration process first.

Because it just wouldn't be fair to Lt. Surge if you curb-stomped him with that awesome Electric/Ground Electrike you got while on vacation to Holon.

Spoilsport! You mean I can't have any Delta Pokémon at all?

Well...providing that you meet certain criteria, we would probably be okay with catching just ONE Delta Pokémon. Which would be the following:

  1. Ask everyone first! We'd be much more receptive to your request to get a Delta Pokémon is you ran it through with us in advance, as opposed to capturing it and then asking.

  2. Justify it. Think of a good reason to have a Delta come along with you. Did they make friends with one of your team members? Did you save it's life? Does it want to come with you to prove that Deltas are just like any other Pokémon? Or did it get dealt a terrible type combination and wants to prove that it can be strong in spite of that? We'd be much more willing to let you have Pokémon like these than something like and awesome Dark/Ghost Hydreigon you added to your team because it has "No Weak".

Is there anything else I should know?

As mentioned a few times before, the electrical storms in Holon will disable your Pokéballs, meaning that you will not be able to switch out your Pokémon with the PC storage system. So make sure that you decide on the Pokémon you want to bring with you before the start of the arc.

Keep in mind that you're not bound by the Six Pokémon Limit as in the main games, so you're free to bring along as few or as many Pokémon as you like. We do suggest that you don't bring something like thirty odd Pokémon with you, though. For the sake of your own sanity.

Do I have to take part in this arc?

Not at all! If you feel this arc isn't for you, then you're more than welcome to take part in the Pokémon Conquest Arc, which will be playing at the same time in the same thread as this arc. Ask around for link to the Conquest Primer for more information.

Oh, and if you feel up to it, you can take part in both arcs at once. Which would admittedly, be pretty hardcore.
