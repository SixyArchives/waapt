Isn't this how it goes? Here a month, gone for 2, then back again. Let's see how long I can stick around this time before I am struck by another horrible malady.

Your Trainer's name: Garret Belmont

Age: 20

Gender: Male

Brief description of his/her appearance: Tall, almost shockingly so. Deep-set, searching eyes, framed by an unkempt black mane, which seems to fade into the scraggly beard of a vagabond. His frame is not gaunt by any means- tone, taut muscles can be seen underneath tight, slightly-small clothes consisting of a hopping hitmontop-colored beige button-up shirt under a faded grey waistcoat and a pair of well-worn slacks. He wears no shoes, his feet calloused from the open road.

Any ties to an organization: None

Region of origin: Kalos

Backstory: (As told by Belmont himself)

My name is Garret Belmont, and I have lost all I cared for. I was once a student at the Universit Kalosian des Sciences. Geology. It was something that mattered to me- I was good at it, and I enjoyed the work I did. Mega Stones were my main area of study; I had been inspired by Augustin Sycamores research, and decided to use his notes as a jumping-off point to discover the origin of Mega Stones.

That was my first mistake.

As I delved into Kalosian lore, I discovered the closeness of the Great Kalosian War and the first appearance of the Mega Stones. I attempted to further examine the connection, but found myself stopped at every turn, be it by a lack of funding or a dearth of support. This would not dissuade me, however, as my research continued. At least, until I came across a truly horrifying connection between the Great Kalosian War and the first appearance of the Mega Stones.

In all the lithographs and pictures of the war I could find, the present pokemon were all ones confirmed to be able to Mega Evolve in the present era. All of them were killed quite horribly by the legendary Ultimate Weapon Could their life essences be the source of the power held by the Mega Stones? I think so. And if this is true, I must do all in my power to stop the use of Mega Stones, to put the tormented spirits of the dead pokemon to rest.

However, when I announced my intentions, I was ostracized by the pokemon research community. My grants rescinded, my education annulled by heartless monsters only interested in science. I was branded a dangerous individual for my efforts, and had my passport and Trainer ID taken from me. All I have left are the 5 pokemon on my belt, and my cause. And I will triumph this I swear.

[Let it be known that Belmont is a dangerous individual thoroughly lost in his obsessions. He is Ahab, his dark theories a white whale that taunts him, be they true or not. Psychological evaluation by La Police Kalosian. It's been several years since this interview, and it is believed that Belmont's mental state has further deteriorated- who knows by how much.]

Pokemon:

Lithos- Aerodactyl, Male, level 44, Adamant. A wise old Aerodactyl, the Iroh to Garrets Zuko, he has been with Garret the longest. His heart breaks seeing what Garret is becoming, but he would sooner return to the amber than abandon him.

Portent- Absol, Male, level 37, Hasty. Portent appeared to Garret just before he began his research, as if to signal his oncoming descent. However, he is also fiercely loyal to Garret, who showed no fear of him when they met and treated him like family.

Gilgamesh- Gyarados, Male, Level 36, Hasty. Once a Magikarp thrown onto land by a bad storm, a young Garret rescued him and named him Gilgamesh, confident that he would grow to deserve that name. Now, several years later, Gilgamesh (Gil to friends) is a powerful Gyarados, and will never leave the boy who believed in him.

Hiyori- Heracross, Female, Level 35, Docile. Always a peaceful and friendly creature, the Heracross that was Hiyori found herself tormented by the Meditite that would become Guru over a misunderstanding. Garret saved her by capturing her, and nursed her back to health, always keeping a supply of honey for the sweet-toothed beetle.

Guru- Meditite, Male, Level 35, Rash. Once the self-appointed guardian of a sacred tree outside of Shalour, he was overtaken with righteous fury when he found his tree destroyed by some sort of bug pokemon. Attacking the Heracross that would be Hiyori after jumping to the conclusion that it was her fault (it was instead done by a thoughtless Pinsir migrating through the area), he had to be captured by Garret to keep either of them from being badly hurt. His rage has since cooled, but he thanks Garret every day for preventing a terrible mistake on his part.
