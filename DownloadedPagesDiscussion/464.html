Now that that is done, I feel obligated to start writing up culture notes and other things, since we're making this kingdom up.

The Kingdom of Paxlia

Geography

Paxalia is located on an island, to the west of mainland Ransei. It is practically a straight shot west of the "tail" of the island-continent. The island has the same name as the nation.

The island was formed by a now extinct undersea volcano. It rises steeply out of the sea, surrounded by cruel currents on almost all sides. The only sane place to land is at the port city of Nova Concordia. There, a strange break in the currents leaves a small, safe path for ships to sail through.

Inland, the island is mostly rolling hills and farmland, dotted with villages. The exception is Mount Sagax. Gently rising from the hills around it, this mountain lies near the center of the island. At its base lies the capital city, Pacemnas*
pronounced "Pachemnas"
.

The island's soil is very rich and good for farming, thanks to the volcano that created it.

Halfway up Mount Sagax, built into the mountainside, stands the Arx Vatium, the palace of the nation's ruler. It is considered one of the Wonders of the World for its time period.

People

Rulers:

The official title of the ruler of Paxlia is The Poet-King. His palace, the Arx Vatium, is where he traditionally spends most of his time. He wears the Golden Laurel Crown, and sits upon the Balancing Throne, symbolizing the country's traditional position of absolute neutrality.

Culture:

Paxlia is considered to be the artistic and intellectual capital of ancient Ransei. Most of the greatest artists, in all mediums, were either born on the island, or came to learn in the schools of Pacemnas. Because of its peaceful history, Paxlia has been able to freely develop the greatest achievements in the arts and sciences of its era.

Paxlia is also the host of an odd custom. Every five years, the Ransei Ultimate Championship is held in Nova Concordia. It can be best described as a hybridization of the modern Olympic Games, and Ancient Roman gladiator battles. Each nation sends a five representatives, to fight in the Championship, and they each pay a certain amount of money to enter their representatives. Though there are five per nation, those five aren't on the same team- each battle is one v one. Whoever wins the final match brings great honor and glory to themselves and their nation. The winning nation also receives 45% of the entry money. The rest of that money, as well as the profits from the ticket sales, goes to Paxlia.

Foreign Relations:

Due to the difficulty in reaching the island, Paxlia has been for the most part left alone by the constantly warring nations of mainland Ransei. This has been also aided by its strict policy of neutrality. In fact, the Peace-Kings are known throughout Ransei to be wise and just judges, and are often requested to mediate between nations during peace negotiations.

In somewhat recent times, during the rule of the previous Poet-King, an astonishing event happened. All of the nations of Ransei in an unprecedented move, unanimously agreed to a single treaty. This treaty is known as the Treaty of Pacemnas. In essence, it means that if any nation should attack Paxlia, all of the others would be obligated to go to Paxlia's aid and fight against that nation. The reasoning behind this was that all of the nations wished for this neutral nation, remote and impartial, to remain that way so that they may have a place to meet, to talk, and to negotiate.
