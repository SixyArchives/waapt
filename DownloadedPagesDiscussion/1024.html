25592 ConflictedPsyches7th Jun 2015 02:40:39 PM from Tarkir Get RP Mod , Relationship Status: In Lesbians with you
Caroline Woereaper
Ok, so it's that time of year again where I have a crisis about Psyche's vague, bullshit, potentially OP powerset again.

Even after 4 or 5 revisions at this point, I cannot seem to define Psyche's powerset at all, and this mildly upsets me. So, I'm going to take a bit of time to nail down every power Psyche has shown, and what powers she continues to show.

I'll be breaking these down into my favorite method of analyzing Psychic potential, Telepathy, Psychokinesis (for the purposes of this I'm counting Teleportation in with PK), and ESP.

Telepathy

There are three main forms of Telepathy: Passive, Active, and Coercive

Passive Telepathy is rather akin to listening to "broadcast" thoughts and emotions, those which the thinker is "projecting" out from themselves. Passive telepathy is limited to those 'surface thoughts" and active emotional states of those around the telepath.

Active telepathy, on the other hand, is usually focused on a single target, and "enters" the mind of the subject. Depending on the power and skill of the projecting telepath, the subject may be aware that their mind is being entered. Indeed, the penetration may be anything from not noticeable to actively painful. Active telepathy isn't limited to surface thoughts or passions. Deep, well-hidden thoughts, memories and associations can be revealed this way. We could also consider Mindscape travel a highly advanced form of Active Telepathy.

Coercive telepathy deals with causing changes in the mind-state of another, causing them to think things that aren't their own ideas, feel emotions that they normally wouldn't, or perceive things that their senses don't detect. Coercive telepathy is risky to the target, and has been known to trigger psychotic episodes in the emotionally imbalanced.

Psyche has noted competence in all three forms of Telepathy at one point or another. She has, at least at the point of her introduction in the RP always had the capacity to turn off her Passive Telepathy, and her Active telepathy is a core part of her character (except when she's dealing with losing it). She is probably one, if not the most adept Active Telepaths in the RP, definitely among "heroic" characters. She no longer uses her abilities for Coercive Telepathy. She does not currently know if she even still has them, and is afraid to find out.

Psychokinesis

There are 6 main PK traits I see being relevant in this RP, Telekinesis, Pyrokinesis, Cryokinesis, Electrokinesis, Psychokinetic fields, and Teleportation (technically a different power in the system I'm thinking of, but close enough).

Pyrokinesis and Cryokinesis are considered highly specialized Obscuric traits as opposed to Psychic traits in this RP, so I'll discount those right off the bat.

Telekinesis (TK) is the basic movement of typically macroscopic items, Psyche has shown little to no skill in this field of TK at all, unless she's relying on the extra power granted to her by Lina/Grohl. The only exception is through her TK talent to manipulate her own body, which she only discovered accidentally. She has been slowly building this one back up.

Electrokinesis has only been shown by Psyche one time when she was relying on her Lina power, and may be far beyond the scope of most humans to use without massive amounts of power and/or Psychic Pokemon interference. Or perhaps, it's simply too dangerous for all but the most demented psychics to attempt, and this is why it's basically unheard of. Regardless, Psyche will likely never use this ability again.

Psychokinetic fields would be the energy used in attacks like Psyshock, Psycho Cut, Protect, Light Screen, and Reflect. Pretty cut and dry, Psyche has never used these abilities, even as Lina, and I don't feel like she'd ever gain this ability.

Teleportation. Psyche has shown two forms of Teleportation, one being the basic form, again only useable during periods of Lina influence, and the second has been collectively known as Lostportation, which is possibly a family inherited trait? Lostportation has Psyche basically teleporting at random, to a random place. It was originally blamed as Psyche's abysmally bad sense of direction, but has solidified as an actual Psychic trait. As such, Psyche has gained the ability to control WHEN she teleports, but it wouldn't be Lostportation otherwise, so she still hasn't and likely will never gain the ability to control WHERE she teleports. An interesting quirk is that Psyche has been known to be unaffected by the problems inherent in teleporting at random, as she has teleported to the moon and between dimensions with no seen side effects, and her mother has actually traveled through time with Lostportation. It is currently uncertain the future of this quirk of Lostportation.

ESP:

Any form of intaking information through senses unknown to the normal human senses.

There are far too many actual ESP skills to list here, so I'm just going to list the ones that Psyche has shown skill in.

Notably, she has dabbled in Clairvoyance, being able to see flashes of the Winding Woods in Kalos. She also has various other New Senses, notably her "Yaoi/Yuri sense" (self explanatory) and her "significance sense", which is her ability to know plot important details when people close to her are in danger, when something exciting is about to happen, or simply where she needs to be to communicate with someone without needing to be notified.

Her skill in ESP is now considerably weaker than it used to be, and she probably would not be able to use her true clairvoyance anymore, though her New Senses are probably still just as strong.

All right, so now that I feel I have rambled on long enough, let's construct a clear picture of Psyche's powerset.

Known Powers of Psyche:

Active Telepathy

Passive Telepathy (typically controllable)

Telekinesis (strictly limited to the control of her own body)

Lostportation

New Senses, particularly Significance Sense.

Her current skill in each of the three categories of Psychic power (1-10 scale):

Telepathy: 7-8

PK: 2-3

ESP: 3-4

Potential Skills to reintroduce at a later plot relevant date/likelihood

Coercive Telepathy: 7, she probably still has it, she's still a quite strong telepath. I could conceive of another opportunity in which she may be forced to break it out. However, her likelihood to use it unless absolutely forced is about a 1.

Electrokinesis: -5. It's plain not happening, I will not let it. Bad Powers, Bad People holds true here.

Telekinetic fields: 2. Probably not happening, but maybe in the distant future, if she trains enough.

Unlocalized TK: 4. It's a little more likely than TK fields, given her skill in localized TK, but again, not without a lot of training.

Directed Teleportation: 5. She's halfway there, it's mostly going to determine on whether I want convenience or comedy for her teleportation.

Clairvoyance: 3. She knows how to do it, but she's a little less powerful than she used to be, and it typically raises more plot questions than fixes them.

25596 ConflictedPsyches7th Jun 2015 04:53:39 PM from Tarkir Get RP Mod , Relationship Status: In Lesbians with you
Caroline Woereaper
Doubleposting to respond to Silent's post.

I see all of your concerns and am quite honestly just as concerned. I realize that my definitions of each category of telepathy might be a bit problematic (I derived them from another wiki, detailing powers for a different universe altogether).

My scale was a bit ahem, problematic, as well, I realize. I was trying to use it more as a general idea of how powerful other people regarded Psyche as, I did not intend to imply that Psyche is anywhere near that powerful.

Taking that into consideration, I think a more plausible definition taking into consideration those concerns for each would be something like this.

Passive Telepathy: Surface thoughts and Feelings, this is typically hard to control, and most people with this ability learn to turn the potency down as opposed to off. The maximum range for this type of Telepathy is roughly the distance one could hear a whispered conversation, and being closer to a subject gives you a stronger indication of what they are thinking about, much like focusing on said whispered conversation.

Active Telepathy: This is the ability to delve deeper and read thoughts that are not currently in the forefront of someone's mind. Whether that is something rather small, and easily accessible, like someone's name or birthday, or something more locked away, like someone's deepest fear or their early childhood memories, determines how much effort is required to extract the information. Active Telepathy is often assisted with a series of leading questions, used almost like typing something into a search engine. Diving deeper than anything closely guarded than basic information is, at least for human psychics, is nearly impossible to pull off without tipping off the victim that you are doing it, a reason why consent is very important in the Psychic code of ethics. It is also nearly impossible to engage in Active Telepathy without being within 2-3 meters of someone, and is increasingly easier the closer you get, and the more distracted the victim is.

Active Telepathy comes with a host of problems for human psychics, who are not capable of handling the large amounts of information that looking intently through someone else's head entails. Engaging in it without consent of the person one is examining can cause considerable distress to both the psychic and the victim. In extreme cases, the Psychic is violently thrown out of the other's mind, causing severe trauma to the Psychic's psychological state, though this usually only happens if they happen to delve too far into the head of another Psychic, or someone with specialized Psychic defenses. Mindscape Travel is a highly specialized version of Active Telepathy, that requires unique precautions, even more than normal Active Telepathy.

Coercive Telepathy is even more strenuous on both the Psychic and the victim, and is frowned upon even among Psychic Pokemon communities. Coercive telepathy deals with causing changes in the mind-state of another, causing them to think things that aren't their own ideas, feel emotions that they normally wouldn't, or perceive things that their senses don't detect. It is typically accepted that manipulated the senses is a little easier than manipulating emotions, which is a little easier than manipulating thoughts, but it should be said that none of them are easy by any stretch of the imagination. It requires a Psychic with a singular focus of will to overcome the most basic of mental defenses. Most human Psychics are not powerful, nor demented enough to attempt to do more than distract a person's senses. Coercive Telepathy is the most likely to cause psychological trauma to the attempting Psychic, if they are not of the proper skill to wield it appropriately. Of course, if one is attempting it, it's likely the least of their concerns.

Coercing someone to believe something requires a certain amount of suspension of disbelief. The most potent of human psychics will have a hard time making someone blind, or see something that clearly couldn't be there, like a horde of legendary mons. Actively inserting thoughts also requires a certain level of belief. You can't make someone kill their best friend without a large amount of power and concentration, for example. Enhancing emotions is typically easier than creating emotions wholesale, and is typically used as a shortcut for Coercive Telepaths. Also, it is a lot easier to calm an emotion that is already there than it is to spark an emotion that currently isn't being felt.

So that all said, let me try to actually make a scale that sounds reasonable.

So, going off of a distance of 3 meters, a telepath of skill level X could conceivably:

1: Feel surface feelings of most of the people around him, but not thoughts.

2: Hear surface thoughts and feelings of most of the people around him, but probably could not turn it off, nor accurately discern specific thoughts without a large amount of effort and concentration.

3: Have a reasonable ability to tone down the voices around them, as well as being able to actively listen to specific surface thoughts.

4: Be pretty able to turn off their Passive Telepathy, except under stress, and with a modicum of effort, could use Active Telepathy to figure out small things that aren't necessarily being thought about now, but could be brought up with some leading questions.

5: Be proficient enough at Passive Telepathy to turn it on and off at will, and able to use Active Telepathy to probe deeper into the mind, finding out minor details that aren't too guarding without requiring leading questions, though this requires a great amount of concentration.

6: Use leading questions to find out a great deal about a person with Active Telepathy, and with a great deal of concentration and fortitude, could find out more closely guarded thoughts within someone's mind, more on the level of Bank account numbers than early childhood memories, though. Could conceivable also distract one's senses slightly with Coercive Telepathy, usually with a great deal of concentration.

7: Can find out a great deal of information about a person without leading questions, though requires leading questions, a lot of persuasion, and willpower to find out more than what someone could be conceivably likely to tell you without Telepathy, again, still more likely only going to be able to access recent-ish memories that are guarded, like bank account numbers, not childhood memories. Active Telepathy does not increase in strength past this point in humans. Could also use Coercive Telepathy to more easily distract one's senses, usually in terms of being able to convince someone that something moved a meter or so, or that there's a spooky noise behind them, misdirection, basically. Few Human Psychics are stronger than level 7.

8: Use Coercive Telepathy reasonably easy to distract one's senses of minor things, or with a great deal of concentration make a personal illusion that's a bit more substantial, like changing the color of something, or making someone ignore a small object that they can clearly see. Can also use Coercive Telepathy to soothe one's emotions with a great deal of persuasion and careful use of their tone of voice, or to increase already extant emotions with a similar amount of persuasion.

9: Use Coercive Telepathy to create substantial illusions with a reasonable degree of ease, though they typically can only affect one sense at a time. Use Coercive Telepathy to influence one's emotions with only a modicum of thought and the right tone of voice. Use Coercive Telepathy to sneak in a small thought into someone's head, and have them believe they thought of it, but only with a great deal of effort, and a large risk of backlash.

10: Use Coercive Telepathy to create full fledged personal illusions with a modicum of effort, able to affect multiple senses at once. Use Coercive Telepathy to influence a wide arrangement of emotions with the right tone of voice and effort. Use Coercive Telepathy to implant a small thought into someone's head with a reasonably high chance of success.

The Human Psychic Code of Ethics

1. Use as little power as possible.

2. When necessary to use power, ask permission before, not forgiveness after.

3. Be not proud, lest ye be made foolish. Be not stubborn, lest ye be made weak. Be not cruel, lest ye be made cold.

4. These are the freedoms humans have been given. Free Will, Knowledge, Emotion. Take none from any other, lest they be taken from thee.

5. Do not harm another, save to help two.

6. Do not harm another to help thineself, lest ye atone sevenfold.

7. Deceive none, lest ye be left in the dark.

8. The power is a burden, a responsibility, not a gift. Remember this, or have it weigh on your soul.

9. The way to a full life is in finding your own meaning, not others.

10. Know your worth without your power, for your power is not your worth.
