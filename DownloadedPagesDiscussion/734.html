18344 ChaoticMarin21st Jan 2014 12:42:23 PM from Nintendoland Get RP Mod , Relationship Status: 700 wives and 300 concubines
Chiptune DJ
So I'm looking for opinions. I've been thinking a lot about a second character and I don't want to be an altoholic so I'm only really considering one or two extras at most. The thing is, I have like 10 characters to choose from. Which do you guys like best?

1) Ghost monotype trainer with a debatable grip on reality from Hoenn. Independent villain character with a pretty high strength level. Would never be seen inside a city as she is often unable to control herself and is prone to attacking people unprovoked. She seems to have developed an obsession with souls alongside her pokemon. She would have an obscura aura around her constantly but otherwise demonstrates no significant powers of her own beyond this aura having a tendency to attract wild ghost type pokemon and create synergy with her own ghosts. In battle she excels in brute force but lacks in actual strategy due to her generally having difficulty controlling herself.

2) A cowgirl themed pokemon trainer from Unova. She has gender issues which are in turn caused by father issues. Namely, her father wanted a boy and she knows it; she also very much wants her father's approval. While she has since very much gotten that approval it did shape her personality regardless. As a result she has become a half-tomboy, her dress and mannerisms fluctuating between feminine and masculine with a certain inconsistency. While normally extremely lax to the point that she would probably shrug off most of the things that don't actually threaten her safety, she is very self-conscious about what femininity she does have and will react very defensively if it is brought up. As a trainer she mostly uses normal type pokemon, her signature mons being Boufallant, Tauros and Stoutland. In general it's supposed to be ranch themed. Unlike the other characters on this list she would actively struggle with gym leaders even on her best day. She is also one of my older characters, being roughly 17-19. Her hobbies are socialism and exploring and pokemon training tends to be a social thing for her; basically she's a casual.

3) Minor teen celebrity by virtue of being very photogenic and an extraordinary battler. Her specialty is the power of teamwork and she apparently created her very own set of battling rules called the 'quadruple battle' which is exactly what it sounds like. She's a goody two shoes and generally very pleasant to be around. While her pokemon aren't individually all /that/ strong she specializes in devastating strategies that are usually able to help her compensate for this and then some. Apparently most of her pokemon were given to her by friends from back in Sinnoh as a sort of going away present. Her defining trait would probably be loyalty; she is quick to form very strong, lasting bonds with others.

4) A bitter adult male that constantly either literally or metaphorically shakes his fists at 'those damn kids'. It seems impossible to please this man, a bigot among bigots who may or may not also be a sociopath. (This is unclear.) He is an independent villain character, though Eskay planted the idea in my head of putting him into Neo Team Galactic. He would be heavily manipulative and rely mostly on a team of carefully chosen pokemon to pull cloak and dagger shenanigans with other characters. The full depth of his character is unclear, not that I'd give it anyway.

5) See #4, only an ex Team Plasma Grunt who never moved on from ideals. He probably considers Ghetsis and N /both/ traitors and he is a bit more quiet with his bigotry than #4. His hatred is mostly misanthropic in nature and he uses both cloak/dagger and badassery to spite his foes.

Details Basically #4 and #5 are two different origin stories for the same character archetype. Because all I've really done is state the character premise I will be hard pressed to accept changes, as unless it's something really asinine it's probably easier to just make a new concept. I will however gladly and readily consider ideas for more fully fleshing out the premises.
