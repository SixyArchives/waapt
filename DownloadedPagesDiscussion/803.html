I see absolutely no point in having to establish that a Hidden Power is undecided; that's the default anyway. As long as you haven't explicitly stated what you want it to be, it can very easily be assumed it's undecided. Having to say "I haven't decided" helps absolutely nobody, and it's not going to be enforced anyway; we're not going to have surveys asking "have you decided on all of your Hidden Power types yet" and nobody is going to say anything about it on a regular basis. It's not even a concern ninety percent of the time.

There's no need to overcomplicate things with addendums and addendums to addendums. It doesn't need to be any more than "if it's not established, it's fair game". It's exactly how we've operated in the past, and we can still operate with it now. The question was "Hidden Powers for new types, yes or no", so I can't understand why we can't make our answers a simple "yes" or "no".
