World movies and shows that delve into idiosyncrasies I also find quite useful. Delivered in more forms than just non fiction.

The film Yeelen is a fantasy story about a wizard named Niankoro, and yet I learnt more about Bambara culture from that film than reading a many books on Mali.

Similarly. Things Fall Apart is an excellent tragedy in it's own right, but you can learn so much about Igbo culture and law from reading the book.

Stuff like Crash Course [X] History also present stuff like this in an entertaining fashion, if that's more your style.

Point is. There's more ways to find out about cultural stuff than just strictly reading non-fiction infodumps.

I kinda want to offer my own perspective. While I do enjoy learning and researching (seriously, I used to read Wikipedia for fun all the time, just Wiki Walking all over) and I loved my Humanities courses in school, I feel incredibly... well I guess anxious is the best word, about trying to tackle issues that have a deep personal effect on me in creative writing as it is. Issues that aren't immediately relevant to myself would terrify me. So I stick to what I know, if that.

Then again, I haven't properly done creative writing in a long time. Hell, I've been in a limbo here for about the past year.

But I have no problem with putting ethnicity in signups. It's not that big of a deal. And as for why it matters: Because the RP has had heavy issues taken on in its narrative; because representation is pretty important. There's been the "nothing exists in a vacuum" line used a few times regarding this already, well there's this similar phrase that's controversial that "the personal is political" and I thought about it and I certainly do believe the reverse of it, that the political is personal.
