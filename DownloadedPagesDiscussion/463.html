Quarter/Eskay: The idea is that Wolf's ancestor knows about the rumour, and is conquering nations in order to keep it out of Umbra's hands (taking as many regions with his superior force as he can, so that Umbra can't have them, or will at least have a harder time getting them himself). To everyone who isn't a direct ally of his, this looks like he's simply trying to take over the world for himself. Since he figures that the less people who know that there's a way to gain magic (which I figure Aura/Obscura would probably be referred to as in this era) the better, and he doesn't particularly care about his PR, he allows this perception to spread (think a combination of Nobunaga from the actual game, Lord Vyce from Atop the Fourth Wall, and what people suspect the Emperor was doing to prepare for the Yuuzhan Vong).

Basically, Umbra spread misinformation in order to get his enemies focused on each other, instead of him, giving him more time to find the Lunar Pendant. So for the first half of the arc, Wolf's ancestor seems like the Big Bad, but he's really a Red Herring.
