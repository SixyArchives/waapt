Stopping by to comment a couple of things. On issues with tropers:

  • Ori's Nosebleeds: the only thing that caused me discomfort there was the sheer frequency. Hard to tell if it was being used as a joke or as a... what.

  • Parisse's Mind Reading: that was discussed in the chatroom. The only thing that worried me was the depth of reading, whether it could read semi-formed or transient thoughts. Such things have been resolved.

  • Wrapping up the AU: eeeehh... seems to be mostly solved.

Now, to the pressing issue in my opinion.

Use of the Chatroom for RP development

The metaevents of the last weeks surely have caught the attention of you peoplemons. First it was Anom's issues, then Luke's issues, sometimes Crewe's issues, or Tagg's issues. Now, I don't have issue, no pun intended, with us having issues and treating them in the chatroom, but the planning for the Hoenn arc and beyond has brought out some unhealthy trends in our behaviour that I think are very important news damaging to the RP and to us as a whole.

As I have mentioned sometime, the chatroom as a medium lends itself to a sort of GIFT Effect. When we get in there, we support our own positions in the different debates. However, as of the latest days we are defending our positions and stretching debates way over the point where it is reasonable. Take for example that people like Luke are going to sleep at 4 AM because of the Hoenn plot discussions. People like him and me pretty much spend a good portion of our days paying the chatroom and the RP more than a trivial level of attention.

Now, tiemzones can be blamed for some of that (all my hæt) but the core issue as I feel it is that, most of the days, once we start discussing, we don't stop. We keep posting and posting and start moving from the overall debate, whichever it is, to arguments in circles, until one of us gets tired and/or fed up and logs out. Not a good way to end the day or build mood for the next one, if you ask me.

Add to that that we are overusing the chatroom as a means to make decisions about the RP. While it is a good way to communicate, it is not a good way of passing policy, considering that we don't have a central GM-ing authority for the RP. So it comes down to the point where we start more or less wildly throwing out plot ideas, only for the next poster down the line shutting it down saying "I don't like it, don't want it" and then sticking with that, without further (senseful) discussion by both sides. The problem? We tire ourselves out pingponging and eventually one of us feels attacked.

Given the events in the last days and after a quick check of the time we spend discussing (thanks, public logs) I'd suggest we plan our usage of the chatroom a bit better, for example: dedicating only up to a certain amount of hours to discuss each subject, assigning important decisions solely to this thread (or spoilers in RP thread), and recapping ideas here once we're done discussing them in the chatroom.

Opinions on this issue?
