12699 BadWolf217th Jul 2013 12:29:19 AM Get RP Mod
The Watcher on the Wall
Okay, I laid this out in the chat, but it's going to be posted here for everyone to see. Here's my plan for main plot of the Conquest arc, starting on Friday, when I get home.

If you have concerns, talk to me. I'm going on vacation tomorrow evening, but I'll address any concerns I can tomorrow, if I have the chance on my trip, and once I get back, before I post.

Also, since I haven't managed to name them yet, my ancestor characters will just be referred to as their usual names here.

Friday

The allied forces of Nixtorm and Valora suddenly and unexpectedly invade Dragnor, conquering them easily (because element of surprise + type advantage), causing them to retreat to an allied neighbour. They rapidly do the same to Avia. News begins to spread that Nixtorm and Valora are on the warpath. Wolf himself is part of the invasion of Dragnor, but he mostly stays either in Dragnor or Nixtorm after this. Further mentions of "Wolf" from here on out mean his generals, unless otherwise specified.

Saturday

Wolf's forces march on Cragspur and offer them the chance to surrender and join them. Whether they accept or not, Wolf adds Cragspur to his growing collection. He makes a similar offer to Viperia, before taking on Yaksha in a climactic battle. Once Yaksha retreats, Wolf controls the entirety of Eastern Ransei, with the exception of Spectra.

Sunday

There is seemingly no way to successfully take over Terrera and Illusio. Invading one would just cause them to retreat to the other, reinforcing them for a counterstrike. This causes a stalemate that probably lasts for a few weeks, during which people can develop their characters in the new status quo, and form their alliances to help Illusio and Terrera repel Wolf's forces.

Stalemate

During this time, Wolf's spies leak the news that Wolf is sending as many of his forces to the front lines as he can in an effort to take Illusio and Terrera at the same time. This is a ruse. Once everyone has focused their attention elsewhere, Wolf enacts a navel assault on Chrysalia.

That's the point where the plan goes from story-based to gameplay-based. Wolf doesn't have to capture Chrysalia like he did the previous nations, although he still might if he manages to win. It's also at this time that people have a chance at winning if they try to lead an attack on Wolf's territory. This is also a good time to start bringing in the other Regions. Getting help from Sinnoh, Unova, etc. would scare Wolf, and cause him to look for his own allies across the sea, since he has a lot of coastline to worry about at that point.

Wolf gets personally involved in the war again once he starts losing ground, and he begins to grow desperate, because Spectra is growing stronger. It's only once he's cornered in Dragnor and it's clear that he's lost that he has a chance to reveal his motivations, but of course by then, it's too late. Umbra's deception has been effective for too long, and everyone makes a mad rush to Spectra for the climax.

During all of this, Katie is doing her own thing, starting in Pugilis, and working her way around Western Ransei, and occasionally crossing the sea if needed.

If you are the Warlord of Valora, Dragnor, Avia, Cragspur, Viperia, or Yaksha, and you have concerns, please talk to me. Talk to me anyway. Talking through the plan is good. I've done it twice tonight, and it's helped. The default response to Wolf conquering your nation so far seems to be retreating to a friendly neighbour, but if you want to do something else, it can be discussed.

Well, I think that's about it for now.
...for this night and all the nights to come.
