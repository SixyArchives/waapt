Mega Post Ahoy!

"All right. Volt Gang territory... The fastest way back is... East."

The Dewott proceeded through the labyrinthine maze of storm drains, pushing his cart and box until reaching a drain with three faint claw marks at his eye level.

"This is it."

The Dewott threw the more valuable objects in the box into a plastic bag amongst the clutter, looked carefully to ensure that no one was coming, and then emerged into an alleyway. He then paced down the alley and crawled into a space where a basement window once was on an abandoned building and proceeded into a hovel in a corner consisting of some cardboard and scrap metal.

The Dewott slid a piece of scrap metal at one end of the hovel, revealing an entrance.

"Alright, let's make sure that everything's here..."

After proceeding into the hovel. Travis left his bag of goods by the entrance.

''The Dewott scanned the room, the walls of the hovel were covered in colorful posters, with a small lightbulb dangling from the ceiling. The left side was occupied by an array of multicolored boxes and cables connected to a small, beaten monitor. The side immediately opposite of the entrance contained a few cardboard boxes with a small number of shells hung above them beside some exposed wiring. The right side was occupied by a bed and small box of disks.

"Okay, looks like no one's figured out how to get in here yet... Right. Stuff for the job."

Travis fished a drawstring bag out of one of the boxes along the back wall and began stuffing items into it.

"Let's see here, Duct Tape, 4 Sitrus Berries, one of those Dire Hits, a Lum Berry, a Smoke Ball for me, another one in case if I lose that one, and one if I need to make a quick exit..."

Travis' attention turned to a small tin container in one of the boxes.

"Right, might as well hedge my bets... What do I even still have in that thing?"

Travis removed the lid from the container, inside it were a number of small, dingy orbs. Travis began pawing through the orbs, carefully avoiding an orb with bite marks in the uppermost corner of the container.

"Let's see here: Poke Ball, Poke Ball, Great Ball, Poke Ball, Premier Ball, Poke Ball, Poke Ball, my Poke Ball, Poke Ball, and another Great Ball... It's a shame that I don't have one of those black ones anymore, I've never seen a ball work that well before..."

The Dewott removed an ancient Great Ball and two ratty Poke Balls.

"Let's see if these pieces of junk still work..."

Travis picked up the Great Ball, ran two of the digits on his right paw along its side. The decrepit ball grew dramatically in size.

"Okay, it still expands..."

Travis aimed the center of the ball at the room's lightbulb, and quickly tapped the center of the ball with a claw. A beam of red light shot out.

"And it still catches stuff..."

The Dewott ran its paws upward along the sides of the ball, and it shrank back to its initial diminutive state. He repeated this process with the two Poke Balls that he had retrieved before placing the three balls into a small plastic bag that he put into his drawstring bag.

"Okay, everything works, but I can't help but think that I've got something that would make this job easier... Oh right."

The Dewott retrieved an object in a dingy blue wrapper out of one of the boxes in the back and unwrapped it, revealing a translucent orb.

"I knew that this thing would come in handy eventually..."

The Dewott set the orb on the floor of his hovel, and jammed one of the shells he had on his person against it, splitting it into roughly equal halves. The Dewott repeated the process again on one of the halves.

"Hmm, quarters are probably too much, eighths are probably safer if this guy pulls a dumb stunt..."

The Dewott split one of the quarter pieces of the orb and placed both pieces into another plastic bag that he place amongst the contents of his drawstring bag, and placed the remainder of the orb into the wrapper.

"Looks like that's all. Time to go hunt a Psyduck, and if those Volt dorks are right about this Psyduck being a west-side dealer, I bet I know where to find him..."

The Dewott slung the drawstring bag over his left shoulder, left his hovel, carefully slid the metal sheet back over its entrance. And warily left the basement through the window he entered before returning to the storm drains.
