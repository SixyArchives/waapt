Cubone Line

Morphs [Oak Catalog #]

  • Cubone [104]
  • Marowak [105]

Physical description

Like all members of the Monster Egg Group the members of the Cubone line are repto-mammalian bipeds. This line is characterized by the common social and biological groups that possess a number of shared characteristics including a brown and tan hide, diet, a common social structure, and the use of bones as weapons and tools. The initial form, Cubone, is a short, pudgy creature that typically stands roughly 40 centimeters in height and weighs between 6 and 7 kilograms. These creatures possess scaled hides, composed of a light brown region surrounding a tan region that extends from its chest to a point along its lower tail. Members of this line are noted for wearing bone helmets with a resemblance to the skulls of its later form, Marowak, that that typically obscure all regions of its head barring a small area surrounding its eyes and the underside of its snout. Cubone have a notorious protective nature about these skulls and due to their notable conservation of their dead a common hyperbole is that no one has ever seen a Cubone beneath its head. These obscured features, as inferred by life specimens include a head structure similar to that of the helmet protecting it, with a shorter snout, less prominent, non-rigid spikes located near the back of the top of the head, and jaws with a moderate overbite with small exposed teeth towards the rear. Individuals have two prominent rounded spikes running along their spinal column, the larger of the two located closer to a Cubone's shoulders than its smaller counterpart. Cubone possess legs with feet terminating with singular, nailed digits, and short arms with hands consisting of a nailed thumb and several other fused digits, giving an appearance akin to a spiked oven mitt. Marowak, the second and (presumed) final form of this line, is a creature with a similar physiology to their younger brethren that usually stands around a meter in height and weighs approximately 45 kilograms. Unlike their younger brethren, Marowak possess natural bony "helmets" that cover all regions of its head barring a pair of fleshy eyelids. These "helmets" possess two large, rigid spikes located in the same region as both the spikes on its younger counterpart's helmet and true head, two ridges located along the back, and a snout behaving as a ridge-like extension of its upper head terminating with two small nostrils. A Marowak's lower jaw is also ossified in a similar fashion to the rest of its head. Although its hide possesses markings almost identical to those of its prior form, Marowak hides possess a darker-colored underbelly region with more prominent scales, giving this region a horizontally banded appearance. Other physiological changes include the loss of both back spikes coupled with the emergence of a small nub-like spike located near the end of the tail and a lither build. Reports of Cubone and Marowak with green scales occasionally surface, and are highly sought after among some trainer circles.

Notable Biology

One of the defining characteristics of members of this line is their phenomenal tool-making and problem solving skills. These capabilities have enabled these creatures to develop a number of learned behaviors that have been passed down through successive generations of offspring for untold ages and have become ingrained into their psyche. Cubone are noted as the most civilized of Pokémon species, having several widespread Clans around there native regions and being the only documented Pokémon to hold burial customs One of these behaviors is the development of the Cubone skull. When a Cubone sow gives birth, the underdeveloped and soft head of the calf is extremely weak and the large hole within the head (similar to the human anterior fontanelle) is quite vulnerable. To rectify this situation, the female will take one of her own rapidly shedded skullcaps and place it on the infant head. Once the infant has grown to breeding age (the same time it begins to grow its own skulls) it will shed its mothers skull (or rather keep it and give its young fresh skulls, a product of their civilization) and grow one of its own, starting the cycle anew. When a Cubone has exhausted its Skull supply (one directly proportional to its breeding capacity) it will allow one permanent skull to grow upon its head; fusing into it to become a Marowak. It is noted however, that when Cubone are bred in captivity, the constant need to supply new young with skulls will prevent this procedure form happening as the final skull is always given to new young. This is a noted reason why Cubone will not evolve in captivity.

Social Structure

Cubone are known for having the most complex social structure of any Pokémon known in the Nation. The Cubone and Marowak work in complex tribal clans with several tribes existing within a certain habitable range. Known as a Nation (The so dubbed KJHS-Nation is known to have six tribes) each tribe will consist of a constantly traversed territory and a hidden Bone Yard where graves are kept. A notable aspect of the Cubone is their worship and love of the dead body parts of their species. Whenever a Cubone in the wild has been documented to be killed the Cubone in its tribe have been shown to take the body into one of their secluded areas to be placed in a Bone Yard which are currently unreachable by modern means. However scraps of Cubone have been shown to exist in certain areas of a Nation and it is seen that Cubone have an istly knwdeg of what to do with these. An evidence of this is the use of the item known as the Thick Club. This Bone of the Marowak - Cubone line is known for the instinctual use of the members of the family to wield it with great skill and it has be shown that a Well-Bred member with the right temperament and proper training can wild attacks stronger than an Explosion. As Cubone that we bred in captivity are also capable of using this it is believed that the knowledge is instinctual, and certain noble members caught in the wild were seen to have Thick Clubs on them. It is ale apparent that imported Thick Clubs from other regions (and thus tribes) are just as effective, further backing of the claim that the use is instinctive and not bound by any social rule. Customarily a small percentage (one in twenty currently thought it may have been higher in the past) are Noble and thus carry these Clubs.
