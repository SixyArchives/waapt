I've never really been that interested in the glitches (I got into Pokemon after they stopped having big glitches) and I dunno how many other people are, but if you get a good idea for them, write it.

And I just thought of a more positive way to put my above rant, in that straight-out abuse probably isn't nearly the biggest problem trained Pokemon face. After all, the nature of the creatures and game mechanics themselves support Rosseau Was Right, as a Pokemon treated with love, trust and respect will be stronger, smarter, healthier and more confident than Pokemon trained with merely brute force.

Trainers are less likely to show malice than mere incompetence- improper training, neglect, overfeeding, poor hygiene or supervision, and other problems due to a lack of education or laziness are likely to make a Pokemon less healthy, trustful and effective in battle.

I imagine that's probably the reason that in-game, most trainers only have two or three Pokemon, and the more they have the less well-trained they are: properly raising and training Pokemon for battle takes a LOT of effort and dedication, something that's a lot easier for, say, ten-year-olds without additional responsibilities than it is for a 9-5 working parent, corporate executive or people in poverty. Unless they devote most of their life to training (such as Elite Four and Gym Leaders, and even they seem to have their own jobs, duties or are otherwise self-funded) or their job requires use of their Pokemon (police, farmers, criminals etc) most people can probably only afford to raise as many Pokemon as the average person does pets.

Half the point of this Pokedex would be to inform people of how to look after their Pokemon, which would be most suited for their lifestyle and needs, and what risks they take by training them.

EDIT: Y'know, I could probably take this rant and modify it into a foreword for the Pokedex.
