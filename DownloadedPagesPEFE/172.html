Outer Cloyster
This may be impossible to explain using any natural phenomena we know about, but I'll try some ideas. Maybe there's some light-shifting chemicals in their skin and fur?

The Illusion ability seems to be predominantly visual, which means that the only thing it needs to do to work is to alter the wavelengths of light that get absorbed and reflected by the Zorua/Zoroark. So you need pigments that can be rapidly adjusted. You also need to be able to change the scattering of light, where it's being reflected and so on, in order to give the illusion of depth and such like.

The right kind of protein or collection of proteins could theoretically do this. They would have to be controlled by the Pokemon somehow. Pulling on its tail, taking a direct attack and so on would disrupt their concentration causing the illusion to fail. How they would control it is something that can't be explained easily and it would cause the illusion to look very wavery in even a slight breeze in all likelihood unless they can lock the pigments or what have you into a fixed place relative to them.

This is not a very good explanation either, and we might simply not know beyond some tentative conclusions. In a case like that, one can only explain what is known for certain, and how it visually works
