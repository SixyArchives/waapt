Whew... sorry I've been away for so long, first schoolwork, then getting home and relaxing / doing other projects.

So. Read all the articles to date, no objections. One bit of clarification on the now-published Darmanitan article, though- it says that females seek out mature Darmanitan for protection from the less restrained adolescents, but that the mate leaves after mating? That just seems like rather shoddy protection to seek after.

A more general issue, that seems to not be thought about in a lot of the more xeno articles, is the matter of diet. Obviously, exotic lifeforms may likely eat quite different things than more mundane biochemistry; however, whatever they eat, realize it has to be a plausible energy source.

Living things extract energy from compounds like any chemical reaction does- by converting high-potential-energy chemicals to low-potential-energy chemicals. That's why, for instance, water is near-useless as a fuel, no matter what cranks will tell you. It's a very stable chemical.

So, for instance, if a species eats rocks, be clear about what kind they eat. Ferrous iron is a plausible agent to reduce, as are some sulfur compounds. Iron oxide ("rust") and quartz crystals are not plausible, the former being already spent and the latter being, again, a very stable chemical.

None of this applies to the chemicals used to compose the body, obviously. Your body is made of quartz, then eating quartz is needed to grow. It does not keep you alive, though.
