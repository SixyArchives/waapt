Sorry it took so long to answer. I had school today.

So yeah, I read all the things said/written, so here's the answers. (Sorry for it being huge)

About Kyurem: I chose Lacunosa Town to be their home town especially because of Kyurem living nearby, actually. What happened is that sometime during their childhood, they wandered off into Kyurem's cave and got attacked. They tried to run back to Lacunosa, but Kyurem gained on them and tried to attack Norm. Landon took the blow instead, giving him a scar on his forehead, which is why he's wearing a headband to cover it up. Landon's survival was pure luck and willpower, but it gave him a small dislike towards Ice Type Pokémon, which made it hard for him to grow to like Sneasel, but he eventually did for Sneasel's sake. Their grandfather's Salamence, the same one who eventually became the mother of Norm's Mencer, chased Kyurem away after that. Due to not wanting a repeat of this ever happening again, the family moved to Hoenn. Landon isn't chasing after Kyurem or anything, but if they were to meet again, Landon would likely try to battle and catch it, and then never use it out of spite. (Since they're not allowed to have Legendary Mons, I doubt that this will ever happen anyway). So it's just one attack on Landon, and that's it. If anyone has a backstory with Kyurem as well, it can always be from before that, or if that's not an option (if Kyurem's been inactive for x years or something), it maybe wasn't Kyurem who attacked him. (Could be excused by an evil experiment gone or a Ditto transforming into Kyurem and feeling threatened by the twins suddenly appearing). It just says that it was an Ice-Dragon Type, so there's room for it not being the real Kyurem in some sort of twist.

About Mega Evolution: I kinda thought it'd be that way, and I didn't plan on having him have the Key and Mega Stone from the beginning. But that's also why I only put Mega Evolution with Aeryl and not with Mencer. Because they'll only go after the Aerodactylite and not the Salamencite. The reason being that Mencer may go rogue upon Mega Evolution because it's mother can't handle it either. Aeryl, on the other hand, may be able to restrain himself due to his bond with Landon. But, being a child, it might still be too much for him. And Aeryl being capable of Mega Evolution, and not Mencer, will give him even more of a rivalry complex towards Aeryl.

About Haunter and his telepathy: I'm fine with Landon and Norm having to earn the right to be able to understand Mon language, so that's why Haunter has this capability. If Haunter couldn't talk to Landon in some way, how would he have helped him survive all these years, since Haunter also doesn't like to come in cities, so they likely stayed in routes and forests. As for Haunter being in Hoenn, where Haunter don't thrive, there is a good reason for that, but only he himself knows and will likely only explain when feeling like he should. Still, his reasons don't affect his battles whatsoever, so he's willing to fight in any battle that Landon sends him out to, even if he does consider himself a wild Pokémon.

About Norm's height: 5'12" is about 1 meter 82, and 5'10" is about 1 meter 78, which are Norm's and Landon's heights. They're 16, so I gave them around the height I had when I was sixteen. I live somewhere where we use meters, so I'm not used to using feet instead.

About starting out as two people: The reason is simply because their twin brothers and foils to one another. Norm keeps Landon nice and calm, and Landon helps Norm traverse the many routes with ease thanks to his knowledge and experience on things that aren't cities or Gym Battles. They don't always travel together though.

About the Lore: The only thing set is their history with Kyurem(?) and Haunter's reasons, so everything else that happens will play off the lore of the story going on now.

About the Tone: While their backstory might not be the cheeriest, that doesn't mean they can't be happy fun times now. That's the entire reason they're foils too, to get in awkward situations every now and then. They're both good at heart, so there is absolutely zero chance of either of them ever trying to doublecross others, trap others or anything. When in conflict with evil, they will both choose good without hesitation. Drama will likely not come up much at all, since there's nothing for them to be dramatic about. Landon likely despises Kyurem, but that's about it. And Norm's a cheery guy. If drama ever happens to him, it's because he's concerned about someone else. I'm known to put some angst in the stories I write in my free time, but I can easily leave it out without issues. So the brothers angsting is very unlikely to happen, seeing as they also have a strong spirit. Especially Landon, who survived Kyurem of all Pokémon. They'll have awesomeness too. Especially Haunter at times, who will defend Landon if he is in serious danger, like if he loses a battle against a criminal gang or something. Of course, he won't go overboard. I know Haunters are feared because of their... 'unique' ability with their tongue, but Haunter will not go that far. Also, the twins won't be a generic 'good guy, rude guy' duo. They both have hidden depths, and so do their Pokémon. Especially Haunter.

I'll continue this in a second post, because it's getting too big.

Here's part two.

About Every's Essay: While English isn't my first language, I'm good at it, so I usually don't make too many mistakes, and if I do, I try to edit the post as fast as possible to rectify the mistake. Both Landon and Norm accept any challenge headed for them, and the Pokémon interact the most, way more than the trainers. The Pokémon will react to attacks in the realm of what's possible. I can't just have Nugget dodge a Surf, that'd be ridiculous. Battles are supposed to be fair, and I have no problems with letting my trainers lose battles. But that doesn't mean that the Pokémon will be weak, and they'll fight to the best of their capabilities. As for moves and effects being the opponent's choice, I'm already used to battles like that from the Arena, so that's the default kind of battling I do. I never ignore anyone's attacks. If I don't mention it, I just got gre'd and will put it in a new post with apologies for missing it. While having powers for Landon and Norm would be nice, I don't mind if they stay powerless for the whole game, actually. Haunter's psychic powers are very strong, and will protect Landon when he needs it. Landon's very agile though due to his survival on the routes and in the forests for several years. I'm not a shipping guy either, so if any shipping regarding my characters happens, it'll have to come from the other character. Bolt might act a little naughty, but that's because of her Attract strategy. Of course, it's important to let my characters settle in first instead of making them jump into plot the second they enter the game. I won't rush people either, as I myself know what it's like to be busy. As for plot arcs, I feel like, if I want to propose one, it won't be for a while. I think it's better if I become aquainted with the current plot, the people, etc. first. Rushing things is never a good thing to do. Working together is important too. If I propose an idea, and people don't like it, then I won't do it. I'm not going to insist on it, I'll just move on, otherwise it might ruin the flow of the game. As for being polite, I'm always polite, and I have a lot of patience. Getting me to be mad takes a whole lot.

Also informing you that everything they have has a reason, nothing was just added for the heck of it. Even their Pokémon. Landon has fast, agile Pokémon who can deal quite a punch because that's how he learned to survive: be fast, and be strong. This reflects in his Pokémon. Norm has sturdiern more powerful Pokémon because he's a trainer trying to get the Gym Badges. He gets more actual training in, so his Pokémon naturally outpower Landon's in a battle, even in a three on four. In the wild, however, Landon's Pokémon will outperform Norm's everyday. Also their amount of Pokémon has reasons. Most normal trainers who aren't the Chosen Heroes have about 3 Pokémon at most, unless they're really experienced. Or use 6 Magikarp. Landon has more Pokémon because the more he has, the more they can back each other up in trouble. (Plus, a bit of sibling rivalry tempting him to have one more Pokémon than his brother)

Lastly, there will likely be foreshadowing in regards to Landon and Haunter, as they both have something to hide. Of course, it'll be a while before anything comes out of that. Time helps people and Pokémon evolve, and only after some time will this foreshadowing begin.
