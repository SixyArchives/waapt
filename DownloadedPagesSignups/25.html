A few thoughts regarding the version of your proposal that you put up around 4 hours ago. If I sound mean / snarky, I'm really not trying to be, it means that my inner judge of tact has fallen asleep at the wheel due to the hour.

Age: Young Adult, between 25-30

Ren has dark green eyes and wears glasses due to astigmatism. Ren prefers dark colored clothes and to dress smartly, wearing vests over his shirts with a pair of matching pants and sensible shoes and occasionally, a matching hat. Ren's left leg is heavily scarred due to a riding accident in his teens and carries a collapsible cane with him for extra balance when needed.

Any ties to an organization: Ren is often commissioned by various people, groups, and organizations of varying degrees of importance to design and produce everything from clip art to magazine ads to the outfits to be worn by families at their holiday parties.

This part sounds okay... And gives a hefty Sanae Hanekoma vibe. Though I question the viability for a graphics designer to maintain such a broad scope of work, though there ARE designers who survive on such varied work, so I think it is ultimately fine. I would presume that barring any outside help, Ren is an "up and comer" given that he's been in the workforce for only 3-7 years.

Region of origin: Born in Goldenrod City, Raised in Celadon City, Attend college in La Rousse City. Currently lives in Castelia City.

This seems like it could be interesting. Kanto and Johto are close enough to each other to justify the move between Goldenrod and Celadon. It's not unheard of to go a decent ways away from college, and ditto for work afterwards. Though interested as to what your char would identify most closely with for an identity.

The next parts are where I start getting concerned.

Backstory: Quirky, hammy, a bit snarky, and a fan of pop culture, Renard Valenum Clavis is the son of high profile lawyer Erik Tobias Clavis and Clara Sophia Desmond Clavis. Ren spent his early years with his family as a normal, mid-class family until his father took on a case for Silph. Ren's father won that case, and thus earning him international recognition - and all the money that comes along with that.

The backstory of sudden riches seems like it would be a bit of a stretch. Most professions, especially education-heavy fields such as law aren't something where you can jump from the middle tier to the big time in a clean shot. Given the stakes of the sorts of suits a large industrial conglomerate like Silph would be involved in, it seems unlikely they would trust a lawyer without an already sterling background in the field, or at least one with a LOT of understudy under one who fit the above criteria. In addition, Law tends to be a rather dry field. The lawyers that rack up millions per case in reality tend to not be terribly well known outside of different entities' legal departments.

Thanks to the money Ren's father earned, Ren attended the La Rousse College of Art and Technology earning a BA in computer graphics and fashion design. Due to a riding accident in his teens, Ren has trick left ankle and walks with a cane in cold and/or stormy weather or whenever he needs extra support.

Ren is a freelance artist and seamster with several clients as well as being a Pokemon co-ordinator taking part in contests and musicals whenever he can.

As someone who took a computer graphics course and got utterly owned by its final project, I'm not sure if this particular double major would really suit Ren's skillset described earlier well. Computer Graphics courses tend to be very math-heavy and focus on pushing a very tight focus on some combination of realism, getting a stylistic depiction down to a tee, or unobtrusive integration of animations or renders. Given that Ren likely isn't building the software that he works with from scratch and focusing on static designs, it seems overkill to me that he would need more than some sort of design degree.

Of course, if Ren somehow DID double major in the two fields, it might make an interesting story for his college years. Both of the majors mentioned are VERY time intensive, and likely would have been a major drag on any social life Ren had in college.

Working from his house in the suburbs of Castelia; Ren, in conjunction with his pokemon - Alpha the Porygon, Beta the Porygon2, Gamma the Porygon-Z, Spinner the Swaddle, Weaver the Swadloon, and Seamstress the Leavanny, and Socar the Alakazam - Ren is able create beautiful digital art and clothes for his clients in very little time. In his travels Ren has captured a fair amount of Pokemon and prefers to not keep his favorites in storage boxes but at his parents' summer home, a ranch on the northern outskirts of Castelia City. Ren likes to visit them them on days he's not busy. Ren has a special license which must be renewed annually for Ren to keep the Pokemon used for his commissions in the city. Ren is a likes to participate in the local theaters - usually as a consultant, but has been cast in a few roles as well.

This part has a number of things that seem like stretches. The first being that design is rarely quite as straightforward as putting pen to paper, especially for impersonal clients. Even if Ren is somehow able to whip out a design going from draft to final stage in a matter of an hour, he still has to deal with the very real possibility of being asked to go back and change things with his present draft, potentially many times. A very real element of commission-based work. Furthermore, with the level of prestige implied to be tied to Ren's position, I find it a bit of a stretch that he would have the time to pursue an active professional stage role without impacting the quality of his work as a designer or otherwise sleeping 4 hour nights on a regular basis (which would probably harm his design work anyways).

Also, you seem to imply that Ren takes up coordinating. Given his already formidable workload, I would be genuinely surprised to see him able to balance more than hobbyist-level competition, even if he happened to be a natural at the circuit. Because in the end, that's not what pays his bills.

617 TracerBullet27th May 2013 11:09:53 AM from A Dark and Rainy Alley Get RP Mod

@ Keys

... That actually seems like a gross oversimplification of how the design process works. I mean, even if an artist had the ability to go directly from thought to finished product, that strikes me as merely frontloading the work. Consider the following example:

Suppose that Ren has been contracted to do a cityscape of Castellia for some client with too much money and zero imagination. When Sorcar pulls out images from Ren's head to work with, he would still need to settle on an overall set to work with (e.g. what angle and what direction to view the skyline from), environmental settings (e.g. time of day, weather in scene), and then a bunch of little flourishes to add agency to the thing (e.g. gawking people, kites in the background). And THEN he needs to go back and make everything fit (e.g. shading).

We'll give Ren and the team the benefit of the doubt, and assume that they're able to get a cohesive image together from mental grab-bag to final submittable renders in the course of 6 hours, or 75% of a normal workday. (Which is usually how long it takes just to draw such images in something like CS[insert number here] before touchup.) Now Ren has an design that's ready to submit... But. He's not guaranteed that his client won't ask him to go back and change things. And with a client potentially doing focus-tested work like an industrial firm, it's more or less unavoidable that you'd have to revise many, many times for that.

So we'll say that Ren got lucky and didn't have to revise at all, without the scourge of artists' / writers' block. That's one image that took the better part of a day. When your client potentially commissioned more than one of them. You can see where this is going, right? Imagine that your cityscape commissioned above wasn't really one image, but a set of 20 for a gallery.

In the grand scheme of things, I think that it's not important enough to derail your signup though, even if it stretches believability a bit. Or at least that sole part on its own isn't. After all, we have a few researchers that somehow haven't managed to get fired while wandering around on and off and not really working while onscreen for the past two years.

  • Name: Beatrice "Bea" Netts

  • Gender: Female
  • Appearance: Short, brunette and bookish. Pale skinned. Wears glasses not because she needs them but because others take her more seriously when she does. Radiates an aura of awkwardness.
  • Hometown: Grew up in Goldenrod City in Johto, but currently resides in a small cottage in Illex Forest.
  • Backstory: Bea has been fascinated by Bug-type Pokemon from an early age. She moved into Illex Forest to study and care for the Bug-types there. Her general awkwardness around other people was also a deciding factor in her leaving Goldenrod City, where she also studied Bug-type Pokemon at a local college. Bea is not much of a battler. Her Pokemon have all of their experience by defending themselves and Bea's precious Bugs from hungry Flying-types. Has a tendency to geek out and gush over Bug-types and hates being called by her full name.

Overly confident and brash, in contrast to her trainer. Has some anger issues; has a lot of pent-up frustration and aggression towards Flying-types in particular. Despite all the odds, Bea and Tegan are the best of friends.
    • Move examples: Bug Buzz, Aerial Ace, Psychic, Sleep Powder etc.
