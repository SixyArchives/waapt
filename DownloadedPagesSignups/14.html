340 JacquesHammer14th Apr 2013 08:55:37 PM from a van down by the river Get RP Mod
Cue the Flying Pigs!
Alright, here's my character signup that I discussed a bit in the chatroom. Do note that there are a couple of unresolved questionable things, namely the Almighty Pink Jirachi (who may end up being somehow replaced by a pre-existing Mew) and the spiky-eared Pichu. Also, be warned, it's LONG.

Name: Benjamin Green

Age: 15

Gender: Male

Appearance: Fairly tall (just over six feet), with an awkward sort of build that manages to simultaneously be wimpy-looking and somewhat athletic. His disorderly, dark brown hair is typically in a state of limbo in between being short enough to keep neat and long enough to lie flat, and while he is virtually incapable of growing any hair on his lip or chin, his sideburns are always messily half-shaved. His skin is tanned from five years of steady travel, and his face looks as though it has been hit by one piece of flying debris too many. He wears an enormous, ragged, many-pocketed <i>thing</i> of vague shape and color that used to be a trench coat and that is several times his age. Under it, he wears whatever clothes happened to be lying around, irregardless of such trivial things as style, compatibility, or the fact that <i>that plaid golf shirt does not go with those athletic shorts</i>! His boots have survived gunshots, car crashes, explosions, vengeful spirits deep-sea conditions, political turmoil, and a teething Trapinch, and will probably live on long after his death. His hat is a faded, floppy, wide-brimmed affair, passed down - like his coat, boots, and Torterra - from his grandfather. Over it he wears a cast-iron saucepan, adopted as a final solution to numerous divinely-induced head injuries. He's also gotten quite good at whacking people with it. (Tartar sauce! I'm not sure this is still brief!)

Affiliation: None. He's just a random guy whose fate has been screwed with far too much for his own good.

Region of origin: Orre. He used to live in Gateon Port, where his parents owned a shop that sold lots of the weird randomly-cobbled-together technology in Orre, but left for Agate Village when he was five after his parents' tragic demise (which involved a homemade particle accelerator, a few dozen used toasters, and several hundred gallons of mayo, and which instilled in him a lifelong fear of mayonnaise).

Backstory: Born the great-great-grandson of a Sinn immigrant miner on his father's side, Ben enjoyed a fairly pleasant, reasonably sane early childhood (a rarity for someone in the Orre Region), helping out around the shop and filling in for his mom or dad when one of them went out hunting for spare parts and bargain machinery. After the aforementioned Mayo Incident, he lived with his grandpa in Agate Village, reading an inordinate number of books and tinkering with various mechanical things of indefinite origin or purpose, until he reached the age of ten. It was at this point that, having decided that his grandson's introverted nature and quiet, thoughtful personality were due to a lack of Proper Adventure and Good Old-Fashioned Roughing It, like in the Good Old Days when he was a boy, he pulled out his old hat, coat and boots from when he was a trainer, grabbed the ancient family Torterra's pokeball, and presented them to Ben. When Ben protested, his grandpa hitched up the rickety old tree-cabin to the Torterra's tree, locked all of Ben's books and gadgets in the cellar, and told him that he could come back home (and have his stuff back) once he had reached the other side of the "American" (Orre-Unova, not sure what to call it) continent on Torterra-back. Thus, Benjamin Green became a rather reluctant Pokemon trainer, traveling across the land and slowly building up his Pokemon team (along with his personal library and supply of random, half-finished machinery, which he keeps in a smallish cavern inside his Torterra's shell (it's a <i>really big</i> Torterra)).

Things would have been fairly ordinary (or rather, as ordinary as a trans-continental journey on a ridiculously large Torterra can be) from that point on if not for the interference of the Almighty Pink Jirachi, an obscure deity of sorts worshipped by an equally obscure cult of Sinn immigrant gold-miners, of which Ben's great-great-grandfather was a part. No one is certain if, why, or how said Jirachi is actually Almighty and Pink; the members of His cult do not care to speculate on such cosmic matters best left to His Almighty Pinkness, everybody else flat-out doesn't care, and presumably, the Almighty Pink Jirachi Himself does not care to question His own nature for fear of finding out that He doesn't actually exist. Anyhow, at around the time Ben set out on his journey, the Almighty Pink Jirachi was conveniently reminded by a certain Pichu (whom I shall get to later) that Ben's great-great-grandfather had once prayed to Him for rain to end the great drought in the region, after which (specifically, five years later, after all of his crops had died and he had given up farming, when some guy with a Politoed was passing through town) it had, in fact, rained, and that his descendants were therefore indebted to Him. Being that He was Almighty (or possibly because some random Pokemon had once skill-swapped Insomnia onto Him - He tends to be rather forgetful in spite of His Almightiness), the Almighty Pink Jirachi was not bound by such insignificant constraints as the thousand-year sleep cycles of mere ordinary Jirachi, and thus spent rather a lot of time sitting around in some long-lost cave somewhere and twiddling his thumbs. Being of the opinion that the material universe, flawed and rather silly as it was, must exist primarily for his amusement, the Almighty Pink Jirachi decided to collect on the debt unknowingly owed to Him by making Benjamin Green his own personal Cosmic Plaything.

And so, from that point on, Ben's travels across the continent have been filled with paranormal encounters, feuding ancient conspiracies, alien abductions, monsters of the day, small towns desperately in need of a hero (or Ben), and gratuitous slapstick comedy (and also some gadget-peddling on the side - Giant Torterra aren't just going to feed themselves (or rather, they ARE, via photosynthesis, but this particular one needs to do more than just lie around absorbing sunlight and conserving energy all day)).

Finally, Ben's goal of reaching the east coast - and therefore his return home - drawing near, the Almighty Pink Jirachi decided that the situation called for drastic action. Thus, he decided to toss Ben in with the WAAPT gang, sit back, and enjoy the results.

(Aaand... that was WAY too long, but I figured that the Almighty Pink Jirachi required some explaining (and will still probably require more before this application makes its way through the approval process))

And now, the Pokemon team...

  • The glorified mobile home, Terry the Torterra
    • Gender: Male
    • Ability: Overgrow
    • Description: When Benjamin's great-great-grandpa came to Orre, he brought with him a single Pokemon, a Torterra who would later come to be known as Terry (his "official" name being a nigh-unpronounceable string of a hundred words of Old Sinn). He hailed from an ancient family renowned throughout Sinnoh as the best Torterra-breeders in the region, for the rare subspecies that they specialized in far outlived the already impressive lifespan of ordinary Torterra and grew to phenominal size. Just as Ben's great-great-grandfather had received his Torterra from his father, who had received it from his father, and so on an so forth, he passed it down to his son, who passed it down to his son, Joseph Green, Ben's Grandfather. Joe had planned on keeping Terry for himself, believing that his son had dishonored the family legacy by denying the Almighty Pink Jirachi and taking up a career working with "that no-good, rotten, worthless, newfangled, Pink-forsaken machinery." However, he eventually decided that Ben needed Terry (and the ensuing adventure) more than he did, thus handing the ancient, iron-clad, bowling-ball-sized monstrosity of a pokeball down to his grandson.

Unlike the rest of Ben's team, Terry doesn't even communicate in Pokemon language. In spite of his colossal size, his brain still possesses the approximate size (and intelligence level)of a golf ball. He mostly just grunts and roars and such. If Terry were to battle, he could demolish towns and level small mountains with his tremendous Earthquake attacks. The operative word being "if." Terry is far too old and lazy to consider doing anything of the sort, and mostly just lies around munching on the grass while absorbing foes' attacks with his massive HP and defense stats and occasionally healing himself with synthesis. He is perpetually grumpy but never angry or violent, considering such things to take far more energy than they're worth. Getting him moving in the morning is a uphill battle that Ben has to face every day, and he will often stop to eat whatever looks interesting (fortunately, his teeth are extremely blunt and made for chewing plants, so Ben can usually convince him to spit people out before too much damage is done). Once his crotchety old bones finally get moving, however, his stamina is tremendous, and his Rock Climb move means that even the steepest of mountains cannot stand in his way. Ben's cabin is built in the tree on his back, and Ben's workshop occupies a cave in his shell. The excess water he drinks is stored in a small spring on his back, which also contains a few assorted small water Pokemon that he has accidentally picked up over the years.
  • Unusual moves: Rock Climb

  • The first Pokemon who would actually battle, Sandy "I-was-ten-when-I-named-him" the Trapinch
    • Gender: Male
    • Ability: Hyper Cutter
    • Description: A Trapinch of simple tastes, Sandy's primary three interests are biting everything in sight, eating anything edible (and quite a few things that aren't), and digging labrythine networks of tunnels (often on Ben's lawn/Terry's shell), in that order. Ben caught Sandy when, after repeatedly failing to convince Terry to actually attack something, Terry accidentally stepped on Sandy, nearly killing him in the process. Ben stuck him in a pokeball, nursed him back to health, and received several severe puncture wounds as thanks. In stark contrast to Terry, Sandy loves to battle, often challenging opponents several times his size (not a difficult task for a Trapinch) and winning through sheer psychotic aggression of the "DEAR GOD, GET IT OFF OF ME!" variety. Despite his violent nature, Sandy is still quite childish, becoming equally excited about the opportunity to get an extra-large ice cream sundae or bite a Probopass's nose off. He also likes making sand-angels, and doesn't care if he has to fill the cave workshop with his Sandstorm attack to do it.
    • Unusual moves: Strength and Rock Smash

  • The 'mon behind the man, Notch the Pichu
    • Gender: Female
    • Ability: Static
    • Description: Since ancient times, the appearance of a strange, spiky-eared Pichu has been seen as a portent of changing fates for cultures across the world. Until recently, most scientists considered the so-called "Gizamimi Pichu" nothing but an odd quirk of folklore, but more recent studies seem to suggest the existence of a rare sub-species, capable of learning the move Pain Split but incapable of evolving. The truth, however, is far stranger than that, for unbeknownst to anyone (including Ben), all of the spiky-eared Pichu are actually a single, unaging, time-traveling individual, subtly manipulating events to ensure the continued survival of the multiverse. It was she who sought out the Almighty Pink Jirachi, convinced him that he actually existed, and pointed him towards Ben; she who set into motion the chain of events that eventually led to him meeting up with the WAAPT; and she who will be there to ensure that Ben fulfills his destiny. What might this destiny be, you ask? Perhaps he will have to become a true hero to slay an ancient evil threatening reality itself, or maybe he'll just need to end up in a certain place at a certain time to serve as a meat shield for somebody who's actually important to the course of history. The Pichu's machinations are as convoluted and labyrinthine as... a very convoluted and labyrinthine thing, such that she has, on multiple occasions, ended up fighting herself nearly to the death in service of two masters on opposing sides of a conflict. She is unfathomably old and impossible to predict and able to outmaneuver everyone, always twenty moves into the game before the other players even have time to put their pieces on the chessboard. She also likes Grepa berries.
    • Unusual Moves: ALL OF THEM!!! (As a result of her experiences serving under hundreds and hundreds of different trainers, she is theoretically capable of using any move that Pichu can learn (with the exception of egg moves (or event-exclusive moves) that she doesn't canonically know). While this sounds as though it would make her obscenely overpowered, it is balanced out by the fact that (a) Pichu's selection of usable moves is actually rather underwhelming, and (b) she's still just a tiny, unevolvable rodent in a world of land-sharks and giant clay robots. Essentially, she's an extreme version of Weak, but Skilled. Her favorite moves include Pain Split, Volt Tackle, Zap Cannon, and Magnet Rise. Her Hidden Power type is Psychic.

  • The resident "eccentric" scientist, Igor the Reuniclus
    • Gender: Male
    • Ability: Magic Guard
    • Description: Igor notable both for being the first Pokemon that Ben actually battled to catch (Sandy having been accidentally crushed by Terry and Notch's pokeball having simply shown up on the bedside table one morning for reasons known only to herself) and for being the ONLY Pokemon that he actually managed to evolve himself. He caught Igor in an abandoned, highly-restricted ruin of a government facility (Why was he in there, you ask? Well, the Kadabra Mafia isn't just going to run from itself!), that was destroyed when an attempt to breed Reuniclus with Acid-Armor Muk and create a sort of living, self-reparing jelly armor went horribly wrong. While the ensuing giant green blob monster ravaged the facility and ate most of the personnel, a few incubation pods remained undamaged, and it was in one of these that Ben found Igor, who in several months' time reached the Reuniclus stage.

As this happened when Ben was in a bit of a competitive battling phase, he sought out a Thunderpunch move tutor to teach his Reuniclus (after trying a demo version of TM Thunder and finding that it didn't work for what he had in mind). This lends itself rather well to an extremely annoying strategy that Igor can use involving Thunderpunch and Acid Armor. While special attacks typically require a level of concentration impossible to achieve in slime form and physical attacks typically require an actual physical body to strike with, Thunderpunch allows Igor to generate electricity biologically rather that psychically (like Thunder), meaning that he can wait for a foe to physically attack him, splatter himself all over them with Acid Armor, then start zapping away with Thunderpunch. On the other hand, the fact that he can't be recalled to his Pokeball while in slime form means that the match is practically over if the opponent has a Ground type on hand, as it can just keep smashing away at Igor every time he tries to reform until he faints.

He loves creating absurdly intricate and completely pointless (or occasionally frighteningly dangerous - he isn't picky, and doesn't even really know the difference) inventions, and every piece of technology on Terry is constantly subject to a three-way salvage war between him, Ben, and Ben's Elgyem, Andromeda. Forget swapping batteries between the remote and the radio - between those three, a vital component of Ben's water-heater for the shower will typically end up in Igor's automated carrot-peeler the next week, and Andromeda's overly-optimistic attempt at a flying saucer the week after that. The lab looks like somebody dropped random pieces of rusty old machinery (bartered for and salvaged by Ben), top-secret government tech ("rescued" from the ruined facility by Igor), highly-advanced alien technology (from Andromeda's ruined spaceship), strange Black Boxes that Notch sometimes shows up with after vanishing for a couple of hours, and Sufficiently Advanced Bamboo Technology (recovered from the same ancient ruins of indefinite purpose as Ben's as-yet-unmentioned sixth team member, Solomon the Golurk), threw it all into a blender, and launched a rather large warhead at said blender (And then dumped sand over the whole thing. Lots of Sand.)
  • Unusual moves: Acid Armor, Thunderpunch, Helping Hand (yes, this does theoretically allow him to give Sandy ACTUAL THUNDER ARMOR! Helping Hand also allows him to extend the whole handshake-mind-meld thing to non-Reuniclus and communicate telepathically)

  • The obligatory alien princess, Andromeda Celestia Cassiopeia Centauri Ligray Von Pygraulon the Third, Sole Heir to the Eternal Throne of the Elgyem and Beheeyem People (Whose Homeworld is, Unfortunately, Rather Less Eternal Than its Throne)
    • Gender: Female
    • Ability: Telepathy
    • Description: Upon meeting Andromeda, Ben's first thought went something along the lines of "OHMYGOSTASHINYPOKEMONIVEGOTTOCATCHITRIGHTNOW!!!" Actually, this was his second thought, his first though being more to the effect of "Screw this, I'm just going to wear that old saucepan on my head from now on!" (After the Almighty Pink Jirachi's "hit Ben in the head with random crap" phase reached its logical conclusion in the form of a flying saucer) Andromeda tends to think like the the unholy fusion of the protagonist of a Magical Girl show, a Purity Sue, and Superman. She misses her home planet (which was blown up by an army of surprisingly violent Clefairy when she was about five years old, leading her parents to launch her out into space and hope for the best), but believes that she was sent to Earth (or whatever the Pokemon world is called) for a reason, and has sworn to use the strange powers imparted to her by the light of our sun to save the people and Pokemon of the world from evil. Said "strange powers" involve storing and redirecting the sun's light to turn her natural sparkliness to dangerous, eye-searing levels (Her usual response when Ben tells her that the ability to use the move Flash hardly constitutes "special powers" is to psychically shout "Special!" and telekinetically throw something at him.). Though not quite as much of a Mad Scientist as Igor, she nonetheless loves playing around in the workshop, and is perpetually embroiled in a sort of three-way technological remote-battery-war (I might just be making up the term "remote battery war." If so, what I mean is when there is only one pair of double-A batteries in the house, and one person wants them for the TV remote but the other person wants them for the radio, and both of them keep taking the batteries from the other's device to use in their own. I wonder if that shows up in fiction often enough to be a candidate for trope status...) with him and Ben. She is also an obsessive Anime and Manga fan (which should be... interesting to write, considering that the only Anime I've ever actually watched is Pokemon (which won't work for obvious reasons), and I can't even say that much for Manga. Now WHERE an I going to find a website with useful, convenient information about every single fictional work to come out of Japan ever...?) In battle, she is very much a Squishy Wizard, having decently powerful psychic attacks but little physical fighting ability or resiliency to enemy moves. Her Teleport does help mitigate this somewhat by allowing her to dodge attacks via Teleport Spam, but one good attack is still often enough to knock her out.
    • Unusual Moves: Teleport, Flash

  • The gratuitous ancient robot, Solomon the Golurk
    • Gender: None
    • Ability: Klutz
