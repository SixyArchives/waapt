Alright, here's my character signup that I discussed a bit in the chatroom. Do note that there are a couple of unresolved questionable things, namely the Almighty Pink Jirachi (who may end up being somehow replaced by a pre-existing Mew) and the spiky-eared Pichu. Also, be warned, it's LONG.

Name: Benjamin Green

Age: 15

Gender: Male

Appearance: Fairly tall (just over six feet), with an awkward sort of build that manages to simultaneously be wimpy-looking and somewhat athletic. His disorderly, dark brown hair is typically in a state of limbo in between being short enough to keep neat and long enough to lie flat, and while he is virtually incapable of growing any hair on his lip or chin, his sideburns are always messily half-shaved. His skin is tanned from five years of steady travel, and his face looks as though it has been hit by one piece of flying debris too many. He wears an enormous, ragged, many-pocketed <i>thing</i> of vague shape and color that used to be a trench coat and that is several times his age. Under it, he wears whatever clothes happened to be lying around, irregardless of such trivial things as style, compatibility, or the fact that <i>that plaid golf shirt does not go with those athletic shorts</i>! His boots have survived gunshots, car crashes, explosions, vengeful spirits deep-sea conditions, political turmoil, and a teething Trapinch, and will probably live on long after his death. His hat is a faded, floppy, wide-brimmed affair, passed down - like his coat, boots, and Torterra - from his grandfather. Over it he wears a cast-iron saucepan, adopted as a final solution to numerous divinely-induced head injuries. He's also gotten quite good at whacking people with it. (Tartar sauce! I'm not sure this is still brief!)

Affiliation: None. He's just a random guy whose fate has been screwed with far too much for his own good.

Region of origin: Orre. He used to live in Gateon Port, where his parents owned a shop that sold lots of the weird randomly-cobbled-together technology in Orre, but left for Agate Village when he was five after his parents' tragic demise (which involved a homemade particle accelerator, a few dozen used toasters, and several hundred gallons of mayo, and which instilled in him a lifelong fear of mayonnaise).

Backstory: Born the great-great-grandson of a Sinn immigrant miner on his father's side, Ben enjoyed a fairly pleasant, reasonably sane early childhood (a rarity for someone in the Orre Region), helping out around the shop and filling in for his mom or dad when one of them went out hunting for spare parts and bargain machinery. After the aforementioned Mayo Incident, he lived with his grandpa in Agate Village, reading an inordinate number of books and tinkering with various mechanical things of indefinite origin or purpose, until he reached the age of ten. It was at this point that, having decided that his grandson's introverted nature and quiet, thoughtful personality were due to a lack of Proper Adventure and Good Old-Fashioned Roughing It, like in the Good Old Days when he was a boy, he pulled out his old hat, coat and boots from when he was a trainer, grabbed the ancient family Torterra's pokeball, and presented them to Ben. When Ben protested, his grandpa hitched up the rickety old tree-cabin to the Torterra's tree, locked all of Ben's books and gadgets in the cellar, and told him that he could come back home (and have his stuff back) once he had reached the other side of the "American" (Orre-Unova, not sure what to call it) continent on Torterra-back. Thus, Benjamin Green became a rather reluctant Pokemon trainer, traveling across the land and slowly building up his Pokemon team (along with his personal library and supply of random, half-finished machinery, which he keeps in a smallish cavern inside his Torterra's shell (it's a <i>really big</i> Torterra)).

Things would have been fairly ordinary (or rather, as ordinary as a trans-continental journey on a ridiculously large Torterra can be) from that point on if not for the interference of the Almighty Pink Jirachi, an obscure deity of sorts worshipped by an equally obscure cult of Sinn immigrant gold-miners, of which Ben's great-great-grandfather was a part. No one is certain if, why, or how said Jirachi is actually Almighty and Pink; the members of His cult do not care to speculate on such cosmic matters best left to His Almighty Pinkness, everybody else flat-out doesn't care, and presumably, the Almighty Pink Jirachi Himself does not care to question His own nature for fear of finding out that He doesn't actually exist. Anyhow, at around the time Ben set out on his journey, the Almighty Pink Jirachi was conveniently reminded by a certain Pichu (whom I shall get to later) that Ben's great-great-grandfather had once prayed to Him for rain to end the great drought in the region, after which (specifically, five years later, after all of his crops had died and he had given up farming, when some guy with a Politoed was passing through town) it had, in fact, rained, and that his descendants were therefore indebted to Him. Being that He was Almighty (or possibly because some random Pokemon had once skill-swapped Insomnia onto Him - He tends to be rather forgetful in spite of His Almightiness), the Almighty Pink Jirachi was not bound by such insignificant constraints as the thousand-year sleep cycles of mere ordinary Jirachi, and thus spent rather a lot of time sitting around in some long-lost cave somewhere and twiddling his thumbs. Being of the opinion that the material universe, flawed and rather silly as it was, must exist primarily for his amusement, the Almighty Pink Jirachi decided to collect on the debt unknowingly owed to Him by making Benjamin Green his own personal Cosmic Plaything.

And so, from that point on, Ben's travels across the continent have been filled with paranormal encounters, feuding ancient conspiracies, alien abductions, monsters of the day, small towns desperately in need of a hero (or Ben), and gratuitous slapstick comedy (and also some gadget-peddling on the side - Giant Torterra aren't just going to feed themselves (or rather, they ARE, via photosynthesis, but this particular one needs to do more than just lie around absorbing sunlight and conserving energy all day)).

Finally, Ben's goal of reaching the east coast - and therefore his return home - drawing near, the Almighty Pink Jirachi decided that the situation called for drastic action. Thus, he decided to toss Ben in with the WAAPT gang, sit back, and enjoy the results.

(Aaand... that was WAY too long, but I figured that the Almighty Pink Jirachi required some explaining (and will still probably require more before this application makes its way through the approval process))

And now, the Pokemon team...

  • The glorified mobile home, Terry the Torterra
    • Gender: Male
    • Ability: Overgrow
    • Description: When Benjamin's great-great-grandpa came to Orre, he brought with him a single Pokemon, a Torterra who would later come to be known as Terry (his "official" name being a nigh-unpronounceable string of a hundred words of Old Sinn). He hailed from an ancient family renowned throughout Sinnoh as the best Torterra-breeders in the region, for the rare subspecies that they specialized in far outlived the already impressive lifespan of ordinary Torterra and grew to phenominal size. Just as Ben's great-great-grandfather had received his Torterra from his father, who had received it from his father, and so on an so forth, he passed it down to his son, who passed it down to his son, Joseph Green, Ben's Grandfather. Joe had planned on keeping Terry for himself, believing that his son had dishonored the family legacy by denying the Almighty Pink Jirachi and taking up a career working with "that no-good, rotten, worthless, newfangled, Pink-forsaken machinery." However, he eventually decided that Ben needed Terry (and the ensuing adventure) more than he did, thus handing the ancient, iron-clad, bowling-ball-sized monstrosity of a pokeball down to his grandson.

Unlike the rest of Ben's team, Terry doesn't even communicate in Pokemon language. In spite of his colossal size, his brain still possesses the approximate size (and intelligence level)of a golf ball. He mostly just grunts and roars and such. If Terry were to battle, he could demolish towns and level small mountains with his tremendous Earthquake attacks. The operative word being "if." Terry is far too old and lazy to consider doing anything of the sort, and mostly just lies around munching on the grass while absorbing foes' attacks with his massive HP and defense stats and occasionally healing himself with synthesis. He is perpetually grumpy but never angry or violent, considering such things to take far more energy than they're worth. Getting him moving in the morning is a uphill battle that Ben has to face every day, and he will often stop to eat whatever looks interesting (fortunately, his teeth are extremely blunt and made for chewing plants, so Ben can usually convince him to spit people out before too much damage is done). Once his crotchety old bones finally get moving, however, his stamina is tremendous, and his Rock Climb move means that even the steepest of mountains cannot stand in his way. Ben's cabin is built in the tree on his back, and Ben's workshop occupies a cave in his shell. The excess water he drinks is stored in a small spring on his back, which also contains a few assorted small water Pokemon that he has accidentally picked up over the years.

    • Gender: Male

In stark contrast to Terry, Sandy loves to battle, often challenging opponents several times his size (not a difficult task for a Trapinch) and winning through sheer psychotic aggression of the "DEAR GOD, GET IT OFF OF ME!" variety. Despite his violent nature, Sandy is still quite childish, becoming equally excited about the opportunity to get an extra-large ice cream sundae or bite a Probopass's nose off. What might this destiny be, you ask? The Pichu's machinations are as convoluted and labyrinthine as... a very convoluted and labyrinthine thing, such that she has, on multiple occasions, ended up fighting herself nearly to the death in service of two masters on opposing sides of a conflict. She also likes Grepa berries.
